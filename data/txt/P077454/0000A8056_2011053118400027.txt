                         Project Development Objectives
                         Community Agriculture & Watershed Management Project (P077454)
                         Project Development Objective (from Project Appraisal Document)
                         The objective of the Project is to build productive assets of rural communities in selected mountain watersheds in ways that sustainably increase productivity
                         and curtail degradation of fragile lands and ecosystems.

                         Implementation Status Overview
                         The field implementation of the originally planned CAWMP activities is now completed, with a total of 3,845 subprojects financed by small grants with an aggregate value of over US

                         $7.4 million covering 402 villages. About 43,000 (75%) of the households participated in the farm productivity and land resource management investments, and 60% of the
                         households participated the rural infrastructure investments. Monitoring shows that 96,600 ha are covered by land resource management and other project activities that address land

                         and ecosystem degradation. Of this area, about 85,000 ha results from pasture-related activities, and about 3,000 ha results from the planting of over 1.3 million trees. An inventory
                         of rare and endangered plants in the four project watersheds has been finalized and 425 species have been described.

                         The Global Environment Facility (GEF) has approved the inclusion of Tajikistan's US$ 5.4 million grant proposal for a follow-on project in the GEF 5 work program.

                         The period for the ongoing project has been extended for an additional year, to April 30, 2012. The legal amendment which reflects this revision and associated adjustments is
                         effective.
