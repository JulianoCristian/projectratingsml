Amendment to the Development Credit Agreement, the PADME Project 
Agreement, the PAPME Project Agreement and the ADEX Project Agreement
Excellency, Sirs:
We refer to the Development Credit Agreement (the Agreement), the PADME Project 
Agreement (the "PADME Project Agreement"),  the PAPME Project Agreement (the "PAPME 
Project Agreement"), and the ADEX Project Agreement (the "ADEX Project Agreement") for 
the Private Sector Development Project, dated December 30, 1999, between the Republic 
of Benin (the "Borrower") and the International Development Association (the 
"Association"), and the Association and Association pour la Promotion et l’Appui au 
Développement des Micro-Entreprises ("PADME"), the Association and Agence pour la 
Promotion et l’Appui aux Petites et Moyennes Entreprises ("PAPME"), and the 
Association and  Association de Développement des Exportations ("ADEX"), respectively.
Further to the letter from the Minister of Finance and Economy dated May 9, 2001 
requesting amendments to the Agreement, the PADME Project Agreement, the PAPME Project 
Agreement and the ADEX Project Agreement, and the discussions held with the 
representatives of the Borrower, PADME, PAPME and ADEX, we are pleased to inform you 
that the Association hereby agrees to your request and consequently proposes to hereby 
amend the Agreement, the PADME Project Agreement, the PAPME Project Agreement and the 
ADEX Project Agreement as indicated in the attachment hereto.
Please indicate your agreement with the foregoing, on behalf of the Borrower and 
PADME, PAPME and ADEX, respectively, by countersigning and dating the form of 
confirmation on the five original copies of this letter provided and returning one 
original, as countersigned, to us.  Upon receipt by the Association of the copy of 
this letter countersigned by the four of you, this amendment shall become effective as 
of the date of countersignature of this letter by the authorized representative of the 
Borrower, as of the date of countersignature by the authorized representative of 
PADME, as of the date of countersignature by the authorized representative of PAPME, 
or as of the date of countersignature by the authorized representative of ADEX, 
whichever is the later.
Sincerely,
By /s/ Antoinette Sayeh
Country Director for Benin
Africa Region

Annex 2
"SCHEDULE 3
Procurement and Consultants’ Services
Section I:  Procurement of Goods
Part A:
General
Goods shall be procured in accordance with the provisions of Section I of the 
"Guidelines for Procurement under IBRD Loans and IDA Credits" published by the Bank in 
January 1995 and revised in January and August 1996, September 1997 and January 1999 
(the Guidelines) and the following provisions of this Section.
Part B:
International Competitive Bidding
1.
Except as otherwise provided in Part C of this Section, goods with an estimated 
value of $100,000 equivalent or more shall be procured under contracts awarded in 
accordance with the provisions of Section II of the Guidelines and paragraph 5 of 
Page  12
Appendix 1 thereto.
2.
The following provisions shall apply to goods to be procured under contracts 
awarded in accordance with the provisions of paragraph 1 of this Part B.
(a)
Preference for domestically manufactured goods
The provisions of paragraphs 2.54 and 2.55 of the Guidelines and Appendix 2 
thereto shall apply to goods manufactured in the territory of the Borrower.
(b)
Grouping of Contracts
To the extent practicable, contracts for goods shall be grouped in bid packages 
estimated to cost $100,000 equivalent or more each.
(c)
Notification and Advertising
The invitation to bid for each contract estimated to cost $100,000 equivalent or 
more shall be advertised in accordance with the procedures applicable to large 
contracts under paragraph 2.8 of the Guidelines.
Part C:
Other Procurement Procedures
1.
National Competitive Bidding
Goods estimated to cost $30,000 equivalent or more but not exceeding $100,000 
equivalent per contract,  up to an aggregate amount not to exceed $400,000 equivalent, 
may be procured under contracts awarded in accordance with the provisions of paragraph 
3.3 and 3.4 of the Guidelines.
2.
National and International  Shopping
Goods estimated to cost less than $30,000 equivalent per contract, up to an 
aggregate amount not to exceed $450,000 equivalent, may be procured under contracts 
awarded on the basis of national or international shopping procedures in accordance 
with the provisions of paragraphs 3.5 and 3.6 of the Guidelines.
Part D:
Review by the Association of Procurement Decisions
1.
Procurement Planning
Prior to the issuance of any invitations to prequalify for bidding or to bid for 
contracts, the proposed procurement plan for the Project shall be furnished to the 
Association for its review and approval, in accordance with the provisions of 
paragraph 1 of Appendix 1 to the Guidelines.  Procurement of all goods and works shall 
be undertaken in accordance with such procurement plan as shall have been approved by 
the Association, and with the provisions of said paragraph 1.
2.
Prior Review 
(a)
All contracts for goods and works estimated to cost $100,000 equivalent 
or more shall be subject to the Association’s prior review.
(b)
Notwithstanding the foregoing subparagraph, the first three contracts for 
goods and works, irrespective of their single or aggregate value, shall be submitted 
to the Association’s prior review.
(c)
With respect to each contract for international or national shopping 
estimated to cost the equivalent of $10,000 or more, the following procedures shall 
apply: 
(i) 
prior to the selection of any supplier under shopping 
Page  13
procedures the Borrower shall provide to the Association a 
report on the comparison and evaluation of quotations received;
(ii) 
prior to the execution of any contract procured under direct 
contracting or shopping procedures, the Borrower shall provide 
to the Association a copy of the specifications and the draft 
contract; and
(iii) the procedures set forth in paragraphs 2 (f), 2 (g) and 3 of 
Appendix 1 to the Guidelines shall apply.
3.
Post Review
With respect to each contract not governed by paragraph 2 of this Part, the 
procedures set forth in paragraph 4 of Appendix 1 to the Guidelines shall apply.
Section II:  Employment of Consultants
Part A:
General
Consultants’ services shall be procured in accordance with the provisions of the 
Introduction and Section IV of the "Guidelines: Selection and Employment of 
Consultants by World Bank Borrowers" published by the Bank in January 1997 and revised 
in September 1997 and January 1999 (the Consultant Guidelines) and the following 
provisions of Section II of this Schedule.
Part B:
Quality- and Cost-based Selection
1.
Except as otherwise provided in Part C of this Section, consultants’ services 
from firms costing $100,000 equivalent or more, shall be procured under contracts 
awarded in accordance with the provisions of Section II of the Consultant Guidelines, 
paragraph 3 of Appendix 1 thereto, Appendix 2 thereto, and the provisions of 
paragraphs 3.13 through 3.18 thereof applicable to quality- and cost-based selection 
of consultants.
2.
The following provisions shall apply to consultants’ services to be procured 
under contracts awarded in accordance with the provisions of the preceding paragraph.  
The short list of consultants for audit services for the Project, to the exception of 
Part B.3, and estimated to cost less than $50,000 equivalent per individual contract, 
may comprise entirely national consultants in accordance with the provisions of 
paragraph 2.7 of the Consultant Guidelines.
Part C:
Other Procedures for the Selection of Consultants 
1.
Least-Cost Selection
Services for the carrying out of audit requirements for the Project, estimated 
to cost less than $25,000 equivalent per contract may be procured under contracts 
awarded in accordance with the provisions of paragraph 3.1 and 3.4 of the Consultant 
Guidelines.
2. 
Selection Based on Consultants’ Qualifications
Services for training as well as other similar assignments estimated to cost 
less than $50,000 equivalent per contract may be procured under contracts awarded in 
accordance with the provisions of paragraphs 3.1 and 3.7 of the Consultants 
Guidelines.
3.
Commercial Practices
(a)
Technical advisory services to assist PAPME and PADME in carrying out 
activities under Part C.1 and C.2 of the Project and estimated to cost less than 
$100,000 equivalent may be procured in accordance with commercial practices acceptable 
to the Association.
(b)
Services to assist ADEX in carrying out activities under Part B.3 for the 
operation of the Matching Grant Fund may be procured in accordance with commercial 
Page  14
practices acceptable to the Association, and the relevant provisions of its Procedures 
Manual.
4.
Individual Consultants
Services for technical advisory assistance for tasks that meet the requirements 
set forth in paragraph 5.01 of the Consultant Guidelines shall be procured under 
contracts awarded to individual consultants in accordance with the provisions of 
paragraphs 5.1 through 5.3 of the Consultant Guidelines.
Part D:
Review by the Association of the Selection of Consultants
1.
Selection Planning 
(a)
Prior to the issuance to consultants of any requests for proposals, the 
proposed plan for the selection of consultants under the Project shall be furnished to 
the Association for its review and approval, in accordance with the provisions of 
paragraph 1 of Appendix 1 to the Consultant Guidelines.  Selection of all consultants’ 
services shall be undertaken in accordance with such selection plan as shall have been 
approved by the Association, and with the provisions of said paragraph 1.
(b)
Prior to the issuance to consultants of any requests for proposals, the 
proposed Procedures Manual referred to under Part C.1 (b) of the present Schedule 
shall be furnished to the Association for its review and approval.
2.
Prior Review
(a)
With respect to each contract for the employment of consulting firms 
estimated to cost the equivalent of $100,000 or more, technical evaluation report, 
terms of references and copy of proposals, if requested, shall be submitted to the 
Association and the procedures set forth in paragraphs 1, 2 (other than the third 
subparagraph of paragraph 2(a)) and 5 of Appendix 1 to the Consultant Guidelines shall 
apply.
(b)
With respect to each contract for: (i) the employment of consulting firms 
estimated to cost the equivalent of $50,000 or more, but less than the equivalent of 
$100,000; and (ii) amendments to contracts with consulting firms which upgrade the 
value of the base contract to $100,000 equivalent or more, the procedures set forth in 
paragraphs 1, 2 (other than the second subparagraph of paragraph 2 (a)) and 5 of 
Appendix 1 to the Consultant Guidelines shall apply.
With respect to contracts for audit services procured under Part C, paragraph 1 of the 
present Schedule, the shortlist, selection report, qualifications experience, terms of 
reference and terms of employment shall be submitted to the Association for its prior 
review.
With respect to each contract for: (i) the employment of individual consultants 
estimated to cost the equivalent of $50,000 or more; and (ii) amendments to individual 
contracts which upgrade the value of the base contract to $50,000 equivalent or more, 
the shortlist, selection report, qualifications, experience, terms of reference and 
terms of employment of the consultants shall be furnished to the Association for its 
prior review and approval.  The contract shall be awarded or the amendment shall 
become effective only after said approval shall have been given.
3.
Post Review
With respect to each contract not governed by paragraph 2 of this Part, the 
procedures set forth in paragraph 4 of Appendix 1 to the Consultant Guidelines shall 
apply."
