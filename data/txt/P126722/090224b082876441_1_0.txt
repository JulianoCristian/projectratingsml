                         Project Development Objective (from Project Appraisal Document)

                         A.    Proposed PDO

                         The Project Development Objective is to support improvement of the quality and availability of water supply in selected service areas of the Armenian Water and
                         Sewerage Company.

                         This is a repeater operation. The original project is scheduled to close in February 28, 2012, with satisfactory PDO and implementation rating. This Project will replicate
                         the original project activities in three other cities and scale up the institutional development and sustainability measures.

                         The Project has made a good progress since the latest implementation support mission. Key project performance indicators were reviewed, and these are mostly on track with some
                         of them awaiting results of civil works that are on-going in Ashtarak and Echmiatsin and some that are nearing completion in Masis. The mission carried out field trips to project sites
                         in Masis, Ashtarak and Echmiatsin. Overall, the team is satisfied with the quality of works. The Project has been advancing well with most of procurements and project activities on
                         track. The project has accumulated savings of $2 million and during the mid-term review it was agreed to utilize this saving to: (a) extend on-going rehabilitation works in water supply

                         systems rehabilitation in Echmiatsin and Ashtarak; (b) procure chlorination equipment; (c) procure vehicles for branches to improve operational efficiency of the utility by introducing
                         new approach in the methodology of water meter data collection processes, improving accuracy of the billing calculations. The procurement of this additional works and goods is in
                         progress. Details are provided in the subsequent sections of the ISR.
