                         Implementation Status Overview
                         The disaster risk reduction and management program, as stated in the Policy Matrix, substantially remains in place. The project became effective in the latter half of 2011, and it is on
                         a path to achieving its expected outcomes and development objectives.
Public Disclosure Copy




                         - 80 provinces have established their Disaster Risk Reduction and Management Offices (DRRMOs) vis a vis the original target of 14.
                         - NDRRMC, DILG, DBM and the Civil Service Commission issued Joint Memorandum Circular No. 2014-1 in April 2014, setting forth the implementing guidelines for Local DRRM
                         Offices and Barangay DRRM Councils.
                         - The Project Monitoring and Evaluation System for Disaster Risk Reduction and Management (PMESD) to track disaster-related financing was developed, and arrangements for
                         hosting are being made between the Department of Science and Technology and the Office of Civil Defense.
                         - A joint circular was issued by the Department of Budget and Management (DBM), Department of Interior and Local Government (DILG), and OCD in March 2013 to provide
                         guidelines on the utilization of Local Disaster Risk Reduction and Management Funds.
                         - The capacity building for Post-disaster Needs Assessment has been completed with the submission of 15 sector-specific guidance notes for PDAs. Training was provided in
                         response to Typhoon Pablo and Typhoon Yolanda. Multi-sectoral disaster preparedness drills and training are conducted periodically.
                         - 54 provinces have prepared Provincial Development and Physical Framework plans taking into account disaster risk and climate change considerations. By the end of 2013, the
                         National Economic Development Authority expects that all 81 provinces will submit their plans, vis a vis the target of 30 provinces.
                         - The Department of Health (DOH) completed assessment/audit of 199 hospitals in Metro Manila and Luzon against the original target of 100.

                         - The Department of Public Works and Highways (DPWH) has retrofitted/reconstructed 10 bridges in Metro Manila, meeting the target of 10.
                         - The Department of Social Welfare and Development (DSWD) conducted disaster risk reduction training in 24,026 villages across 1,188 municipalities , exceeding the target of 1,000
                         communities. Three DSWD Field Offices are covered by a community-based post-disaster response window. With the recent effectiveness of the National CDD Program, 16 out of
                         17 regional DSWD Field Offices will have such a window.
