Amendment to the Loan Agreement
Excellency, 
 
Please refer to the Loan Agreement (the Loan Agreement) between the Republic 
of Ecuador (the Borrower) and the International Bank for Reconstruction and 
Development (the Bank) dated September 25, 1998 for the above-captioned Project, as 
amended. In this letter, all the terms defined in the Loan Agreement have the same 
meaning as therein set forth. 
 
We are pleased to inform you that pursuant to your request, dated January 7, 
2002, and our discussions on the subject, the Bank hereby agrees to amend the Loan 
Agreement so as to update the Project financial management, disbursement and 
procurement arrangements. 
 
Consequently, the Loan Agreement is amended as follows: 
1. 
Paragraph (g) of Section 1.02 is amended to read as follows: 
 
“(g) 
‘Indicators’ means the performance monitoring indicators for the Project 
set forth in Attachment II of the Amendment Letter dated April 5, 2002;” 
2. 
Subparagraph (ii) of Paragraph (b) of Section 4.01 is amended to read as follows: 
“(b)  
The Borrower shall: 
 
(ii)  
furnish to the Bank as soon as available, but in any case not later 
than six (6) months after the end of each such year: (A) certified 
copies of the financial statements referred to in paragraph (a) of 
this Section for such year as so audited; and (B) an opinion on such 
financial statements, records and accounts and a report of such 
audit by said auditors, of such scope and in such detail as the Bank 
shall have reasonably requested; and”.

The table in paragraph 1 of Schedule 1 to the Loan Agreement is amended to read

“Part A
:

Goods and works shall be procured in accordance with the provisions of Section I

“(a)  
Grouping of Contracts
To the extent practicable, contracts for goods shall be grouped in bid packages estimated 
to cost $250,000 equivalent or more each.”. 
6. 
Part C of Section I of Schedule 4 is amended to read as follows: 
“1. 
National Competitive Bidding
(a) 
Works estimated to cost less than $500,000 but more than $100,000 
equivalent per contract, up to an aggregate amount not to exceed $3,500,000 equivalent; 
and  
(b) 
goods (other than pharmaceuticals and medical supplies) estimated to cost 
less than $250,000 but more than $50,000 equivalent per contract, up to an aggregate 
amount not to exceed $3,400,000 equivalent,  
shall be procured under contracts awarded in accordance with the provisions of 
paragraphs 3.3 and 3.4 of the Guidelines. In the procurement of goods and works under 
the provisions of this paragraph, the Borrower shall use standard bidding documents 
satisfactory to the Bank. 
2. International 
Shopping
Pharmaceuticals and medical supplies estimated to cost $100,000 or less but more 
than $50,000 equivalent per contract, up to an aggregate amount not to exceed 
$1,800,000 equivalent, may be procured under contracts awarded on the basis of 
international shopping procedures in accordance with the provisions of paragraphs 3.5 
and 3.6 of the Guidelines. 
3. National 
Shopping

Goods (other than pharmaceuticals and medical supplies) estimated to cost 
$50,000 equivalent per contract or less, up to an aggregate amount not to exceed 
$2,800,000 equivalent, may be procured under contracts awarded on the basis of national 
shopping procedures in accordance with the provisions of paragraphs 3.5 and 3.6 of the 
Guidelines. 
4. 
Procurement of Small Works
Works estimated to cost $100,000 equivalent per contract or less, up to an 
aggregate amount not to exceed $2,500,000 equivalent, may be procured under lumpsum, 
fixed-price contracts awarded on the basis of quotations obtained from three (3) qualified 
domestic contractors in response to a written invitation. The invitation shall include a 
detailed description of the works, including basic specifications, the required completion 
date, a basic form of agreement acceptable to the Bank, and relevant drawings, where 
applicable. The award shall be made to the contractor who offers the lowest price 
quotation for the required work, and who has the experience and resources to complete 
the contract successfully.
”

Paragraph 2 of Part D of Section I of Schedule 4 is amended as follows: 
“2.  
Prior Review

evaluation of quotations received; 
(ii)

provide to the Bank a copy of the specifications and the 
draft contract; and  
(iii)  
the procedures set forth in paragraphs 2 (f), 2 (g) and 3 of 
Appendix 1 to the Guidelines shall apply.”.

Selection

equivalent per contract, up to an aggregate amount not to exceed $3,000,000 may be 
procured under contracts awarded in accordance with the provisions of paragraphs 3.1 
and 3.6 of the Consultant Guidelines.”.  
11. 
Part C of Section II of Schedule 4 is amended to read as follows: 
“3. Post 
Review
With respect to each contract not governed by paragraph 2 of this Part, the 
procedures set forth in paragraph 4 of Appendix 1 to the Consultant Guidelines shall 
apply provided, however, that the terms of reference for assignments estimated to cost 
more than $15,000 equivalent shall be provided to the Bank for its prior review and 
approval.”. 
Please confirm your agreement to the foregoing by signing and dating this letter 
in the space provided below and returning the same to the undersigned. This amendment 
letter shall be executed in two counterparts, each of which shall be an original. 
Upon your confirmation, please return one fully executed original to us. The 
provisions of this amendment letter will become effective, as of the date of this letter, 
once the Bank has received one fully executed original of this amendment letter.
