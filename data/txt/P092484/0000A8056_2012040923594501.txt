                         Implementation Status Overview

                         Overall progress on program implementation is satisfactory. The program continues to provide essential support consisting primarily of technical assistance and analytical inputs
                         across the macro, revenue, expenditure and corporate directorates of the Ministry of Finance. A significant achievement against the Project Development Objective was the launch in
                         March 2012 of the Ministry of Finance Strategic Plan 2011-2030, which includes an Institutional Development and Capacity Building Plan directorate by directorate. In State Finances,
                         there has been significant progress in the preparation of the entire state budget by civil servants; publication of a Citizens Budget; implementation of Financial Management
Public Disclosure Copy




                         Information Systems upgrades; introduction of the Transparency Portals; strengthened control of state assets; improved governance of Autonomous Public Agencies; increased
                         support to line ministries; and completion of cash flow and bank reconciliations. In Revenue and Customs, there has been progress in stabilizing core tax administration functions;
                         improving the level of compliance; introducing petroleum tax audits; conducting capital gains tax assessments; and strengthening customs clearance and post-clearance audit. In
                         Policy Analysis and Research there have been improvements in data sources for macro modeling and analysis; continuing work on the Medium Term Fiscal Framework; the first
                         publication of national accounts (2004 - 2010); economic growth auditing based on the national accounts; analysis of sources of growth; publication of the Business Activity Survey,
                         Timor-Leste in Figures, and the Census; and amendments to the Petroleum Fund Law. Corporate Services are now implementing the Strategic Plan;conducting a workforce mapping
                         exercise; and upgrading IT systems. Program management by the Ministry of Finance has improved substantially over the past twelve months, particularly in resource planning,
                         recruitment of consultants, and reporting.
