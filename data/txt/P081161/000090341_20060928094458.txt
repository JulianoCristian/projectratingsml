Project Components:
a)  Regional Water Supply Schemes 
1. Dianjiang: 30,000 m3/d water supply plant, 50km distribution network 
2. Songji: 155,000 m3/d raw water extraction from Yangtze river, pumping 
station, 35km trunk main,

3. Tongguanyi: 350,000 m
3
/d raw water extraction from Yangtze river, 
112km trunk distribution network, pumping stations 
4. Tongnan: 1,000 m
3
/d water supply; 5,000 m3/d water plant; 120km 
distribution network 
5. Yongchuan: Linjiang: 1,500 m
3
/d water plant, 145 km network; Zhangjia: 
500 m
3
/d plus 2.5 km network; Jinlong: 1,000 m3/d plus 113 km 
network. 
b) Flood Protection 
1. Nanchuan: 9.6 km flood control embankment, bund road 5.12km, 4 
bridges, sewage pipeline 5.1 km. 
2. Rongchang: 10.2 km embankment, 8.9 km bund road for Laixi; 1.2km 
embankment, 800 m bund road for Chishui; 5 bridges 
3. Shizhu: 6km embankment 
c) Roads 
1. Qiangjiang: 8.5 km Class II urban road, four lanes with separate NMV 
lanes, one bridge, 1.2 km connection road. 
2. Shizhu: 5.3 km class II link road between Nanbin town and Chengdu-
Shanghai expressway 
3. Tongnan: 11 km Class III road between Tongba and Chengdu-
Chongqing Expressway 
4. Yubei: 14 km 2-lane Class II road linking Tongjing with Yu-Lin 
Expressway (incl. 8.5 km rehabilitation of existing road) 
d) Environmental 
Sanitaton 
Public Toilet Rehabilitation: Upgrading and reconstruction of about 200 
public toilets. 
e)  Institutional Strengthening and Training  
1. Design Review and Advisory (DRA) services, for design review and 
certification, during detailed design and bid document preparation 
(conforming to design and implementation requirements), to be 
incorporated the legal agreements. The DRA consultant will have an

3
enhanced ‘sign-off’ (certification) responsibility for review of detailed 
designs, geotechnical and topographic investigations, and review bid 
documents prior to invitation. 
2. Construction supervision and contract management to FIDIC terms, for 
all project towns.  
3. Capacity building (institutional and financial) support for utility 
companies, and the district and county governments 
4. Carrying out studies on solid waste management, ground water, regional 
water supply price policy, etc. 
5. Support on management and operation of local governments. 
6. Organizing study tour and training.
