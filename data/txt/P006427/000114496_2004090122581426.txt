LOAN AGREEMENT
AGREEMENT, dated August 25, 1993, between FEDERATIVE REPUBLIC OF BRAZIL (the 
Borrower) and INTERNATIONAL BANK FOR RECONSTRUCTION AND DEVELOPMENT (the Bank).
WHEREAS: (A) the Borrower, having satisfied itself as to the feasibility and 
priority of the Project described in Schedule 2 to this Agreement, has requested the 
Bank to assist in the financing of the Project;
(B)
Part A of the Project will be carried out by the States of Ceara, 
Maranhao, Sergipe and Pernambuco (the Participating States, as hereinafter defined) 
with the Borrower's assistance and, as part of such assistance, the Borrower will make 
available to the Participating States a portion of the proceeds of the Loan as 
provided in this Agreement; and
WHEREAS the Bank has agreed, on the basis, inter alia, of the foregoing, to 
extend the Loan to the Borrower upon the terms and conditions set forth in this 
Agreement and in the Project Agreements of even date herewith between the Bank and 
each of the Participating States;
NOW THEREFORE the parties hereto hereby agree as follows:

General Conditions; Definitions

Agreements" of the Bank, dated January 1, 1985, with the modifications set forth below

The last sentence of Section 3.02 is deleted.
 
(b)

ARTICLE II
The Loan
Section 2.01. The Bank agrees to lend to the Borrower, on the terms and 
conditions set forth or referred to in the Loan Agreement, various currencies that 
shall have an aggregate value equivalent to the amount of two hundred and twelve 
million dollars ($212,000,000), being the sum of withdrawals of the proceeds of the 
Loan, with each withdrawal valued by the Bank as of the date of such withdrawal.
Section 2.02. (a) The amount of the Loan may be withdrawn from the Loan Account 
in accordance with the provisions of Schedule 1 to this Agreement for expenditures 
made (or, if the Bank shall so agree, to be made) in respect of the reasonable cost of 
goods and services required for the Project described in Schedule 2 to this Agreement 
and to be financed out of the proceeds of the Loan.
(b)
The Borrower, shall, for purposes of the Project, open and maintain in 
dollars a special deposit account in a commercial bank on terms and conditions 
Page  4
satisfactory to the Bank, including appropriate protection against set-off, seizure or 
attachment. Deposits into, and payments out of, the Special Account shall be made in 
accordance with the provisions of Schedule 5 to this Agreement.
Section 2.03. The Closing Date shall be December 31, 1998 or such later date as 
the Bank shall establish. The Bank shall promptly notify the Borrower of such later 
date.
Section 2.04. The Borrower shall pay to the Bank a commitment charge at the rate 
of three-fourths of one per cent (3/4 of 1%) per annum on the principal amount of the 
Loan not withdrawn from time to time.
Section 2.05. (a) The Borrower shall pay interest on the principal amount of the 
Loan withdrawn and outstanding from time to time, at a rate for each Interest Period 
equal to the Cost of Qualified Borrowings determined in respect of the preceding 
Semester, plus one-half of one percent (1/2 of 1%). On each of the dates specified in 
Section 2.06 of this Agreement, the Borrower
shall pay interest accrued on the principal amount outstanding during the preceding 
Interest  Period, calculated at the rate applicable during such Interest Period.
(b)
As soon as practicable after the end of each Semester, the Bank shall 
notify the Borrower of the Cost of Qualified Borrowings determined in respect of such 
Semester.
(c)
For the purposes of this Section:
 
(i)
"Interest Period" means a six-month period ending on the date 
immediately 
preceding each date specified in Section 2.06 of this 
Agreement, beginning 
with the Interest Period in which this 
Agreement is signed.
(ii)
"Cost of Qualified Borrowings" means the cost, as reasonably 
determined by the 
Bank and expressed as a percentage per annum, of the 
outstanding borrowings of 
the Bank drawn down after June 30, 1982, 
excluding such borrowings or portions 
thereof as the Bank has allocated 
to fund: (A) the Bank's investments; and (B) 
loans which may be made by 
the Bank after July 1, 1989 bearing interest rates 
determined otherwise 
than as provided in paragraph (a) of this Section.
(iii)
"Semester" means the first six months or the second six months of 
a calendar 
year.
 
(d)
On such date as the Bank may specify by no less than six months' notice 
to the Borrower, paragraphs (a), (b) and (c) (iii) of this Section shall be amended to 
read as follows:
"(a)  The Borrower shall pay interest on the principal amount of 
the Loan 
withdrawn and outstanding from time to time, at a rate for 
each Quarter equal to the 
Cost of Qualified Borrowings determined in respect of 
the preceding Quarter, plus 
one-half of one percent (1/2 of 1%). On 
each of the dates specified in Section 2.06 
of this Agreement, the Borrower 
shall pay interest accrued on the principal amount 
outstanding during the 
preceding Interest Period, calculated at the rates applicable 
during such 
Interest Period."
"(b)  As soon as practicable after the end of each Quarter, the 
Bank shall 
notify the Borrower of the Cost of Qualified Borrowings 
determined in respect of such 
Quarter."
 
"(c) (iii) `Quarter' means a three-month period commencing on 
January 1, April 
1, July 1 or October 1 in a calendar year."
 
Section 2.06. Interest and other charges shall be payable semiannually on 
February 1 and August 1 in each year.
Section 2.07. The Borrower shall repay the principal amount of the Loan in 
accordance with the amortization schedule set forth in Schedule 3 to this Agreement.
Page  5
Section 2.08. The Minister of Education and Sports of the Borrower is designated 
as representative of the Borrower for the purposes of taking any action required or 
permitted to be taken under the provisions of Section 2.02 of this Agreement and 
Article V of the General Conditions.
ARTICLE III
Execution of the Project
Section 3.01. (a) The Borrower declares its commitment to the objectives of the 
Project as set forth in Schedule 2 to this Agreement, and, to this end:
(i)
shall carry out Part B of the Project with due diligence and 
efficiency and in 
conformity with appropriate administrative, financial 
and educational 
practices and shall provide, promptly as 
needed, the funds, facilities, 
services and other resources 
required for such Part of the Project (including, 
inter alia, the 
funds required to meet all operation and maintenance costs not 
financed by the proceeds of the Loan); and
(ii)
without limitation or restriction upon any of its other 
obligations under the 
Loan Agreement, the Borrower shall: (A) 
cooperate with the Participating 
States in carrying out Part A of 
the Project; (B) cause the Participating 
States, through the 
Subsidiary Agreements, to carry out their respective parts 
of such Part 
of the Project and to perform all their obligations set forth in 
the 
Project Agreements; and (C) shall take or cause to be taken all action, 
including the provision of funds, facilities, services and other resources, 
necessary or appropriate to enable the Participating States to perform such 
obligations (including, inter alia, those funds required to meet all 
operation 
and maintenance costs not financed by the proceeds of the 
Loan), and shall not 
take or permit to be taken any action which 
would prevent or interfere with 
such performance.
 
(b)
The Borrower shall transfer part of the proceeds of the Loan allocated to 
Categories (1) through (5) of the table set forth in paragraph 1 of Schedule 1 to this 
Agreement to the Participating States under Subsidiary Agreements to be entered into 
between the Borrower and each of the Participating States, satisfactory to the Bank, 
such Agreements to include provisions whereby such Participating States shall be 
obligated, inter alia, to:
(i)
without limitation or restrictions upon the obligation of the 
Borrower set 
forth in Section 3.01 (a) above, provide funds 
equivalent to at least 23% of 
the amounts required for the carrying 
out of their respective activities under 
Part A of the Project, 
additionally to the funds required to finance all 
operational 
and maintenance costs related to the carrying out of
such activities not financed by the Loan;
(ii)
distribute the books and didactic materials received under Part 
A.2 of the 
Project;
 
(iii)
adequately supervise the performance of teachers, directors and 
other educational 
staff;
 
(iv)
allow and encourage teachers, directors and other educational 
staff to obtain 
training under Part A.3 of the Project; and
 
(v)
provide all the information required by the Borrower and the Bank, 
related to 
the carrying out of Part A of the Project, for the purposes 
of Sections 3.06, 
3.07 and 3.08 of this Agreement.
 
(c)
The Borrower shall exercise its rights under each Subsidiary Agreement in 
such manner as to protect the interests of the Borrower and the Bank and to accomplish 
the purposes of the
Loan, and, except as the Bank shall otherwise agree, the Borrower shall not assign, 
Page  6
amend, abrogate or waive any Subsidiary Agreement or any provision thereof.
Section 3.02. (a) Except as the Bank shall otherwise agree, procurement of the 
goods, works and consultants' services required for the Project and to be financed out 
of the proceeds of the Loan shall be governed by the provisions of Schedule 4 to this 
Agreement.
(b)
Standard bidding documents and letters of invitation satisfactory to the 
Bank shall be utilized in the procurement of works, goods and consultants' services 
(as applicable) required for the Project and to be financed under the Loan.
Section 3.03. The Bank and the Borrower hereby agree that the obligations set 
forth in Sections 9.04, 9.05, 9.06, 9.07, 9.08 and 9.09 of the General Conditions 
(relating to insurance, use of goods and services, plans and schedules, records and 
reports, maintenance and land acquisition, respectively) in respect of Part A of the 
Project shall be carried out by each of the Participating States, pursuant to Section 
2.03 of the respective Project Agreement.
Section 3.04. The Borrower shall, through SEF:
(a)
by not later than September 30, 1993, develop, install and thereafter 
maintain, a computerized information system, satisfactory to the Bank, for recording 
the financial commitments
and disbursements under the Project, as part of the management information system 
referred to in paragraph (b) below, such system to be used in UNESP, UESCN and in each 
UESP; and
(b)
by not later than June 30, 1994, establish and thereafter maintain a 
management information system related to the carrying out of the activities under the 
Project, satisfactory to
the Bank.
Section 3.05. Without limitation or restriction upon the provisions of Section 
3.01 above, the Borrower shall, until completion of the Project:
(a)
maintain, within SEF, UNESP and UESCN, as referred to in Section 6.01 (d) 
of this Agreement; and
(b)
provide, promptly as needed, the facilities, services and other resources 
required for the discharge of UESCN's and UNESP's functions under the Project.
Section 3.06. The Borrower shall, through SEF:
(a)
by not later than October 31, 1993, and, thereafter, by not later than 
May 30 of each year, starting on May 30, 1994, furnish to the Bank a report, 
satisfactory to the Bank, assessing
the progress made by each Participating State in achieving its respective AMET;
(b)
by not later than June 30 of each year, starting on June 30, 1994, and 
based on the findings of the reports referred to in paragraph (a) above, determine, in 
consultation with, and in a manner satisfactory to, the Bank, the amounts to be 
allocated to each Participating State, for the carrying out of Part A.4 of the Project 
during the subsequent calendar year;
(c)
(i) by not later than September 30 of each year, starting on September 
30, 1993, furnish to the Bank, for comments, a draft of the PAA for the subsequent 
twelve-month period starting on January 1 of each year; and (ii) by not later than 
November 30 of each year, starting on November 30, 1993, review, jointly with the 
Bank, any such draft, and furnish to the Bank the final version of any such PAA, 
taking into account the comments made by the Bank;
(d)
monitor the execution, by each of the Participating States, of their 
respective parts of Part A of the Project;
(e)
contract and supervise technical assistance, studies and training to be 
provided under Part B of the Project, according to a timetable satisfactory to the 
Bank;

(f)
coordinate the execution of Part B of the Project;
 
(g)
by not later than December 31, 1993, revise the Operational Manual, in a 
manner satisfactory to the Bank; and
(h)
by not later than January 31, 1994, furnish to the Bank terms of 
reference, satisfactory to the Bank, for a study of the efficiency of the national 
school lunch program, such terms of reference to include a timetable for the carrying 
out of such study.
Section 3.07. Without limitation upon the provisions of Section 9.01 of the 
General Conditions, the Borrower shall, through SEF:
(a)
by not later than February 28 and August 31 of each year, until the

the Project, of such scope and
in such detail as the Bank may reasonably request;
(b)
by not later than October 31, 1993, and by not later than June 30 of each 
subsequent year, starting in 1994, excepting in 1995, review, jointly with the Bank 
and the Participating States, the progress in the execution of the Project, any such 
review to be based upon the reports referred to in Section 3.06 (a) and 3.07 (a) of 
this Agreement; and
(c)
promptly take or cause to be taken all such remedial action as shall be 
required for the efficient execution of the Project or the achievement of its 
objectives, as recommended by any
such review.
Section 3.08. Without limitation upon the provisions of Section 9.01 of the 
General Conditions and of Section 3.07 of this Agreement, the Borrower shall:
(a)
by not later than May 31, 1995, furnish to the Bank the terms of 
reference for the review referred to in paragraph (b) below, satisfactory to the Bank;
(b)
by not later than June 30, 1995 review, jointly with the Bank and the 
Participating States, the progress in the carrying out of the Project, the performance 
of their respective obligations under this Agreement, the Project Agreements, and the 
Subsidiary Agreements, and the achievement of the objectives of the Project, such 
review to be based on the terms of reference referred to in paragraph (a) above; and
(c)
if, as a result of the above review, the progress in the execution of the 
Project, or in the achievement of its objectives is not satisfactory, the Borrower 
shall promptly take or cause to be taken, all such remedial action, satisfactory to 
the Bank, as shall be necessary for the efficient execution of the Project or the 
achievement of its objectives.
Section 3.09. The Borrower shall, by not later than March 31 of each year, 
starting on March 31, 1994, prepare and furnish to the Bank a report, satisfactory to 
the Bank, showing:
(i)
the amounts transferred to each Participating State and Eligible 
Municipality 
for the financing of basic education, during the respective

year; and
 
(ii)
the amounts to be allocated to each Participating State for the 
financing of 
basic education during the year in which the report is

allocation to be made in accordance with 
criteria consistent with those set

dated December 31, 1992.

ARTICLE IV
Financial Covenants
Section 4.01. (a) The Borrower shall maintain or cause to be maintained records 
and separate accounts adequate to reflect in accordance with sound accounting 
practices the operations, resources and expenditures in respect of Part B of the 
Project of the departments or agencies of the Borrower responsible for carrying out 
such Part of the Project.
(b)
The Borrower shall:
 
(i)
have the records and accounts referred to in paragraph (a) of this 
Section 
including those for the Special Account for each 
fiscal year audited, in 
accordance with appropriate auditing 
principles consistently applied, by 
independent auditors 
acceptable to the Bank;
(ii)
furnish to the Bank as soon as available, but in any case not 
later than six 
months after the end of each such year, the report of 
such audit by said 
auditors, of such scope and in such detail as 
the Bank shall have reasonably 
requested; and
 
(iii)
furnish to the Bank such other information concerning said records 
and 
accounts and the audit thereof as the Bank shall from time 
to time reasonably 
request.
 
(c)
For all expenditures with respect to which withdrawals from the Loan 
Account were made on the basis of statements of expenditure, the Borrower shall:
(i)
maintain or cause to be maintained, in accordance with paragraph 
(a) of this 
Section, records and accounts reflecting such expenditures;
 
(ii)
retain, until at least one year after the Bank has received the 
audit report 
for the fiscal year in which the last withdrawal from the 
Loan Account or 
payment out of the Special Account was made, 
all records (contracts, orders, 
invoices, bills, receipts and other 
documents) evidencing such expenditures;
(iii)
enable the Bank's representatives to examine such records; and
 
(iv)
ensure that such records and accounts are included in the annual 
audit 
referred to in paragraph (b) of this Section and that the 
report of such audit 
contains a separate opinion by said auditors 
as to whether the statements of 
expenditure submitted during such fiscal 
year, together with the procedures 
and internal controls involved in 
their preparation, can be relied upon to 
support the related 
withdrawals.
ARTICLE V
Remedies of the Bank
Section 5.01. Pursuant to Section 6.02 (l) of the General Conditions, the 
following additional events are specified:
(a)
any of the Participating States shall have failed to perform any of its 
obligations under the respective Project Agreement;
(b)
the Borrower or any of the Participating States shall have failed to 
perform any of their obligations under the respective Subsidiary Agreement; and
(c)
as a result of events which have occurred after the date of the Loan 
Agreement, an extraordinary situation shall have arisen which shall make it improbable 
that the Participating States will be able to perform their obligations under the 
respective Project Agreement.
Page  9
Notwithstanding the rights contained in Section 6.02 of the General Conditions, 
it is understood that, if any of the events specified in paragraphs (a) and (b) above 
shall have occurred, the Bank may, by notice to the Borrower, suspend in whole or in 
part the right of the Borrower to make withdrawals from the Loan Account for 
expenditures incurred by the Participating State in question, for the Part of the 
Project affected by such event or, in the case of Part A.4 of the Project, for the 
State or Municipal Subproject affected by such event.
Section 5.02. Pursuant to Section 7.01 (h) of the General Conditions, the 
following additional event is specified, namely, that any event specified in 
paragraphs (a) or (b) of Section 5.01 of this Agreement shall occur and shall continue 
for a period of sixty days after notice thereof shall have been given by the Bank to 
the Borrower.

SCHEDULE 2
Description of the Project
The objectives of the Project are to increase student learning, reduce 
repetition and dropout, and increase graduation rates in the Participating States, 
through improvements in the
quality of state and municipal Basic Education and in the efficiency of education 
management.
The Project consists of the following parts, subject to such modifications 
thereof as the Borrower and the Bank may agree upon from time to time to achieve such 
objectives:
Page  13
Part A:
State-level Program
 
1.
Improvement of SEs' education management capabilities by: 
 
(a)
strengthening SEs planning capability, through:
 
(i)
provision of technical assistance to planning units in each SE for 
(A) 
analyzing financial and system data; (B) developing plans 
for school 
construction and maintenance, material and human 
resources development; (C) 
managing resources mobilization and 
allocation; and (D) overall education 
policy-making;
 
(ii)
carrying out of a study to define minimum school quality standards 
with 
respect to the deployment and level of human and physical 
resources; and
(iii)
provision of training to staff;
 
(b)
improving SEs' human resources management capability, through:
 
(i)
carrying out of studies to:
 
(A)
review the quality and timeliness of the personnel census 
database and 
its correspondence with the payroll database;
 
(B)
assess the procedures and practices in their human resources 
departments;
(C)
survey staffing requirements and salary schedules by 
functional task; 
and
 
(D)
analyze policies and practices on personnel compensation and 
incentives;
(ii)
provision of technical assistance to implement the recommendations 
of these 
studies, as appropriate; and
 
(iii)
provision of training to senior staff;
 
(c)
improving SEs' school quality monitoring, through:
 
(i)
establishment of a basic monitoring system of schools for each SE, 
in order to 
develop updated school profiles; and
 
(ii)
provision of technical assistance for the implementation and 
monitoring of the 
minimum standards identified by the study referred to 
in Part A.1 (a) (ii) of 
the Project, and for the dissemination of the 
respective information to the 
public;
 
(d)
developing a management information system for each SE, through:
 
(i)
provision of technical assistance for:
 
(A)
designing any such system and developing an integrated 
relational 
database of school profiles, personnel, 
materials and facilities; and
(B)
installation, testing, and routine and emergency maintenance 
of computer 
systems;
 
(ii)
acquisition and utilization of computer systems, including, inter 
alia, local 
area networks and relational database software; and
 
(iii)
provision of training on:
 
(A)
survey instrument development, data gathering and 
Page  14
compilation 
procedures, and data entry; and
 
(B)
computer fundamentals.
 
2.
Improvement of the quality of Basic Education in state and municipal schools, 
through the provision of books and didactic materials and technical assistance, 
including:
(a)
2 textbooks and 2 workbooks per year for each student in grades 1 and 2, 
and 4 textbooks and 2 workbooks per year for each student in grades 3 and 4;
(b)
2 curriculum guides and 3 textbooks to each teacher, every 2 years;
 
(c)
approximately 100 reading books for each classroom in 1993, and 
approximately 30 additional books for each classroom in each of the subsequent years, 
from 1994 to 1997; and
(d)
(i) development and implementation of an alternative book selection and 
distribution system; (ii) improvement of content quality and physical production 
specifications of books; and (iii) training of teachers and school directors in basic 
book conservation techniques.
3.
Improvement of the quality of Basic Education staff, through the provision of 
technical assistance, as follows:
(a)
planning, development and provision of training, of refresher seminars 
and of self-instruction materials for teachers, aiming at using effectively books and 
didactic materials and class time, and reducing repetition.
(b)
planning, development and provision of leadership training for school 
directors, administrators, and technical staff; and
(c)
planning, development and provision of school-based training focused, 
inter alia, on the school performance as a unit, teacher effectiveness, interaction 
among teachers, effectiveness of school directors, student performance and enthusiasm, 
and connection between the school and the community.
4.
Carrying out of State and Municipal Subprojects.
 
5.
Establishment of a fund within MEC to provide grants:
 
(a)
to schools which adopt promising innovations in Basic Education, 
particularly those aimed at reducing repetition; and 
(b)
for the carrying out of research evaluations of innovative Basic 
Education programs.
Part B:
National Program
 
1.
Improvement of SAEB through:
 
(a)
provision of scholarships for advanced degree or non-degree training in 
education evaluation;
(b)
provision of grants in order to foster the establishment of regional 
centers of excellence specialized in technical requirements of education evaluation; 
and
(c)
provision of technical assistance aiming at improving and consolidating 
SAEB.
2.
Development of the institutional capability of MEC in systematic policy 
formulation, analysis and review, through: 
(a)
provision of funds for the carrying out of studies on basic education;
 
(b)
dissemination of research findings and recommendations; and
Page  15
(c)
establishment of a computerized data bank of information on basic 
education standards and innovative policies and programs.
3.
Strengthening of MEC's capabilities in systematic planning and management in 
basic education, through:
(a)
provision of technical assistance to improve the efficiency of staff and 
managers in carrying out routine and strategic activities;
(b)
development and consolidation of an educational management information 
system, including statistical and bibliographical data banks; and
(c)
provision of training for mid-level managers on strategic planning and 
decision making.
4.
Evaluation of:
 
(a)
alternative financing mechanisms for the national textbook program; and
 
(b)
the national school lunch program.
 
5.
Provision of the technical assistance necessary to allow the carrying out of: 
(i) Part A.2 of the Project; and (ii) other parts of Part A of the Project.

The Project is expected to be completed by June 30, 1998.
