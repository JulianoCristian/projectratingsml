                         Project Development Objectives
                         Project Development Objective (from Project Appraisal Document)

                         The Project Development Objectives of the proposed project are to: (i) increase access of targeted underserved rural communities to quality basic health services
                         known to help reduce under-five child mortality; and (ii) support the development of strategic planning, regulatory, and monitoring mechanisms known to improve health
                         system performance.

                         Implementation Status Overview
                         The Project continues to show significant progress towards achieving the PDOs. Under the Extension of Coverage Strategy (EEC for its Spanish acronym) Health Protection Program
                         for the Poor and Vulnerable Populations (PSPV), the basic care package supported by this project, 213,059 beneficiaries were reached. Ten of the project's Component 1
                         performance indicators have increased compared to data captured during the first year of coverage . Key indicators are: percentage of pregnant women completing at least 3 pre-

                         natal controls by the 3rd gestational trimester from 20 to 36 percent, percentage of live births attended by skilled personnel from 6 to 27 percent, percentage of children (< 1 year of
                         age) with a complete vaccination scheme for their age from 26 to 59 percent.

                         The Panama Health Equity and Performance Improvement Project was approved on August 5, 2008 and became effective on December 8, 2008. As of January 25, 2013, the project
                         has disbursed US$22.97 million which represents 57.43% of the loan. The project's loan agreement has been restructured to address initial implementation delays the project
