ARTICLE II
The Credit
Section 2.01. The Association agrees to lend to the Borrower, on the terms and 
conditions set forth or referred to in the Development Credit Agreement, an amount in 
various currencies equivalent to three million five hundred thousand Special Drawing 
Rights (SDR 3,500,000).
Section 2.02. (a) The amount of the Credit may be withdrawn from the Credit 
Account in accordance with the provisions of Schedule 1 to this Agreement for 
expenditures made (or, if the Association shall so agree, to be made) in respect of 
the reasonable cost of goods and services required for the Project described in 
Schedule 2 to this Agreement and to be financed out of the proceeds of the Credit.
(b)
The Borrower may, for the purposes of Parts A.1, B.1, B.2 C and D of the 
Project, open and maintain in dollars a special deposit account in its Central Bank on 
terms and conditions satisfactory to the Association, to be titled Special Account for 
Procurement Unit. Deposits into, and payments out of, the Special Account for 
Procurement Unit shall be made in accordance with the provisions of Schedule 4 to this 
Agreement.
(c)
The Borrower may, for purposes of Parts A.2, B.3, B.4 and B.5 of the 
Project, open and maintain in dollars a special account in its Central Bank on terms 
and conditions satisfactory to the Association, to be titled Special Account for PEU.  
Deposits into, and payments out of the Special Account for PEU shall be made in 
accordance with the provisions of Schedule 5 to this Agreement.
(d)
Promptly after the Effective Date, the Association shall, on behalf of 
the Borrower, withdraw from the Credit Account and pay to itself the amount required 
to repay the principal amount of the Project Preparation Advance withdrawn and 
outstanding as of such date and to pay all unpaid charges thereon.  The unwithdrawn 
balance of the authorized amount of the Project Preparation Advance shall thereupon be 
cancelled.
Section 2.03. The Closing Date shall be December 31, 1998 or such later date as 
the Association shall establish. The Association shall promptly notify the Borrower of 
such later
date.
Section 2.04. (a) The Borrower shall pay to the Association a commitment charge 
on the principal amount of the Credit not withdrawn from time to time at a rate to be 
set by the Association as of June 30 of each year, but not to exceed the rate of 
one-half of one percent (1/2 of 1%) per annum.
(b)
The commitment charge shall accrue: (i) from the date sixty days after 
the date of t this Agreement (the accrual date) to the respective dates on which 
amounts shall be withdrawn by
the Borrower from the Credit Account or cancelled; and (ii) at the rate set as of the 
June 30 immediately preceding the accrual date and at such other rates as may be set 
from time to time thereafter pursuant to paragraph (a) above. The rate set as of June 
30 in each year shall be applied from the next date in that year specified in Section 
2.06 of this Agreement.
(c)
The commitment charge shall be paid: (i) at such places as the 
Page  4
Association shall reasonably request; (ii) without restrictions of any kind imposed 
by, or in the territory of, the Borrower; and (iii) in the currency specified in this 
Agreement for the purposes of Section 4.02 of the General Conditions or in such other 
eligible currency or currencies as may from time to time be designated or selected 
pursuant to the provisions of that Section.
Section 2.05. The Borrower shall pay to the Association a service charge at the 
rate of three-fourths of one percent (3/4 of 1%) per annum on the principal amount of 
the Credit withdrawn and outstanding from time to time.
Section 2.06. Commitment charges and service charges shall be payable 
semiannually on February 1 and August 1 in each year.
Section 2.07. (a) Subject to paragraphs (b) and (c) below, the Borrower shall 
repay the principal amount of the Credit in semi-annual installments payable on each 
April 15 and October 15 commencing April 15, 2006 and ending October 15, 2035. Each 
installment to and including the installment payable on October 15, 2015 shall be one 
percent (1%) of such principal amount, and each installment thereafter shall be two 
percent (2%) of such principal amount.
(b)
Whenever: (i) the Borrower's gross national product per capita, as 
determined by the Association, shall have exceeded $790 in constant 1985 dollars for 
five consecutive years; and (ii) the Bank shall consider the Borrower creditworthy for 
Bank lending, the Association may, subsequent to the review and approval thereof by 
the Executive Directors of the Association and after due consideration by them of the 
development of the Borrower's economy, modify the terms of repayment of installments 
under paragraph (a) above by requiring the Borrower to repay twice the amount of each 
such installment not yet due until the principal amount of the Credit shall have been 
repaid. If so requested by the Borrower, the Association may revise such modification 
to include, in lieu of some or all of the increase in the amounts of such 
installments, the payment of interest at an annual rate agreed with the Association on 
the principal amount of the Credit withdrawn and outstanding from time to time, 
provided that, in the judgment of the Association, such revision shall not change the 
grant element obtained under the above-mentioned repayment modification.
(c)
If, at any time after a modification of terms pursuant to paragraph (b) 
above, the Association determines that the Borrower's economic condition has 
deteriorated significantly, the Association may, if so requested by the Borrower, 
further modify the terms of repayment to conform to the schedule of installments as 
provided in paragraph (a) above.

SCHEDULE 2
Description of the Project
Page  10
The objectives of the Project are to assist the Borrower in: (a) implementing 
the reforms mandated by the Capitalization, Power Sector, Privatization and SIRESE 
Laws, including the refinement of the regulations thereto; (b) carrying out key 
transition activities which would ensure the long term sustainability of such reforms; 
(c) completing the restructuring of  the power sector assets and corporatizing the 
smaller utilities of each SA; and (d) completing the privatization of Borrower-owned 
power sector assets.
The Project consists of the following parts, subject to such modifications 
thereof as the Borrower and the Association may agree upon from time to time to 
achieve such objectives:
Part A:
Operations of the Technical Group and strengthening of the regulatory 
system for the power sector
1.
Technical Group
 
Supporting the Technical Group by means of providing the resources necessary for 
the operations thereof, including staff and specialized consultant services.
2.
Regulatory system
 
Assessment of the suitability of current regulations to the Power Sector Law 
(and if warranted, the pertinent provisions of the regulations to the SIRESE and 
Capitalization Laws) in light of the experience gained in the operation of the SIN up 
to September 30, 1996 and, if so required by the assessment, putting into effect, 
through the appropriate channels, amendments to such regulations, particularly on the 
following subjects: pricing, dispatch, quality of service, penalties, rights of way, 
terms and conditions for concessions and licenses, and environmental aspects.
Part B:
Transition requirements
 
1.
Pricing studies
 
Execution of one or more pricing studies:
(a)
to review or determine, as required, the economic costs of: (i) 
generation in the SIN; (ii) distribution in the SIN; and (iii) each SA whose 
generation and distribution activities
could be integrated in the future;
(b)
to determine the costs of the economically adapted SIN including the 
ancillary system reserve, reliability, and subtransmission costs;
(c)
to determine the economic costs of power supply at the retail 
distribution level; and
(d)
to determine a policy to provide life-line subsidies consistent with the 
regional environs and causing a minimal impact to market price signals.
2.
Wholesale power market
 
Putting in place phase 1 (the first of 2 phases) of the creation of the Comite 
Nacional de Despacho de Carga (national load dispatch committee), the entity charged, 
pursuant to the provisions of the Power Sector Law, with implementing a wholesale 
power market. Such Phase 1, will include the upgrading of the current regional 
national load dispatch and transmission system to enable the prompt adaptation of the 
present system to operate under the open access conditions and
effective commercial practices, the detailed design of phase 2, and the following 
activities:
(a)
adaptation of the existing  optimization and commercial settlement 
software and hardware of ENDE's Centro de Despacho de Carga (Load Dispatch Center);
(b)
determination of the organization (including regulations such as those 
for procedural and administrative operation), staffing, and operating budget, all for 
the aforesaid national load dispatch committee for both phases 1 and 2;
(c)
as a complement to activities under Part A.2 of the Project, reviewing, 
and as required, making recommendations, for refining the new dispatch rules, pricing 
and clearing house functions of the aforesaid committee in view of the experience 
gained in the first months of operation of SIN and in line with the pertinent 
Page  11
provisions of the Power Sector Law;
(d)
training of the personnel of the aforesaid Centro on load dispatch  
operational activities;  
(e)
determination of specifications for the procurement of metering and 
communications equipment to reinforce the existing dispatch facilities, and for 
assisting in international competitive bidding processes; and
(f)
designing in detail the configuration of the aforesaid committee to be 
implemented under phase 2, including: (i) determination of specifications for hardware 
and additional metering and communication systems and equipment therefor to be 
procured under such phase 2; and (ii) preparation of terms of reference and action 
plans for, and estimated costs of, implementation of phase 2.
3.
Environmental assessment
 
Carrying out the second and final phase of the environmental assessment of the 
power sector under the terms of reference prepared during the first phase (carried out 
under terms of reference dated August 19, 1994) and which include the following 
principal tasks:
(a)
the carrying out of: (i) environmental audits of those ENDE-owned diesel 
fired plants designated for capitalization and not visited during the aforesaid  first 
phase (i.e. plants below 4 MW); and (ii) surveys and measurements required to develop 
detailed action plans to mitigate environmental impacts identified during such first 
phase and relating to asbestos, diesel-oil and noise contamination by the plants 
designated for capitalization;
(b)
the carrying out of: audits to determine the specific environmental and 
social issues associated with the existing transmission facilities of the SIN and 
preparation of action plans to mitigate any adverse environmental impact revealed by 
each of said audits in a manner consistent with the prevailing Borrower's 
environmental legislation; 
(c)
a review of the most recent environmental policy activities of both the 
power sector and the Borrower's Ministry of Sustainable Development and Environment, 
development of recommendations for amendments to specific items of draft regulations, 
and assessment of the effectiveness of the existing and proposed framework for the 
implementation and enforcement of the Borrower's environmental policy and legislation 
in the power sector; and
(d)
a progress report on the mitigation work that was identified during the 
aforesaid first phase for the facilities of each of the three MECs specifically 
mentioned in Section 1.02 (c) of this Agreement, including a brief assessment of the 
obligations of said MECs to carry out said works under existing legislation, 
environmental regulations and terms of their generation licenses.
4.
Strengthening of NSE
 
Development of key functions to enable NSE to coordinate and monitor the power 
sector policy objectives, balance of energy supply and demand, and strategic energy 
mix, in urban and rural areas, to promote new direct private investment in the power 
sector and to ensure the dissemination of sectoral statistics, including the following 
activities:

development of a power sector strategic analysis capability to monitor 
and adapt the sectoral policy objectives to evolving circumstances in a manner 
consistent with the national energy strategy and legal framework, by means, inter 
alia, of: (i) the establishment and start-up operations of a unit charged with the 
responsibility of developing power sector policy and promoting private investment in 
the sector; (ii) the assessment of staff and other human resources requirements for 
said unit as well as its operational physical requirements including computer hardware 
and software, database, and communication tools therefor; and (iii) the execution, 
through such unit of the activities under (b) through (e) below;
(b)
(i)
definition of the role of the public sector in ensuring the supply 
of electricity in a privatized power sector, including public 
sector action regarding the response of power utilities and 
private sector enterprises;
(ii)
execution of the first indicative least cost investment plan for

(iii)

SCHEDULE 3
Procurement and Consultants' Services
Section I.
Procurement of Goods
 
Part A:
General
 
Goods shall be procured in accordance with the provisions of Section I of the 
"Guidelines for Procurement under IBRD Loans and IDA Credits" published  by the Bank 
in January 1995 (the Guidelines) and the following provisions of this Section, as 
applicable.
Part B:
Procurement Procedures
 
1.
Limited International Bidding
 
Goods and groups of goods estimated to cost the equivalent of $75,000 per 
contract or more without exceeding the equivalent of $250,000, and goods, which the 
Bank agrees can only be purchased from a limited number of suppliers, regardless of 
the cost thereof, shall be procured under contracts awarded in accordance with the 
provisions of paragraph 3.2 of the Guidelines.
2.
International and National Shopping
 
Goods estimated to cost less than $75,000 equivalent and $15,000 equivalent or 
less, per contract, may be procured under contracts awarded, respectively, on the 
basis of international shopping procedures and national shopping procedures, all in 
accordance with the provisions of paragraphs 3.5 and 3.6 of the Guidelines; provided, 
however, that contracts awarded under national shopping procedures shall not exceed 
the aggregate amount of $30,000 equivalent.
Part C:
Review by the Association of Procurement Decisions
 
1.
Procurement Planning
 
Prior to the issuance of any invitation to prequalify for bidding or to bid for 
contracts, the proposed procurement plan for the Project shall be furnished to the 
Association for its review and approval, in accordance with the provisions of 
paragraph 1 of Appendix 1 to the Guidelines. Procurement of all goods shall be 
undertaken in accordance with such procurement plan as shall have been approved by the 
Association, and with the provisions of said paragraph.
2.
Prior Review
 
With respect to each contract for computer hardware and software, irrespective 
of its estimated cost, and each contract for other goods estimated to cost the 
Page  14
equivalent of $25,000 or more, the procedures set forth in paragraphs 2 and 3 of 
Appendix 1 to the Guidelines shall apply.
Section II.
Employment of Consultants
 
1.
Consultants' services shall be procured under contracts awarded, in accordance 
with the provisions of the "Guidelines for the Use of Consultants by World Bank 
Borrowers and by the World Bank as Executing Agency" published by the Bank in August 
1981 (the Consultant Guidelines). For complex, time-based assignments, the Borrower 
shall employ such consultants under contracts using the standard form of contract for 
consultants' services issued by the Association, with such modifications as shall have 
been agreed by the Association. Where no relevant standard contract documents have 
been issued by the Association, the Borrower shall use other standard forms agreed 
with the Association.
2.
Notwithstanding the provisions of paragraph 1 of this Section, the provisions of 
the Consultant Guidelines requiring prior Association review or approval of budgets, 
short lists, selection procedures, letters of invitation, proposals, evaluation 
reports and contracts shall not apply to: (a) contracts for the employment of 
consulting firms estimated to cost less than $60,000 equivalent each; or (b) contracts 
for the employment of individuals estimated to cost less than
$30,000 equivalent each. However, this exception to prior Association review shall not 
apply to: (a) the terms of reference for such contracts; (b) single-source selection 
of consulting firms; (c) assignments of a critical nature, as reasonably determined by 
the Association; (d) amendments to contracts for the employment of consulting firms 
raising the contract value to $60,000 equivalent or above; or (e) amendments to 
contracts for the employment of individual consultants raising the contract value to 
$30,000 equivalent or above.
Section III.
Special Provisions for the Employment of Consultants
 
In addition and without limitation to any other provisions set forth in this 
Schedule or the Consultant Guidelines, the following principles and rules of 
procurement shall expressly govern all procurement of consultants' services referred 
to in Section II of this Schedule.
1.
Foreign consultants shall not, as a condition for participating in the selection 
process, be required to enter into a joint venture agreement with local consultants.
2.
Foreign consultants shall not be required to legalize their proposals or any 
documentation related to such proposals with Bolivian authorities as a prerequisite 
for participating in the selection procedure.
3.
Foreign consultants shall not be required to be registered in the Registro 
Nacional de Consultoria (National Registry of Consultants) or any other registry as a 
prerequisite for participating in the selection procedure.
