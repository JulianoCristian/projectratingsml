                         Implementation Status Overview
                         The project has three components. The first component envisages rehabilitation of 350 km of roads in three provinces (Eastern, Northern and Uva). Component two would carry out a
                         road maintenance program. Component three is for institutional capacity building.

                         Overall:
                         • All the project components are progressing at an advance stage in all three provinces (Uva, Eastern and Northern). Overall the cumulative progress of the project is 90 percent

                         against 92 percent target in the three provinces for all three components (Part A 91%, Part B 94%, Part C 87%). Ten contracts have been completed (4 of 7 in Uva, 4 of 5 in EP, and
                         2 of 4 in NP). Six contracts are under implementation (3 in Uva, 1 in EP and 2 in NP), namely Uva-02, Uva-06, Uva-07; EP-07, NP-03R and NP-05R. Ten contracts have been
                         completed. Ongoing contracts under implementation in Uva province are expected to be completed in November, in Eastern province EP-07 is scheduled to be completed by the
                         September 30, 2014, and the two contracts in Northern Province NP-03R and NP-05R are scheduled to be completed by March 11, 2015. Overall time elapsed is 88 percent (loan

                         effectiveness to the loan closing).

                         • Road Maintenance- 89 contracts were awarded for maintenance, of this 82 contracts have been completed as of September 15, 2014. Recruitment of a Consulting firm to develop a
                         Road Maintenance Strategy for Uva Province has however run into delays due to the failure of negotiations with the first and second ranked firm. The Implementing agencies (Uva
                         and PCU) will hold a workshop with the stakeholders and come to some consensus way forward by early October, 2014.

                         • Institutional Capacity Building and Training. Of the total budget planned of US$4.3m (SLR 534.5) at current exchange rate parity, US$3.6m (SLR 444.7) have been incurred. About
                         87% of the local and foreign program has been completed. Overall 83% of the activities have been completed under this component (80% Capacity building part and 87% training
                         part). The post completion road user satisfactory survey has been initiated. The supervision and monitoring through the consultants in the three provinces (through PIUs, PCU) is
                         ongoing

                         • The overall financial progress of the project is 89% (Part A 83%, Part B 94%, Part C 84%). The Project confirmed that adequate budgetary provisions have been received for the
                         financial year.
