ANNEX 2
SCHEDULE 2
Description of the Project
The objectives of the Project are: (a) to improve the quality of Basic Education 
in the territory of the Borrower; (b) to increase student enrollment in Basic 
Education, including the enrollment of children from low-income families; and (c) to 
strengthen the management capacity of EEBAC in respect of Basic Education.
The Project consists of the following parts, subject to such modifications 
thereof as the Borrower and the Bank may agree upon from time to time to achieve such 
objectives:
Part A:   Educational Development
1.
Curriculum Reform
 
(a)
Carrying out of a national campaign to promote the introduction of a new 
curriculum for Basic Education.
(b)
Provision of technical assistance to SEEBAC in order to assist SEEBAC in 
introducing the new curriculum referred to in Part A.1(a) above in their respective 
public schools which provide Basic Education, including the provision of training 
required therefor.
(c)
Design and distribution of manuals for teachers and Parents' Associations 
to provide teachers and Parents' Associations in the area of Basic Education with 
information on the contents of the new curriculum referred to in Part A.1(a) above and 
to assist SEEBAC in monitoring the introduction of such curriculum in public schools 
providing Basic Education, including the provision of training required therefor.
(d)
Creation of additional curriculum resource centers in SEEBAC's regional 
offices in order to assist public schools providing Basic Education in adopting the 
new curriculum referred to in Part A.1 (a) above, including the provision of training 
to such centers'  personnel and the acquisition and utilization of the equipment 
required therefor.
(e)
Carrying out of Curriculum Innovations.
 
(f)
Design and distribution of guidelines for SEEBAC to manage the 
implementation of the new curriculum referred to in Part A.1(a) above.
(g)
Carrying out of a review on the progress made in introducing the new 
curriculum referred to in Part A.1(a) above in public schools providing Basic 
Education.
2.   Educational Materials
 
(a)
Design, production and distribution of textbooks and workbooks (which 
will be designed based on the new curriculum referred to in Part A.1(a) above) in the 
areas of mathematics, natural sciences, social sciences and Spanish for students of 
public schools attending first through eighth grades.
(b)
Design and distribution of teachers' guides that will enable the teachers 
to use the textbooks and workbooks referred to in Part A.2(a) above.
(c)
Acquisition and distribution of required educational materials (other 
than textbooks and workbooks referred to in Part A.2(a) above) under the new 
curriculum referred to in Part A.1(a) above for students attending public schools 
providing Basic Education.
Page  7
(d)
Acquisition and distribution of classroom didactic packages (each package 
consisting, inter alia, of a map, a globe and educational charts) for all public 
schools providing Basic Education.
(e)
Acquisition and distribution of school supplies consisting, inter alia, 
of notebooks, pencils and erasers for low-income students attending public schools 
providing Basic Education, in accordance with the criteria set forth in the 
Operational Manual.
(f)
Creation of school libraries for the benefit of pre-school classes and 
first through eighth grades.
(g)
Creation of an educational materials entity that will coordinate and 
manage: (i) the design, production, printing or acquisition (as the case may be) and 
distribution of textbooks, workbooks and other educational materials to public schools 
providing Basic Education and (ii) the sale of textbooks, workbooks and other 
educational materials to private schools providing Basic Education, including the 
provision of technical assistance and the acquisition and utilization of the equipment 
required therefor.
3.   Human Resources Development
 
(a)
Carrying out of an in-service training program (based on the new 
curriculum referred to in Part A.1(a) above) for school directors, school supervisors 
and teachers of public schools providing Basic Education consisting of: (i) training 
in the areas of mathematics, natural sciences, social sciences and Spanish; (ii) 
training on teaching techniques; and (iii) granting training certificates thereafter.
(b)
Carrying out of an in-service training program for school directors, 
school supervisors and teachers who have completed the training program referred to in 
Part A.3(a) above consisting of: (i) training on pedagogical skills, school management 
and teaching specialties; and (ii) granting associate, bachelors or masters degrees, 
as the case may be, thereafter.
(c)
Carrying out of a program which consists of providing pre-service 
training to candidates for teaching positions in the same areas described under the 
program referred to in Part A.3(a) above.
(d)
Provision of technical assistance to SEEBAC to strengthen its capacity  
to coordinate teacher training activities.
(e)
Carrying out of studies to evaluate the impact of the in-service teacher 
training program under Part A.3 (a), (b) and (c) above for the purposes of improving, 
if necessary, said training activities. 
4.   National Student Achievement
(a)
Provision of technical assistance to SEEBAC to strengthen its capacity to 
design, distribute, administer and evaluate a student achievement test for Basic 
Education, including the provision of training and the acquisition and utilization of 
the equipment required therefor and the printing and distribution of such test during 
Project implementation.
(b)
Provision of training to teachers of public schools providing Basic 
Education and SEEBAC's personnel in designing and analyzing each of the questions to 
be included in a student achievement test.
(c)
Carrying out of surveys within the Borrower's territory to assess the 
impact of school and socio-economic factors in student achievement.
(d)
Carrying out of an analysis of the student achievement test.
(e)
Carrying out of an evaluation of SEEBACs national student assessment 
program.
5.
School Nutrition Programs
 
(a)
Strengthening of school nutrition programs for the provision of food to 
low-income students attending urban and rural public schools providing Basic Education 
by means of training on nutritional matters to SEEBAC's personnel, the parents of the 
students attending such schools and other members of the community where such public 
Page  8
schools are located.
(b)
Provision of iron and vitamin A to the students attending the public 
schools benefited by the school nutrition programs referred to in Part A.5(a) above.
(c)
Design and implementation of an iron and vitamin A supplementation 
program for the benefit of students not benefited by the school nutrition programs 
referred to in Part A.5(a) above.
(d)
Provision of pharmaceuticals for the students being benefited by the 
school nutrition programs referred to in Part A.5 (a) above.
(e)
Institutional strengthening of INDOFANE in designing and executing 
programs on school nutrition and health, including the provision of technical 
assistance, equipment and training required therefor.
(f)
Carrying out of studies on: (i) the nutrition status and needs of 
children who attend public schools providing Basic Education; and (ii) the 
cost-effectiveness of schools' nutrition programs.
(g)
Design and implementation of information, education and communication 
programs on school nutrition, including the provision of educational materials and 
training to SEEBAC's personnel that will implement such programs.
6.   School Facilities
 
(a)
Construction of about 70 classrooms in the public schools providing Basic 
Education, to be selected in accordance with the criteria set forth in the Operational 
Manual.
(b)
Rehabilitation of classrooms in public schools providing Basic Education, 
to be selected in accordance with the criteria set forth in the Operational Manual.
(c)
(i) Carrying out of preventive maintenance of public schools providing 
Basic Education; and (ii) carrying out of routine maintenance activities of public 
schools providing Basic Education (including the improvement of classrooms of said 
schools, as appropriate), all to be selected in accordance with the criteria set forth 
in the Operational Manual.
(d)
Acquisition and utilization of classroom furniture for the public schools 
providing Basic Education, to be selected in accordance with the criteria set forth in 
the Operational Manual.
7.   Pilot Program to Support Private Schools
 
(a)
(i) Provision of technical assistance to SEEBAC in order to identify and 
employ NGOs for purposes of assisting SEEBAC in the identification and selection of 
private schools providing Basic Education to low-income students which are in need of 
school improvements; and (ii) carrying out of the identification and selection of 
private schools providing Basic Education mentioned in (i) herein.
(b)
(i) Provision of technical assistance to the private schools which have 
been identified and selected by NGOs as referred to in Part A.7 (a) (ii) above in 
order to improve such schools' facilities, and (ii) carrying out of minor works within 
the schools referred to in (i) herein in order to improve the health and educational 
services of such schools.
(c)
Provision of in-service training to teachers of the private schools which 
have been identified and selected by the NGOs as referred to in Part A.7(a)(ii) above.
(d)
Acquisition and distribution of educational and teaching materials for 
the private schools which have been identified and selected by NGOs as referred to in 
Part A.7 (a) (ii) above.
(e)
Institutional strengthening of SEEBAC's department of private schools in 
order to incorporate the private school management information system into SEEBAC's 
management information system.
8.
Pre- Primary Education
(a)
Construction or rehabilitation, as the case may be, of about 50 
classrooms in public schools which provide pre-primary education, said schools to be 
Page  9
selected in accordance with the criteria set forth in the Operational Manual.
(b)
Acquisition and utilization of classroom furniture and educational 
materials for those classrooms benefited under Part A.8 (a) above.
(c)
Carrying out of: (i) analyzes of the pre-primary education subsector; and 
(ii) studies to monitor and evaluate the activities under Part A.8 (a) and (b) above.
Part B:   Management Development
1.
Training Program for Administrators
 
(a)
Provision of technical assistance to SEEBAC in order to improve its 
management, budgetary, accounting and financial procedures, including the acquisition 
and utilization of the equipment required therefor.
(b)
Carrying out of a study on administrative deconcentration of SEEBAC.
 
(c)
Design and implementation of an ongoing training program for SEEBAC's 
administrative personnel based on the improved procedures resulting from the carrying 
out of Part B.1(a) above.
2.
Management Information System
 
(a)
Linkage of SEEBAC's national and sub-national offices, Affiliated 
Entities and urban public schools providing Basic Education (with more than 600 
students each) through the installation of a telecommunications network.
(b)
Installation of telephones in urban and rural public schools providing 
Basic Education which have met the criteria set forth in the Operational Manual.
(c)
Installation of a radio communication system in rural public schools 
providing Basic Education in order to improve the dissemination of educational and 
management information to students and teachers.
(d)
Improvement of SEEBAC's central data processing systems in order to 
incorporate such systems to SEEBAC's management information system, including the 
acquisition and utilization of the equipment required therefor.
(e)
Provision of training to SEEBAC's personnel and the directors of public 
schools providing Basic Education on the operation of the management information 
system referred to in Part B.2 (d) above.
3.
Parents' Associations
 
Design and implementation of a national program for Parents' Associations of 
public schools providing Basic Education in order to foster their participation in the 
Basic Education system, through: (a) the provision of training to such Parents' 
Associations in the areas of school organization and maintenance, leadership and fund 
raising; (b) the provision of training to SEEBAC's personnel in order to carry out the 
program referred to herein; (c) the acquisition and utilization of motorcycles for 
SEEBAC's personnel to enable such personnel to carry out their duties and 
responsibilities under the program referred to herein; (d) the design and distribution 
of the necessary information materials to the Parents' Associations with respect to 
the contents of the
program referred to herein; and (e) the carrying out of educational improvements.
4.
Monitoring and Evaluation System
 
Design and implementation of  a monitoring and evaluation system to assist 
SEEBAC in monitoring and evaluating the execution of the Project in accordance with 
the Project indicators and targets set forth in the Implementation Letter.
* * *
The Project is expected to be completed by December 31, 2002.".
