PROGRAM AGREEMENT 
 
AGREEMENT dated June 5, 2006, entered into between INTERNATIONAL 
BANK FOR RECONSTRUCTION AND DEVELOPMENT      (
“the Bank”) and 
PROVINCE OF PUNJAB acting by its Governor (“Punjab” or the “Program 
Implementing Entity”) (the “Program Agreement”) in connection with the Loan 
Agreement of same date between Islamic Republic of Pakistan (the “Borrower”) and the 
Bank (the “Loan Agreement”).  The Bank and the Program Implementing Entity hereby 
agree as follows: 
 
ARTICLE I - GENERAL CONDITIONS; DEFINITIONS 
 
1.01. 
The General Conditions (as defined in the Appendix to the Loan Agreement) 
constitute an integral part of this Agreement. 
 
1.02. 
Unless the context requires otherwise, the capitalized terms used in the Program 
Agreement have the meanings ascribed to them in the Loan Agreement or the General 
Conditions. 
 
ARTICLE II - PROGRAM
 
2.01. 
The Program Implementing Entity declares its commitment to the objectives of 
the Program.  To this end, the Program Implementing Entity shall carry out the Program 
in accordance with the provisions of Article V of the General Conditions and provide 
promptly as needed, the funds, facilities, services and other resources required for the 
Program.  
 
2.02. 
Without limitation upon the provisions of Section 2.01 of this Agreement, and 
except as the Bank and the Program Implementing Entity shall otherwise agree, the 
Program Implementing Entity shall carry out the Program in accordance with the 
provisions of the Schedule to this Agreement. 
 
ARTICLE III - REPRESENTATIVE; ADDRESSES
 
3.01. 
The Program Implementing Entity’s Representative is Chairman, Planning and 
Development Board.

SCHEDULE 
 
Actions Taken by Punjab Under the Program 
 
I. 
Institutional and Policy Reforms
1.  
Standards based on market prices have been adopted for maintenance and 
rehabilitation (M&R) allocation in the budget and based on these standards the M&R 
allocation for FY 2005-2006 has been increased to two billion Rupees 
 
(Rs 2,000,000,000). 
 
2. 
FOs have been authorized to collect and retain agreed percentage of the water 
charges (
abiana
). 
 
3. 
An inventory of all of IPDs irrigation assets has been prepared. 
 
4. 
A system of fortnightly departmental accounts committee (DAC) meetings has 
been instituted for the purpose of reviewing and settling all arising audit observations and 
progressively clearing backlogs. 
 
II. 
Water Resource Management Reforms
5. 
Data on actual canal withdrawals, including water entitlements, actual diversions 
and balance shares, has been posted on the IPD website. 
 
6. 
Preparation of an irrigation management information system has been initiated, 
to allow IPD to monitor key indicators of the system including designed and actual 
discharges. 
 
III. 
Irrigation Service Delivery Reforms
7. 
Irrigation management transfer agreements have been entered into with all 
eighty-three (83) FOs in LCC (East) Lower Chenab Canal. 
 
8. 
A
preliminary institutional analysis of IPD’s operations has been carried out and 
a
set of reform actions has been prepared for the different cost centers managed by it.
