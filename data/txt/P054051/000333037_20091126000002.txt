Overview 
 
This Environmental Management Plan (EMP) is an updated version reflecting developments 
since the first disclosure on November 26, 2007. The EMP aims at protecting the environment 
and the quality of life of the population and is based on the objectives of the Palestinian 
Authority’s environmental strategy and law. It relates to the environmental mitigation actions 
that will be included in the project design and taken into account during and after construction 
of a transfer station in Anabta. The Transfer Station will serve to collect solid waste from 
surrounding areas now disposed of at several dump sites and transfer the waste to the newly 
constructed and operational Zahrat Al-Finjan sanitary landfill in Jenin Governorate. The plan 
complements, and is to be read along with, the report “West Bank and Gaza Solid Waste 
Management Project (SWEMP): Solid Waste Management Study, Volume 2: Environmental 
Analysis of the Zahrat Finjan Site. In this latter report, the environmental mitigation actions 
required for the larger project area were defined and have been implemented. This latter 
report was disclosed in the West Bank and Gaza, at the project sites and in the Bank’s 
INFOSHOP in March 2000. 
 
This EMP requires persons engaging in possible polluting activities to prevent environmental 
degradation and adverse risks to human health and ecosystems, make progressive 
environmental improvements, achieve effective integration of environmental, economic and 
social considerations in the decision making process, promote shared responsibility for the 
protection of the environment, and promote the principles of ecologically sustainable 
development. The primary purpose of the EMP is to provide the Palestinian Environmental 
Quality Authority (EQA) and Wadi Al-Shaeer JSC with a structured tool for addressing key 
issues of environmental concern with potential negative impacts, identify measures to 
mitigate such impacts, delegate to responsible agencies the project follow up and monitoring, 
and determine the cost associated with the mitigation measures for inclusion in the project.  
 
An EMP has been prepared in accordance with the environmental regulations of the 
Palestinian Authority and World Bank environmental safeguard policies and procedures. 
Mitigation and monitoring measures have been prepared as an element of the Project.  The 
mitigation actions focus on supervision of construction to ensure compliance with 
environmental regulations. The monitoring plan provides for periodic measurement of  air 
quality, noise, dust, and water pollution to support management decisions. In addition, the 
project includes a training program in environmental management, which targets the capacity 
needs of concerned staff from the participating municipalities. This version of the EMP will 
be translated into Arabic and disclosed in both languages locally and at the World Bank’s 
InfoShop.

Environmental Management Plan (EMP) 
 
In the context of improving the solid waste management system in the West Bank and Gaza, 
the Government of Italy (GOI) has contributed to the Palestinian Authority (PA) a grant in the 
amount of EUR 1,363,636.36 (One million, three hundred sixty three thousands and six 
hundred thirty six Euro and 36 Eurocents) to implement the Anabta solid waste management 
project. The objective of this project is to improve the environmental living conditions of the 
Page  8
8
people of Anabta and surrounding villages through an environmentally sound solid waste 
management system. This project consists of  providing a transfer station in Anabta region 
and equipment for transfering the solid waste to the Zahrat Al-Finjan (ZF) sanitary landfill. 
The sanitary landfill was constructed under the SWEMP and has been in operation since July 
2007.  
This project includes investments in the Wadi Shaeer area in Tulkarm governorate for the 
purpose of providing solid waste management services to Anabta and selected surrounding 
villages. The main investments consists of: (i) construction of a regional transfer station 
including off-site water pipeline, electrical power line, and access road to the new facility; (ii) 
the closure of 3 random dumpsites (Ramin, Kufr Al-Labad and Iktaba); and (iii) the 
temporary rehabilitation and management of the current regional dumpsite in Anabta and its 
closure once the new transfer station is operational.  
The EMP provides an environmental impact assessment and recommendations of appropriate 
mitigation measures to be taken into account during project design and implementation by the 
project executing agency (WS JSC), participating municipalities, design and supervision 
consultants and contractors. The EMP in compliance with the relevant Bank Safeguard 
Policies (OP/BP 4.01 and 4.12).  
 
Environmental impacts.
 
The proposed project, in compliance with the EMP mitigation 
measures, will result in an overall improvement in the environmental quality in the Wadi 
Shaeer area, particularly on the quality of life of the residents in all participating 
municipalities. The project aims to stop or substantially reduce random dumping and burning 
of municipal waste. It is expected that most of if not all the municipal waste generated in the 
Wadi Shaeer area will be disposed off at the proposed transfer station and then transferred to 
the Zahrat Al-Finjan sanitary landfill.

Social Impacts.
 
An Abbreviated Resettlement Action Plan (ARAP) has been prepared which 
addresses the waste pickers that work at the main Anabta.dumpsite About four (4) full-time 
waste pickers have been identified, which increases to about 7 during the school breaks. The 
ARAP presents a more detailed discussion of issues related to the waste pickers, including 
consultation sessions with waste pickers carried out by the social consultant, and 
compensation measures agreed with them to assist them to redirect their current practices.
 
Site specific Environmental Screening, Review and Assessment. 
The Project Document 
was prepared by an independent consultant, which included detailed review of the project and 
assessment of the environmental feasibility of the proposed project. The alternative of  
constructing a small scale sanitary landfill at an  abandoned stone quarry to serve the seven 
small communities of Wadi Al-Shaeer in Tulkarem Governorate was considered but resulted 
Page  9
9
too costly when compared with providing a transfer station for final disposal at the recently 
constructed Zahrat Al-Finjan Sanitary Landfill. Further consultations with the World Bank 
were carried out and it was agreed that the alternative of a Transfer Station would be the best 
option especially  since the Zahrat Al-Finjan Sanitary Landfill, located only at about 23 
kilometers away, had been placed in satisfactory operation since July 2007, .  
Mitigation Measures: 
The main objective of the EMP is to identify the necessary mitigation 
measures that would be carried out at the various stages of project implementation (design, 
construction and post construction). The EMP preparation adopted a methodology of 
consultation and requested feedback from potential key stakeholders and representatives of 
the participating municipalities and local councils.  
The results of the stakeholder consultation are considered as valuable inputs in identifying 
key environmental issues and mitigation measures. Both positive and negative environmental 
impacts are presented in detail. Daily supervision of the construction activities will be carried 
out by the PIU and complemented by consultants. The attached responsibility matrix shows 
the expected project impacts and lists the mitigation measures to be implemented.  
Implementation Arrangements:
 The implementation responsibility of this Project will be 
with the WSJSC in close cooperation and coordination with Ministry of Local Government 
(MOLG) and the EQA. The Project Implementation Unit (PIU), acting as the executive arm 
of the WSJSC, will be responsible for all contracting, monitoring, reporting and supervision 
of works throughout the implementation of the project. The PIU will be aided by specialized 
individuals to carry out the various activities including the implementation of relevant items 
in the EMP. Similarly, the EQA will carry-out its responsibilities as delineated by Palestinian 
Environmental Law.  
This Project would further develop such capacity by financing (a) additional training to 
specific operational staff at the PIU and participating municipalities, (b) the recruitment of an 
environmental specialist  to support the PIU team and to be responsible for reviewing, 
advising and reporting on environmental issues throughout the project implementation.  The 
environmental specialist will also assist the participating municipalities to develop their 
capacities for better solid waste management service delivery. 
Implementation Cost:
 The project will finance technical assistance, which will support the 
PIU in implementing the EMP and the ARAP. The implementation costs are estimated at 
US$50,000. This would include the cost of supervision and monitoring the EMP as well as 
the proposed training programs addressed to WSJSC,PIU staff and participating municipal 
staff.  
The costs associated with implementing operational measures will be financed initially by the 
project through 
the Trust Fund’s closing date, April 30, 2010; thereafter, such costs will be 
financed from the annual WSJSC budget. The operating costs will be financed by income 
generated from service fees paid by the  participating municipalities. During the project 
implementation, the Bank supervision team will review the WSJSC budget and confirm that 
such budget includes specific line items for post-project mitigation measures.
