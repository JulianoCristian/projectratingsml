1. Introduction


The mine liquidation process is conducted in accordance with the Polish effective laws,
harmonised with the EU legislation. The primary acts of legislation, governing the mine
liquidation process, including secondary legislation, are as follows:

       -   of 4 February, 1994. the Mining and Geological Law;
       -   of 27 April, 2001 Environment Protection Law;
       -   of 7 July, 1994 Construction Law;
       -   of 27 April, 2001 on Waste;
       -   of 27 March, 2003 on Land Development and Zoning Plans;
       -   of 16 October, 1991 on Protection of Nature;
       -   of 18 July, 2001 the Water Law;
       -   of 3 February 1995 on Protection of Farming and Forestlands.

Considering the specificity of the SRK S.A. operations i.e. mine liquidation, the Company
believes the entire undertaking to have moderate and unique impact upon the environment, to
be subject to specific mitigation measures and defined scope of necessary monitoring. The
project requires preparation of an Environmental Impact Assessment including simplified
environment management plan. Compliance with the plan will ensure meeting the
environmental and social standards during the project implementation.

Majority of works related to mine liquidation are conducted by contractors hired through a
public tendering process. In order to ensure appropriate service quality and compliance with
assumptions and provisions of the environment management plan the contracting companies
remain under continuous supervision performed by the Company and its mine liquidation
units due the that fact that it is the Company who bears final responsibility for mitigation of
liquidation impact upon the environment.

Implementation of the environment management plan will be subject to the ongoing
monitoring and assessment by the Office of the Company Management Board.

2.     Determination of the scope of the works and identification of relating threats
under the mine liquidation process- Screening

Primary tasks, within the mine liquidation process, to create negative impact upon the
environment are as follows:


       1.1 Liquidation of surface buildings and structures, creating following threats:
       -   generation of waste including dangerous waste e.g. asbestos containing waste;
       -   noise emission;
       -   dust emission;



       1.2 Liquidation of machines and equipment, with the negative impact in the form
           of:
       -   generation of waste including dangerous waste e.g. asbestos containing waste;



                                                                                               3

       -    noise emission


        1.3 Liquidation of mine workings, this process may have, temporarily, negative
            impact upon underground waters due to backfilling using waste. Examination of
            watercourses from waste disposal locations point out to higher concentration of
            heavy metals and alkali reaction. This reflux discharged to mine waters is pumped
            out, using the main de-watering system and discharged to surface waters. Volumes
            however, are not high enough to have a significant impact upon overall geo-
            chemical water balance.

        1.4 Discharge of underground waters to surface waters, involving:
       -    discharge of pollutant loads in the form of chlorides and sulphates in the
            discharged underground waters;
       -    noise emission;

        1.5 Land reclamation and development, with negative impact due to:
       -    the use of waste;
       -    temporary increase in the noise emission level;
       -    temporary increase in dust emission.

       Detailed information on impact on the environment of the works related to the
       liquidation process is provided in Exhibit 1 hereto.

3.     Documentation constituting the basis for liquidation works

Tasks to be performed by the Company has been designed on the basis of effective Polish
regulations and laws. The list of basic documents that the company is required to develop
under the law, with respect to each liquidated mining plant includes as follows:
        -   the Mining Plant Liquidation Programme;
        -   annual liquidation plans prepared on the basis of the approved Programme;
        -   Plan of Operations of the Liquidated Mining Plant;
        -   Supplement to the Deposit Development Plan;
        -   Hydro-geological documentation;
        -   Reconciling supplement to the geological documentation of the deposit.

The acts of law providing for the need for the liquidating entity to obtain and have such basic
documents in place are listed out below:

The Law of 28 November, 2003 on hard coal mining sector restructuring during the period
2003 ­ 2006, provides for rules and principles governing the process of the hard coal mining
sector restructuring, including:

        1) principles governing financial restructuring of mining enterprises;
        2) principles governing employment restructuring in the mining enterprises;
        3) principles governing liquidation of hard coal mines;
        4) principles governing organisation restructuring in the mining enterprises;
        5) specific rights and privileges of mining gminas;
        6) sources of funding of the hard coal mining sector restructuring process.

Relevant governing body of a mining enterprise shall make a decision regarding mine
liquidation, specifying as follows:

        1) mine liquidation scope;
        2) liquidation process commencement date;
        3) date the coal production will be terminated;
        4) liquidation process completion date;
        5) person responsible ­ mine liquidator.

    Provisions of the Mining and Geological Law shall apply to liquidation process and
activities to be conducted following completion of the liquidation process. According to the
Mining and Geological Law there is an obligation to prepare the Plan of Operations of the
Liquidated Mining Plant specifying the scope of liquidation works. Furthermore, duties of an
entity relative to mine liquidation are also specified, as follows:
 1)protecting or liquidation of mine workings as well as facilities, machines and equipment of
    the mining plant;
 2)protecting unused part of the mineral deposits;
 3)protecting adjoining deposits of useful minerals;
 4)undertaking all necessary measures to protect workings of adjoining mining plants;
 5)undertaking all necessary measures to protect environment and to ensure post-mining land
    reclamation and development.

Furthermore, the effective legislation imposes a duty to prepare hydro-geological
documentation, reconciling supplement to the geological documentation of the deposit and the
social mitigation programme.

4. Documentation agreement and approval process

The Plan of Operations of the Liquidated Mining Plan has to be agreed upon with the relevant
wójt (head of gmina) or town mayor.

Mine liquidator is obliged to draft a social litigation programme for the liquidated mine,
hereinafter referred to as ,,the Social Programme", not later than within 60 days prior to the
date of termination of production in the given mine.

The Social Programme, following provision of an opinion thereon by the Trade Unions active
in the liquidated mine and by the head of the gmina (town mayor) relevant to the mine
location is approved by relevant governing body of the mining enterprise.

The mining plant liquidation programme as well as annual liquidation plans broken down by
individual months are subject to acceptance by the Minister of Economy, Labour and Social
Policy following prior verification of the said documents by the Industrial Development6
Agency (IDA).

In case any post-liquidation works are performed, the relevant plan of post-liquidation actions
will be developed subject to verification and acceptance according to the procedure applicable
to the Programme and the Mining Plant Liquidation Plan.

 5. Consultations

5.1 Social consultations under the Polish law

It is required to ensure participation of the society in proceedings regarding assessment of
environmental impact involving preparation of the Environmental Impact Assessment.
According to the Environment Protection Law the society has the right to participate in
drafting the action plans. The procedure is carried out as follows:

    1. The body of local government conducting the proceedings regarding the
        Environmental Impact Assessment with respect to the planned projects announces that
        the information regarding application for decision is publicly available and informs
        about a possibility to submit comments and remarks within the period of 21 days of
        the date of publishing. Together with information on the procedure regarding
        submittal of comments and remarks, the location (address) to submit thereof is also
        announced.
    2. The above information is published in local press or TV, at the WEB site of the
        relevant government body to perform proceedings regarding environmental impact
        assessment of the planned projects, on the bulletin board of the body conducting such
        proceedings or in any other locations to ensure that the information is available to
        persons that my be directly interested in submitting comments or remarks/questions in
        specific matters.
    3. The local government body conducting the proceedings regarding the environmental
        impact assessment of the planned projects may conduct open administrative hearing.
        Such a hearing provides all stakeholders with an opportunity to meet face-to-face,
        thereby helping all stakeholders to find better solutions reducing negative impact upon
        environment and human health.
    4. The body of local government conducting the proceedings on the environmental
        impact assessment regarding the planned projects will consider all comments, motions
        and remarks submitted.




    5.2 Consultations with local NGOs and local community

Mines under liquidation, individually and in order to disseminate information on
environmental impact of the planned projects, not only in the cases where it is required by
law, will make relevant information publicly available, as follows:

    1. entity's own WEB site;
    2. bulleting board located in the head office of the entity;
    3. bulleting boards to provide access to broader community e.g. bulleting boards of
        housing co-operatives;
    4. local press or leaflets distributed in the area of the planned project;
    5. in regional radio and TV.

Project local community consultation process is designed to exclude any potential protests
against the planned projects.

Local consultations ensure access to social stakeholder groups and facilitate determination of
potential response by the local community prior to project implementation.

On the basis of the action plan, referred to above, the mine under liquidation will prepare a
plan (schedule) of individual meetings, including location and time, description of the project,
environmental impact parameters and group of stakeholders. This plan will be published as

The reports will be made of all meetings referred to above, concluded in keeping with the plan
of the meetings, providing detailed information on, as follows: groups invited to participate in

process.

Personal information regarding participants to the consultation process will be published to
the extent it is permitted under the law on protection of personal information or under
individual approval each participating individual.

6. Documentation available to the public

The list of decisions, permits or agreements obtained with respect to conducting liquidation
works, information regarding public tenders announced with respect to individual liquidation
works and information on contracts awarded will be published at the Company's WEB site.
The list of decisions, permits or agreements obtained with regard to liquidation of a particular

Information of availability of the above information will be published on WEB sites of
relevant units.

7. Documentation regarding individual liquidation tasks and approval process

Decisions and permits issued with respect to individual facilities or areas of the liquidated
mining plant or with respect to projects conducted in connection with the mine liquidation
process will constitute the list of detailed documents required to perform tasks under the
programme, liquidation plan and the plan of operations, including as follows:
        -   Permits to demolish buildings and/or structures;
        -   Decisions determining type and purpose of land reclamation
        -   Decisions regarding land development;
        -   Water management permits;
        -   Approvals of the dangerous waste management programme;
        -   Permits to remove trees and bushes.

The above decisions and permits are issued by relevant administrative bodies at the gmina,
powiat and the Voivodship level in accordance with relevant provisions of the effective laws,
in certain cases following completion of social consultations and on the basis of appropriate
designs and documentation.

Each and every activity interfering with the environment is based upon decisions and permits
issued with respect to a given scope of works, under the effective legislation. Liquidation
works may commence exclusively subject to obtaining relevant decisions, permits and
agreements.




                                                                                               7

Decisions, permits or agreements are issued on the basis of applications submitted to
appropriate administrative bodies. These applications will contain, conditional upon the type
of the task, appropriate designs and/or documentation.

With respect to liquidation of surface buildings and structures the basic documentation
includes: the design including information on demolishing technology, supplemented,
conditional upon the type and scope of the work and the type of the structure, with the
environmental impact assessment and, potentially, agreements with the Voivodship Officer
responsible for preservation of monuments of architecture in case the monument of
architecture is to be liquidated.

With respect to liquidation of machines and equipment, in case there the dangerous waste may
be generated in the liquidation process (primarily oils and lubricants containing PCB), the
dangerous waste management programme should contain methods to render such waste
harmless.

With respect to land reclamation: basic documentation includes the land reclamation design,
supplemented, depending upon the scope of works, with the environmental impact assessment
report, potentially decision regarding recycling of waste in case such waste is utilised.

With respect to discharge of underground waters to surface watercourses; the basic
documentation includes water management assessment report supplemented with hydro-
geological documentation and the project environmental impact assessment.


Information on the documentation approval procedure, including identification of legal basis
requiring preparation of same and identification of the approving entity is provided in
Exhibits 2, 3, 3a and 3b hereto.

9. Supervision of implementation of environmental impact mitigation measures and
monitoring

9.1 Plan to mitigate negative environmental impact

Generation of waste relative to liquidation of surface buildings and structures, machines and
equipment.

Selective disposal of waste generated during liquidation of buildings and structures, with a
specific focus on dangerous waste. Waste such as asbestos, used oils and lubricants containing
PCB or other dangerous waste will be subject to specific monitoring.




                                                                                             8

Liquidation contractors selected in the tender process will be obliged to collect such waste,
acting as its owner, for further transportation and disposal as well as to render such waste
harmless in keeping with relevant applicable regulations.
Bidders for demolishing works relative to buildings and structures containing asbestos will
have the duty to submit relevant permits to conduct this type of activities and to strictly
observe provisions of executive regulations under the Law of 19 June, 1997 on prohibition to
use asbestos containing products.

Noise emission
Contractors selected to render liquidation services will have the duty to use technical means
and technologies to ensure minimum emission of noise to environment. Contractors, while
performing liquidation works, will have to isolate sites from the environment using
appropriate casings or protective screens as well as machinery and equipment having
appropriate certificates.

Dust emission
Contractors selected, by tender, to render liquidation service will have the duty to use
technical means and technologies to ensure minimum emission of noise to environment.
Contractors, while performing liquidation works, will have to isolate sites from the
environment using appropriate casings or protective screens as well as appropriate equipment
to wash means of transport and access roads as well as moisturizing appropriate products or
goods or sprinkling the work site in order to minimize dust emission from the site.

9.2 Responsibility for the execution of the Environment Management Plan

With respect to solid waste:
Specifications under the procurement proceedings will provide for the duty to utilise waste
generated as well as transport thereof to the future utilisation location or to location where
such waste will be rendered harmless. Bidders will have to obtain relevant permits for
transport and recycling of waste. Only such permit holders will be eligible to be awarded
contracts for demolition works. Strict compliance requirements will be applied to contractors
delivering demolition works on buildings and structures containing asbestos. The contractors
will have specific duties to perform, as provided for under relevant laws and regulations,
regarding disposal of asbestos containing waste, transportation, marking thereof and
protecting the site as well as personnel training.


Noise emission:
Contractors will be required to apply appropriate technologies or protective measures to
protect against excessive noise emission to environment. Contractors will have the duty to use
protective measures in the form of noise suppressing screens, to conduct works in specific
times of day using machines and equipment in appropriate technical condition to ensure
reduction of noise emission to environment. Pumping out underground water from
underground workings constitute a specific task. In this area, noise emission reduction
projects will be undertaken by the Company itself and will involve periodical maintenance
and reviews of de-watering equipment to avoid any deterioration of technical condition and
efficiency and excessive noise emission. The above does not apply to permanent pumping
stations. Deep pumping equipment, in case of exceeding permissible noise emission levels,
will be provided with appropriate noise suppressing screens.




                                                                                               9

Dust emission:
Performance of demolition works of buildings and structures, liquidation of machines and
equipment as well as the land reclamation works will be open to bidding by external
contractors. The requirements to be complied with by such contractors will include obligation
to apply technologies or measures protecting against excessive dust emission. Contractors will
have to use protective screens, site area sprinkling, and washing of access roads and means of
transport.

Discharge of underground waters to surface waters:
De-watering of liquidated mining plants will be conducted under strict supervision performed
by environment protection services of the liquidated mine.

The plan regarding reduction of negative impact on environment is presented in the table
format in Exhibit 1 hereto.

9.3 Supervision of execution of the Environment Management Plan

In each case of performance of the works by external contractor, the supervision inspector
will be appointed having appropriate permits and certificates, as required with respect to a
given type of the works. Inspectors will be appointed from within the staff of liquidated mines
or under work-for-hire contracts in cases a given mine does not have appropriate specialist
within its own workforce.
The supervision inspectors will perform ongoing supervision and monitoring of the project
and will confirm progress in project implementation by making entries to the Project
Logbook.
The supervision inspectors shall successively provide appropriate units of the liquidated mine
and, subsequently, to the Office of the Company Management Board, information on progress
in project implementation and, in case of extraordinary events or threats to environment, shall
notify relevant services of the liquidated mine, the mine liquidator and the Office of the
Management Board forthwith.

Liquidation of surface objects and structures:
The construction supervision inspector of the liquidated mine/work safety inspectorate of the
liquidated mine.

Waste:
Environment protection units of the liquidated mine.

Dust emission:
Environment protection units of the liquidated mine/work safety units of the liquidated mine.

Noise emission:
Environment protection units of the liquidated mine/work safety units of the liquidated mine.

Discharge of underground waters to surface waters:
Environment protection units of the liquidated mine/work safety units of the liquidated mine.


9.4 Environment monitoring




                                                                                            10

Exhibit 1 hereto contains a detailed Environment Monitoring Plan.

The list of environment parameters to be covered by monitoring in the course of liquidation
works includes, as follows:
        -   waste generated or utilised by the project;
        -   volumes of waters and pollutant loads in discharged underground waters;
        -   particulate (dust) emission ­ dispersed emission;
        -   noise emission level.

Monitoring will be performed by operations and environment protection services of the
liquidated mine. In cases of non-compliance with terms and conditions of decisions obtained,
agreements etc. the environment protection services responsible for monitoring of the given
parameter and units performing relevant supervision shall notify the Chief Power and
Mechanical Engineer of the liquidated mine and appropriate personnel of the Office of the
Management Board regarding occurrence of threat

Waste monitoring activity shall involve both quantitative and qualitative inspection of wastes.
Monitoring will be performed continuously in line with progress of liquidation works,
following selection of waste by type. Each element of waste shall be subject to identification
and registration in keeping with the effective waste catalogue, as published in the Ordinance
of the Minister of Environment and shall be entered into register with appropriate code.
Monitoring activity will cover waste utilized as well as rendered harmless.

Monitoring of pollution of waters discharged to surface watercourses

In view of the fact that liquidated mines do not operate appropriate chemical laboratories, all
tests and examination of underground water are outsourced. According to conditions included
in water management permits (applicable to time scales of monitoring and its scope) physical
and chemical examinations are outsourced to determine physical and chemical properties of
waters. Tests are focused primarily on the contents of chlorides and sulphates in discharged
waters as well as properties such as: pH, suspended matter, dissolved substances. Usually, full
tests including tests for indicators and parameters required under effective regulations, are
performed quarterly.
Volumes of water discharged to surface watercourses will be subject of monitoring.
Exhibit 1 hereto contains detailed information regarding monitoring of the above mentioned
parameters.


10. Institutional strengthening

Due to the fact that majority of the works will be performed by external contractors, selected
by public tender process and in view of the fact that these contractors have the duty to
minimize environmental impact of individual works and subprojects, the mines do not foresee
any purchases of any new equipment to protect environment against any threats from
liquidation process or any metering and measuring specialist equipment, since appropriate
equipment will be owned by contracting company.

For the same reasons, mines doe not expect to provide specialist training or study tours, since
specialist personnel of the contractor company will deliver the works in compliance with
environment protection requirements.



                                                                                             11

Services and units of liquidated mines, in keeping with their functions and responsibilities,
shall perform ongoing monitoring of any changes in applicable regulations (primary and
secondary legislation) governing environment protection and compliance therewith.
The mines do not expect to use consulting services. Supervision of the performance of
liquidation works will fall under the responsibility of appopi9nted supervision inspectors
having appropriate permits and certificates to ensure performance and delivery of the works in
compliance with environment protection laws and regulations.

No additional studies relative to the environment management process are expected to be
performed in the course of liquidation.

11. Institutional solutions

Liquidation process imposes a duty, upon four units, to ensure strict co-operation in the
following areas:
      - responsibility in the area of threat mitigation and monitoring;
      - dissemination and flow of information on environment;
      - activities of the decision making chain and flow of decisions in the area of
      environment management.

These are:

    1. Contractors ­ applying threat mitigation measures;
    2. Services and units of liquidated mine ­ performing monitoring and reporting;
    3. Office of the Management Board ­ accepting reports and reviewing the liquidation
        process and activities;
    4. Company Management Board ­ a decision making body.

Contractors will have and operate technical measures to mitigate threats to environment.
Hence, the Contractor will be responsible for applying such measures. Activities of outside
contractors will be continuously monitored and supervised by appointed supervision
inspectors and appropriate services of liquidated mines i.e. environment protection function
and work safety function.

Persons responsible for conducting monitoring of individual parameters shall collect data,
conduct ongoing review of data collected and shall report results to supervisors from the staff
of the Principal i.e. liquidated mine.

Persons responsible for threat monitoring shall, on the basis of information gathered, to
prepare monthly reports including presentation of qualitative and quantitative data regarding a
given parameter. The said reports shall be submitted to the management of the liquidated
mine to develop a summary report for the given liquidation process (plant). Review of such
reports may facilitate undertaking potential preventive activities to eliminate certain potential
threats to environment, if required. These reports will also be submitted to the Office of the
Management Board of the Company.

In case, at the stage implementation, a potential threat to environment is identified,
monitoring and supervising personnel shall notify the mine liquidator thereon forthwith. The
Liquidator has the right to make appropriate decision regarding elimination of deficiencies,
change of technology used including interruption of performance of the works. In case actions

undertaken by the liquidator bring about negative financial consequences (i.e. increase in
spending), changes are subject to approval by the Company Management Board.


12. Conclusions

1. It is necessary to underline the importance of selection of contractors, with specific focus
    on contractor's specialist staff, to ensure the highest available quality of services including
    minimising project impact upon the environment.

2. Supervision and monitoring of contractor performance under the contract in order to
    ensure compliance with effective procedures and regulations, as specified in the
    Environment Management Plan.

3. Performance of continuous environment monitoring activity in connection with
    performance of liquidation works within the scope specified under the Environment
    Monitoring Plan.

4. Preparation and publication of detailed information to ensure continuity of the social
    consultation process.
