                         Project Development Objectives
                         Project Development Objective (from Project Appraisal Document)

                         The Project's Development Objective is to support national Government policy to: (i) improve the coverage, efficiency, and quality of the Argentine education
                         system, and (ii) to improve the governance of the Argentine education system through strengthening of the normative, planning, information, monitoring, and
                         evaluation capacity at the national and provincial levels.

                         Implementation Status Overview
                         The project has made progress towards achieving its development objectives, which can be observed through the following: The number of first grade students in rural primary
                         education schools without any initial education has decreased; the transition rate from 7th grade (last grade in primary school) to 8th grade (first grade in secondary school) increased
                         in the rural areas from 73 percent in 2005 to 81 percent in 2009; and the average completion rate of the first three grades of primary education increased from 76 percent in 2005 to
                         86 percent in 2008. In terms of activities, the training for multi-grade primary teachers is under implementation and training has been provided. This training covers the use of the

                         Key Decisions Regarding Implementation
                         In September 2010 the Consejo Federal de Educación approved legal resolution CFE 119/10 which defines the main aspects of Intercultural Bilingual Education (IBE) for the nation
                         and it requires actions from the Provinces to install, consolidate and strengthen IBE at the Province level.
