3. Assessment of Development Objective and Design, and of Quality at Entry

3.1 Original Objective:
The project objective was to expand and deepen the modernization of Nicaragua's public sector by
improving the government's economic planning, policy formulation, and execution capacity.

The objective was consistent with the government's strategy to build capacity in key areas and improve
efficiency in service delivery as well as improved financial management. The objective also reflected key
goals in the Country Assistance Strategy (CAS) regarding modernization of the state, including increasing
transparency and the reduction of the external debt burden to sustainable levels through the Heavily
Indebted Poor Countries Initiative (HIPC).

The objective and design of the Economic Management Technical Assistance (EMTAC) need to be
assessed in the context of (i) the country was in a dual and difficult transition from a centrally planned to
market economy, and from instability to a highly politicized democracy; (ii) the government's commitment
to reform fluctuated at best during the administration of President Aleman, but strengthened under
President Bolaños; (iii) despite progress with reforms the country/government still had and has modest
capacity to mobilize or effectively use resources for economic and social development; (iv) the EMTAC
was a follow-up operation to IDA's Institutional Development Credit (IDC) which helped finance the first
stage of a comprehensive reform of the public sector. It included an integrated financial management
system, design of a civil service reform, a streamlining of the macro structure of the central government,
agency specific reforms in several ministries and initiation of an anti-corruption effort.

In response to the widespread recognition that Nicaragua had to continue the process of structural
adjustment and as the IDC project had made progress in critical reforms, the government saw the need to
continue its efforts of institutional capacity building. Indeed, both the government and Bank continue to
recognize the long term nature of institutional strengthening and the EMTAC has been followed by a Public
Sector TAC.

3.2 Revised Objective:
The objective of the operation was not revised.
However, in the course of the project a new PRSP was approved. The government, the counterpart agency
and the project team in charge at that time ensured that the components that were still in the implementation
phase, were aligned to the new pillars of the PRSP. No substantial changes were necessary as the overall
project objective remained unchanged.

3.3 Original Components:
Project Component 1: Extending Integrated Financial Management Reform

This component focused on extending the Integrated Financial Management System (SIGFA) initiated in
1995 by: (i) deepening of the reforms in various financial management subsystems; (ii) extending financial
management reforms to all central government ministries and several key entities integrated with the
reformed system at the central Ministry of Finance level; (iii) strengthening of the Comptroller General
Office's audit capabilities, as well as the internal audit units of central government entities, and allow
auditors remote on-line access; (iv) developing of a financial management system in line with SIGFA
concepts to be made available on a voluntary basis to the municipal development institute and
municipalities; and (v) supporting a program of activities aimed at promoting public awareness with
respect to public sector reform issues.




                                                       - 2 -

Project Component 2: Deepening Civil Service Reform

This component focused on: (i) capacity building of the public Employee Directorate (DIFUP) in the
Ministry of Finance; (ii) evaluating and classifying all positions within the central government (except for
the military and autonomous educational centers) and creating a registry of all government employees
including their relevant personal and employment data; and (iii) communicating the new rights and
responsibilities of public employees.

Project Component 3: Strengthening Economic Management

This component focused on: (i) the formulation of macroeconomic policy, including public investment and
improved coordination of economic and social programs; (ii) the formulation, implementation and
evaluation of fiscal policy; and (iii) the reengineering and streamlining of processes and services in several
priority ministries.

Project Component 4: Deepening Public Sector Reform

This component focused on: (i) implementing the government reorganization law; (ii) strengthening the
public sector integrity; and (iii) strengthening of the state reform coordinating unit (UCRESEP).

The design of the components and the way they are interrelated were linked to the objective described
above. The economic planning objective was clearly addressed in component 1 and 3. The policy
formulation objective is clearly addressed in component 3 and the execution capacity is addressed in
components 2 and 4.

3.4 Revised Components:
No Components were revised or amended.

3.5 Quality at Entry:
The Project was submitted through streamlined procedures. Nevertheless it was subject to rigorous review
from a wide range of experts. Significant input into the final design was received from reviewers and other
sectors. Moreover, the project built upon the ongoing IDC, benefitting from existing counterparts as well
as the Bank's resident office. A complex project design, broad and politically difficult reforms posed
potential risks for failure. However, collaboration with partner agencies, and frequent supervision missions
were able to manage the risks.

4. Achievement of Objective and Outputs

4.1 Outcome/achievement of objective:
Achievement of objectives and outputs under the EMTAC was satisfactory. Considering the ex-ante
potential political risks on the implementation side, the project managed to transform the initial political
commitment into a reform with a significant momentum. The project was able to reasonably achieve most
of its objectives. Most legal and institutional changes were implemented with minimal resistance, which
allowed the substantive reforms to take place.

During implementation the project met a number of obstacles, which were managed well on the Bank side,
despite the frequent changes in TTLs (four TTL in less than two years). At mid-term the Administration
changed, but the new Administration fully supported project implementation.




                                                       - 3 -

The project built on the progress already achieved through the ongoing public sector modernization
program supported by the Institutional Development Credit project (IDC-2690), and expanded Nicaragua's
successful integrated financial management reform effort, initiated fundamental reform of the civil service,
supported the creation of small units to promote improved macroeconomic, public investment, and fiscal
policy formulation and implementation, target for reform key services in several priority agencies, and met
demands for the streamlining of the central administration, taking practical steps to address corruption and
encouraging citizen and public sector stakeholder knowledge and support for reform.

The Government largely completed project implementation. Most important indicators of progress made
towards the project DO are shown by: (i) implementation and effective use of the financial management
system SIGFA; (ii) sound management of a difficult fiscal situation reflected in the fiscal performance
under the PRGF and achievement of the HIPC debt relief initiative; (iii) implementation of Law 290 to
streamline the public sector and create citizen-oriented mechanisms for public services delivery; and (iv)
establishment of the legal framework for a civil service reform.

4.2 Outputs by components:
Most relevant expected results were achieved, although many of them partially or by different means than
those foreseen in the project design. Nevertheless, the project was effective in providing substantial
support to the government's public sector modernization agenda. Alignment to priorities under the Poverty
Reduction Strategy and the National Development Plan was an unexpected result that has enhanced the
project's value.

l Extending Integrated Financial Management (S)


    -   SIGFA was enhanced, upgraded and migrated to a state-of-the-art/Internet-based IT infrastructure.
    The Budget module is fully operational and is supporting budget formulation and monitoring, and was
    also adapted to produce relevant reports for monitoring the Poverty Reduction Strategy. The
    accounting and treasury modules are fully operational and decentralized to Financial Management
    Units within the Central Government. The system is operating in all central government entities and
    was implemented in 53 executing units of the Ministry of Health and 3 in the Ministry of Government.
    -   Project Administration module was developed and tested. Currently it continues to be operating
    only in three executing units. The Procurement management module was conceptualized and is in the
    process of development.
    -   The legal framework was developed and partially implemented. A new Financial Management
    Law was drafted and is ready to be presented to Congress.
    -   2600 public servants were trained on financial management related topics, including the normative
    framework, concepts and instruments. 200 auditors from the Comptroller Office and Internal Audit
    Units were trained with special attention to auditing process and the use of SIGFA information.

l Deepening Civil Service Reform (S)


This component was most affected by the potential political risks of the project. A number of issues could
not be resolved under this project, and needed to be addressed under the follow-up project (PSTAC), which
had been requested during the implementation of the EMTAC.

    -   Although some objectives under this component were not achieved, the approval of the Civil
    Service Law constitutes a major step towards the development of a sustainable civil service reform in
    the country. The new Law has created the basis of a permanent institutional framework to which the
    project has contributed significant technical assistance. The follow up PSTAC seeks to build on this

The project supported government efforts to both maintain macroeconomic stability under a sound fiscal
program and develop the poverty reduction strategy and the public investment system.

    remarkable:

               agreed upon under the Poverty Reduction and Growth Facility (PRGF);
          (ii) conditions to complete the HIPC completion point were fulfilled and the country has received
               significant debt relief with the support of this project;
          (iii) the Secretary of the Presidency (SECEP) was also strengthened and supported to update the
               country's PRSP and to prepare a new National Development Plan to enhance government's
               efforts to reduce poverty and promote sustainable growth; and
          (iv) the National Public Investment System started to implement its decentralization strategy in
               order to create planning and monitoring capacities at the sectoral and regional levels.

Project Administration

l Project Management (S):
The project was properly managed according to Bank rules. UCRESEP consolidated its management
capacity and expanded its work to serve as executing unit to other Bank sponsored projects. Implementing
capacity in the Ministry of Finance and SECEP was also consolidated.

l Procurement (S):
Procurement processes were managed properly, in a timely manner and according to Bank rules and the
project Operational Manual. No major issues were raises during post-review exercises.

l Financial Performance (S):
The project committed almost all credit proceeds and completed payments within the normal grace period
after its closing date. The government reasonably complied with the provision of counterpart funds.

l Financial Management (S):
Administration of the Special Account and flow of funds was proper and according to Bank rules. No
major issues were raised in audits reports, which were carried out on a timely basis.

4.5 Institutional development impact:
The project's objective aimed at improving the government's capacity in economic planning, policy
formulation, and policy execution. This would be achieved through fundamental institutional reforms in the
areas of the civil service, organizational restructuring and streamlining of the central administration.

In detail the project aimed at:

   o     Establishing an integrated financial management system throughout the central government;

   o     Extending modern, merit based human resource management throughout the central government;

   o     Strengthening economic and financial planning, policy formulation, and execution agencies and
         services;

   o     Consolidating the regulatory framework for the new executive branch organization; and

   o     Promoting anti-corruption initiatives.

The design and implementation of the project ensured capacity building in government. Especially the
decision to develop a single SIGFA system under the leadership of strong government champions and
excellent internal and external advisors proved to be key to the success of the component.

In a very short time-frame the government managed to develop a cutting-edge product. Usually,
institutional change cannot be achieved through pure technological change. However, in this case the
institutional change was not only supported but guided by the technological and technical change. The
SIGFA was able to change and improve many processes and procedures without the usual problems
associated with institutional change.

Though it took more time than anticipated to obtain civil service legislation, the project supported in the
meantime significant programs to improve the overall management of human resources both in the center
and in line agencies. The economic management units created and supported under the project provided
key support to senior officials. The anticorruption initiatives, although limited, provided key funding and
support to an important effort by Vice-President and then President Bolanos to improve transparency and
accountability in the midst of a difficult environment. Proper supervision, and personal visits at crucial
times such as elections, and a new PRSP, contributed to a successful implementation.


5. Major Factors Affecting Implementation and Outcome

5.1 Factors outside the control of government or implementing agency:
During the course of project implementation the Task Team Leadership changed three times. This affected
continuity significantly, as new team leaders, had to get accustomed to the project, local situation and
specifics of the project.
Considering that the project was ambitious, complex, and politically difficult, it is remarkable, that the
TTLs were able to maintain momentum.
Nevertheless, it can be assumed that the change in TTL contributed at least in part to delays in project
implementation. Ultimately this might have caused the extension of the closing date.

5.2 Factors generally subject to government control:



                                                      - 6 -

The government and implementing agencies were overly optimistic about the anticipated approval of civil
service reform. Significant resistance was met in the legislature, delaying passage and affected negatively
the achievement of component objectives. The government could have had more consultations with
Congress and other stakeholders prior to the project implementation to avoid this delay. Given the
substancial content of the legislation, its passage would have been problematic in any case.

5.3 Factors generally subject to implementing agency control:
The implementing agency (UCRESEP) was well prepared for the task, and took full ownership of the
project. The experience, and staffing of the implementing agency, contributed to the continuity of the
project during the change of administration and realignment of the components to the pillars of the PRSP.

5.4 Costs and financing:
The disbursement of funds reflects the implementation of the project very clearly. The delay of
effectiveness significantly reduced the disbursement potential of the loan. A second constraint appeared
around mid-term, when a change of the administration and a new PRSP delayed further disbursement.
Even though the closing date was postponed twice, only eighty percent of the funds were utilized.
However, the disbursement of fund does not reflect the quality of the project, or the funding of the
components. In general the funding was sufficient and well allocated.

6. Sustainability

6.1 Rationale for sustainability rating:
The project overcame a number of serious obstacles that could have derailed the reforms in the long-run.

l The project and the legislative reforms survived a change in government. It is not common that new
    administrations will embrace and actively support such significant reforms. In general new
    administrations tend to put their own stamp on such policy reforms, and either would halt such a
    project, or completely restructure it. In this case, adequate supervision and political sensitivity on the
    Bank side, allowed the new government (which was predisposed to support their reform effort) to give
    the project their own touch, while maintaining the overall goal.
l The political environment for civil service reform is always very difficult. Though approval of the law
    and implementation was delayed on several occasions, the final consensus achieved through prolonged
    political negotiation suggests that the reforms might reflect sufficient consensus and legitimacy to have
    and impact over the long term.



                                                                                        - 8 -

l The decision of developing its own SIGFA under a local leadership ensured the creation of capacity to
    maintain the system and functionality. It is not common for a small country like Nicaragua to acquire
    these skills in such a short time period, and to be able to manage the development, implementation an
    even upgrading of such a system with limited outside help. While in some key areas the government
    still needs international expertise to maintain the components. The ownership of this component should
    ensure sustainability in the long-run.

On the negative side, it is important to remark that the Government was not able to assume and absorb
incremental costs to sustain new developments under its own budget. Absorption of incremental recurrent
costs remains as a challenge for the follow up operation. Overall the project has a high likelihood of being
sustainable. To consolidate the reforms and continue enhancing the institutional capacity, the government
has already started to implement a follow up project with Bank support.

6.2 Transition arrangement to regular operations:
Recurrent costs covered by the project were and are a concern of the operation. Even in the follow-up
project a large part of recurrent costs are financed with Bank support. It will be difficult for this IDA
Borrower to change this, unless it builds internal capacity to take over the responsibility of consultants
hired through the projects. The Public Sector Technical Assistance Credit will deepen the institutional
building capacity building within the government. It will also reach out to expand the SIGFA application
through the public sector. The back-to-back implementation of these two credits should help ensure
continuity and sustainability.

7. Bank and Borrower Performance

Bank
7.1 Lending:
The preparation of the project did not depend heavily on new studies or supporting research. The team was
able to build on existing experience from previous operations, and it was able to acquire in depth
knowledge of the situation and needs of the country in the area of economic management. Despite the short
time span for project preparation the TTL and the Bank team were able to pull together a comprehensive
and well targeted project in the given time-frame. In the course of the project implementation, it became
apparent that some components of the project needed realignment and that due to incomplete government
ownership, the project plan needed revision. Due to issues outside the team's responsibility and reach, the
project effectiveness was delayed. Instead of changing the project objective, or terminating the remainder of
the loan, the Bank Team invested the required energy to lobby the government counterparts to provide the
necessary conditions to proceed with the project as planned. The time-frame allocated for the planning,
preparation and implementation of the project were rather short. A more generous project preparation time,
especially under the political and economic circumstances of the country at the time of the request for Bank
assistance, might have ultimately shortened the total time of the project. Also certain problems would have
been foreseeable and eliminated or mitigated prior to effectiveness. Looking back, a major shortcoming at
the time of project preparation was the lack of a political economy analysis of the country surrounding the
issues affected by the project (i.e. civil service legislation).

7.2 Supervision:
Project supervision by the respective TTL was more than adequate. All task team leaders were able to
motivate the counterpart agency in time of crisis, difficulty, or internal resistance. In addition, the TTLs
were able to get up to date with the project at the time of change and ensured to a notable extent a seamless
transition between TTLs. Nevertheless the frequent change of TTL was disruptive, especially as the
counterpart agency and the government needed the Bank's institutional support to manage a project of such
magnitude and complexity.



                                                        - 9 -

7.3 Overall Bank performance:
Satisfactory. As indicated elsewhere the Bank provided in a timely fashion technical and financial
assistance to support an ongoing and multi-year institutional capacity building program. Signficant results
on the ground were achieved despite the difficult political and economic circumstances of the country,
helping it grow, focus on poverty reduction priorities, and consolidate (if slowly) its democratic
government. The series of operations were strongly supported by the CMU and resident office, which
provided continued guidance and support to the Bank and counterpart teams. The comprehensive nature of
the operations provided in fact in this case resources to respond to emerging opportunities, reflecting the
Bank's well placed faith in the leadership of Vice President and then President Bolanos and an experienced
and dedicated counterpart team.

Borrower
7.4 Preparation:
Under the circumstances, the government provided excellent input at project preparation. However, the
counterpart should have been aware of the risks and mitigated them more, or provide an extensive risk
assessment in order to prepare for eventualities. This could be incorporated in future operations by having
political economy analysis. The project implementation team was adequately chosen and prepared for the
project. It would have been helpful though if funds would have been available to further build capacity of
the project implementation team.

7.5 Government implementation performance:
Government sustained its support even through and after the change of administration. This was
particularly important to continue and consolidate achievements from the previous operations and initial
activities of this one. The Vice-President, who was in charge of the project in the previous administration,
was elected President and therefore supported the project from the beginning of his administration.
Nevertheless, external political situations weakened significantly the Government position vis-à-vis
Congress and opposition political parties, and the general environment became adverse for project
implementation and completion. Some coordination issues arose among executing agencies that partially
affected the proper integration of project results at the end of its implementation. Such problems are being
addressed in the context of the follow-up operation.

7.6 Implementing Agency:
UCRESEP and each of the executing agencies demonstrated the capacity to manage and implement Bank
projects as well as proper application of Bank rules and procedures. Despite minor coordination problems
faced at the end of project implementation, executing agencies were effective in managing the project.
However, most personnel of executing units continue to be paid with project proceeds and they have not
been absorbed in to the Government's budget. Such a situation could threaten project sustainability.

7.7 Overall Borrower performance:
Satisfactory

8. Lessons Learned

The following lessons, which are relevant for future public sector technical assistance operations, can be
highlighted from the implementation of this program:

8.1 Strong political commitment to the reform agenda, together with the general understanding that public
sector reform is a long-term process, are key conditions prior to moving ahead in the reform effort. It is
important that all reform players agree on realistic expectations from the inception of any operation.



                                                      - 10 -

Moreover, we must anticipate setbacks as well as unforeseen opportunities for advances.

8.2 Project sustainability is highly dependant on the level of training and knowledge derived during project
implementation as well as the Government's fiscal capacity to retain trained staff and internalize project
capabilities within permanent institutions.

8.3 As institutional reform usually requires passage of legislation, commitment at the highest political level
is needed. Linkage to policy lending instruments, as this operation had, can constitute a necessary element
to provide the leverage and incentives to undertake the reforms that arre being supported.

Additional Annex 8. Borrower's Comments (Original)

                                              Informe Global

 Este proyecto, fue una continuación del proyecto de Crédito de Desarrollo Institucional (IDC-2690), el
 cual inicio los esfuerzos exitosos de reforma de la gestión integral financiera, la implementación de las
 bases de la reforma fundamental del servicio civil, ordenamiento de la organización, competencias y
 procedimientos del Poder Ejecutivo, apoyo en la creación de unidades pequeñas para promover mejoras
 macroeconómicas, la inversión pública, la formulación y aplicación de la política fiscal, reformas a los
 servicios claves en varios ministerios priorizados del ámbito económico, con medición de demandas para
 la modernización de la administración central, bases crecientes en temas de integridad en la gestión y,
 ciudadanos y stakeholders del sector público con más conocimiento y dispuestos para el apoyo a la
 reforma.

 Durante la ejecución del EMTAC se formuló e inició la implementación de la Estrategia Reforzada de
 Crecimiento Económico y Reducción de Pobreza Versión nacional del PRSP. (ERCERP). No obstante,
 el traslape en el tiempo, los objetivos y alcances del EMTAC se alinearon con el Cuarto Pilar de la
 ERCERP "Desarrollo Institucional y Gobernabilidad", logrando una afinidad natural con ésta, sin
 cambios en los objetivos del proyecto.

 El Gobierno de Nicaragua, mediante la ejecución de este crédito ha logrado mejorar sus capacidades
 para trabajar en la reducción de la pobreza y en la desigualdad del ingreso, principalmente, para ejecutar
 su transición a una economía de mercado apoyada por el fortaleciendo de la capacidad institucional del
 sector público.

 Particularmente, para construir un eficiente, eficaz y transparente sector público que facilita el
 crecimiento del sector privado. Mediante la extensión y profundización de la reforma y modernización
 del sector público, a través del mejoramiento de la planificación económica del gobierno, formulación de
 políticas públicas, reingenierías de procesos con mejoras en la funcionalidad operativa y actualización
 del marco legal vigente en instituciones claves y mayor capacidad en la ejecución de la gestión pública
 en el ámbito económico. Adicionalmente, se fortaleció el marco de la gobernabilidad, aumentando la
 confianza del sector privado para invertir y generar un moderado crecimiento económico.

 El proyecto contó con tres grandes partes o componentes, cada uno de ellos con sus propias fortalezas y
 debilidades, a continuación los hechos más relevantes de cada uno:

 Parte A, correspondiente a la Expansión de la Reforma de la Administración Financiera Integrada
 (SIGFA) a cargo del Ministerio de Hacienda y Crédito Público (MHCP), donde se puede afirmar que se
 lograron importantes avances cualitativos en los procesos presupuestarios con alta participación
 tecnológica.

 En este componente, durante la ejecución del EMTAC, se diseño e implemento la Caja Unica del Tesoro
 y se inicio el proceso de desconcentración del Sistema Integrado de Gestión Financiera Auditoria
 (SIGFA) del MHCP a los ministerios y poderes del Estado, mediante el uso de Internet e
 implementación del Extranet de Gobierno, con estos avances tecnológicos el sistema inicial SIGFA, se
 transforma a un ambiente gráfico y se implementa y consolida el e-SIGFA, donde las operaciones
 presupuestarias, contables, de tesorería y de crédito público se hacen dentro de un marco normativo y
 procedimental moderno y apropiado.



                                                  - 22 -

El e-SIGFA no solo permite el registro y control, sino fomenta el uso de información contenida para
mejorar la capacidad gerencial en los procesos de toma de decisiones; además, fortalece la transparencia
gubernamental dado que se conecta en línea a la Contraloría General de la República y a la Comisión
Económica de la Asamblea Nacional, con una gran cobertura de las instituciones de Gobierno, llegando
a completarse en la actualidad a 45 de ellas.

Durante la ejecución del EMTAC para este componente del SIGFA se logro un cofinanciamiento del
Reino de los Países Bajos (Holanda), realizando una masiva capacitación a más de 5,000 de
funcionarios públicos, asegurando la desconcentración del sistema.

Parte B, sobre Reforma del Servicio Civil, a cargo del MHCP, se creo y funciona la Unidad Ejecutora a
partir del 2000, se elaboro y aprobó la política y base normativa como Ley del Servicio Civil y Carrera
Administrativa (Ley SC y CA), Ley No. 437 en la Asamblea Nacional el 19 de noviembre del 2003 y
publicada en La Gaceta Diario Oficial No. 235 del 11 de diciembre del 2003, para ello se tuvieron que
elaborar y discutir arduamente 16 versiones de la Ley, consumiendo importante tiempo y esfuerzos del
proyecto. No obstante, los efectos de la demora de la Ley, se lograron avances en tareas adyacentes al
tema de servicio civil que no requerían de la Ley. Una vez aprobada la Ley se elaboro, discutió y
presento el Anteproyecto del Reglamento de la Ley SC y CA, así mismo se diseño e implemento un plan
de comunicación y divulgación de la Ley, capacitación a todos los niveles de la Administración Pública.

Ante la demorada aprobación de la Ley No. 437 se elaboro la Normativa para la Administración de los
RRHH del Poder Ejecutivo, conteniendo los aspectos esenciales de la Ley, sin embargo no se aplico.
Adicionalmente se realizo un Coloquio Centroamericano de Función Pública Aprendiendo de la
experiencia comparada con asistencia técnica del CLAD y se realizaron estudios de consultorías
esenciales para la implementación de la Ley (Línea de Base y diseño de la estrategia de implementación
del régimen de SC).

Además, se realizaron diagnósticos, diseños y fortalecimiento técnico de los órganos del Servicio Civil;
Sistema de Clasificación de Puestos implantado en 22 instituciones, con alto contenido de capacitación;
implantación de manuales de descripción de puestos en 14 instituciones, definiendo 14 familias
funcionales y 207 cargos tipos en 12 instituciones y; manual de procedimientos del sistema de
clasificación.

En cuanto a los Sistemas de Gestión de Recursos Humanos, ante la demora en la aprobación de la ley,
estos sistemas igualmente se atrasaron, mientras tanto, como parte de la estrategia se trabajo en la
creación de condiciones para facilitar la implantación, en cuanto al Sistema de Gestión al Desempeño,
esta pendiente una consultoría para el diseño conceptual y metodológico.

Se lograron importantes avances en la definición del marco conceptual del Sistema de Carrera
Administrativa y diseño del proceso de Acreditación; así mismo para el Sistema Retributivo, con tablas
de referencia salarial y estudios de mercado retributivo. Finalmente, el Sistema de Información del SC,
se diagnóstico el Software SISEC y se diseñaron sistema de Descripción de Puestos y Base de Datos de
Estudios Retributivos, entre otras cosas.

Parte C, Fortalecimiento de la Gestión Económica, con tres subcomponentes, el C.1) Fortalecimiento en
la formulación de políticas macroeconómicas, a cargo de la Secretaría de Estrategia y Coordinación de
la Presidencia (SECEP), obteniendo i) Programa Económico y Social formulado y en ejecución y, ii)
Plan de Inversión Pública en coherencia con el programa económico. Este programa económico y social


                                                 - 23 -

recibió el nombre de Estrategia Reforzada de Crecimiento Económico y Reducción de Pobreza
(ERCERP) altamente consultado, documento fundamental para calificar a la iniciativa HIPC, también
como Poverty Reduction Strategy Paper (PRSP).

El equipo a cargo de la SECEP participo en el diseño y negociación del programa de ajuste estructural
con el FMI y de apoyo al seguimiento para llegar al punto de culminación de la HIPC. A partir del
2002, con el cambio de Gobierno encabezado por el Ing. Enrique Bolaños, se imprime un cambio en el
énfasis de la ERCERP y se instaura una visión orientada al desarrollo conforme las potencialidades
productivas del territorio, lanzando el Plan Nacional de Desarrollo (PND), como la base del plan de
acción del GoN.

Adicionalmente, desde la SECEP se apoyo la organización y ejecución del Grupo Consultivo de
donantes y dos Reformas Fiscales reflejadas en dos leyes, la de Ampliación de la Base Tributaria del
2002 y la Ley de Equidad Fiscal del 2003. Así mismo, se diseñaron y elaboraron los reportes del PRSP
del 2002, 2003 y 2004, con importante base de datos de indicadores socioeconómicos y de pobreza de
Nicaragua. Finalmente se trabajo en el diseño y la elaboración del Plan Nacional de Desarrollo
Operativo PDN-O, con un nuevo formato, el cual enriquece la primera versión del PND.

El Fortalecimiento del Sistema Nacional de Inversiones (SNIP) utilizando Internet y Extranet de
Gobierno, el nodo y el sitio Web del SNIP se implemento desde el 2001 (www.snip.gob.ni) en consulta
con el Banco de Proyectos on line actualizado e incorporando indicadores de instituciones claves.

C.2) Edificación de la capacidades de análisis fiscal, a cargo de la Oficina Asesoría Fiscal y Económica
(OAFE) del MHCP, se diseñaron e implementaron indicadores de desempeño para los ingresos del
Gobierno Central por fuente recaudadora y tipo de impuesto, compatible con los objetivos de corto y
mediano plazo; además, se ha actualizado y armonizado los métodos de trabajo de programación
financiera impactando en la calidad de la información estadística. Los indicadores de eficiencia del
Gasto no fueron implementados, su ejecución será retomada en el PSTAC.

Se elaboro el anteproyecto de Ley de Administración Financiera con elementos legales para la
preparación del Presupuesto plurianual, el Presupuesto del 2005 contiene proyecciones de gasto de
capital de dos años subsiguientes; Finalmente, se evaluó integralmente el sistema impositivo, la cual
incluyo un efecto por cambios de tasas, leyes tributarias y otros compromisos en la política tributaria,
como insumo para la elaboración de la Ley de Equidad Fiscal, resultando con mayor impacto i) el
Impuesto sobre la Renta (IR) con pago mínimo del 1% sobre activos brutos y depreciación acelerada
para exportadores y, ii) Impuesto de Valor Agregado (IVA) con eliminación de tasa cero.

procesos, mejorando la ejecución de los procesos internos y servicios prestados en el Ministerio de

Parte D, Profundización de la Reforma del Sector Público, con tres subcomponentes, D.1) Ejecución de
la Ley de reorganización del gobierno, a cargo de UCRESEP, identificándose importantes avances en
los temas de reformas estructurales, sectoriales, institucionales y empresas del Estado, brindando una
mayor productividad al gasto corriente y de capital, de cara a la sostenibilidad fiscal y a la organización
que demanda el desarrollo estratégico del país. De estas propuestas se originan los planes de adecuación
estructural, funcional y territorial (esta ultima propone aglomerar en centros especializados, y
localizados físicamente en el territorio, la atención al sector productivo (Centros de Desarrollo
Productivos ­ CEDEP), social (Centros de Desarrollo Social (CEDES) y un centro especializado en
trámites (Centro de Atención al Público ­ CAP) consignados en el PND; además, se elaboro propuesta
para el funcionamiento de la Oficina de Administración Pública, mediante un análisis de competencias y
el reconocimiento de la urgente necesidad que el Estado cuente con una organización que responda a la
definición y aplicación de temas comunes para la Administración Pública (Decreto 25-2003, que nunca
se publico).

Adicionalmente, se realizo el diseño conceptual de un Sistema Público Agropecuario (SPA), que
responda al plan de adecuación territorial de los Centros de Desarrollo Productivo, articulando las
políticas públicas de orden económicas y social de manera ordenada, conforme la organización y
competencias del Poder Ejecutivo y lo propuesto en el Plan Nacional de Desarrollo.

Partiendo del análisis orgánico funcional y de procesos con respecto a la Ley de Organización
Competencia y Procedimientos del Poder Ejecutivo, Ley No. 290, se elaboraron sendas propuestas de
Adecuación Funcional y de Procesos para el Ministerio de la Familia (MIFAMILIA) empujando la
instauración de la rectoría sectorial de la protección social y, en el Ministerio de Relaciones Exteriores
(MINREX) la integración de la política exterior con cooperación externa, para impulsar un nuevo
enfoque de Diplomacia Económica, en ambos casos han sido implementado con suceso.

Además, se elaboraron propuestas de organización de la Presidencia y del Despacho de la Primera
Dama; así mismo, para una Superintendencia de Servicios Públicos, a solicitud de la Secretaría de
Coordinación y Estrategia de la Presidencia de la República (SECEP) y aanálisis institucionales y
asistencia técnica a Correos de Nicaragua, la Procuraduría de los Derechos Humanos y la Procuraduría
General de la República.

la elaboración de Manual de Organización del Poder Ejecutivo" y la "Guía para elaboración de Manual
de Procedimientos del Poder Ejecutivo" http://www.ucresep.gob.ni/manual.shtml y se prepararon de
manera piloto ambos manuales para el MTI, MARENA y MIFIC.
