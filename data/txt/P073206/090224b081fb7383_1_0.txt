                         Project Development Objectives
                         Project Development Objective (from Project Appraisal Document)

                         The project development objectives are to increase land tenure security and improve land administration services through the implementation of institutional and legal
                         reform, the realization of an accelerated land titling program, and the establishment of a fair and uniform property valuation system.

                         Implementation Status Overview
                         The project has made good progress towards its development objectives and all of the targets have been fully achieved, or have almost been fully achieved.


                                                                                                                                                                                                         Page 1 of 11
                             The World Bank                                                                                                                                        Report No: ISR11765




                         The Land Administration and Management System is being rolled out nationally to support land titling and cadastral mapping. Valuation standards and guidelines, as well as the
                         computerized valuation system have been developed and successfully piloted and are now to be mainstreamed across a complete province. Important new laws are now being
                         implemented including the Free Patent Amendment Act and the Real Estate Services Act, along with critical administrative orders including the enabling governance for LGU-led
Public Disclosure Copy




                         titling. Project management including monitoring and evaluation are functioning well. The key focus of the project teams at this time are preparing for Additional Financing of LAMP2,
                         which will be an upscaling of work under LAMP2 focusing on the mainstreaming of key developments including the management of public lands records, local government unit led
                         land titling, implementation of the national valuation standards and real property tax reform.

                         The Loan Agreement has now been further extended until March 31, 2014 to enable the processing of the additional financing for the project and to also complete the remaining
                         project activities. The additional financing is scheduled for consideration by the Board of the World Bank on November 27, 2013.

                         Key Decisions Regarding Implementation
                         The National Economic and Development Board endorsed the proposed Additional Financing of LAMP2 for $ 61.47 million for LAMP2, which is proposed for World Bank funding.
                         This will focus on the: (i) mainstreaming of the Land Administration System (LAMS) by computerizing the land records and scanning and encoding of maps/plans/applications in 16
                         regions and in the Land Management Bureau (LMB) of the Department of Environment and Natural Resources (DENR); and (ii) Bureau of Local Government Finance (BLGF)
                         implementation of land valuation and taxation reform through the review of schedule of market values in a pilot province, as well as broadening the foundations for valuation
                         education, and adoption of valuation information system. The additional financing is scheduled for consideration by the Board of the World Bank on November 27, 2013.
