Re: HONDURAS (Transport Sector Rehabilitation Project)

Amendment to the Development Credit Agreement 
Excellency:
Please refer to the Development Credit Agreement dated April 28, 1993 entered into 
between the Republic of Honduras (the Borrower) and the International Development 
Association (the Association), as amended.
In response to Vice Minister Mr. Hugo Castillo’s request of November 3, 2000 to 
reallocate a portion of the proceeds of the Credit to finance, 
inter alia,
 the improvement of the 
Road Fund’s offices, including the acquisition of equipment for the operation of the Road Fund, 
the Association hereby agrees to amend the Development Credit Agreement, as follows:

Section 3.01 is hereby amended in its entirety to read as follows:
Page  4
“Section 3.01. The Borrower declares its commitment to the objectives of the Project as 
set forth in Schedule 2 to this Agreement, and, to this end:
(a)  
shall carry out through SECOPT, Parts A (i), (ii) and (iii) (as reflected in the 
action plans referred to therein), B and C of the Project, all with due diligence and efficiency and 
in conformity with appropriate engineering, financial, administrative, technical and 
environmental practices, and shall provide, promptly as needed, the funds, facilities, services and 
other resources required for such Parts of the Project;
(b)  
shall cause ENP to carry out Part A (v) (as reflected in the action plan referred to 
therein) of the Project with due diligence and efficiency and in conformity with appropriate 
financial, administrative, technical and environmental practices, and in accordance with the ENP 
Contractual Arrangement, and shall take or cause to be taken all action, including the provision 
of funds, facilities, services and other resources, necessary or appropriate to enable ENP to carry 
out Part A (v) of the Project, and shall not take or permit to be taken any action which would 
prevent or interfere with the carrying out of Part A (v) of the Project by ENP;
(c)  
shall cause FNH to carry out Part A (iv) of the Project with due diligence and 
efficiency and in conformity with appropriate administrative, financial, technical and 
environmental practices, and in accordance with the FNH Contractual Arrangement and shall 
take or cause to be taken all action, including the provision of funds, facilities, services and other 
resources, necessary or appropriate to enable FNH to carry out Part A (iv) of the Project, and 
shall not take or permit to be taken any action which would prevent or interfere with the carrying 
out of Part A (iv) of the Project by FNH; 
(d)
shall cause the Road Fund to carry out Part D.5 of the Project with due diligence 
and efficiency and in conformity with appropriate administrative, financial, technical and 
environmental practices, and in accordance with the Road Fund Agreement and shall take or 
cause to be taken all action, including the provision of funds, facilities, services and other 
resources, necessary or appropriate to enable the Road Fund to carry out Part D.5 of the Project, 
and shall not take or permit to be taken any action which would prevent or interfere with the 
carrying out of said Part of the Project by the Road Fund; and
(e)  
the Borrower shall exercise its rights and comply with its obligations under the 
ENP Contractual Arrangement and the FNH Contractual Arrangement in such manner as to 
protect the interests of the Borrower and the Association, and to accomplish the purposes of the 
Credit, and, except as the Association shall otherwise agree, the Borrower shall not amend, 
assign, terminate, waive, abrogate or fail to enforce the ENP Contractual Arrangement or the 
FNH Contractual Arrangement or any provision thereof.”.
3.
Section 3.11 is hereby amended in its entirety to read as follows:
“Section 3.11. With regard to Parts D.1 through D.4 of the Project, the Borrower shall 
take all measures necessary or advisable to ensure, to the satisfaction of the Association:
Page  5
(a)
that the civil works to be financed out of the proceeds of the Second Tranche are 
required to reconstruct or rehabilitate infrastructure damaged by the Hurricane; and 
(b)
that the proposed investment:
(i)
is technically and economically sound; 
(ii)
meets appropriate environmental standards; and
(iii)
does not involve any involuntary resettlement of population.”.
4.
Two new Sections are hereby added at the end of Section 3.11 to read as follows:
Section 3.12. (a) For purposes of carrying out Part D.5 of the Project, the Borrower shall 
transfer, on a grant basis, part of the proceeds of the Credit allocated to finance said Part of the 
Project to the Road Fund pursuant to an agreement (the Road Fund Agreement) to be entered into 
between the Borrower and the Road Fund on terms and conditions satisfactory to the 
Association.
(b)
the Borrower shall exercise its rights and comply with its obligations under the 
Road Fund Agreement in such manner as to protect the interests of the Borrower and the 
Association, and to accomplish the purposes of the Credit, and, except as the Association shall 
otherwise agree, the Borrower shall not amend, assign, terminate, waive, abrogate or fail to 
enforce the Road Fund Agreement or any provision thereof.
Section 3.13. Without limitation to the provisions of Sections 3.01 (d) and 3.12 of this 
Agreement, the Borrower shall cause the Road Fund to enter into an agreement (the UNDP 
Agreement) with UNDP, on terms and conditions satisfactory to the Association, including, inter 
alia, the obligation of UNDP to carry out the procurement of Part D.5 of the Project on behalf of 
the Road Fund.”.
5.
Section 5.01 is hereby amended in its entirety to read as follows:
“Section 5.01. The Borrower shall cause ENP, FNH and the Road Fund to carry on its 
operations and conduct its affairs in accordance with sound administrative, financial, managerial, 
technical and environmental practices under the supervision of qualified and experienced 
management assisted by competent staff in adequate numbers, all acceptable to the Association.”.

Part D of the Project is hereby amended in its entirety to read as follows:
“Part D: Hurricane Related Activities
1.
Reconstruction or rehabilitation of road sections in the primary, secondary and 
tertiary road networks affected by the Hurricane.
2.
Construction or rehabilitation of bridges in the primary, secondary and tertiary 
road networks, including acquisition and installation of temporary bridges.
3.
Provision of consultant services for: (a) the supervision of works to be carried out 
under this Part D of the Project; (b) the preparation of detailed damage assessment studies, 
including activities leading to a program of vulnerability reduction to natural hazards in the road 
sub-sector; (c) the preparation of final engineering designs for roads and bridges included in the 
Page  8
short, medium and longer term reconstruction programs; (d) technical assistance to SOPTRAVI.
4.
Provision of vehicles and computers for SOPTRAVI to replace vehicles and 
computers damaged by the Hurricane.
5.
T
he improvement of the Road Fund’s offices, including the acquisition and utilization  
of equipment for the operation of the Road Fund
.”.

Effectiveness
:
This Amendment Letter shall: (a) be executed in two counterparts each of which shall be 
an original; and (b) become effective on the date above written once the Association has
