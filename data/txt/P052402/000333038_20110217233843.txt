MALI GOURMA BIODIVERSITY CONSERVATION PROJECT 
RESTRUCTURING PAPER 
 
A. 
  SUMMARY 
 
1.  This is a level two restructuring of the Mali Gourma Biodiversity Project (the Project). The 
Restructuring Paper seeks the approval of the Acting Country Director to introduce changes to 
the Mali Gourma Biodiversity Conservation Project (P052402), and accompanying amendments 
to the Project’s legal documents.  The changes include:  (i) a reduction in the scope of the Project 
components; (ii) a reallocation of the Project Grant Proceeds; and (iii) an extension of the Project 
Closing Date from June 30, 2011 to December 31, 2012.    
 
2.   
 
 
 
The main reasons for the proposed changes are to: (i) address a 
financing gap caused by a suspension of disbursements by the project’s main co-financier, the 
French GEF (FFEM - Fonds Français pour l’Environnement Mondial); and (ii) need to increase 
water availability to the elephant population during extreme droughts through the deepening of 
existing ponds.   
 
3.  The FFEM withdrew their funding from the project after Government failed to establish the 
Inter-communal Conservation Areas Management Entity (AIG) within an agreed time frame. It 
was finally created with a two-year delay. The AIG was to evolve into the long-term 
management instrument of the Gourma Conservation Areas network.  The FFEM grant is now 
closed and funds are no longer available, leaving a financing gap of about USD1.5 million.      
 
B. 
BACKGROUND  ON THE  GOURMA BIODIVERSITY 
4.  The approximately 350 elephants in the Gourma Region of Mali, are both an important 
biodiversity resource and an ecological icon.  The elephant population is found in the area 
bordering Burkina Faso and they are the most northern desert elephants in Africa. They live in a 
vast territory covering almost 30,000 Km
2
(called the Gourma/Sahel-Burkina) in extreme dry 
conditions, and have adapted to survive in this harsh environment. There is no corridor 
connecting these elephants to other elephants, making their morphology unique in Africa. 
 
5.  The elephants migrate about 450 kilometers between Burkina Faso and Mali each year, in 
search of food and water and co-exist with the local population and livestock with whom they 
share water resources, sometimes leading to human-elephants conflicts.  The Gourma 
Biodiversity Project was specifically designed to reduce these conflicts and to preserve wildlife 
and natural habitats through the establishment of Conservation Areas (CAs), especially on the 
Page  7
2
elephants’ migration routes.    The elephants are however also quite vulnerable to the extreme 
conditions as was seen in 2009 when two dozen elephants died due to a lack of water on the 
migration routes during a particularly harsh drought season.  The situation prompted the 
Government to call for the deepening of three existing ponds along the elephants’ migration 
paths to avoid a similar situation in the future.    
 
C. 
 PROJECT STATUS  
 
6.  The project was approved on September 2, 2004 and became effective on September 9, 2005. 
The grant amount is US$5,500,000 and disbursement amount as of September 30, 2010 is 
US$4,680,000 representing 85% disbursement rate. The project has achieved tangible results in 
the field as measured by: (i) an increase in biodiversity knowledge among the Gourma 
population as a result of strong public awareness activities (through local radios and community 
meetings) and the carrying out of 84 biodiversity conservation; and (ii) the creation of four out of 
seven biodiversity CAs as scheduled to be the case by project mid-term. 
 
7. 
 
The main issues faced by the project were: (i) the lack of baseline data that would have 
allowed measurement of fauna and flora resulting from project interventions; and (ii) the delay in 
the creation of the AIG.  The FFEM was to have financed a study defining the details of the AIG 
including policies and operating procedures, as well as capacity building of the AIG members in 
the management of intercommunal CAs.   
 
8.  The withdrawal of the FFEM has meant that the originally designed baseline studies and the 
financing of the private consulting firm which was recruited by the project as the implementation 
entity, in Douentza (Cellule de Mise en Oeuvre - CMO –) are either no longer funded or are 
partially funded.    
 
9.  After FFEM disbursements’ suspension, the Project Coordination Unit at the central level 
(based in Bamako) took greater responsibility in implementing field activities through increased 
implementation missions in Douentza to support the CMO, as the contracts of two consultants 
from the CMO could not be renewed due to withdrawal of the FFEM.   This arrangement 
enabled the Project to continue to make some progress and the following key activities continued 
to be on track: (i) development of community-based micro projects; (ii) organization of the 18 
mayors in an inter-communal committee; (iii) maintenance in the process of the creation of the 
AIG, which was finally created in July 2010; (iv) demarcation and formalization process of the 
CAs in a consultative and participatory manner; (iv) organization into cohesive groups of the 
communities to form a structure at the Communal level for the management of CAs 
(Organisation de Gestion des Aires de Conservation - OGAC); and (v) capacity building of the 
OGAC in CAs management.   
Page  8
3
10. The team also actively worked with the Government in search for alternative co-financing 
through other donors and other bank-financed projects to support key activities left unfunded: (i)  
the fauna and flora baseline studies; (ii) a study to reassess the elephant migration route; (iii) a 
feasibility study for the development of ecotourism in the Gourma area; (iv) a feasibility study 
on the establishment of a Foundation for biodiversity conservation; and (v) part of the services 
and operating costs of the CMO (in Douentza).  Despite these efforts, alternative funding did not 
materialize, therefore during the December 2009 implementation support and supervision 
mission, the team and the Government discussed and agreed to reduce the project scope as the 
only option remaining to address the funding issue.   
 
D. 
PROJECT RATING  
 
11.  The lack of baseline studies and the delay in the creation of the AIG have prompted an 
unsatisfactory rating of both overall project implementation and progress towards PDO.  This 
rating, unfortunately does not recognize the significant work that has been achieved on the 
ground with respect to a number of the critical indicators; or the work that has been carried out 
by the implementing agency in response to the financing gap.  As an example, the implementing 
agency was able to develop with the communities 84 biodiversity conservation micro-projects at 
the community level instead of the planned 50.  Furthermore, despite the financing gap, four CAs 
were established; and strong public awareness to create ownership of the biodiversity in the 
project areas was carried out. This has resulted in natural resources management and biodiversity 
conservation activities now being an integral part of the Communal Development Plans 
–
a
milestone in Mali as well as for the concerned communities.  In view of these key results, the 
Government of Mali considers this Project as a success story, and plans to replicate it in other 
regions of Mali, especially in Sikasso where there is a small population of elephants.  Moreover, 
it is expected that the proposed restructuring will allow for the Project, to be fully considered 
satisfactory. 
 
E. 
 PROPOSED CHANGES  
12.  On May 25, 2010, six months after an implementation review mission visited Mali, the 
project  underwent a Quality Assessment (QALP-2) which recommended that the project be 
restructured, a conclusion similar to that reached by the mission and fully aligned with the 
Government’s initial intent.  The QALP panel further recommended that the scope of the Project 
be reduced to focus on field activities that can directly contribute to the Project objective and that 
can be financed with the remaining GEF funds.  Subsequently, the Government, in a letter to the 
World Bank dated September 27, 2010 formally requested that the project be restructured. 
 
13. The proposed restructuring will help to: (i) operationalize the AIG and strengthen its capacity 
for the management of the network of the four CAs created; (ii) carry out infrastructure works 
Page  9
4
for the deepening of ponds as part of the habitat management activities of the Project to ensure 
continued availability of water for the Gourma elephants; (iii) consolidate sustainable 
management of community level micro-projects; and (iv) strengthen institutional capacity of 
public services to support communities and local government initiatives in biodiversity 
conservation and management. 
 
Project Development Objectives and Components
14. 
The original PDO “Assist the Recipient in implementing environmental protection and 
biodiversity conservation activities in the Sahelian priority area of Gourma, through the improved 
sustainable management of natural resource use, and improved returns for the local population” 
will be maintained.  This PDO is still attainable.  Although changes will be made to the 
components, the PDO is not affected because the changes mainly relate to the elimination of the 
large baseline data collection, for which the implementing agency has satisfactorily collected 
alternative proxy data that will be used to measure the PDO.   The other change pertains to the 
elimination of the funding source for the CMO, and for the creation of the AIG which was 
subsequently created by the government using their own financing, as indicated above, and the 
cancellation of work and goods for touristic infrastructure.  The specifics of the changes in the 
components are presented in the attached matrix (Annex 1).   The updated results framework is 
also attached (Annex 2). 
 
Safeguards
15. The restructured project will not result in a higher safeguards category or trigger a new 
policy. The Project Implementation Unit has prepared the Terms of Reference for the 
Environmental Assessment (EA) of the three ponds to be deepened.  They will be submitted to the 
World Bank for review before commencement of works.  The deepening of ponds, as contained in 
the existing ESMF,  shows that the civil works are minor and site-specific, and will thus have no 
adverse environmental and social implications.   In addition there is no land take or resettlement 
concerns as stipulated in OP/BP 4.12.   
 
F. 
  APPRAISAL SUMMARY 
16.    
Analysis:  The proposed changes will not have a major effect on the original economic 
and social aspects of the Project.  The environmental category will remain B because the changes 
are limited to existing ponds to be deepened and the restructuring will not involve any exceptions 
to Bank policies. 
 
17.   
Sustainability:   
 The main risk that could jeopardize the sustainability of the 
outcomes of the Project is the possible lack of long term financing of the AIG. To mitigate this 
risk, the team is working closely with the Government to ensure that management costs of the 
CAs are earmarked in the yearly National Budget.

5
18.  There are no outstanding audit reports for this Project. 
 
Reallocation of funds
19.  With the cancellation of FFEM funds, the Government of Mali requested a reallocation of 
GEF proceeds.  The reallocation of funds is necessary to redirect available GEF funding to 
activities that will directly contribute to the achievement of the PDO.  The reallocation includes 
the following changes: (i) an increase in “Consultant Services & Audit”, “Training” and 
“Operating Costs” categories to  support the operationalization of the AIG, capacity building for 
communities and their associations in managing the network of CAs, capacity building to 
communities for the implementation of biodiversity micro-projects, and operating costs related to 
project administration & management, and execution of project internal audits; (ii) a decrease of 
funds in the “Goods” and “Works” categories due to the cancellation of construction and 
management of tourism infrastructure buildings and equipment; (iii) No change in the “Sub-
projects” category given that the restructured project will not finance additional sub-projects.

Extension of closing date  
20. 
 
Extreme drought in the Gourma region followed by heavy rains in June, July and August 
have flooded the Gourma project areas, essentially halting activities in the work program under 
the project.   The Recipient has therefore requested an extension of the project closing date from 
June 30, 2011 to December 31 2012 to enable the project staff to undertake the procurement 
process and to fully complete these small infrastructure works, as well as other ongoing activities 
which need to be completed (i.e. AIG operationalization, community training, etc). The 
Recipient has prepared an action plan covering the extension period which has been assessed as  
acceptable to the Bank.  The procurement plan is attached as annex 3.
