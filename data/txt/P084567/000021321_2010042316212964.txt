FINANCING AGREEMENT 
 
AGREEMENT dated Mar4ch, 2006, entered into between 
PEOPLE’S 
REPUBLIC OF BANGLADESH (“Recipient”) and INTERNATIONAL 
DEVELOPMENT ASSOCIATION (“Association”) for the purpose of providing 
financing in support of the Program (as defined in the Appendix to this Agreement).  The 
Association has decided to provide this financing on the basis, inter alia, of: (a) the 
actions which the Recipient has already taken under the Program and which are described 
in Section I of Schedule 1 to this Agreement; and (b) the Recipient’s maintenance of an 
appropriate macro-economic policy framework.  The Recipient and the Association 
therefore hereby agree as follows: 
 
ARTICLE I - GENERAL CONDITIONS; DEFINITIONS 
 
1.01. 
The General Conditions (as defined in the Appendix to this Agreement) 
constitute an integral part of this Agreement. 
 
1.02. 
Unless the context requires otherwise, the capitalized terms used in the Financing 
Agreement have the meanings ascribed to them in the General Conditions or in 
the Appendix to this Agreement. 
 
ARTICLE II  - FINANCING 
2.01. 
The Association agrees to extend to the Recipient, on the terms and conditions 
set forth or referred to in this Agreement, a credit in an amount equivalent to 
sixty-nine million one hundred thousand Special Drawing Rights 
 
(SDR 69,100,000) (“Credit”).  
2.02. 
The Recipient may withdraw the proceeds of the Financing in support of the 
Program in accordance with Section II of Schedule 1 to this Agreement.
 
2.03. 
The Maximum Commitment Charge Rate payable by the Recipient on the 
Unwithdrawn Financing Balance shall be one-half of one percent (1/2 of 1%) per 
annum. 
2.04. 
The Service Charge payable by the Recipient on the Withdrawn Credit Balance 
shall be equal to three-fourths of one percent (3/4 of 1%) per annum. 
2.05. 
The Payment Dates are February 15 and August 15 in each year.

ARTICLE III - PROGRAM 
 
3.01 
The Recipient declares its commitment to the Program and its implementation.  
To this end: 
 
(a) 
the Recipient and the Association shall from time to time, at the request

the Program; 
 
(b)

(c)

Program, or any action taken under the Program including any action 
specified in Section I of Schedule 1 to this Agreement. 
 
ARTICLE IV - REMEDIES OF THE ASSOCIATION 
 
4.01. 
The Additional Event of Suspension consists of the following, namely that a

significant part of it, will be carried out.

SCHEDULE 1 
 
Program Actions; Availability of Financing Proceeds 
 
Section I.  Actions Taken Under the Program
The actions taken by the Recipient under the Program include the following:
 
System Management 
 
1. 
The Recipient has revised its salary subvention system, as agreed with the 
Association, to better link subventions of
 teachers’ salaries to outcomes on standardized 
examinations, by, inter alia: (i) providing poorly performing schools a two-year period to 
improve their performance; and (ii) increasing subvention to well performing schools. 
 
2. 
The Recipient has contracted out services of outside experts to carry out an 
evaluation of schools and colleges, inter alia: (i) to award them government recognition;  
and (ii) make them eligible to receive MPO subventions, under all the education boards 
in the country. 
 
3. 
The Recipient has completed the identification of over-served and underserved 
Upazilas, and has begun setting up secondary schools in underserved Upazilas, as agreed 
with the Association. 
 
4. 
The Recipient has designed, and is piloting, a means tested stipend program for 
the poorest students, in a manner satisfactory to the Association. 
 
5. 
The Recipient has completed an expenditure tracking survey, and has disclosed 
its outcomes, and begun implementing its recommendations in a manner satisfactory to 
the Association. 
 
6. 
The Recipient has implemented the MOE’s financial management plan and made 
the MOE’s financial unit fully operational. 
 
Teacher Effectiveness 
 
7. 
The Recipient’s parliament has approved the NTRCA legislation allowing 
certification of a pool of qualified teachers for SMCs whose recruitment shall be based on 
academic qualifications and examinations. 
 
8. 
NTRCA has administered the first test for applicants, in a manner satisfactory to 
the Association.

9. 
The Recipient’s Cabinet has: (i) approved a new in-service and pre-service 
teacher training policy, including an incentive framework for qualified teachers, as

Curriculum and Textbooks 
 
10. 
The Recipient has launched the implementation of the NCTB restructuring plan 
agreed with the Association. 
 
11. 
The Recipient has approved revised textbook manuscript approval criteria, and 
taken all measures to implement and disseminate such criteria, as agreed with the 
Association. 
 
12. 
The Recipient: (i) has completed the evaluation of the first phase of competition 
for textbook publishing and printing, in a manner satisfactory to the Association; and  
(ii) has commenced the implementation of an action plan related thereto.
