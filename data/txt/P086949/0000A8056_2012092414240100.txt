                         Implementation Status Overview
                         The Project continues to make progress towards meeting its revised objectives. The Romanian Government has successfully increased its commitment of EU-CAP funds as the
                         projects submitted by farmers have become better prepared, also as a result of theProject'scontribution, which disseminated information and provided training on EU accession and


                                                                                                                                                                                                           Page 1 of 6
                             The World Bank                                                                                                                                      Report No: ISR7944



                         CAP funding to about 1.3 million farmers as part of 11 multi-media campaigns. In particular, Project activities financed under the Competitive Grant Scheme have begun generating
                         economic, social and environmental impacts through over 100 new technologies and improved practices for management and marketing and contributed to a steady increase of the
                         weight of Romania's agro-food exports to EU-member countries and beyond. To exemplify, over 30 new technologies compatible with EU standards have been developed under 412
                         sub-projects completed.The training activities in the four Training and Information Centers set up with Project support successfully continue with funds from other sources under the
Public Disclosure Copy




                         close supervision of the Consultative Council, to provide training to private farmers, agro-processors,food safety inspectors, extension staff, researchers.

                         The works for rehabilitation and modernization of three institutes for research and development in agriculture (National Agricultural Research and Development Institute (NARDI)
                         Fundulea, National Institute forAnimal Biology and Nutrition (IBNA) Balotesti, Research and Development Institute for Potato and Sugar Beet (NIRDPSB) Braov continued and are
                         heading towards completion before the end of 2012, thus contributing to the reform of R&D sector.

                         Improvement of the Management Information Systems in the Agricultural Administration continued through procurement of modern IT hardware for the Agricultural Paying and
                         Intervention Agency (APIA), which, once installed, will be a core element of the reform action plan for the Integrated Administration and Control System (IACS) for EU-CAP funds, and
                         an activity in support of an EC-GOR agreed action plan towards improving the administration of EU rural funds.
