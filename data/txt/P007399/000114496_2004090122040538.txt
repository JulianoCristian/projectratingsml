First Amendment to the Development Credit Agreement 
Excellency:
Please refer to the Development Credit Agreement dated June 21, 1995 entered 
into between the Republic of  Honduras (the Borrower) and the International 
Development Association (the Association).
As a result of the damage caused by Hurricane Mitch in the Borrower’s territory, 
the Borrower requested the Association to reallocate part of the proceeds of the 
Credit to finance, inter alia, the carrying out of Emergency Education Subprojects and 
Emergency Education Food Subprojects under the Project. In response to such request,

ANNEX 2
“SCHEDULE 2
Description of the Project
The objectives of the Project are to:
(a)
improve the academic achievement levels and reduce the repetition rate of 
students enrolled in public primary schools;
(b)
provide bilingual education in public primary schools that serve the 
Miskito and the Garífuna ethnic groups; and
(c)
improve the management efficiency of MOE.
The Project consists of the following parts, subject to such modifications 
thereof as the Borrower and the Association may agree upon from time to time to 
achieve such objectives:
Part A:
Basic Education Quality
1.
Provide technical assistance and educational materials to:
(a)
strengthen pre-service teacher training for primary teachers; and
(b)
train in-service pre-primary and primary teachers, school supervisors and 
school principals of MOE.
Page  7
2.
(a)
Provide the Borrower with:
(i)
technical assistance and equipment to: (A) review existing primary 
school 
textbooks and teacher’s and student’s guides 
therefor; and (B) develop Modular 
Textbooks for primary schools; 
and
(ii)
warehouse facilities for educational materials;
(b)
print or procure textbooks, library books and other educational materials 
for public pre-primary and primary schools and for primary teacher training programs;
(c)
distribute textbooks, library books and other educational materials for 
public pre-primary and primary schools and for primary teacher training programs; and
(d)
carry out field tests for new educational materials prepared under (a) 
(i) above.
3.
(a)
Provide the Borrower with technical assistance and equipment to develop: 
(i)
dictionaries, grammar books, textbooks and students and teachers' 
guides to 
provide bilingual education in public primary schools that 
serve the Miskito 
and the Garífuna ethnic groups;
(ii)
teachers training materials and audio-visual teaching aids to 
provide 
bilingual education in public primary schools that 
serve the Miskito and the 
Garífuna ethnic groups;
(iii)
training programs in bilingual education for primary school 
teachers and 
school supervisors working in public primary schools 
that serve the Miskito 
and the Garífuna ethnic groups; and
(iv)
a program for operations research and evaluation of the activities 
referred to 
in (a) (i) to (iii) above; and (b) strengthen the Borrower's 
program for 
bilingual education of ethnic groups through the 
provision of vehicles and 
office equipment required for the 
carrying out of its activities.
4.
Develop the Borrower's institutional and technical capabilities for external 
evaluation of academic achievement of students enrolled in public primary schools, 
through the provision of technical assistance, equipment and operating costs.
5.
(a)
Design, construct and equip approximately 290 additional classrooms in 
the public primary schools and rehabilitate, as needed, the existing facilities of 
such schools;
(b)
provide maintenance for the public primary schools with new classrooms or 
rehabilitated facilities;
(c)
assign approximately 290 teachers therefor; and
(d)
expand the CCIEs program, through the creation of approximately 1200 
additional CCIEs in poor rural communities.
Part B:
Institutional Strengthening
1.
(a)
Reorganize and decentralize MOE, through the consolidation of existing 
units, and creation of new administrative units, including, inter alia, the 
establishment of the Departmental Directorates of Education;
(b)
strengthen MOE’s management capabilities and information systems;
(c)
design, construct or rehabilitate the facilities for the Departmental 
Directorates of Education, as needed; and
(d)
acquire office equipment and vehicles, as needed, for the Departmental 
Page  8
Directorates of Education.
2.
Design and carry out a pilot program to improve teacher's classroom performance 
in public primary schools.
3.
Provide technical assistance and equipment for the strengthening of PCU in the 
administration, monitoring and evaluation of the Project, including the carrying out 
of studies therefor.
Part C:
Emergency Subprojects
1.
Carrying out of Emergency Education Subprojects which consist of the acquisition 
and distribution of teaching materials, textbooks and other educational materials, 
acquisition of school furniture, provision of psychological assistance to the 
teachers, students and their families and/or any other investment for productive 
purposes which meets the criteria set forth in the Project Operational Manual. 
2.
Carrying out of Emergency Education Food Subprojects which consist of the 
provision of food supplements to students who are enrolled in those public pre-primary 
and primary schools selected by the Borrower in accordance with the criteria set forth 
in the Project Operational Manual.
* * *
The Project is expected to be completed by June 30, 2000.”.

1.

employ consultants, whose qualifications, experience and terms and conditions of

Section III. Special Provisions
A.
In addition and without limitation to any other provisions set forth in this 
Schedule or the Guidelines, the following principles of procurement shall expressly 
govern all procurement of goods referred to in Parts B.1 (a), B.2 (a) and B.3 (a) of 
Section I of this Schedule:
1.
Contracts shall be awarded to the lowest evaluated bid in accordance with 
criteria set forth in the bidding documents, and without taking into account, in the 
evaluation, the financial cost of foreign exchange components.
2.
Foreign bidders shall not, as a condition for submitting bids, be required to:
(i)
be registered in Honduras;
(ii)
have a representative in Honduras;

B.
In addition and without limitation to any other provisions set forth in this 
Schedule or the Consultants Guidelines, the following principles of procurement shall 
expressly govern all procurement of consultants' services referred to in Section II of 
this Schedule:
1.
Foreign consultants shall be permitted to participate in the selection process 
even if there is availability of Honduran consultants for the services being procured.
2.
Foreign consultants shall not be required to be registered with Honduran 
associations or to be associated with Honduran consulting firms as a condition for 
participating in any selection process.
