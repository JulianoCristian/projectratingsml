Amendment to the Loan Agreement and SC-Loan Agreement
Excellency:
Please refer to the Loan Agreement and SC-Loan Agreement, both dated July 20, 
1994 entered into between the Republic of Paraguay (the Borrower) and the 
International Bank for Reconstruction and Development (the Bank), as amended.
In response to the Borrower’s letter of February 2, 1998  requesting the Bank to 
amend the Loan Agreement and the SC-Loan Agreement by expanding the objectives of the 
Project for purposes of including activities to prevent or rehabilitate damages to the 
economic and social public infrastructure which has been, or may be, caused by the 
floods resulting from the El Niño Phenomenon in the Borrower’s territory, the Bank 
hereby agrees to amend the Loan Agreement and the SC-Loan Agreement as set forth in 
Annexes 1 and 2 to this Amendment Letter, respectively.
This Amendment Letter shall:  (a) be executed in two counterparts each of which 
shall be an original; and  (b) become effective as of March 15, 1998 once the Bank has 
received: 
(i) 
one fully executed original of this Amendment Letter;
(ii) 
evidence, satisfactory to the Bank, that the CEN Unit has been 
established and staffed as provided in paragraph 3 of Schedule 8 to the 
Loan Agreement;
(iii)
evidence that the amounts outstanding in the Special Account "A" and the 
SC-Special Account "A" as of March 15,1998 have been transferred to the 
Special Account "B" and the SC-Special Account "B" (as such terms are 
defined in the Loan Agreement and the SC-Loan Agreement, as amended 
pursuant to Annexes 1 and 2, respectively), respectively, in a manner 
satisfactory to the Bank;
(iv)
a procurement plan for Part B of the Project, satisfactory to the Bank 
(which shall include, inter alia, the list of Subprojects, a detailed 
description of the cost of each Subproject and a timetable to carry out 
each such Subproject); 
(v) 
evidence, satisfactory to the Bank, that the financial management system 
referred to in Section 4.03(a) of the Loan Agreement has been 
established;  
(vi) 
evidence, satisfactory to the Bank, that the Operational Manual has been 
approved by the Borrower;
(vii)
a list of indicators, satisfactory to the Bank, to monitor the carrying 
out of Part B of the Project;
(viii)evidence, satisfactory to the Bank, that the execution and delivery of 
this Amendment Letter on behalf of the Borrower has been duly authorized 
or ratified by the Borrower’s Congress; and
(ix) 
a legal opinion, satisfactory to the Bank, of counsel, acceptable to the 
Bank, stating that this Amendment Letter has been duly authorized or 
ratified by, and executed and delivered on behalf of, the Borrower and 
is legally binding upon the Borrower in accordance with its terms.
References to the Loan Agreement in the previous paragraph are references to 
such agreement as amended pursuant to Annex 1.

Schedule 2 is hereby amended to read as follows:
"SCHEDULE 2
Description of the Project"
The objectives of the Project are to: (a) strengthen the private sector in order 
that such sector can assist the Borrower in fostering economic growth in its 
territory; (b) strengthen the ongoing reform of the financial sector in order to 
increase the efficiency on resource mobilization and allocation and financial 
intermediation; (c) provide medium and long-term credit to Investment Enterprises in 
order to overcome the current investment constraints in the Borrower's territory; and 
(d) assist the Borrower, on an emergency basis, to: (i) reduce the loss of human life 
and a deterioration in living standards, prevent damages to the economic and social 
public infrastructure, and rehabilitate economic and social public infrastructure 
which has been damaged by the floods resulting from the El Niño Phenomenon; and (ii) 
strengthen its capabilities to forecast and respond to future El Niño Phenomena or 
other natural phenomena.
The Project consists of the following parts, subject to such modifications 
thereof as the Bank and the Borrower may agree upon from time to time to achieve such 
objectives.
Part A:
Investments
1.
Financing of Investment Projects.
2.
Financing of SC-Investment Projects.
3.
Strengthening of the UTEP through the provision of consultants’ services 
and training.
Part B:
El Niño Emergency Assistance 
1.
Carrying out of Preventive Subprojects (in areas to be selected by the 
Borrower in accordance with the criteria set forth in the Operational Manual) which 
Page  7
may consist of river bed cleaning and strengthening of river defenses (including 
levees, embankments and other related river protection investments).
2.
Carrying out of Flood Rehabilitation Subprojects (in areas to be selected 
by the Borrower in accordance with the criteria set forth in the Operational Manual) 
which may consist of rehabilitation and/or reconstruction of  economic and social 
public infrastructure which has been damaged by the floods resulting from the El Niño 
Phenomenon, such as bridges, roads, river defenses (including levees, embankments and 
other type of river defenses), school and health facilities and any other investment 
of a similar nature acceptable to the Bank.
3.
Carrying out of Mitigation Subprojects which consist of: (a) the 
construction of about three earth terraces (in areas to be selected by the Borrower in 
a manner acceptable to the Bank) for the benefit of the population which has been 
temporarily displaced as a result of the El Niño Phenomenon; and (b) the construction 
of about three public service facilities (which shall include the installation of 
water and sanitation systems) in the city of Asuncion for the benefit of the 
population which has been temporarily displaced as a result of the El Niño Phenomenon.
4.
Acquisition and utilization of bailey bridges, mobile power plants and 
water pumps and other capital goods, acceptable to the Bank, urgently required during 
any flood emergency caused by El Niño Phenomenon. 
5.
Strengthening, through the provision of technical assistance and training 
and the acquisition and utilization of equipment, of the administrative and technical 
capacity of Selected Entities.
6.
(a)
Design of a national flood emergency plan to respond to future El 
Niño Phenomena and other similar natural phenomena; and (b) carrying out of a social 
impact analysis of the population that is affected by recurrent floods in the city of 
Asunción.
7.
Carrying out of semi-annual technical audits of  Part B of the Project.
8.
Strengthening, through the provision of technical assistance, of the 
capacity of the CEN Unit to coordinate and monitor the implementation of Parts. B.1 
through B.7 of the Project.
* * * The Project is expected to be completed by December 31, 2000.".

Part C:
Other Procurement Procedures 
1.
Limited International Bidding
Goods estimated to cost $200,000 equivalent or more per contract shall be 
procured under contracts awarded in accordance with the provisions of paragraph 3.2 of 
the Guidelines.
2.
National Competitive Bidding
(a)

awarded on the basis of international or national shopping procedures, at the option 
of the Borrower, in accordance with the provisions of paragraphs 3.5 and 3.6 of the

Procurement of Small Works
Small works estimated to cost less than $250,000 equivalent per contract, up to 
an aggregate amount not to exceed $4,000,000 equivalent, may be procured under

qualified domestic contractors in response to a written invitation. The invitation 
shall include a detailed description of the works, including basic specifications, the 
required completion date, a basic form of agreement acceptable to the Bank, and 
relevant drawings, where applicable. The award shall be made to the contractor who 
offers the lowest price quotation for the required work, and who has the experience 
and resources to complete the contract successfully.
