                         Project Development Objectives
                         Project Development Objective (from Project Appraisal Document)

                         The project development objective is to rehabilitate and develop critical national and rural feeder roads and transport infrastructure, improve critical urban infrastructure
                         in the major towns that form the national and state capitals of Southern Sudan (i.e. Juba, Wau, Malakal, Rumbek, Aweil, Warap, Yei, Yambio,Torit, Bor and Bentiu) and
                         build capacity for planning, construction and sustainable operation, maintenance and management of the infrastructure in Southern Sudan.

                         Implementation Status Overview
                         Roads Improvement: The Emergency Accessibility Improvements Project was completed and 860 km of roads were maintained and improved. The rehabilitation of the Faraksika
                         toYambio road (170 km), and the design services for the Nadapal toJuba and Kaya-Juba roads are completed. A feeder roads development plan was delivered in September 2010
                         and the design of 1400 km of feeder roads was completedby the end of June 2011.

                         Urban Infrastructure Rehabilitation in Juba: The rehabilitation of Ministerial office buildings, the Assembly, the Juba Hospital and Water Treatment Plant, as well as, the construction
                         of the Water Care Unit are completed.
Public Disclosure Copy




                         Institution Development: Training for 175 staff of the implementing institutions and TA are provided in the areas of: urban development, roads management, procurement, contract
                         management, human resources development, and social and environment assessment. Roads materials laboratory has been established and Guidelines for Environment Monitoring
                         for the road sector has been developed.

                         The project has achieved its objectives. Key interstate and regional roads, with a total length of 1030 km are reopened to traffic. Design for 560 km of asphalt roads and 1400 km of
                         feeder roads is completed and readily available for implementation. The ministerial office buildings and the Juba teaching hospital are fully functional. The water treatment plant is
                         providing clean water to about 250, 000 residents of Juba. The water care unit in Juba is in use by the Juba commission. The project has contributed to the creation of core capacity
                         in the areas of urban development, roads management, procurement, contract management, and social and environmental assessment, under the Ministry of Roads and Bridges and
                         Ministry of Housing and Physical Planning.
