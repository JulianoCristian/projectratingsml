                         Project Development Objectives
                         Project Development Objective (from Project Appraisal Document)

                         The project development objective is to rehabilitate and develop critical national and rural feeder roads and transport infrastructure, improve critical urban
                         infrastructure in the major towns that form the national and state capitals of Southern Sudan (i.e. Juba, Wau, Malakal, Rumbek, Aweil, Warap, Yei, Yambio,Torit,
                         Bor and Bentiu) and build capacity for planning, construction and sustainable operation, maintenance and management of the infrastructure in Southern Sudan.

                         Implementation Status Overview
                         Roads Improvement: The Emergency Accessibility Improvements Project was completed and 860 km of roads were maintained and improved. The rehabilitation of the Faraksika –
                         Yambio road (170 km) was completed in November 2010. The design services for the Nadapal –Juba and Kaya-Juba roads will be completed by the end of April 2011. A feeder
                         roads development plan was delivered in September 2010 and the design of 1500 km of feeder roads is expected to be completed by the end of May 2011.

                         Urban Infrastructure Rehabilitation in Juba: The rehabilitation of Ministerial office buildings, The Assembly, Juba Hospital and Water Treatment Plant, as well as, the construction of a
Public Disclosure Copy




                         Water Care Unit are fully completed.

                         Institutional Development: Training for 175 staff of the implementing institutions and TA are provided in the areas of: urban development management, roads management,
                         procurement, contract management, human resources development, and social and environment assessment. Roads materials laboratory has been established and Guidelines for
                         Environment Monitoring for the road sector has been developed.

                         The project has achieved its objectives. Key interstate and regional roads, with a total length of 1030 km are reopened to traffic. Design for 560 km of asphalt roads and 1500 km of
                         feeder roads will be completed by the end of May 2011, and readily available for implementation. The ministerial office buildings and Juba teaching hospital are fully functional. The
                         water treatment plant is providing clean water to about 250, 000 residents of Juba. The sole water care unit in Juba is operational by the Juba commission. The project has
                         contributed to the creation of core capacity in the areas of urban development management, roads management, procurement, contract management, and social and environment
                         assessment, under the Ministry of Transport and Roads and Ministry of Housing and Physical Planning.
