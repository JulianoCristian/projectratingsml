                         Implementation Status Overview
                         The Citizen's Damage Compensation Program (CDCP), implemented by the Cabinet Division and the National Database and Registration Authority (NADRA) with support of all
                         provincial/regional governments has made good progress in implementing the project in accordance with the approved Operations Manual. Overall, the project is moving satisfactorily
                         towards achieving its objectives:
                         More than a million eligible households have been enrolled in the program, and the majority of them have received both tranches of their benefit payments. The operational audits are
                         providing timely information on the due diligence, which need to continue until completion of project implementation. While the pace of grievance redressal remains slow, more than
                         150,000 households have been cleared through eligibility appeals. To support the resolution of remaining eligibility appeals and complaints, the Joint Supervision and Evaluation

                         Key Decisions Regarding Implementation
                         1. The project has been restructured to improve the pace of CDCP's grievance redressal and necessary due diligence.
                         2. The Future Disaster Response Action Plan for early recovery cash transfers (approved by the Prime Minister) will be disseminated to all key stakeholders after which the
                         appropriate institutional arrangements will be put in place to allow for its future application. (in progress)
                         3. The GoP to decide on how to meet the funding gap due to excess appeals and bring up its funding contribution to the required level. (In progress)
