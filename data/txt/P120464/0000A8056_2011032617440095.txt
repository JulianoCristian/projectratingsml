                         Project Development Objectives
                         Project Development Objective (from Project Appraisal Document)

                         The project's development objective is to: (i) improve the delivery of the Basic Package of Health Services (BPHS) in four states(Upper Nile, Jongeli, Central
                         Equatoria, and Eastern Equatoria); and (ii) strengthen key stewardship functions of the MOH,including monitoring.

                         Implementation Status Overview
                         There is ongoing supervision of this project by Juba-based staff. , the implementation is progressing reasonably and the main challenges identified during the first months of the
                         project have mostly been resolved:

                                                                                                                                                                                                               Page 1 of 7
                             The World Bank                                                                                                                                        Report No: ISR2457



                         i.       Delivery of Basic Package of Health Services trough Lead agencies in four states are progressing significantly, although there is a need for further work to meet the agreed
                         results;
Public Disclosure Copy




                         ii.      The contracts for the procurement of the drugs (LIB&ICB) under the project are signed, the distribution of the first lot of drugs procured under LIB has been completed
                         successfully before the referendum. The second lot of drug has started to arrive in Juba and distribution is expected to start in March 2011;
                         iii.     Implementation of routine HMIS tools and quantitative supervisory checklist on pilot basis in three states started supported by the project and in few counties supported by
                         others agencies such as USAID has been completed. The percentage of health facilities submitting their monthly report on time to the county health department increase from 0 to
                         34% in the last 6 months. The review of the implementation progress with the objective to assess the relevance of the HMIS tools based on partners and SMOH experiences, fine
                         tune them as required, and discuss how to proceed with the roll out of the definitive tool has started and to concluded on February 15, 2011. The outcomes of the review and the
                         scale up plan should be submitted to the World Bank for review.
