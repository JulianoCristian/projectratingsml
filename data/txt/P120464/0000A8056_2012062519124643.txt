                         Project Development Objectives
                         Project Development Objective (from Project Appraisal Document)

                         The project's development objective is to: (i) improve the delivery of the Basic Package of Health Services (BPHS) in four states(Upper Nile, Jongeli, Central Equatoria,
                         and Eastern Equatoria); and (ii) strengthen key stewardship functions of the MOH,including monitoring.

                         Implementation Status Overview
                         The implementation of the project is progressing and the project is on track to achieve its development objectives. As of the end of May, 2012, all the funds allocated under the project
                         were fully committed and a total of US$67.7 million out of the US$ 70 million had been disbursed. The Ministry of Health has requested to extend the project closing date from June

                         30, 2012 to September 28, 2012 and the extension is under consideration.

                         i.       Delivery of Basic Package of Health Services through Lead agencies in four states has progressed significantly. According to the available HMIS data, the availability and
                         use of high impact services in the project supported four states (Upper Nile, Jonglei, Central Equatoria and Eastern Equatoria) have been improved significantly. The number of
Public Disclosure Copy




                         health facilities providing immunization, antenatal care and skilled clean delivery has increased from Quarter 3 2010 to Quarter 1 2012 from 184 to 379, from 190 to 531 and from 125
                         to 457 respectively. Over the same period the number of children under 5 treated in facilities increased from 29,121 to 205,181; the number of children under 12 months who
                         received DPT3 vaccination increased from 14,022 to 44,815 and the number of pregnant women receiving antenatal care during a visit to a health provider increase from 4,913 to
                         20,175.

                         ii.      The procurement and distribution of drugs has been implemented successfully, The last shipment of the drugs will arrive in the country at the end of June 2012 and should
                         cover the needs of the country till the end of September 2012.

                         iii.    The planned M&E activities (Health Management Information System, Quantitative Supervisory Checklist, Lot Quality Assurance Sampling Survey and Health Facility
                         Assessment) under the project have been completed; The data collected through various means helped MOH as well development partners to identify priorities, recognize
                         successes, and appreciate gaps.
