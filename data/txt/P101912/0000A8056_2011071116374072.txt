                         Project Development Objectives
                         Project Development Objective (from Project Appraisal Document)

                         To build capacity of the public sector in Southern Sudan to fulfill its mandated role in livestock and fisheries, and to promote continuity in provision of key
                         services in the livestock sector in five selected states of Southern Sudan.

                         Implementation Status Overview
                         This is the last ISR before project closing which is scheduled for June 30, 2011. Progress towards achievement of the PDO is rated moderately satisfactory and implementation
                         progress remains moderately satisfactory as assessed in the sixth ISR. All grant proceeds have been disbursed. Some project funds (out of the GoSS contribution) remain for which
                         use will be decided by MARF and the MDTF-SS Oversight Committee. The government has finished writing its completion report and the Team has launched an ICR mission.

                                                     restitute and support a
                                                     community-based animal
                                                     health system in which
                                                     community animal health
                                                     workers (CAHWs), Stock
                                                     Persons (SPs) and Animal
                                                     Health Auxiliaries (AHAs)
                                                     handle vaccination and
                                                     livestock disease outbreaks at
                                                     the village and cattle camp
                                                     levels. Between themselves,
                                                     the retained NGOs trained,
                                                     equipped and deployed over
                                                     1200 CAHWs, SPs and
                                                     Animal Health Auxiliaries to
                                                     help with early detection,
                                                     reporting and treatment and
                                                     vaccination of animals against
                                                     major diseases. Over 580,000
                                                     animals were vaccinated and
                                                     about 330,000 animals
                                                     treated against economically
                                                     important diseases. Twelve
                                                     mobile veterinary clinics (with
                                                     diagnostic and treatment
