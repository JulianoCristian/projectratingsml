                         Main Achievements include:

                         - PDO rating has been upgraded from MS to S following the July 2011 Board Level Restructuring, which aligned the PDO with the available project resources in view of additional
                         stocks of obsolete pesticides to be treated;
Public Disclosure Copy




                         - Completion of a nation-wide inventory and database;
                         - Preparation of a proposal for treatment options and cost estimates for inventoried contaminated soil with support from an internationally-hired TAD;
                         - Completion and disclosure of a CESA;
                         - Rehabilitation of six major collection centers for safeguarding of obsolete pesticides, which have been receiving low risk obsolete pesticides from small sites spread out all over the
                         country;
                         - Contracting of a disposal firm, and the work is expected to complete by the closing date;
                         - Beginning of low risk stocks’ safeguarding;
                         - Implementation of a national prevention work plan, including communication and awareness raising activities and a sustainability roadmap to ensure project objective is carried
                         forward after the project closes;
                         - A comprehensive strategy for dealing with future stocks of obsolete pesticides and associated wastes has been prepared and discussed with stakeholders;
                         - National stakeholder forum has been established; and
                         - A final draft Container management plan submitted for endorsement by the Steering Committee.
