(Second Social Development Project - Health and Nutrition)
Fifth Amendment
Dear Sir,
Please refer to the Loan Agreement (Second Social Development Project - Health 
and Nutrition) dated February 8, 1993 between the Republic of Ecuador (the Borrower) 
and the International Bank for Reconstruction and Development (the Bank), as amended 
on August 5, 1993, April 13, 1994, June 2, 1995 and September 7, 1995.
Pursuant to your request for assistance in the financing of certain 
reconstruction activities for damage caused by El Niño Phenomenon detailed in the aide 
memoire signed by the Bank on October 8, 1997, the Bank hereby agrees to amend the 
Loan Agreement as follows:
1.
Section 1.02
(a)
paragraph (i) is deleted and replaced by the following:
(i)
“Health Service Areas” means a network of facilities in 
selected geographical areas providing health services to 
the 
population as generically referred to in Decree 3292 
published in the 
Borrower’s Registro Oficial on May 11, 
1992;
(b)
paragraph (t): the words “and Pichincha” are deleted and replaced by “, 
Imbabura and Pichincha”;
(c)
New definitions are added as paragraphs (w), (x) and (y) to read as 
follows:
(w)
“Emergency Subprojects” means investments for the 
rehabilitation, repair and equipment of health facilities and for the 
acquisition 
and distribution of medicines and other medical supplies 
required to prevent and/or 
mitigate the damage caused by the El Niño 
Phenomenon and which are eligible for 
financing in accordance with the  Operational Manual, as hereinafter defined;
(x)
“El Niño Phenomenon” means the appearance, from time to 
time, 
of anomalously warm sea surface temperature in the Central and 
Eastern Pacific 
ocean near to the equator which gives rise to unusual 
changes in normal climate 
patterns around the globe including the 
Western coastal region of South 
America;
(y)
“Operational Manual” means a manual, satisfactory to the 
Bank, 
to be approved by the Borrower pursuant the provisions of 
paragraph (b) at the 
end of this Amendment Letter, setting forth the 
procedures for implementation of 
Part D of the Project including, inter 
alia, the eligibility criteria of Emergency 
Subprojects, the contractual 
and administrative arrangements for implementation of 
Subprojects, and 
the flow funds, and the monitoring and evaluation procedures for 
Part D 
of the Project, as the same may be amended from time to time by 
agreement between the Borrower and the Bank;
2.
Section 3.01 is amended:
(a)
by deleting the words “and Chimborazo” in paragraph (a) and replacing it 
by “, Chimborazo and Imbabura”; and

by adding after the acronym “CEMEIN” the words:
“Part D of the Project through MSP, in under the ovecoordination 
with 
of El Niño emergency coordination unit established within the 
Borrower’s 
presidency, pursuant to presidential decree No. 740 dated 
October 22, 1997”.
3.
Section 3.08: Paragraph (d):  The first two sentences are deleted and replaced 
by:
“(d)
The Borrower, through MSP, shall use the vaccines and 
insecticides provided under Part A.3 (a) of the Project to combat 
outbreaks and 
potential outbreaks of cholera, malaria and dengue fever.”
4.
Section 3.12
(a)
The preamble in paragraph (a) is amended by deleting “and Chimborazo” and 
adding: “, Chimborazo and Imbabura”;
(b)
paragraph (a) is amended by deleting “and Pichincha” and adding: “, 
Pichincha and Imbabura”;
(c)
paragraphs (a) (vi) (A) and (B) and (a) (x) are amended by deleting “and 
Pichincha” and adding:  “, Pichincha and Imbabura”.
5.
Section 3.21
(a)
paragraph (g): the amounts of “$5,000” and “$5,000” are deleted and 
replaced respectively by the amounts “$25,000” and “$50,000”;
(b)
paragraph (h): the amount “$5,000” is deleted and replaced by the amount 
“$50,000”.
6.
A new Section 3.22 is added to read as follows:
Section 3.22.  The Borrower shall ensure that Emergency Subprojects are 
appraised, selected, carried out, financed, monitored and supervised in accordance 
with the provisions of the Operational Manual.

the table in paragraph 1 of Schedule 1 is replaced to read as per the

“(vi)

personnel in

medical supplies under 
Part D of the Project.”

9.
Schedule 4
(a)
Part C
New paragraphs 3, 4 and 5 are added to read as follows:
“3.
Works for Subprojects estimated to cost less than 
$3,000,000 equivalent per contract may be procured under lump-sum, 
fixed-price 
contracts awarded on the basis of quotations obtained from 
three qualified domestic 
contractors in response to a written 
invitation.  The invitation shall 
include a detailed description of the 
works, including basic specifications, the 
required completion date, a 
basic form of agreement acceptable to the Bank, and 
relevant drawings, 
where applicable.  The award shall be made to the contractor who 
offers 
the lowest price quotation for the required work, and who has the 
experience and resources to complete the contract successfully.
4.
Shopping
Goods for Emergency Subprojects estimated to cost less than 
$300,000 equivalent per contract may be procured under contracts awarded  on the basis 
of shopping procedures in accordance with procedures 
satisfactory to the Bank.
5.
Goods (including vehicles and medicines) and works for 
Emergency Subprojects estimated to cost the equivalent of $100,000 or 
less per 
contract not exceeding in the aggregate $700,000 equivalent may 
be procured under 
contract awarded on the basis of direct contracting 
following procedures satisfactory 
to the Bank.”
(c)
Part D.  Subparagraph 1 (a)
(a)
the word “and” is deleted before the numerical “(iii)”; and
(b)
a new subparagraph “(iv)” is added to read as follows:
“and (iv) the first contracts for goods and for works under 
Emergency Subprojects procured pursuant to the provisions 
of Parts C.3 and 4 of this Section,”
Please confirm your agreement with the foregoing, on behalf of the Borrower, by 
signing, dating, and returning to us the enclosed original of this letter.  This 
agreement will become effective on the date the Bank receives:
(a)
the duly countersigned copy of this letter; and
(b)
evidence satisfactory to the Bank that the Operational Manual has been 
adopted by MSP.
