PROJECT AGREEMENT
AGREEMENT, dated December 16, 1997, among INTERNATIONAL DEVELOPMENT ASSOCIATION 
(the Association) and WATER AND POWER DEVELOPMENT AUTHORITY OF PAKISTAN (WAPDA), 
PROVINCE OF PUNJAB acting by its Governor (Punjab), PROVINCE OF SINDH acting by its 
Governor (Sindh), NORTH WEST FRONTIER PROVINCE acting by its Governor (NWFP) and 
PROVINCE OF BALOCHISTAN acting by its Governor (Balochistan).
WHEREAS by the Development Credit Agreement of even date herewith between the 
Islamic Republic of Pakistan  (the Borrower) and the Association, the Association has 
agreed to make available to the Borrower an amount in various currencies equivalent to 
one hundred ninety-eight million six hundred thousand Special Drawing Rights (SDR 
198,600,000) on the terms and conditions set forth in the Development Credit 
Agreement, but only on condition that WAPDA and Punjab, Sindh, NWFP and Balochistan 
(the Provinces) agree to undertake such obligations toward the Association as are set 
forth in this Agreement; and
WHEREAS WAPDA and the Provinces, in consideration of the Association’s entering 
into the Development Credit Agreement with the Borrower, have agreed to undertake the 
obligations set forth in this Agreement; 
NOW THEREFORE the parties hereto hereby agree as follows:

Definitions

Section 1.01. Unless the context otherwise requires, the several terms defined

Conditions (as so defined) have the respective meanings therein set forth.
ARTICLE II
Execution of the Project
Section 2.01. (a)  WAPDA and the Provinces  declare their commitment to the 
objectives of the Project as set forth in Schedule 2 to the Development Credit 
Agreement, and, to this end, shall carry out their activities under the Project with 
due diligence and efficiency and in conformity with appropriate administrative, 
financial, engineering, irrigation and drainage, environmental and resettlement  
practices, and shall provide, or cause to be provided, promptly as needed, the funds, 
facilities, services and other resources required for their activities under the 
Project.
(b)
Without limitation upon the provisions of paragraph (a) of this Section 
and except as the Association and WAPDA and the Provinces shall otherwise agree, WAPDA 
and the Provinces shall carry out their activities under the Project in accordance 
with the Implementation Program set forth in Schedule 4 to this Agreement and an 
Action Plan satisfactory to the Association.
Section 2.02. Except as the Association shall otherwise agree, procurement of 
the goods, works and consultants’ services required for the activities of WAPDA and 
the Provinces under the Project and to be financed out of the proceeds of the Credit 
shall be governed by the provisions of Schedule 3 to the Development Credit Agreement.
Section 2.03. WAPDA and the Provinces shall carry out the obligations set forth 
in Sections 9.03, 9.04, 9.05, 9.06, 9.07 and 9.08 of the General Conditions (relating 
to insurance, use of goods and services, plans and schedules, records and reports, 
maintenance and land acquisition, respectively) in respect of the Project Agreement 
and their activities under the Project.
Section 2.04.  Except as the Association shall otherwise agree, WAPDA and the 
Provinces shall, with respect to each Fiscal Year of Project implementation, furnish, 
and the Provinces shall cause the PIDAs, AWBs and FOs participating in the Project to 
similarly furnish, evidence to the Association, satisfactory to the Association, 
showing that they are, and shall continue during such Fiscal Year to be, in compliance 
with the relevant eligibility criteria set forth in Schedule 1 to this Agreement.
Section 2.05.  Except as the Association shall otherwise agree, the Provinces 
shall enter into a Participation Agreement with each PIDA, AWB and FO proposing to 
participate in the Project, under terms and conditions which shall have been approved 
by the Association which shall include the terms and conditions set forth in Schedule 
2 to this Agreement.
Section 2.06.  WAPDA and the Provinces undertake that, unless the Association 
shall otherwise agree:
(a)
prior approval of the Association will be obtained for any institutional 
reform program to be carried out under Part A of the Project, or any research or 
sector planning program to be carried out under Part B of the Project, or any program 
of coordination and supervision activities to be carried out in respect of the 
Project, and to be financed out of the proceeds of Credit; and
(b)
approval of the Association will be obtained for any Investment Project 
to be carried out under Part C of the Project and to be financed out of the proceeds 
of the Credit, on the terms and conditions and in accordance with the procedures set 
forth in Schedule 3 to this Agreement.
Section 2.07. (a)  WAPDA and the Provinces shall, at the request of the 
Association, exchange views with the Association with regard to the progress of their 
activities under the Project, the performance of their respective obligations under 
this Agreement, and other matters relating to the purposes of the Credit. 
(b)
WAPDA and the Provinces shall promptly inform the Association of any 
Page  3
condition which interferes or threatens to interfere with the progress of their 
activities under the Project, the accomplishment of the purposes of the Credit, or the 
performance by WAPDA and the Provinces of their respective obligations under this 
Agreement.
Section 2.08.  WAPDA and the Provinces shall cooperate with the Borrower, in a 
manner satisfactory to the Association, in the carrying out of the obligations set 
forth in Sections 3.04, 3.05, 3.06 3.07 and 3.08 of the Development Credit Agreement.
ARTICLE III
Management and Operations of  WAPDA
Section 3.01. WAPDA  shall carry on its operations and conduct its affairs in 
accordance with sound administrative, financial, irrigation and drainage practices 
under the supervision of qualified and experienced management assisted by competent 
staff in adequate numbers.
Section 3.02. WAPDA shall at all times operate and maintain its plant, 
machinery, equipment and other property, and from time to time, promptly as needed, 
make all necessary repairs and renewals thereof, all in accordance with sound 
engineering, financial and  irrigation and drainage  practices.
Section 3.03. WAPDA shall take out and maintain with responsible insurers, or 
make other provision satisfactory to the Association for, insurance against such risks 
and in such amounts as shall be consistent with appropriate practice.
Section 3.04.  Except in the ordinary course of its business, WAPDA shall not, 
without the prior consent of the Association, sell, lease, transfer, distribute or 
otherwise dispose of any of its property or assets which shall be required for the 
efficient conduct of its water operations.

SCHEDULE 3
Terms and Conditions and Procedures referred to
in Section 2.06(b) of this Agreement
A.  Terms and Conditions
1.
Except as the Association shall otherwise agree, no Investment Project to be 
carried out under Part C of the Project shall be eligible for financing out of the 
proceeds of the Credit unless it shall have satisfied the following criteria:
(a)
it is included in the current Three-Year Rolling Public Sector 
Development Program for the Project prepared pursuant to the provisions of Section 
3.04(b)(i)(bb) of the Development Credit Agreement;
(b)
in the case of main off-farm drains, it is located in an area which has 
lost at least 30% of its drainage capacity or which does not have any drainage 
capacity at all;
(c)
 it supports  a basin concept for providing drainage to the area  where 
it is located and is, or would be, connected to an out-fall drain;
(d)
a financial and economic analysis has been carried out for it, 
demonstrating that it would meet economic and financial criteria for viability and 
sustainability, and would have, in particular, an economic rate of return (ERR) of not 
Page  9
less than 15%;
(e)
a social assessment has been carried out for it, demonstrating that, to 
the extent possible, its interventions have been or would be targeted to benefit the 
poorest one-third of the members of the farming community for which it is intended and 
women beneficiaries, and ascertaining the extent of projected beneficiary 
participation, including willingness to make contributions (cash and non-cash) and the 
extent of such contributions;
(f)
an initial environmental scoping and, if necessary, an Environmental 
Impact Assessment have been carried out for it, and, to the extent that land 
acquisition and involuntary resettlement of people would be involved, a resettlement 
action plan has been prepared for it, all in accordance with paragraphs 6 and 7 of 
Schedule 4 to this Agreement;
(g)
it would not involve draining of an area in which highly sodic soils 
cover more than 15 percent of such area;
(h)
it would not involve draining of wetlands;
(i)
a plan has been prepared for it demonstrating that groundwater and 
surface water supplies for irrigation in the areas where it is to be carried out are, 
or would be, adequate to generate the expected benefits, and the operation of channels 
in such areas would be modified to follow crop water requirements as closely as 
possible, and to distribute the available water equitably;
(j) 
it would have a net positive environmental impact on rural areas, and, in 
particular, it would not be  located in an area where excess irrigation water supplies 
have caused waterlogging, unless a plan has been prepared demonstrating that measures 
would be taken to reduce such excess irrigation water supplies;
(k)
its total cost is less than the equivalent of $30 million, or such amount 
as the Association may agree in a given case, on an exceptional basis; a financing 
plan has been prepared for it setting forth, inter alia, the terms and conditions of 
financing to be provided for it out of the proceeds of the Credit consistent with 
those set forth in Schedule 2 to this Agreement; it is ready for implementation, with 
its detailed designs, procurement plan and tender documents already prepared; 
satisfactory supervisory arrangements, including the appointment of independent 
consultants as necessary, have been made for it; its implementation period is not more 
than 3 years, with the projected completion date being not later than December 31, 
2003; and a plan has been prepared for its post-completion O&M, demonstrating, inter 
alia, that such O&M  would be carried out through contractors engaged under 
performance contracts; and
(l)
it is to be carried out by a Participating Agency which (i) is eligible 
for financing under the Project, having satisfied the eligibility criteria set forth 
in Schedule 1 to this Agreement, and (ii) has operating jurisdiction over it in 
accordance with the provisions of the statute under which the Participating Agency is 
established and operating.

SCHEDULE 4
Implementation Program
1.
WAPDA shall maintain for the duration of the Project, an NDP Coordination Cell: 
(i) with organization, terms of reference and staff satisfactory to the Association; 
and (ii) headed by a Program Coordinator with rank, qualifications, authority and 
responsibilities satisfactory to the Association.
2.
Each Province shall maintain for the duration of the Project, a Provincial 
Project Coordination Cell: (i) with organization, terms of reference and staff 
satisfactory to the Association; and (ii) headed by a Provincial Program Coordinator 
with rank, qualifications, authority and responsibilities satisfactory to the 
Association.
3.
Each Province shall, not later than one year after the establishment of its 
PIDA, establish at least one pilot AWB for a canal command selected in consultation 
with the Association.
4.
WAPDA shall, not later than June 30, 1998 and pursuant to the provisions of 
Section II of Schedule 3 to the Development Credit Agreement, appoint financial 
management and information systems, environment and social design, sector planning and 
studies, and monitoring and evaluation consultants for purposes of the Project.
5.
WAPDA and the Provinces shall, with respect to the Investment Projects, complete 
all detailed designs and award contracts promptly so as to ensure that all such 
contracts, including the maintenance period specified in such contracts, will be 
completed not later than December 31, 2003.
6.
WAPDA and the Provinces shall, with respect to each Investment Project, ensure:
(a)
that an initial environmental scoping will be carried out;
(b)
that where such initial environmental scoping has established the need 
for an EIA, such EIA will be prepared incorporating the principles of the Drainage 
Sector Environmental Assessment Study and other requirements of the Association;
(c)
that such EIA will be furnished to the Association for its review and 
approval on a timely basis;
(d)
that, in any case, unless and until such EIA has been found satisfactory 
and approved by the Association, no works will be commenced under the Investment 
Project; and

7.
WAPDA and the Provinces shall, with respect to each Investment Project, ensure:
Page  11
(a)
that all land required for the Investment Project will be acquired, and 
all persons affected by the acquisition of such land will be compensated, resettled 
and/or rehabilitated, all in accordance with a RAP or such other arrangements as shall 
be required under the Framework for Land Acquisition and Resettlement satisfactory to 
the Association;
(b)
that such RAP or other arrangements will be prepared by incorporating the 
principles of the Framework for Land Acquisition and Resettlement and other 
requirements of the Association;
(c)
that such RAP or other arrangements will be furnished to the Association 
for its review and approval on a timely basis; and
(d)
that, in any case, unless and until such RAP or other arrangements have 
been so approved by the Association, no works will be commenced under the Investment 
Project.
8.
WAPDA and the Provinces shall, prior to the completion of the Project, implement 
an Environmental Management Plan, satisfactory to the Association,  covering the 
cumulative basin-wide environmental aspects and implications of the Project as a 
whole.
9.
WAPDA and the Provinces shall: (a) ensure that, in constructing field drains 
under Investment Projects, adequate measures will be taken to protect wetland areas; 
and (b) from time to time provide or cause to be provided to the Association, an 
updated map of, and the latest information on, reclaimed areas, including the date of 
reclamation of, and the land use patterns for, such areas.
10. 
(a) 
WAPDA shall, not later than December 31, 1997, appoint its Environmental 
Cell or an internationally recognized environmental specialist, acceptable to the 
Association, to be the environmental auditor for the activities to be carried out by 
WAPDA under the Project.
(b)
The Provinces shall, not later than December 31, 1997, appoint their 
respective Provincial Environmental Protection Agencies or internationally recognized 
environmental specialists, acceptable to the Association, to be the environmental 
auditors for the activities to be carried out by their respective PIDAs, AWBs, FOs and 
PAD-OFWMs under the Project.
(c)
WAPDA and the Provinces shall: (i) not later than December 31 in each 
Fiscal Year, beginning FY98/99, cause such environmental auditors to prepare 
independent Annual Environmental Audit Reports (AEARs) on the Project; (ii) cause to 
be included excerpts or highlights from the AEARs in the quarterly reports on the 
Project to be furnished to the Association pursuant to the provisions of Section 3.05 
of the Development Credit Agreement; and (iii) cause their agencies and departments 
and their respective PIDAs, AWBs, FOs and PAD-OFWMs participating in the Project, 
responsible for carrying out the Project, to implement the recommendations of the 
AEARs to the satisfaction of the Association.
