                         Project Development Objectives
                         Project Development Objective (from Project Appraisal Document)

                         The objectives of the project are to strengthen governance, promote growth and improve service delivery in participating provinces and participating districts, and
                         support the recovery of affected areas through (a) strengthening of local participation in development planning; (b) promotion of private investment and job creation;
                         and (c) an increase in the utilization of effective education health and dispute resolution services.

                         Implementation Status Overview
                         The SPADA Aceh-Nias Project formally closed in December 31, 2011. The formal ceremony of project closing arranged in Jakarta by the GOI’s implementing agency (The Ministry for


                                                                                                                                                                                                             Page 1 of 7
                             The World Bank                                                                                                                                      Report No: ISR9097



                         Disadvantaged Areas, KPDT) on December 14, 2011. The project has finalized a total of 1,770 sub-projects, utilizing about IDR 86 billion (or 93% of the total grants allocated). Hand
                         over process of the project assets from The Ministry for Disadvantaged Areas (KPDT) to local governments has been completed, and follow up to complaints, audit findings, and
                         financial issues has been finalized. Local authorities in the targeted districts have expressed their commitment to integrate the promoted planning process, and maintain the project
                         assets after the project has ended. Rapid assessment by Task Team and GOI in 2011 indicated that SPADA block grants have opened up access to previously isolated villages,
Public Disclosure Copy




                         improved opportunities to start small businesses, and improved the access to health and education services in disadvantaged communities. The projects in general have successfully
                         strengthened quality of community proposals, roles of multi-stakeholder groups at local level, as well as local participation in the development planning process. A workshop on
                         project lessons learned took place in Medan, in December 2011, while the formal study to assess accomplishments of project development objectives is at the final stage of
                         procurement and expected to be conducted in March-April 2012, in conjunction with the study for SPADA National Project. A total of $4.2 million unused funds were returned to the
                         Multi-Donor Trust for Aceh and Nias in May 2011 and reprogrammed by the Government of Indonesia.
