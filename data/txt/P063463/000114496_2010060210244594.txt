AGREEMENT, dated October 29, 2001, among UNITED MEXICAN STATES (UMS) and BANCO 
NACIONAL DE OBRAS Y SERVICIOS PUBLICOS, S.N.C. (the Recipient) and INTERNATIONAL BANK 
FOR RECONSTRUCTION AND DEVELOPMENT (the Bank) acting as an implementing agency of the 
Global Environment Facility (GEF) in respect of grant funds provided to the GEF Trust 
Fund by certain members of the Bank as participants of the GEF.
WHEREAS (A) the Bank, pursuant to Resolution No. 91-5 of March 14, 1991 of the 
Executive Directors of the Bank, established the GEF to assist in the protection of 
the global environment and promote thereby environmentally sound and sustainable 
economic development;
(B)
following the restructuring of the GEF, such arrangements continued in 
place on the basis set forth in Resolution No. 94-2 of May 24, 1994, of the Executive 
Directors of the Bank which, inter alia, established the GEF Trust Fund, authorized 
the first replenishment of the GEF Trust Fund and appointed the Bank as trustee of the 
GEF Trust Fund (Resolution No. 94-2);
(C)
the second replenishment of the GEF Trust Fund was approved on the basis 
set forth in Resolution No. 98-2 of July 14, 1998, of the Executive Directors of the 
Bank (Resolution No. 98-2);
(D)
the Recipient, having satisfied itself as to the feasibility and priority 
of the Project described in Schedule 2 to this Agreement (the Project), has requested 
assistance from the resources of the GEF Trust Fund for funding the Project, and said 
request having been approved in accordance with the provisions of the Instrument for 
the Establishment of the Restructured Global Environment Facility approved under 
Resolution 94-2, and to be funded from the resources of the GEF Trust Fund; 
(E)
the Bank has relied on the representations and warranties by the UMS and 
the Recipient that the Project has been designed and will be implemented in accordance

May 9, 1992, which was ratified by the UMS on March 11, 1993, and its related National

the Bank has also relied on the representations and warranties by the UMS

ARTICLE II
The GEF Trust Fund Grant
Section 2.01. The Bank agrees to make available to the Recipient, on the terms 
and conditions set forth or referred to in this Agreement, the GEF Trust Fund Grant in 
an amount in various currencies equivalent to five million Special Drawing Rights (SDR 
5,000,000).
Section 2.02. The amount of the GEF Trust Fund Grant may be withdrawn from the 
GEF Trust Fund Grant Account in accordance with the provisions of Schedule 1 to this 
Agreement for expenditures made (or, if the Bank shall so agree, to be made) in 
respect of the reasonable cost of goods, works and services required for the Project 
and to be financed out of the proceeds of the GEF Trust Fund Grant.
Section 2.03. The Closing Date shall be June 30, 2006 or such later date as the 
Bank shall establish.  The Bank shall promptly notify UMS and the Recipient of such 
later date.

SCHEDULE 2
Description of the Project
The objectives of the Project are to expand the assistance provided to the 
Recipient under the Baseline Project for the improvement of solid waste management by: 
(a) demonstrating a cost-effective technology for LFG capture and use in a selected 
Page  19
facility; (b) demonstrating an institutional structure for the implementation of LFG 
projects, including private sector participation; (c) strengthening the UMS’s 
regulatory, policy and social frameworks for the introduction of LFG capture and use 
in Mexico; (d) designing a dissemination strategy to share the lessons learned 
throughout Project implementation with relevant stakeholders in Mexico and Latin 
America; and (e) designing a strategy to encourage the replication of the Project in 
Mexico.
The Project consists of the following parts, subject to such modifications 
thereof as the UMS, the Recipient and the Bank may agree upon from time to time to 
achieve such objectives.
Part A:
Detailed Engineering Design and Construction of a Plant for Methane 
Capture and Use 
1.
Development and implementation of a public-private partnership (through the 
establishment of the Cogeneration Company) for the design, construction and operation 
of the LFG power plant at the SIMEPRODESO site, consisting of:
(a)
the design and implementation of a system for LFG capture and use, which 
will result in the capture of about 214 million cubic meters of methane gas from the 
SIMEPRODESO site for the generation of electricity, through the provision of civil 
works and equipment for the construction of : (i) wells for the methane gas collection 
system; (ii) a piping network to facilitate drainage of condensated water vapor into 
condensated traps; (iii) blowers for the collection of methane gas; and (iv) a 
treatment plant to remove moisture and or contaminants found in the LFG before 
combustion;
(b)
the design, construction and operation of: 
(i)
the LFG power plant (with an estimated capacity of 7MW), including 
a substation (for the transformation of the voltage produced into 
34.5MV), electrical connection lines to the CFE grid and a flare 
that burns off excess methane gas not used by the LFG power plant 
for the production of electricity; and
(ii)
supporting infrastructure required for the operation of the LFG 
power plant, including, inter alia, roads, sewerage, water supply 
connections, buildings and lighting structures;
(c)
the strengthening of SIMEPRODESO’s capabilities for: (i) Project design 
and supervision and plant operation; and (ii) Project management and implementation, 
through the provision of consultants’ services and training; and
(d)
the reduction of methane gas emissions, by distributing among the 
Eligible Electricity Users, the electricity generated by the LFG power plant.
2.
Strengthening of the technical and institutional capabilities of SIMEPRODESO to 
demonstrate the technical, financial and institutional features of the Project, 
through the preparation of the reports referred to in Section 3.06 of this Agreement 
and technical documentation regarding the eventual replication of the Project.
Part B: 
Capacity Building
1.
Development and implementation of a program to strengthen the institutional 
capacity of SEDESOL, government agencies and private companies in the solid waste 
industry for the purposes of designing, managing, and replicating LFG capture and use 
facilities, consisting of:
(a)
the carrying out of a training program, including, inter alia: (i) the 
provision of overseas training for SEDESOL staff and staff of government agencies and 
private companies in the solid waste industry; (ii) the distribution of instructional 
materials among public and private entities interested in the implementation of LFG 
projects, including the development of instructional materials, manuals and 
guidelines; (iii) the carrying out of workshops; and (iv) the implementation of a 
twinning arrangement aimed at providing managerial and technical assistance to Mexican 
Page  20
operators interested in developing a similar LFG capture and use project, including 
the carrying out of practical internships and exchanges of staff; and 
(b)
the carrying out of a public awareness campaign to disseminate the 
results of the Project among the general public in Mexico, through the distribution of 
instructional materials, the preparation of  press releases and the carrying out of 
promotional tours and dissemination activities.
2.
Design of a national replication strategy for LFG development in Mexico, through 
the provision of technical assistance for:
(a)
the preparation of a report detailing a replication framework for future 
projects, including, inter alia: 
(i)
the development of LFG capture and use systems in existing and new 
facilities;
(ii)
the integration of LFG management issues in the process of 
converting an open solid waste dumpsite into a Sanitary landfill, 
including methodologies and options to deal with any negative 
impact on populations (including Scavengers) living in, or in the 
proximity of such landfill;
(iii)
the proposed modality for the integration of LFG management in the 
planning, design and construction of Sanitary landfills;
(iv)
the assessment of the prospects of using non-grant financing 
modalities for future support to municipal governments interested 
in developing a LFG technology similar to the one developed under 
the Project; and
(v)
the evaluation of the prospects for LFG use as a source of energy 
for the provision of municipal services in other Mexican cities; 
and
(b)
the carrying out of consultations with a wide range of public and private 
stakeholders interested in solid waste and LFG matters, including  the organization of 
workshops.
Part C: 
Regulatory Reform Studies
Strengthening of the capacity of SEDESOL for the future development of a modern 
legal and regulatory framework applicable to LFG management issues, through the 
provision of technical assistance.
Part D: 
Regional Dissemination 
1.
Development and implementation of a program for the dissemination of experience 
gained by the Recipient and SIMEPRODESO throughout Project implementation, and other 
similar projects in Latin America, through the provision of technical assistance and 
training for:
(a)
the carrying out of a worldwide review of economic and technical 
effectiveness of LFG power plants, including, inter alia, an identification of 
existing barriers for the introduction of such plants in developing countries, and the 
proposed models to overcome such barriers within the Latin American context; 
(b)
the carrying out of a consultation workshop to seek the views and 
opinions of a wide range of public and private stakeholders interested in LFG matters;
(c)
the production and publication of dissemination materials, including 
Page  21
newsletters and technical publications, and the development of websites;
(d)
the carrying out of workshops (in Mexico and abroad) for government 
officials and private sector stakeholders in the Latin American region to discuss LFG 
matters; and 
(e)
the implementation of a twinning arrangement aimed at providing 
managerial and technical assistance to Latin American operators interested in 
developing a similar LFG capture and use project, through the provision of overseas 
training and practical internships and exchanges of management staff.
Part E: 
Project Management, Supervision and Monitoring
1.
Improving the technical and managerial capabilities of SIMEPRODESO for LFG use 
and management, through provision of consultants’ services for: 
(a)
the carrying out of administrative oversight of Parts A, E.3. and E.4. 
(a) of the Project; and
(b)
ensuring coordination with SEDESOL for the development of dissemination 
materials, the carrying out of public awareness campaigns and the carrying out of the 
capacity building activities referred to in Parts B and D of the Project.
2.
Improving the technical and managerial capabilities of SEDESOL for LFG use and 
management, through the provision of consultants’ services for the implementation of 
capacity building, policy and regulatory activities referred to in Parts B, C,  D and 
E.4. (b) of the Project.
3.
Strengthening of the capacity of SIMEPRODESO and the Cogeneration Company to 
implement the EMP, through:
(a)
the provision of equipment required for the carrying out of periodic 
inspections of the works referred to in Part A.1. of the Project in order to detect 
the emission levels of methane gas and the carrying out of engine stack tests; and
(b)
the provision of technical assistance and administrative support for the 
monitoring and administration of the implementation of the EMP.
4.
(a)
Strengthening of the technical and administrative capabilities of the 
SIMEPRODESO Project implementation staff for Project management, administration and 
monitoring, through the provision of technical assistance, including, inter alia, 
procurement, auditing, financial management and other specialized consultants’ 
services for the preparation of technical, audit and progress reports.
(b)
Strengthening of the technical and administrative capabilities of the 
SEDESOL Project implementation staff for Project management, administration, 
coordination and monitoring, through the provision of technical assistance, including, 
inter alia, procurement, auditing, financial management and other specialized 
consultants’ services for the preparation of technical, audit and progress reports.
