Re:  Credit Number 3259KG  (Second Rural Finance Project) 
Amendment to the Development Credit Agreement
Dear Mr. Abildaev: 
 
We refer to the Development Credit Agreement between the Kyrgyz Republic (the 
Borrower) and the International Development Association (the Association) for the above-
mentioned Project dated October 1, 1999, (the Credit Agreement), and to your letter dated 
February 21, 2003, requesting amendment to the Credit Agreement. 
 
We hereby agree to amend the Credit Agreement as follows: 
 
1. 
A new subsection (p) is added to Section 1.02 to read: 
 
“(p) 
“PMU” means the Project Management Unit, established within the Ministry of 
Finance of the Borrower for day-to-day Project administration, management and 
coordination and referred to in paragraph 2 of Schedule 4 to this Agreement.”. 
 
2. 
Subsection (c) of Section 1.02 is amended to read: 
 
“(c) 
“Eligible Categories” means categories (1), (2), (3), (4), (5) and (6) set forth in 
the table in Part A.1 of Schedule 1 to this Agreement;”. 
 
3.

4. 
A new subparagraph (c) is added in paragraph 2 of Part A of Schedule 1 to read: 
 
“(c) 
the term “Operating costs” means incremental operating expenditures incurred by 
PMU on account of Project implementation, management and monitoring, including 
office supplies, vehicle operation, travel and supervision costs, training, bank charges, 
communication costs and other reasonable expenditures directly associated with the 
Project implementation, all based on an annual budget acceptable to the Association.”. 
 
5. 
Paragraph 4 of Part A of Schedule 1 is amended to read: 
 
“4. The Association may require withdrawals from the Credit Account to be made on the 
basis of statements of expenditure for expenditures for: (a) Sub-loans; (b) goods under 
contracts costing less than $200,000 equivalent each; (c) training; (d) services under 
contracts costing less than $100,000 equivalent each, or awarded in accordance with the 
provisions of Section II, Part C.2 of Schedule 3 to this Agreement, for consulting firms, 
and less than $50,000 equivalent each for individual consultants;  and (e) operating costs,

6. 
A new Part C is added in Schedule 2 to read: 
 
“Part C
:
Project Management
Strengthening the Borrower’s Project implementation and management capacity through 
the provision of goods, services, including accounting and auditing, training and 
Operating costs under the Project.”. 
 
7. 
Paragraph 1 of Part C of Section I of Schedule 3 is amended to read: 
 
“1. International 
Shopping
Goods under: (i) Part A of the Project, estimated to cost less than $500,000 
equivalent per contract, (ii) Part B of the Project, estimated to cost less than $200,000 
equivalent per contract, and (iii) Part C of the Project, estimated to cost less than $25,000 
equivalent per contract, up to an aggregate amount not to exceed $290,000 equivalent, 
may be procured under contracts awarded on the basis of international shopping 
procedures in accordance with the provisions of paragraphs 3.5 and 3.6 of the 
Guidelines.”. 
 
8. 
New paragraph 2 is added in Part C of Section I of Schedule 3 to read: 
 
“2. National 
Shopping
Goods under Part C of the Project estimated to cost less than $20,000 equivalent 
per contract, up to an aggregate amount not to exceed $40,000 equivalent, may be 
procured under contracts awarded on the basis of international shopping procedures in 
accordance with the provisions of paragraphs 3.5 and 3.6 of the Guidelines.”. 
 
9. 
Paragraph 2 of Part C of Section I of Schedule 3 is renumbered as paragraph 3. 
 
10. 
Paragraphs 2 and 3 of Schedule 4 are amended to read: 
 
“2. 
The Borrower shall assign to the PMU day-to-day Project administration, 
management and coordination, including activities related to procurement, accounting, 
disbursement, auditing, reporting, as well as selection, preparation, monitoring and 
evaluation of Sub-projects.  Throughout the implementation of the Project, the PMU shall 
be maintained with a sufficient number of qualified staff, consultants, adequate resources 
and terms of reference satisfactory to the Association. 
 
3. 
The Borrower shall cause the PMU to prepare and furnish to the Association 
semi-annual reports, satisfactory to the Association, on the status of Project 
implementation.”. 
 
11. 
In subparagraph (d) of Part C of Schedule 5 the term “KAFC” is changed to the term 
“PFIs”.
