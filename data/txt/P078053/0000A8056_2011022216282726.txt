                         Implementation Status Overview
                         The evolution of the HIV epidemic in Mozambique suggests that, on average, the epidemic is leveling-off although in the south region there are no signs of reduction of the spread of
                         HIV. In the context of the National HIV/AIDS Strategic Plan (PEN 2005-2009), the country implemented an HIV prevention, care and treatment program that resulted in an increase in
                         the number of people volunteering to undertake the HIV test. According to the National AIDS Council (CNCS) report of first semester of 2010, nearly 195,0000 people were put on

                         received ART to prevent transmission of the virus to their new born increased to 45.8% from 17% in 2007.
