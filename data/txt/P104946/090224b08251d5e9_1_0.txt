                         Project Development Objectives
                         Project Development Objective (from Project Appraisal Document)

                         The revised objectives of the Project are to: (i) provide quality maternal care to eligible women in the targeted districts in Sana’a; and (ii) design and implement a model
                         of maternal care which demonstrates how Yemeni public policy on maternal and child care can be effectively integrated with private health provision.

                         Implementation Status Overview
                         Throughout the lifetime of the project, the implementing NGO (SOUL) together with the Science and Technology University Hospital (USTH) were well operating through at least three
                         satellite clinics in Sana’a City. Enrollment and provision of services to the targeted beneficiaries were efficiently implemented based on the performance indicators according to the


                                                                                                                                                                                                                Page 1 of 5
                             The World Bank                                                                                                                                          Report No: ISR14314



                         second restructuring of the project. Independent verification process during all phases of the project was continuous and the project managed to sustain services even during the
                         uprising events of 2011.
                         Based on the report of the Independent Verification Expert (IVE) antenatal, delivery and postnatal services were well administered. In addition, and during the last year of the project,
                         more attention was given to the presentation of Family Planning methods and vaccination against Tetanus after being reviewed by the Bank team.
Public Disclosure Copy




                         The IVE verified that services were provided in accordance with the terms described in the Operations Manual.

                         Number of women enrolled during the period July-December, 2013 reached 250. The enrollment indicator has been decreased due to the closing of the project on 28 February, 2014
                         to avoid deliveries beyond the closing date that can't be reimbursed to the service provider. The total cumulative number of women enrolled in the project till 31 December, 2013 was
                         16,628 exceeding the target of 15,000 beneficiaries.

                         The accumulative total number of deliveries reached 15,612. Total number of women attended health education sessions was 17,456 and the total number of antenatal care visits
                         reached 66,301.
