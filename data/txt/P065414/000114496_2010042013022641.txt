GUARANTEE AGREEMENT 
AGREEMENT, dated December 21, 1999, between REPUBLIC OF KAZAKHSTAN, represented 
by the Government of the Republic of Kazakhstan, as represented by the Ministry of 
Finance, (the Guarantor) and INTERNATIONAL BANK FOR RECONSTRUCTION AND DEVELOPMENT 
(the Bank). 
WHEREAS (A) the Guarantor and Kazakhstan Electricity Grid Operating Company (the 
Borrower or KEGOC), having been satisfied as to the feasibility and priority of the 
Project described in Schedule 2 to the Loan Agreement, have requested the Bank to 
assist in the financing of the Project; 
(B)
by the Loan Agreement of even date herewith between the Bank and the 
Borrower, the Bank has agreed to extend to the Borrower a loan in an amount equal to 
one hundred forty million Dollars ($140,000,000), on the terms and conditions set 
forth in the Loan Agreement, but only on condition that the Guarantor agree to 
guarantee the obligations of the Borrower in respect of such loan as provided in this 
Page  2
Agreement; 
(C) 
the Bank has received from the Guarantor a Statement of Electricity 
Sector Policy letter, dated September 16, 1999, describing a program of actions, 
objectives and policies designed to achieve various reforms in the electricity sector 
in Kazakhstan (hereinafter called the Program), declaring the Guarantor’s commitment 
to the execution of the Program, and requesting assistance from the Bank in support of 
the Program during the execution thereof; and 
WHEREAS the Guarantor, in consideration of the Bank’s entering into the Loan 
Agreement with the Borrower, has agreed so to guarantee such obligations of the 
Borrower;
NOW THEREFORE the parties hereto hereby agree as follows:
ARTICLE I 
General Conditions; Definitions 
Section 1.01. The “General Conditions Applicable to Loan and Guarantee 
Agreements for Single Currency Loans” of the Bank, dated May 30, 1995 (as amended 
through December 2, 1997) (the General Conditions) constitute an integral part of this 
Agreement. 
Section 1.02. Unless the context otherwise requires, the several terms defined 
in the General Conditions and in the Preamble to this Agreement and in the Preamble to 
and Section 1.02 of the Loan Agreement have the respective meanings therein set forth.
ARTICLE II 
Guarantee 
Section 2.01. The Guarantor declares its commitment to the objectives of the 
Project as set forth in Schedule 2 to the Loan Agreement, and, to this end, without 
limitation or restriction upon any of its other obligations under the Guarantee 
Agreement, the Guarantor hereby unconditionally guarantees, as primary obligor and not 
as surety merely, the due and punctual payment of the principal of, and interest and 
other charges on, the Loan, and the premium, if any, on the prepayment of the Loan, 
and the punctual performance of all the other obligations of the Borrower, all as set 
forth in the Loan Agreement.
Section 2.02. Without limitation or restriction upon the provisions of Section 
2.01 of this Agreement, the Guarantor specifically undertakes, whenever there is 
reasonable cause to believe that the funds available to the Borrower will be 
inadequate to meet the estimated expenditures required for the carrying out of the 
Project, to make arrangements, satisfactory to the Bank, to promptly to provide the 
Borrower or cause the Borrower to be provided with such funds as are needed to meet 
such expenditures.
ARTICLE III 
Other Covenants
Section 3.01.  The Guarantor shall: 
(a) 
by December 31, 1999, restructure KEGOC into a core high voltage 
transmission and dispatch company by fully separating all its non-core activities such 
as generation, distribution and trading of electricity and capacity, and terminating 
its existing interim management control in ten regional electricity distribution 
companies ors and two power plants, namely he electricity generator;
(b)
 by December 31, 2000, issue a regulation (En electricity Grid Code), 
acceptable to the Bank, which shall establish the rules governing access to the 
transmission and distribution network and the services to be provided by KEGOC;
(c) 
by September 30, 2000, adopt and thereafter gradually implement 
transmission tariff methodology, acceptable to the Bank, which: (i) is based on the 
Page  3
peak capacity used on the transmission system, (ii) gradually reduces the weight of 
the distance factor between the seller and buyer in the tariff structure to the 
geographic distance between the seller and buyer;, (iii) incorporates the sale of 
tradable right for the use of the capacity of congested transmission lines, and (iv) 
ensures that the transmission tariff is adequate to cover all justifiable costs, 
including the cost of ancillary services (frequency and voltage regulation). The said 
transmission tariff methodology shall allow for be adjusted quarterly tariff 
adjustments to a level enabling to a level enabling KEGOC to fully: (i) recover its 
costs;, and (ii) meet its counterpart funding and debts service requirements under the 
Project;
(d) 
cause to carry out a staged development of a competitive Wholesale 
Electricity Spot Market Pool as follows: (i) by December 31, 1999, cause to establish 
a Reserve Capacity Pool; and (ii) by October 31, 2002, cause to establish and 
thereafter maintain an  Wholesale Electricity Spot MarketPool, including an 
Independent System and Market Operator, in a manner acceptable to the Bank. The 
following terms used herein shall have the following meanings: (1) “Reserve Capacity 
Pool” means a formal arrangement among electricity generators for trading reserve 
power among themselves and setting a spot price for reserve power; (2) “Wholesale 
Electricity Spot MarketPool” means a formal trading arrangement among electricity 
generators for settling short-term imbalances in the generation and consumption of 
electricity and setting a spot market price for electricity; and (3) “Independent 
System and Market Operator” means a non-profit entity, independent of other market 
participants, for administering the Wholesale Electricity Spot Market Pool in 
accordance with established and transparent market operation rules; and 
(e) 
grant the requisite approval to the Borrower to enable it to undertake 
the activities under the CPP in accordance with paragraph 6 of Schedule 5 to the Loan 
Agreement.
