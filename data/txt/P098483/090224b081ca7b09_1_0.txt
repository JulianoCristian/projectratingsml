                         Implementation Status Overview
                         This is the final implementation status update as the project will close on June 30, 2013. The project has achieved positive results: all five project development objective indicators -
                         namely outpatient consultations per person per year; % of pregnant women who attended at least one antenatal care consultation; births attended by skilled health staff (incl. village
                         midwives; % of households who possess at least one insecticide-treated net; and no. of health personnel receiving training - were surpassed. The project has also made satisfactory
                         progress on the five intermediate indicators.

                         RESULTS. Under the 1st and 2nd Additional Financings, the project's results are as follows:
                         PDO 1 (Outpatient consultations per person per year in the project’s target areas): Against a target of 0.30, the project achieved 0.32 (107%) as of December 31, 2012 (and 0.41 for
                         the first quarter of 2013).
                         PDO 2 (Percentage of deliveries attended by skilled personnel-doctor, nurse, midwife-in rural areas of the four target states): Against a target of 55%, the project achieved 52% (95%)
                         as of December 31, 2012 (and 63% for the first quarter of 2013).
                         PDOs 3 and 4 (Number of LLINs and Percentage of households with at least one net): No change, as the objective was achieved under the original credit and not retained for the
                         additional financing.
                         PDO 5 (Percentage of pregnant women who attended at least one antenatal care consultation): Against a target of 65%, the project achieved 68% (105%) as of December 31, 2012
                         (and 68% for the first quarter of 2013).
                         Among the Intermediate Objectives (as of December 31, 2012): (i) all of the planned 18 PHC facilities had been renovated and equipped; (ii) 4 of the 7 midwifery nursing schools
                         were completed (and the other 3 to be completed before project closing); (iii) 6 of 9 rural hospitals had been renovated (and the other three to be completed before project closing; (iv)
