                         Project Development Objective (from Project Appraisal Document)

                         The objective of the project is to assist the Recipient in enhancing the quality of education in rural primary schools, grades 1-5, by improving students' access to, and

                         Implementation Status Overview
                         The Rural Education and Development Project (READ) will close at the end of February. The project has made a significant difference in the lives of rural Mongolian children through


                                                                                                                                                                                                           Page 1 of 17
                             The World Bank                                                                                                                                       Report No: ISR9559



                         improving their access to and use of learning materials by providing classroom libraries to all rural primary schools. The project has also developed strong professional networks of
                         teachers. READ has delivered its outputs and made good progress to achieving the outcome indicators in the Results Framework. All of the project development objective (PDO)
                         indicators selected to measure achievement of the PDO have been achieved, and all but 4 of the very many intermediate results have been achieved. The READ surveys conducted
                         over the life of the project showed that books and learning materials became increasingly accessible to students in READ classrooms and that students were using the books. The
Public Disclosure Copy




                         project has also upgraded teachers’ skills by introducing new pedagogical practices to help utilize the learning materials effectively and by promoting information sharing through
                         professional networks, practices which have been mainstreamed into pre-service and in-service teacher training in Mongolia. In addition, all 2000 netbook computers were procured
                         and delivered under the additional financing.
