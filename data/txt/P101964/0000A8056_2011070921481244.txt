                         carried out a review of the financial management and procurement of the project. Third, it discussed with BIR key steps ahead for the Bank to be able to consider the possible
                         extension of the project since Commissioner Jacinto-Henares expressed her desire to have NPSTAR extended beyond its December 2011 closing date. An acceleration of project

                         Key Decisions Regarding Implementation
                         The Bank mission laid out the main steps ahead to be able to consider project extension. These are: a) presentation of an acceptable procurement plan for 2011 that is consistent
                         with a stepped-up disbursement and commitment rate (currently standing at 18 and 27 percent respectively) and an upward trend in project commitments and disbursements; b)
                         presentation of a clearly identified list of the main interventions to be supported in each of the project components and their cost estimates; c) the full staffing of TRAG in line with its
                         recently approved rationalization plan; d) the formal adoption (through an RMO) of a sound medium-term strategic plan for BIR, together with a set of agency-level key performance
                         indicators conforming to good international practice; and e) improved performance in the project#s financial management through the timely submission of the financial reports as
