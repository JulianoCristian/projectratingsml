Association to assist in the financing of the Project;
(B)
the Project will be carried out by MOPH (as defined in Section 1.02 
hereof) and  by Zhejiang, Jiangxi and Shaanxi Provinces (hereinafter collectively

available to MOPH and each of the Provinces the proceeds of the Credit as provided in 
this Agreement; and
WHEREAS the Association has agreed, on the basis, inter alia, of the foregoing, 
to extend the Credit to the Borrower upon the terms and conditions set forth in this 
Agreement and in the Project Agreement of even date herewith among the Association and 
the Provinces;

ARTICLE II
The Credit
Section 2.01. The Association agrees to lend to the Borrower, on the terms and 
conditions set forth or referred to in the Development Credit Agreement, an amount in 
various currencies equivalent to thirty-nine million four hundred thousand Special 
Drawing Rights (SDR 39,400,000).
Section 2.02. (a) The amount of the Credit may be withdrawn from the Credit 
Account in accordance with the provisions of Schedule 1 to this Agreement for 
expenditures made (or, if the Association shall so agree, to be made) in respect of 
the reasonable cost of goods and services required for the Project and to be financed 
out of the proceeds of the Credit.
(b)
The Borrower shall, for the purposes of the Project, open and maintain in 
the name of Zhejiang Province in dollars a special account in a bank and on terms and 
conditions satisfactory to the Association. Deposits into, and payments out of, the 
Special Account shall be made in accordance with the provisions of Schedule 3 to this 
Agreement.
(c)
The Borrower shall, for the purposes of the Project, open and maintain in 
the name of Jiangxi Province in dollars a special account in a bank and on terms and 
conditions satisfactory to the Association. Deposits into, and payments out of, the 
Special Account shall be made in accordance with the provisions of Schedule 4 to this 
Agreement.
(d)
The Borrower shall, for the purposes of the Project, open and maintain in 
Page  3
the name of Shaanxi Province in dollars a special account in a bank and on terms and 
conditions satisfactory to the Association. Deposits into, and payments out of, the 
Special Account shall be made in accordance with the provisions of Schedule 5 to this 
Agreement.
(e)
The Borrower shall, for the purposes of the Project, open and maintain in 
the name of MOPH in dollars a special account in a bank and on terms and conditions 
satisfactory to the Association. Deposits into, and payments out of, the Special 
Account shall be made in accordance with the provisions of Schedule 6 to this 
Agreement.
Section 2.03. The Closing Date shall be December 31, 1995 or such later date  as  
the  Association shall establish. The Association shall promptly notify the Borrower 
of such later date. 
Section 2.04. (a) The Borrower shall pay to the Association a commitment charge 
on the principal amount of the Credit not withdrawn from time to time at a rate to be 
set by the Association as of June 30 of each year, but not to exceed the rate of 
one-half of one percent (1/2 of 1%) per annum.
(b)
The commitment charge shall accrue: (i) from the date sixty days after 
the date of this Agreement (the accrual date) to the respective dates on which amounts 
shall be withdrawn by the Borrower from the Credit Account or cancelled; (ii) at the 
rate set as of the June 30 immediately preceding the accrual date and at such other 
rates as may be set from time to time thereafter
pursuant to paragraph (a) above.  The rate set as of June 30 in each year shall be 
applied from the next payment date in that year specified in Section 2.06 of this 
Agreement. 
(c)
The commitment charge shall be paid: (i) at such places as the 
Association shall reasonably request; (ii) without restrictions of any kind imposed 
by, or in the territory of, the Borrower; and (iii) in the currency specified in this 
Agreement for the purposes of Section 4.02 of the General Conditions or in such other 
eligible currency or currencies as may from time to
time be designated or selected pursuant to the provisions of that Section.
Section 2.05. The Borrower shall pay to the Association a service charge at the 
rate of three-fourths of one percent (3/4 of 1%) per annum on the principal amount of 
the Credit withdrawn and outstanding from time to time.
Section 2.06. Commitment charges and service charges shall be payable 
semiannually on April 1 and October 1 in each year.
Section 2.07. (a) Subject to paragraphs (b) and (c) below, the Borrower shall 
repay the principal amount of the Credit in semiannual installments payable on each 
April 1 and October 1 commencing October 1, 1999 and ending April 1, 2024. Each 
installment to and including the installment payable on April 1, 2009 shall be one and 
one-fourth percent (1-1/4%) of such principal amount, and each installment thereafter 
shall be two and one-half percent (2-1/2%) of such principal amount.
(b)
Whenever (i) the Borrower's gross national product per capita, as 
determined by the Association, shall have exceeded $790 in constant 1985 dollars for 
five consecutive years, and (ii) the Bank shall consider the Borrower creditworthy for 
Bank lending, the Association may, subsequent to the review and approval thereof by 
the Executive Directors of the Association and after due consideration by them of the 
development of the Borrower's economy, modify the terms of repayment of installments 
under paragraph (a) above by requiring the Borrower to repay twice the amount of each 
such installment not yet due until the principal amount of the Credit shall have been 
repaid. If so requested by the Borrower, the Association may revise such modification 
to include, in lieu of some or all of the increase in the amounts of such 
installments, the payment of interest at an annual rate agreed with the Association on 
the principal amount of the Credit withdrawn and outstanding from time to time, 
provided that, in the judgment of the Association, such revision shall not change the 
grant element obtained under the above-mentioned repayment modification. 
(c)
If, at any time after a modification of terms pursuant to paragraph (b) 
above, the Association determines that the Borrower's economic condition has

deteriorated significantly, the Association may, if so requested by the Borrower, 
further modify the terms of repayment to conform to the schedule of installments as 
provided in paragraph (a) above.
Section 2.08. The currency of the United States of America is hereby specified 
for the purposes of Section 4.02 of the General Conditions.
ARTICLE III
Execution of the Project
Section 3.01. (a) The Borrower declares its commitment to the objectives of the 
project as set forth in Schedule 2 to this Agreement, and, to this end, shall carry 
out the Project or cause the Project to be carried out, with due diligence and 
efficiency and in conformity with appropriate public health practices, and shall 
provide, promptly as needed, the funds, facilities, services and other resources 
required for the Project.
(b)
Without any limitation or restriction upon any of its other obligations 
under the Development Credit Agreement, the Borrower shall cause the Provinces to 
perform in accordance with the provisions of the Project Agreement all the obligations 
of the Provinces therein set forth, shall take and cause to be taken all action, 
including the provision of funds, facilities, services and other resources, necessary 
or appropriate to enable the Provinces to perform such obligations, and shall not take 
or permit to be taken any action which would prevent or interfere with such 
performance.
(c)
The Borrower shall make part of the proceeds of the Credit available to 
the Provinces for the purposes of their respective parts of the Project under 
procedures and terms and conditions satisfactory to the Association. 
Section 3.02. Except as the Association shall otherwise agree, procurement of 
the goods, works and consultants' services required for the Project and to be financed 
out of the proceeds of the Credit shall be governed by the provisions of the Schedule 
to the Project Agreement.
Section 3.03. (a) The Borrower and the Association shall annually prior to the 
beginning of each fiscal year exchange views on the progress achieved in carrying out 
the Policy Guidelines, the Regional Health Plans and the Provincial components under 
the Project.
(b)
Prior to each such exchange of views, the Borrower shall furnish to the 
Association for its review and comment a progress report, in such detail as the 
Association shall reasonably request including the progress in carrying out the 
Regional Health Plans and the Provincial components according to indicators agreed 
with the Association.
(c)
The Borrower shall ensure that during it's fiscal year disbursements by 
each of the Implementing Regions and the Provinces out of the proceeds of the Credit 
will be made only after the broad parameters of such fiscal year's program and budget 
under each of the Regional Health Plans and the Provincial components shall have been 
reviewed and found generally acceptable by both MOPH and the Association.
Section 3.04. The Borrower and the Association hereby agree that the obligations 
set forth in Sections 9.03, 9.04, 9.05, 9.06, 9.07 and 9.08 of the General Conditions 
(relating to insurance, use of goods and services, plans and schedules, records and 
reports, maintenance and land acquisition, respectively) in respect of relevant part 
of the Project shall be carried out by each of the Provinces pursuant to Section 2.03 
of the Project Agreement.
Section 3.05. The Borrower shall cause the Project to be carried out under an 
Implementation Arrangement among the Implementing Regions, the Provinces and MOPH, 
with terms and conditions acceptable to the Association.
Section 3.06. The Borrower shall maintain the World Bank Loan Office within MOPH 
with functions and staffing acceptable to the Association.

SCHEDULE 2
Description of the Project
The objectives of the Project are to improve the quality and effectiveness of 
health services in the Implementing Regions in the Provinces through (a) the 
introduction of improved systems for gathering and analyzing epidemiological data, 
formulating regional health plans to improve and expand programs, and managing and 
monitoring the implementation of investment plans in accordance with the Policy 
Guidelines; (b) the financing of select investments and expansion of health services 
under the regional health plans and in the Provinces; (c) the strengthening of the 
MOPH and provincial health bureaus in the Provinces, to provide policy guidance, 
operational  research, technical advice and outreach support services necessary to 
improve health services in the Implementing Regions, with a view to eventual national 
replication of effective approaches.
The Project consists of the following parts which will include the following 
activities, subject to such modifications thereof as the Borrower and the Association 
may agree upon from time to time to achieve such objectives:
Part A:
Strengthening of Health Management
 
1.
Improvement of disease surveillance and information management and strengthening 
of data collection, analysis and utilization capability and systematic analysis of 
problems and testing of solutions.
2.
Improvement of planning, monitoring and readjustment of health management 
systems, and associated training programs, to facilitate reforms and improvements 
throughout the regional health system, in accordance with the Policy Guidelines.
Part B:
Improvement of Disease Prevention and Surveillance Capabilities
 
Development of capability in (a) identification of vulnerable groups at high 
risks of death and illnesses; and (b) targeting delivery of primary and secondary 
prevention programs to reduce morbidity and mortality.
Part C:
Improvement of Health Education
 
Improvement of health status and reduction of risk factor prevalence through the 
development of programs of health maintenance, promotion and education for 
communicable and chronic disease prevention and injury control in schools, worksites 
and in the community.
Part D:
Improvement of Maternal and Child Health Services
 
Strengthening of maternal and infant health services through upgrading the 
quality of existing midwives, improving and consolidating the available facilities for 
maternal and child health care, and developing comprehensive programs to address the 
Page  10
common diseases prevalent among children and women.
Part E:
Development of Emergency Services
 
Development of a system of coordinated emergency medical services by upgrading 
the quality and delivery of pre-hospital rescue services, dispatch and transport 
management, training of field rescue staff, coordination among health service units 
and by selective investments in a rational network of emergency units which are part 
of existing hospital operations.
Part F:
Improvement of Hospital Services
 
Improvement of the quality, efficacy and cost-effectiveness of health services 
available from hospitals and clinics through improvement in facilities, equipment, 
staff and in-patient and out-patient services and by efficiency improvements through 
reduction of the average, in-hospital length of stay, strengthening of pre-admission 
diagnostics services, and out-patient services, and the establishment of admission, 
referral and quality control protocols.
Part G:
Development of Rehabilitation Services
 
Development of a community-based rehabilitation service to reduce the burden on 
hospitals in the provision of rehabilitative care by establishing community 
rehabilitation services and enhancing utilization of community resources in 
rehabilitation programs.
Part H:
Improvement of Medical Education and Training
 
Improvement of the quality of medical education and training for health staff to 
meet changing health needs through reforms and investments to enhance the content and 
methodology of teaching and of continuing education programs and to increase qualified 
graduates, especially nurses and technicians, and the numbers of health workers to 
whom continuing education can be offered.
Part I:
Improvement of Equipment Management Capabilities
 
Improvement of training and facilities, and establishment of units and systems 
for the coordinated maintenance and repair of medical instruments and equipment. 
Part J:
Strengthening of National Health Management
 
Strengthening of national health management capabilities through policy 
evaluation, development and dissemination of cost-effective strategies and technical 
guidance in the following areas: disease and risk factor surveillance and information 
management; prevention and treatment of communicable, endemic and chronic disease, 
maternal and child health services; hospital planning and management; emergency 
service networks; community-based rehabilitation programs; and medical and health 
education and training programs.
