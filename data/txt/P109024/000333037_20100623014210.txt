I. General
1. Project information
:
PESAT (Proyek Ekonomi Sosial Aceh Terpadu / Integrated Social Economy in Aceh Project) 
Canadian Co-operative Association and PASKA 
Economic Development Financing Facility, Grant TF093358/ P109024 
Aceh, Indonesia 
 
Executive Summary 
 
PESAT is a market-
oriented solution to rural Acehnese women and men’s vulnerability to poverty. It 
takes an integrated approach that will empower women and men to develop self-reliance and the 
opportunities for poverty alleviation. The central position of PESAT is that by engaging smallholders 
in entrepreneurial activity, and addressing the systemic factors that limit opportunities for them in the 
social and economic environment, they will be able to lift themselves out of poverty. This can be 
accomplished by developing skills that increase the resilience of communities, increase access to 
markets, and develop an entrepreneurial ethos in communities that contributes to livelihood 
development. These activities will be combined with addressing the pre-conditions of the enabling 
environment in which all social and economic activity occurs. The PESAT approach is to work with 
smallholders to take as much control over the value chain as possible in order to derive profit not 
only from primary production, but also from the value-added activities of processing, marketing, 
volume purchasing and collective selling. 
 
The project will procure goods and services in order to engage in these activities. The project will 
commence with CCA establishing its office in Banda Aceh. PASKA has current offices in Sigli and 
Meulaboh already. The establishment of an office will include office equipment and furniture 
required to enable eleven people to work effectively. At the same time, the project will equip 42 field 
staff to access the communities that the sub-project will serve. Each field worker will have a 
motorbike that will facilitate access to the communities. 
 
One of the central features of the sub-project is a marketing co-operative that is owned by the 
production co-operative who are involved in rice, fish and emping production. This co-operative will 
be based in Banda Aceh for sales and have processing facilities in both Nagan Raya and Pidie. It will 
procure equipment to process rice, produce fish pellets, and for cold storage, storage of agricultural 
inputs, and storage and transport of products for the purposes of marketing. Since this is a new entity, 
it will also require office set up and the capitalisation of major equipment, including vehicles. It also 
has a marketing budget and will be an independent business by the end of the sub-project.

Additional equipment will be purchased for market-ready co-operatives such as emping processing to 
modernise production and increase production volume. Similarly, basic rice drying facilities will be 
added in communities as will storage for all commodities in most villages. 
 
A
number of procurement items also relate to the communications strategy to ensure transparency of 
activities to the public and communicating the messages of the project to the producers.  
 
The sub-project will procure the majority of its goods and services in the first half of the time period, 
primarily because the equipment and services procured are required to be the basis for market 
activities.  
 
By March 2012, PESAT will leave a legacy of sustainable private sector enterprises owned and 
operated by rural small holder Acehnese women and men. They will be diverse in activities, 
contributing to a diversified economy, yet work together through a centralised marketing facility that 
will lead the continued charge to develop the capacity of these enterprises to continue to flourish 
after the completion of the project and reduction or elimination of external funding. This vision is 
supported by local governments, which will have benefited from improved participation of local 
enterprises engaged in governance and be able to contribute to their own enabling environment in the 
future. PESAT will improve the resilience of communities and their businesses toward an improved 
Aceh with improved markets, enhanced production, the ability to manage peace, a commitment to 
gender equality, consciousness of the natural environment, and practice of good governance.
