Please refer to the Loan Agreement between the International Bank for 
Reconstruction and Development (the Bank) and the Republic of Colombia (the Borrower) 
dated November 8, 1991 as amended up to the date herewith (the Loan Agreement).
Pursuant to your request to amend the captioned Loan Agreement, as communicated 
to the Bank in letter 020210 of Ms. Viviana Lara of the Public Credit Directorate of 
the Ministry of Finance and Public Credit dated August 1, 1997, and in letter 10679-2 
of the Ministry of Economic Development dated July 25, 1997, we are pleased to advise 
you that the Bank agrees to amend the Loan Agreement as follows, so as to address your 
request:

A new Part E is added to the project at the end of Schedule 2, after Part D, as 
follows: 
"Part E
A program for institutional improvement of the water and sanitation 
sector companies, 
including: an assistance program to support a process to increase 
private sector 
participation in the water and sanitation sector, 
primarily aimed at carrying out said 
process up to the point of privatization 
of one or more medium size water utilities, each 
serving a population of about 
500,000; and
Page  4
an assistance program of institutional improvement support to community organizations 
responsible for water supply in small municipalities (of up to 30,000 inhabitants), as 
well as of promoting the establishment of regional water supply entities, responsible 
for providing water supply and sanitation services in a group of small neighboring 
municipalities, including: (a) purchase and design of instruments (including software 
and hardware goods) for managerial and institutional development of public service 
providers (accounting systems, financial and commercial management systems, and 
operation and maintenance procedures); (b) providing support to the institutional 
development of at least 10 community organizations of water supply, (c) providing 
support for the establishment of regional water supply entities and creation of 
regulatory incentives for favoring combined management of regional entities and 
adjacent metropolitan systems; (d) development of capacity at the national level, 
including MDE and FINDETER, as well as the departmental and municipal levels, to 
support the institutional strengthening of public water and sewerage companies; (e) 
training of water companies in practical aspects of application of the Borrower’s Law 
142 of July 11, 1994 and in responding to regulatory norms and incentives; and (f) 
providing support to Selected Project Entities in their transformation, reorganization 
and strengthening processes.”
