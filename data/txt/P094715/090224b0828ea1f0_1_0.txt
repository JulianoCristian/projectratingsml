                         Implementation Status Overview
                         Project implementation is satisfactory. The Project has supported Brazil in achieving 21 of the 50 quantitative national targets defined by the UN Convention on Biological Diversity.
                         Among the important results in terms of mainstreaming biodiversity in governmental sectors are the adoption in 2012 of the National Policy on Agroecology and Organic Production
                         (Decree No 7,794/2012) and the related National Plan officially launched on October 2013 and the creation of the Virtual Institute for Biodiversity (Painel Bio) in 2014 with the

                         The Project has also developed innovative biodiversity criteria and policy guidelines for strategic sectors (agriculture, fisheries, energy and health), including the implementation of
                         private sector subprojects in more than four million hectares in four major Brazilian biomes (Amazonia, Cerrado, Pampa and Atlantic Forest) involving various productive sectors and
                         approaches (traditional cattle ranching, forest management)
