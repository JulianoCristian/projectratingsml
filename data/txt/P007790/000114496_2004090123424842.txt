                      DEVELOPMENT CREDIT AGREEMENT
     AGREEMENT, dated October 1, 1996, between REPUBLIC OF NICARAGUA (the
Borrower) and INTERNATIONAL DEVELOPMENT ASSOCIATION (the Association).
     (A)  WHEREAS the Borrower, having satisfied itself as to the feasi-
bility and priority of the Project described in Schedule 2 to this
Agreement, has requested the Association to assist in the financing of
the Project;
     (B)  the Borrower intends to obtain from the Global Environment
Trust Fund (GET) a grant (the GET Grant) in an amount in SDRs equivalent
to $7,000,000 to assist in financing Part C of the Project on the terms
and conditions set forth in an agreement (the GET Grant Agreement) to be
entered into between the Borrower and GET; and
     WHEREAS the Association has agreed, on the basis, inter alia, of
the foregoing, to extend the Credit to the Borrower upon the terms and
conditions set forth in this Agreement;
     NOW THEREFORE the parties hereto hereby agree as follows:

                                ARTICLE II
                                The Credit
     Section 2.01. The Association agrees to lend to the Borrower, on the
terms and conditions set forth or referred to in the Development Credit
Agreement, an amount in various currencies equivalent to twenty million
nine hundred thousand Special Drawing Rights (SDR 20,900,000).
     Section 2.02. (a) The amount of the Credit may be withdrawn from
the Credit Account in accordance with the provisions of Schedule 1 to
this Agreement for: (i) expenditures made (or, if the Association shall
so agree, to be made) in respect of the reasonable cost of goods and
services required for the Project (other than Part A.4 thereof) described
in Schedule 2 to this Agreement and to be financed out of the proceeds of
the Credit; and (ii) amounts paid (or, if the Association shall agree, to
be paid) by the Borrower on account of withdrawals made under a Grant to
meet the reasonable cost of goods and services required for the Sub-
project in respect of which the withdrawal from the Credit account is
requested.
     (b)  The Borrower may, for the purposes of the Project, open and
maintain in dollars in a commercial bank or banks acceptable to the
Association: (i) a special deposit account (the INIFOM Special Account)
for purposes of depositing the corresponding proceeds of the Credit for
Part A of the Project; and (ii) a special deposit account (the MARENA
Special Account) for purposes of depositing the corresponding proceeds
of the Credit for Part B of the Project, on terms and conditions satis-
factory to the Association, including appropriate protection against
set-off, seizure or attachment. Deposits into, and payments out of, the
Special Accounts shall be made in accordance with the provisions of
Schedule 4 to this Agreement.
     (c)  Promptly after the Effective Date, the Association shall, on
behalf of the Borrower, withdraw from the Credit Account and pay to
itself the amount required to repay the principal amount of the Project
Preparation Advance withdrawn and outstanding as of such date and to
pay all unpaid charges thereon. The unwithdrawn balance of the
authorized amount of the Project Preparation Advance shall thereupon be
canceled.
     Section 2.03. The Closing Date shall be March 31, 2002 or such later
date as the Association shall establish. The Association shall promptly
notify the Borrower of such later date.
     Section 2.04. (a)  The Borrower shall pay to the Association a
commitment charge on the principal amount of the Credit not withdrawn
from time to time at a rate to be set by the Association as of June 30
of each year, but not to exceed the rate of one-half of one percent
(1/2 of 1%) per annum.
     (b)  The commitment charge shall accrue: (i) from the date sixty
Page  5
days after the date of this Agreement (the accrual date) to the
respective dates on which amounts shall be withdrawn by the Borrower
from the Credit Account or canceled; and (ii) at the rate set as of the
June 30 immediately preceding the accrual date or at such other rates
as may be set from time to time thereafter pursuant to paragraph (a)
above.  The rate set as of June 30 in each year shall be applied from
the next payment date in that year specified in Section 2.06 of this
Agreement.
     (c)  The commitment charge shall be paid: (i) at such places as
the Association shall reasonably request; (ii) without restrictions of
any kind imposed by, or in the territory of, the Borrower; and (iii)
in the currency specified in this Agreement for the purposes of Section
4.02 of the General Conditions or in such other eligible currency or
currencies as may from time to time be designated or selected pursuant
to the provisions of that Section.
     Section 2.05. The Borrower shall pay to the Association a service
charge at the rate of three-fourths of one per cent (3/4 of 1%) per
annum on the principal amount of the Credit withdrawn and outstanding
from time to time.
     Section 2.06. Commitment charges and service charges shall be
payable semiannually on June 1 and December 1 in each year.
     Section 2.07. (a) Subject to paragraphs (b) and (c) below, the
Borrower shall repay the principal amount of the Credit in semi-annual
installments payable on each June 1 and December 1 commencing December
1, 2006 and ending June 1, 2036.  Each installment to and including
the installment payable on June 1, 2016 shall be one percent (1%) of
such principal amount, and each installment thereafter shall be two
percent (2%) of such principal amount.
     (b)  Whenever: (i) the Borrower's gross national product per
capita, as determined by the Association, shall have exceeded $790 in
constant 1985 dollars for five consecutive years; and (ii) the Bank
shall consider the Borrower creditworthy for Bank lending, the
Association may, subsequent to the review and approval thereof by the
Executive Directors of the Association and after due consideration by
them of the development of the Borrower's economy, modify the terms
of repayment of installments under paragraph (a) above by requiring
the Borrower to repay twice the amount of each such installment not
yet due until the principal amount of the Credit shall have been
repaid. If so requested by the Borrower, the Association may revise
such modification to include, in lieu of some or all of the increase
in the amounts of such installments, the payment of interest at an
annual rate agreed with the Association on the principal amount of
the Credit withdrawn and outstanding from time to time, provided that,
in the judgment of the Association, such revision shall not change the
grant element obtained under the above-mentioned repayment modifica-
tion.
     (c)  If, at any time after a modification of terms pursuant to
paragraph (b) above, the Association determines that the Borrower's
economic condition has deteriorated significantly, the Association
may, if so requested by the Borrower, further modify the terms of
repayment to conform to the schedule of installments as provided in
paragraph (a) above.
     Section 2.08. The currency of the United States of America is
hereby specified for the purposes of Section 4.02 of the General
Conditions.

                         Execution of the Project

          (i)  shall carry out Parts B and C of the Project through

               MARENA with due diligence and efficiency and in con-
               formity with appropriate financial, administrative,
               economic and environmental practices, and shall
               provide, promptly as needed, the funds, facilities,
               services and other resources required for such Parts
               of the Project; and
         (ii)  shall cause INIFOM, under the provisions of the INIFOM
               Subsidiary Agreement, to carry out Part A of the
               Project, with the assistance of the Municipalities
               and the Support Agencies, as the case may be, with
               due diligence and efficiency and in conformity with
               appropriate financial, administrative, economic,

               accordance with the Operational Manual, and shall
               take or cause to be taken all action, including the
               provision of funds, facilities, services and other

               take or permit to be taken any action which would
               prevent or interfere with the carrying out of such
               Part of the Project by INIFOM.

for Part A of the Project available to INIFOM under a subsidiary
agreement to be entered into between the Borrower and INIFOM, under
terms and conditions which shall have been approved by the Association,
which shall include:
          (i)  INIFOM's obligation to carry out Part A of the Project
               in accordance with the provisions of this Agreement and
               the Operational Manual;
         (ii)  INIFOM's obligation to enter into:

                    to participate in the implementation of Part A.4 of
                    the Project and to comply with the provisions of
                    the Operational Manual in the financing and/or
                    implementation of Subprojects; and (II) INIFOM's
                    obligation to make available, in the name and on
                    behalf of the Borrower, the funds required for the
                    implementation of Parts A.1 and A.4 of the Project,
                    as the case may be;
               (B)  with each Municipality an implementation agreement,
                    in the terms of the respective Model Form, setting
                    forth their respective obligations in the financing
                    and/or execution of Subprojects (other than
                    Productive Subprojects);
               (C)  with each Municipality, on the one hand, and with
                    a Beneficiary, on the other hand, in respect of
                    each Productive Subproject, in the terms of the
                    respective Model Form, a beneficiary grant agree-
                    ment setting forth their respective obligations in
                    the financing and/or implementation of a Productive
                    Subproject;
               (D)  with CERAP an agreement, under terms and conditions
                    satisfactory to the Association, setting forth
                    their respective obligations in the implementation
                    of Parts A 2. (b) (i) and (ii) and (c) (i) of the
                    Project; and
               (E)  with the Support Agencies an agreement or agreements,
                    under terms and conditions satisfactory to the
                    Association, setting forth their respective obliga-

                    Manual; and
        (iii)  INIFOM's undertaking to: (A) exercise its rights under
               the Eligibility and Financing Agreements, the Imple-
               mentation Agreements, the Beneficiary Grant Agreements,
               the Support Agency Agreement and the Decentralization
               Agreement to protect the interest of the Borrower, the
               Association, and INIFOM, and to accomplish the purposes
               of the Credit; and (B) except as the Association and

Borrower shall:
          (i)  include in its annual budget proposals submitted to the
               National Assembly the following minimum amounts, as
               counterpart funds for the financing of Parts A and B of
               the Project:
               (A)  $243,000 equivalent for calendar year 1997;
               (B)  $534,000 equivalent for calendar year 1998;

               (D)  $807,000 equivalent for calendar year 2000; and

               (d) (i) of this Section, and no later than the first

               counterpart funding requirements for the respective

     Section 3.02. Except as the Association shall otherwise agree,
procurement of the goods, works and consultants' services required for

     (b)  prepare, under terms of reference satisfactory to the

                                 ARTICLE IV
                             Financial Covenants
     Section 4.01. (a) The Borrower shall maintain in respect of Parts
B and C of the Project, and shall cause INIFOM in respect of Part A of
the Project to maintain, records and separate accounts adequate to
reflect in accordance with sound accounting practices the operations,
resources and expenditures in respect of the Project.
     (b)  The Borrower shall, and shall cause INIFOM to:
          (i)  have the records and accounts referred to in paragraph
               (a) of this Section, including those for the INIFOM
               Special Account and the MARENA Special Account, as
               applicable, for each fiscal year audited, in accordance
               with appropriate auditing principles consistently
               applied, by independent auditors acceptable to the
               Association;
         (ii)  furnish to the Association as soon as available, but in
               any case not later than six months after the end of
               each such year, certified copies of the report of such
               audit or audits by said auditors, of such scope and in
               such detail as the Association shall have reasonably
               requested; and
        (iii)  furnish to the Association such other information
               concerning such records, accounts, and the audit or
               audits thereof as the Association shall from time to
               time reasonably request.
     (c)  For all expenditures with respect to which withdrawals from
the Credit Account were made on the basis of statements of expenditure,
Page  9
the Borrower shall in respect of Part B of the Project, and shall cause
INIFOM, in respect of Part A of the Project to:
          (i)  maintain, in accordance with paragraph (a) of this
               Section, records and separate accounts reflecting such
               expenditures;
         (ii)  retain, until at least one year after the Association
               has received the audit report or reports for the fiscal
               year in which the last withdrawal from the Credit
               Account or payment out of the INIFOM Special Account
               or the MARENA Special Account, as the case may be, was
               made, all records (contracts, orders, invoices, bills,

               audits contains a separate opinion by said auditors as
               to whether the statements of expenditure submitted
               during such fiscal year, together with the procedures
               and internal controls involved in their preparation,
               can be relied upon to support the related withdrawals.

                               SCHEDULE 2
                       Description of the Project
     The objectives of the Project are: (a) to reduce rural poverty; and
(b) to improve natural resources management.
     The Project consists of the following parts, subject to such
modifications thereof as the Borrower and the Association may agree
upon from time to time to achieve such objectives:
Part A:   Rural Municipalities Development
Page  14
1.   Institutional Development of Municipalities
     (a)  Operation and strengthening of Municipality Technical Units
through the provision of equipment, training and technical assistance,
and office remodeling and/or expansion required therefor.
     (b)  Provision of technical assistance and training to Municipa-
lities in: (i) the design and planning of rural development, naturalresources and 
environmental programs; (ii) community participation;
(iii) the Subproject cycle; (iv) municipal accounts, financial
controls, and monitoring and evaluation systems; and (v) selected
aspects of local government management including, inter alia, financial
management, administration, procurement, personnel management, account-
ing and auditing, tax and cadastral matters, local revenue generation
and maintenance cost recovery mechanisms.
2.   Institutional Development of INIFOM
     (a)  Strengthening of INIFOM's capacity to implement Part A of the
Project through the provision of technical assistance and training, and
the remodeling and/or expansion of the offices of the INIFOM Unit
required therefor.
     (b)  Provision of technical assistance for:
          (i)  the design and implementation of a decentralization
               strategy for the transfer of selected public sector
               services from the Borrower to the municipalities and
               other autonomous agencies;
         (ii)  the drafting and dissemination of a new municipalities
               law and the regulations thereof;
        (iii)  the drafting of a municipal workers' code; and
         (iv)  the review of possibilities for the granting of
               juridical personality to communities.
     (c)  Carrying out of: (i) a study on costs and benefits of the
decentralization strategy referred to in Part A. 2. (b) (i) of the
Project; and (ii) other studies as the Association may agree.
3.   Information, Participation and Training
     (a)  Promotion and dissemination of Part A.4 of the Project at
the community level through the provision of technical assistance and
training required therefor.
     (b)  Training of selected community members, NGOs and private
consultants in:  (i) the Subproject cycle; (ii) participatory planning;
(iii) technical aspects of selected Subprojects; (iv) methodologies
for technology transfer; and (v) Subproject administration.
     (c)  Establishment and monitoring of a registry of providers of
technical assistance at the municipal level in the fields of - inter
alia - community organization and management, Subproject preparation,
and maintenance for on-farm and off-farm activities of the rural poor.
4.   Subprojects
     Financing of Subprojects through Grants.
Part B:   Natural Resource Policies and Institutions
1.   Institutional Development of MARENA
     (a)  Strengthening of MARENA's capabilities for implementation of
Parts B and C of the Project through the provision of technical
assistance and training and the remodeling and/or expansion of MARENA's
field offices required therefor.
     (b)  Preparation and dissemination of municipal and regional
Page  15
environmental and land use plans, and of an informal system for
monitoring threats to natural resources and complying with the
related plans, with the full involvement of regional and local
governments, and consultations and participation of indigenous and
non-indigenous communities residing therein.
     (c)  (i)  Preparation and dissemination of legislative proposals
               and regulations covering, inter alia: (A) regulations
               of the Borrower's general environmental law; (B) a
               protected areas and/or biodiversity law and the regu-
               lations thereof; (C) a forestry law and the regulations
               thereof; and (D) a fisheries law and the regulations
               thereof;
         (ii)  Carrying out of studies to address: (A) initiatives for
               the harmonization of decentralization, natural resources
               and indigenous peoples legislation; and (B) the relation-
               ship between rural poverty and environmental degradation;
               and
        (iii)  preparation and dissemination of other legislative
               proposals, and carrying out of other studies, as the
               Association may agree.
2.   Inter-institutional Assistance Program
     Provision of technical assistance and training for:
     (a)  members of the National Assembly in the elaboration and review
of legislative proposals related to natural resources and the environ-
ment;
     (b)  the Judiciary to prepare trainers, curricula and training
materials, and train about sixty Supreme Court and other judges in
environmental matters;
     (c)  the Borrower's Office of the Attorney General to operate the
Environmental Unit;
     (d)  police environmental units to prepare trainers, curricula and
training materials on control of illegal logging and fishing, wildlife
trade and contamination from mining and other industries;
     (e)  army environmental units to prepare trainers, curricula and
training materials on monitoring of turtle landings in the Pacific
Ocean, control of forest fires, monitoring of resource degradation and
patrolling of protected areas;
     (f)  legal offices of selected central government agencies to
strengthen their capability in environmental and natural resources
legislation and regulation; and
     (g)  selected municipal governments to prepare trainers, curricula
and training materials, providing training in the role and respon-
sibilities of municipalities in natural resource and environmental
management, including natural resource taxation by the municipalities,
and elaboration, use and monitoring of environment and land use plans.
Part C:   Atlantic Biodiversity Corridor
1.   Community Involvement in Biodiversity Management
     (a)  Promotion of Part C of the Project in the Atlantic region and
the Borrower's department of Rio San Juan through the provision of
technical assistance and training.
     (b)  Training of indigenous and non-indigenous communities in
sustainable biodiversity use and protection.
     (c)  Provision of technical assistance and training for indigenous
communities to pursue recognition of their land and natural resources
rights.
Page  16
2.   Biodiversity Planning, Information, Monitoring and Evaluation
     Planning, monitoring, evaluation and implementation of biodiversity
activities and strategies through the provision of the equipment,
technical assistance and training required therefor.
3.   Biodiversity Projects and Indigenous Lands
     Financing of projects for:
     (a)  biodiversity use and conservation;
     (b)  regularization of the land tenure situation of indigenous
peoples in the Atlantic coast region; and
     (c)  management of protected areas.

     The Project is expected to be completed by September 30, 2001.
                                  SCHEDULE 3
                    Procurement and Consultants' Services
Section I.     Procurement of Goods and Works
Part A:   General
     Goods and works shall be procured in accordance with the provisions
of Section I of the "Guidelines for Procurement under IBRD Loans and IDA
Credits" published by the Bank in January 1995 and revised in January
1996 (the Guidelines) and the following provisions of this Section, as
applicable.
Part B:   Procurement Procedures
1.   Limited International Bidding
     Vehicles and computers shall be procured under contracts awarded
in accordance with the provisions of paragraph 3.2 of the Guidelines
to suppliers with established maintenance and service facilities in
the Borrower's territory.
2.   National Competitive Bidding
     Goods (other than vehicles and computers), and goods and works for
Subprojects shall be procured under contracts awarded in accordance
with the provisions of paragraphs 3.3 and 3.4 of the Guidelines. In the
procurement of goods and works in accordance with this Part B.2, the
Borrower shall use standard bidding documents satisfactory to the
Association.
3.   International Shopping
     Goods estimated to cost less than $50,000 equivalent per contract,
up to an aggregate amount not to exceed $70,000 equivalent, of which
the aggregate of goods other than vehicles should not exceed $40,000
equivalent, may be procured under contracts awarded on the basis of
international shopping procedures in accordance with the provisions
of paragraphs 3.5 and 3.6 of the Guidelines.
4.   National Shopping
     (a)  Goods (other than for Subprojects) estimated to cost less than
$25,000 equivalent per contract, up to an aggregate amount not to exceed
$100,000 equivalent each in respect of Part A and Part B of the Project;
and (b) goods for Subprojects estimated to cost less than $25,000
equivalent per contract, up to an aggregate amount not to exceed
$22,000,000 equivalent, may be procured under contracts awarded on the
basis of national shopping procedures in accordance with the provisions
Page  17
of paragraphs 3.5 and 3.6 of the Guidelines.
5.   Procurement of Small Works
     (a) Works for Parts A.1 (a), A.2 (a), and B.1(a) of the Project;
and (b) works for Subprojects estimated to cost less than $25,000
equivalent per contract, up to an aggregate amount not to exceed
$22,000,000 equivalent, may be procured under lump-sum, fixed-price
contracts awarded on the basis of quotations obtained from three (3)
qualified domestic contractors in response to a written invitation.
The invitation shall include a detailed description of the works,
including basic specifications, the required completion date, a basic
form of agreement acceptable to the Association, and relevant drawings,
where applicable. The award shall be made to the contractor who offers
the lowest price quotation for the required work, and who has the
experience and resources to complete the contract successfully.
6.   Direct Contracting
     Goods and works for Subprojects to be carried out predominantly
in distant or scattered rural areas and where competitive proposals
cannot be obtained, estimated to cost less than $10,000 equivalent per
Subproject and contract, and costing $1,000,000 equivalent or less in
the aggregate, may, with the Association's prior agreement, be procured
in accordance with the provisions of paragraph 3.7 of the Guidelines.
Part C:   Review by the Association of Procurement Decisions
1.   Procurement Planning
     Prior to the issuance of any invitations to bid for contracts, the
proposed procurement plan for the Project shall be furnished to the
Association for its review and approval, in accordance with the pro-
visions of paragraph 1 of Appendix 1 to the Guidelines. Procurement of
all goods and works shall be undertaken in accordance with such pro-
curement plan as shall have been approved by the Association, and with
the provisions of said paragraph 1.
2.   Prior Review
     With respect to:  (a) each contract for goods procured pursuant to
the provisions of Parts B.1 hereof; and (b) (i) the first two contracts
for goods (other than for Subprojects); and (ii) the first contract
each for goods and works for Subprojects procured pursuant to the
provisions of Part B.2 hereof, the procedures set forth in paragraphs
2 and 3 of Appendix 1 to the Guidelines shall apply.
3.   Post Review
     With respect to each contract not governed by paragraph 2 of this
Part, the procedures set forth in paragraph 4 of Appendix 1 to the
Guidelines shall apply.
Section II.    Employment of Consultants
1.   Except as provided in paragraph 3 hereof, consultants' services
shall be procured under contracts awarded in accordance with the
provisions of the "Guidelines for the Use of Consultants by World Bank
Borrowers and by the World Bank as Executing Agency" published by the
Bank in August 1981 (the Consultant Guidelines).  For complex, time-
based assignments, such contracts shall be based on the standard form
of contract for consultants' services issued by the Bank, with such
modifications as shall have been agreed by the Association.  Where no
relevant standard contract documents have been issued by the Bank,
other standard forms acceptable to the Association shall be used.
2.   Notwithstanding the provisions of paragraph 1 of this Section,
the provisions of the Consultant Guidelines requiring prior Association
review or approval of budgets, short lists, selection procedures,
letters of invitation, proposals, evaluation reports and contracts
shall not apply to: (a) contracts for the employment of consulting
firms estimated to cost less than $100,000 equivalent each; or (b)
Page  18
contracts for the employment of individuals estimated to cost less
than $30,000 equivalent each.  However, said exceptions to prior
Association review shall not apply to: (a) the terms of reference
for such contracts; (b) single-source selection of consulting firms;
(c) assignments of a critical nature, as reasonably determined by the
Association; (d) amendments to contracts for the employment of
consulting firms raising the contract value to $100,000 equivalent
or above; or (e) amendments to contracts for the employment of
individual consultants raising the contract value to $30,000 equiva-
lent or above.
3.   Consultants' services for Subprojects dealing with the subjects
of the registry to be established and monitored under Part A.3 (c) of
the Project, may be provided by the consultants registered in such
registry following procedures acceptable to the Association.
