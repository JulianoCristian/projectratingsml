Re: 
Loan No.4544-DO (Water Disposal in Tourism Centers Project) 
Amendment to the Loan Agreement
Excellency: 
 
Please refer to the Loan Agreement between the Dominican Republic (the Borrower) and 
the International Bank for Reconstruction and Development (the Bank), dated April 21, 2000, 
with regard to the above-captioned Project (the Loan Agreement). 
 
We are pleased to inform you that, pursuant to your letter of request dated October 18, 
2002, and our recent discussions on the subject, the Bank hereby agrees to: 
 
(a) 
extend the closing date of the above mentioned Loan to June 30, 2004 for 
purposes of Section 2.03 of the Loan Agreement, and 
 
(b) 
amend the Loan Agreement so as to incorporate a new “operational costs” 
disbursement category in Section A of Schedule 1, and reallocate the funds among categories 
accordingly as set forth in the Annex of this Amendment Letter.  
 
Please confirm your agreement with the foregoing amendment by signing and dating this 
letter in the spaces provided below.  This amendment letter shall be executed in two counterparts 
each of which shall be an original. 
 
Upon confirmation please return one fully executed original to us. The provisions set 
forth in this amendment letter shall become effective as of the date of this letter upon receipt by 
the Bank of one fully executed original of this amendment letter.

For the purposes of this Schedule:  
 
(a) 
the term “foreign expenditures” means expenditures in the currency of any

(b) 
the term “local expenditures” means expenditures in the currency of the

Project, including travel costs and per diem of the trainees, rental of facilities and equipment, and 
training materials; and 
 
(d) 
the term “operational costs” means expenditures for incremental costs incurred 
by the PCU in connection with the carrying out of its functions and responsibilities including: 
office supplies, equipment and utilities, office rent, and the salaries of the PCU staff (which 
expenditures would not have been incurred absent the Project). 
 
3. 
Notwithstanding the provisions of paragraph 1 above, no withdrawals shall be made in 
respect of:  
 
(a) 
payments made for expenditures prior to the date of this Agreement, except that 
withdrawals, in an aggregate amount not exceeding $500,000, may be made in respect of 
Categories (1), (2), (3) and (4) set forth in the table in paragraph 1 of this Section on account of 
payments made for expenditures before that date but after November 15, 1999; and 
 
(b) 
payments made for expenditures under Category (1) set forth in the table in 
paragraph 1 of this Section, unless the Bank has received evidence, satisfactory to the Bank, that 
the Borrower shall have acquired all required land titles of the Sosua area or shall have entered 
into possession of said area in accordance with the Borrower’s legal framework, and 
authorizations to construct in said area shall have been issued by the appropriate authorities.  
 
4. 
The Bank may require withdrawals from the Loan Account to be made on the basis of 
statements of expenditure for expenditures for: (a) goods under contracts costing less than 
$50,000 equivalent each; (b) works under contracts costing less than $250,000 equivalent each; 
(c) consulting firm services under contracts costing less than $50,000 equivalent each; (d) 
individual consultants services under contracts costing less than $30,000 equivalent each; and (e) 
all Training and Workshops, under such terms and conditions as the Bank shall specify by notice 
to the Borrower.”
