                         Project Development Objectives
                         Project Development Objective (from Project Appraisal Document)

                         ATIP will contribute to improved access to judicial and legal services and in the accountable and transparent use of public financial resources. This will be
                         achieved by improving the skills and systems to deliver judicial/legal services and public financial management, and strengthening the capacity of oversight
                         and watchdog institutions (OWIs) to perform their role.

                         Implementation Status Overview
                         Following a restructuring of the project design to reflect capacity of the government to implement the project and project realities on the ground, implementation has picked up since
                         June 2010. Five out of seven key actions that were agreed in the last mission, mostly relating to legal and judicial reform have been achieved. Under the legal and judicial reform
                         component, the construction contract for the law school has been awarded. A consultant for construction supervision of 9 primary courts (originally 20 were envisaged, but rising

                         strengthening oversight and watchdog institutions component, results from two out of the first nine grantees funded by the Facility for Ethics, Accountability and Transparency(FEAT)
                         have been useful in improving ethics and accountability in the public service. One of the studies by the nursing council has contributed to improving ethics in the nursing profession
                         and another by the medical womens' association has helped in creating awareness on the magnitude of professional misconduct in the medical profession. The second round of
                         grantees has been approved and work is in progress. The third round of grantees has been approved in December 2010 and cleared by the Bank.
