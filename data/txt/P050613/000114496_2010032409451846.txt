SCHEDULE
2
Description of the Project
The objectives of the Project are: (a) to increase the coverage of preschool and 
primary school education;
(b)
to improve the quality and efficiency of preschool and
primary school education; and
(c)
to strengthen the institutional capacity of MECD.
The Project consists of the following parts, subject to such modifications
thereof as the Borrower and the Association may agree upon from time to time to
achieve such objectives:
Part A:
Expansion of the Coverage of Preschool and Primary School Education
1.
Construction of classrooms in existing or new schools for preschool education of 
children in rural areas. 
2.
Provision of scholarships to Eligible Primary Schools Students.
Part B:
Improvement of the Quality of Preschool and Primary School Education
1.
In-service training for preschool teachers. 
2.
Provision of educational materials to preschools. 
3.
In-service training for primary school teachers and directors. 
4.
Provision of textbooks, classrooms and school library, and educational materials 
for primary schools, including bilingual textbooks for the schools on the Atlantic
Coast. 
5.
Rehabilitation and/or construction of primary school infrastructure, including 
basic water and sanitation facilities and teachers’ housing for schools in remote 
areas. 
6.
Provision of Extracurricular School Activities Grants.
Part C:
Strengthening of MECD and of Primary Schools Management 
1.
Training of: 
(a)
directors in basic management skills; and 
(b)
school councils, technical and administrative staff of MECD at the 
central and local levels and other stakeholders, in the Borrower’s decentralization 
process.
Page  13
2.
Carrying out of:
(a)
an assessment to re-evaluate the needs, information gaps and concerns of 
different stakeholders affected by the community managed model of autonomous public
schools;
(b)
a communication campaign on such model; and
(c)
a beneficiary assessment of the impact of the Project. 
3.
Development, testing and pilot implementation of a system to measure academic 
performance among primary school students and provision of the training required
therefore.
4.
Development and installation of a monitoring and evaluation system for MECD, 
including the monitoring and evaluation of the Project. 
5.
Development and implementation of a system to supervise and monitor the 
academic, pedagogic and administrative performance of schools, teachers, and
supervisors. 
6.
Preparation of a medium term strategy for the preschool and primary education 
sector, including the specific investments and financing requirements thereof.
Part D:
Project Administration Provision of appropriate equipment for the PCU. 
***
The Project is expected to be completed by December
31,
2002.

Train teachers in the teaching of Spanish as a second language.

Carry out the rehabilitation and replacement of about 73 classrooms in primary 
schools in the Atlantic Coast Region.

Provide fifty percent of Extracurricular School Activities Grants to 
beneficiaries in the Atlantic Coast Region. 
7.
Provide materials for training of parents in the three native languages referred 
to in Part A.3
above.

Develop a culturally appropriate communication campaign on the school autonomy 
model targeted at the indigenous population of the Atlantic Coast Region
.

discuss issues related to the school autonomy model. 
10.
Develop culturally appropriate information on the training of parents program 
referred to in B.7 above, and the school autonomy model to target the more isolated 
indigenous populations of the Atlantic Coast Region.
