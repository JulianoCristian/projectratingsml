                                               1. INTRODUCTION

L1       Introduction

1.1.1  The Kamataka State Highways Improvement Project is being implemented in two phases.
       Phase I consists of 394 kms of Upgradation and 848 kms of Major Maintenance. Phase II
       consists of 548 kms of Upgradation and 429 kms of Major Maintenance.
1.1.2  A Resettlement Action Plan (RAP) was formulated to implement the R&R policy for Phase-I
       roads. The impacts identified during the socio-economic surveys in March to May 2000 are
       being mitigated under the RAP Phase L However, during the course of the project, some
       additional impacts were reported as a result of modification of alignments, geometrical
       improvements, safety considerations and grievance redressal measures. Therefore, this
       Supplementary RAP has been formulated to address the additional impacts in Phase-I roads.
1.1.3  This S-RAP consists of three chapters. Chapter 1. Introduction outlines the objectives of
       S-RAP and the details of the identified additional impacts. Chapter 2. Baseline Socio
       Economic Characteristics, provides an analysis of the socio-economic data collected through
       the surveys, to measure the magnitude of social impacts on project affected persons and
       families. The analysis identifies severely impacted categories and helps in estimating the
       extent of costs involved for mitigating the social impacts in addition to analysing the survey
       data. Chapter 3. Implementation Mechanisms outlines the implementation mechanisms for
       resettlement and rehabilitation. It takes into account requirement for rehabilitation centres,
       costs and budget implications and monitoring and evaluation of the resettlement components.


1.2      Objectives

         a.  To assist the additionally identified P AFs to improve their livelihood.
         b.  To present a supplementary action plan for the delivery of compensation and assistance
             to the additionally identified P AF s in accordance with the R & R polity adopted for the
             project.

             2.         BASELINE SOCIO-ECONOMIC CHARACTERISTICS
    2.1      The main findings of the socio-economic survey of Project Affected Families / Persons
             (PAFs/ PAPs) are presented in this chapter.


    2.2       Socio-economic survey


             The socio-economic survey was required in order to generate necessary additional data/input
             for preparing the S-RAP. The survey, alongwith the data available from the earlier survey
             conducted for RAP phase I, would provide a baseline measurement of potential impacts on
             affected families/persons that form the eligibility criteria for entitlement spelt out in the
             R & R Policy of the KSHIP.

    2.3      Methodology and Data Base


             A socio-economic survey of the additional project-affected population was conducted. This'
J            has provided a baseline against which mitigation measures have been worked out. For this
             purpose, comprehensive information related to the P AF s' assets, incomes and other relevant
             socio-economic information have been collected.

                           3.  IMPLEMENTATION MECHANISMS
 3.1      Introduction

          This chapter outlines resettlement site selection, Construction of Rehabilitation Centres,
          mitigation of other additional impacts, grievance redressal mechanism, implementation
          schedule, costs & budget and monitoring & evaluation of the resettlement components.


 3.2      Resettlement Site Selection

          The selection of resettlement sites was done based on available land and in consultations with
          the PAPs, their representatives~ local officials and landowners. Accordingly, 7 acres of land
          in survey no. 276B of Sindbnur village, Tq&Dist: Raichur has been selected for resettlement
          of Aragin.a.mara camp PAPs and 1 acre of land in survey no. 161 of Hothpet village, Taluk:
          Shahpur, Dist: Gulbarga has been selected. for resettlement of Bhimarayanagudi PAPs.


 3.3      Construction of Rehabilitation Centres (RCs)


 3.3.1  The construction of houses, internal roads, water supply & sanitation works and drainages
        "ill be undertaken by an agency involved in low-cost housing on a turnkey basis. The
        KPTCL will execute all electrification works. The Social Development and Resettlement Cell
        (SDRC) in the Project Implementation Unit will be responsible for the overall co-ordination
        of the work.
 3.3.2.  The Res are proposed to have dwelling units of 230 sft each with RCC roofing, bath and
         toilet thus having better amenities than those in prevailing economically weaker section
         housing schemes. Electrification, water supply & sanitation, roads and drainage facilities will
         be provided. A cormnunity hall, parks and open areas are proposed for ~mmon use. A school
         is not proposed as there are accessible schools near the proposed RCs. The estimated cost of
         the Araginamara camp RC is Rs. 72 lakhs and that of the BhimaraY8llahrudi RC is lUi. 18
         lakhs.

            and in Yaramaras and Askihal villages of Raichur District in contract paekage U5 will be

        c.  Land acquisition proceedings initiated to acquire 15acres 25guntas of additional lands
            identified for acquisition are in various stages of progress and will be completed.
