SECOND AGREEMENT AMENDING 
DEVELOPMENT CREDIT AGREEMENT 
AGREEMENT, dated November 25, 2003, between THE REPUBLIC OF 
UGANDA (the Borrower) and INTERNATIONAL DEVELOPMENT ASSOCIATION 
(the Association). 
WHEREAS (A) the Borrower and the Association have entered into a 
Development Credit Agreement dated December 22, 1999, (the Development Credit 
Agreement), for the purpose of financing the execution of Second Economic and 
Financial Management Project, described in Schedule 2 to the Development Credit 
Agreement (the Project); 
(B) 
the Borrower has requested the Association to provide additional 
assistance towards financing of the Project, by increasing the amount made available 
under the Development Credit Agreement by an amount in various currencies equivalent 
to ten million five hundred thousand Special Drawing Rights (SDR 10,500,000); and 
WHEREAS the Association has agreed on the basis, 
inter alia
,
of the foregoing, to 
provide such additional assistance to the Borrower upon the terms and conditions set forth 
in this Amending Agreement (as hereinafter defined); 
NOW THEREFORE the parties hereto hereby agree as follows:

ARTICLE II 
Effective Date; Termination 
Section 2.01. This Amending Agreement shall not become effective until 
evidence satisfactory to the Association shall have been furnished to the Association that 
the execution and delivery of this Amending Agreement on behalf of the Borrower has 
been duly authorized or ratified by all necessary governmental action. 
Section 2.02. As part of the evidence to be furnished pursuant to Section 2.01 of 
this Amending Agreement, there shall be furnished to the Association an opinion or 
opinions satisfactory to the Association showing, on behalf of the Borrower, that this 
Amending Agreement has been duly authorized or ratified by, and executed and 
delivered on behalf of the Borrower and is legally binding upon the Borrower in 
accordance with its terms. 
Section 2.03. This Amending Agreement shall come into force and effect on the 
date upon which the Association shall dispatch to the Borrower notice of its acceptance 
of the evidence required by Section 2.01 of this Amending Agreement. 
Section 2.04. If this Amending Agreement shall not come into force and effect by 
a
date ninety (90) days after the date of this Amending Agreement, this Amending 
Agreement and all obligations of the parties hereunder shall terminate, unless the 
Association establishes a later date for the purposes of this Section. If this Amending 
Agreement shall terminate under the provisions of this Section, the Development Credit 
Agreement shall continue in full force and effect, as if this Amending Agreement had not 
been executed.

SCHEDULE 6 
Performance Indicators 
1. 
Improved planning and budgeting of Public Expenditure both at national and 
local government as determined by better management of the budget management 
including budget discipline measured by: 
(a) 
maintaining supplementaries within 3% of Government funded 
budget; 
(b) 
reducing domestic arrears as measured by outstanding stock (in 
billions Uganda Shillings (Ushs)); and 
(c) 
Reduction in deviation between revenue forecasts and revenue 
collections. 
2. 
Improved financial management, accounting and auditing of Government 
expenditure measured by: 
(a) 
timeliness in the preparation of Central Government financial 
statements as measured by reduction in completion time for audit; 
(b) 
continuous improvement in compliance with the nine month 
statutory period within which audited financial statements must be 
submitted to Parliament; 
(c) 
online availability of financial information, through integrated 
financial management systems, for an increasing number of 
agencies; 
(d) 
continuous improvement in the percentage of Local Governments 
producing financial statements using IFMS; 
(e) 
continuous improvement in the number of accountants with 
professional accounting qualifications in: 
i. 
Central Government (including Internal Auditors), and 
ii.    Local Government; 
(f) 
number of Auditors with professional accounting qualifications in 
the OAG; 
Page  11
-
10
-
(g) 
continuous improvement in the percentage of Accounting Officers 
with Audit Committees after the new Finance Bill comes into 
effect; and 
(h) 
number of Internal Auditors with professional accounting 
qualifications in the Central Government. 
3. 
Improved availability  of statistical data and information to monitor development 
performance, public service delivery and impact of public expenditure on poverty as 
determined by: 
(a) 
more timely publicity of statistical survey results measured by 
reducing period between end of work to dissemination; and 
(b) 
increased access to statistical data by users measured by increasing 
numbers of channels for statistical dissemination. 
4. 
Improved cost effectiveness of capacity building efforts through sustained use of 
distance learning center programs as determined by: 
(a) utilization 
rate; 
and 
(b) 
generation of Revenue from Operation of the Center as a percentage 
of Operating Costs.
