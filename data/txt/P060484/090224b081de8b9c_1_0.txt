                         Implementation Status Overview
                         Project continues to make progress, and its now in its final stage of commitment of all the loan funds.

                         To date (July 2013) all expected subprojects, 22 in total, have been approved and their contracts awarded covering 100 percent of the loan proceeds under Component 1 (Municipal
                         Infrastructure, accounting for 94 percent of total Project amount).

                                                                                                                                                                                                         Page 1 of 7
                             The World Bank                                                                                                                                      Report No: ISR11543




                         Subproject implementation progress is as follows: Finished contracts (6 subprojects) have benefited directly and indirectly about 245,500 people of which 53,000 are direct
                         beneficiaries. These contracts account for a total of 21 percent of the loan amount. Finished subprojects cover an improvement of a wastewaster treatment plant, construction of
                         sewerage collectors, urban drainage and urban and rural roads in 5 provinces. Ongoing contracts (9 subprojects) account for about 42 percent of the loan amount Four of these
Public Disclosure Copy




                         contracts have reached more than 50% physical progress and it is estimated that these contracts will benefit directly 141,000 people and about 947,000 indirectly. Ongoing contracts
                         include works in urban and rural roads and improvements in water and sewerage services.

                         There are 7 new subprojects, of which 3 are still pending contract signing which is expected to happen before the end of August 2013. These 7 subprojects are expected to benefit an
                         additional 172,000 people directly and about 145,000 indirectly. About 64 percent of the loan resources have been disbursed thus far.
