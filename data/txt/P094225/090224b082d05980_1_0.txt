Project Development Objectives

 Project Development Objective (from Project Appraisal Document)
 The objective of the Project is to support the Government's policy to raise the living standards of the poor and vulnerable groups through: (i)
 Improving the quality, access, and coverage of community infrastructure and services in poor communities, and for the most vulnerable groups; and
 (ii) Promoting complementary institutional capacity building at the community and municipal level so as to improve the quality and sustainability of
 service delivery and municipal investments, increase accountability, and enhance greaterstakeholder empowerment at the local level.


 Has the Project Development Objective been changed since Board Approval of the Project Objective?
 Yes

 Board Approved Revised Project Development Objective (If project is formally restructured)PHRPDODEL
 The objective of the Project is to support the Government's policy to raise the living standards of the poor and vulnerable groups through: (i)
 improving the quality and access, and increasing the coverage of community infrastructure and services in poor communities, and for the most
 vulnerable groups in response to critical needs; (ii) promoting complementary institutional capacity building at the community and municipal level so
 as to improve the quality and sustainability of community investments and service delivery, increase accountability, and enhance greater
 stakeholder empowerment at the local level; and (iii) creation of employment associatedwith the provision of community infrastructure and services.

Implementation Status and Key Decisions

 The project closed on 31 March 2015, having achieved its development objectives, with targets met or exceeded for all the outcome indicators.
 The project improved the quality and access and increased the coverage of community infrastructure and services in poor communities. This was
 measured through the following indicators:
 1.          Number of community infrastructure and services micro-projects providing higher quality and better access in poor and vulnerable
             communities targeted under the project - total 320 (939 under ASIF I, II, III) (target 283 from ASIF III, or 902 from ASIF I, II, III);
 2.          Number of integrated social service centers strengthened - total 3 (target 3).
 The microprojects were selected through participatory approaches. The project also promoted complementary institutional capacity building, which
 empowered beneficiary communities and enhanced the accountability of municipal authorities. This was measured through the following indicators:
 3.          Number of officials at the municipal level involved in micro-projects trained in financial management - total 716 (2516 under ASIF I, II, III)
             (target 715 under ASIF III, or 2515 under ASIF I, II, III);
 4.          Number of beneficiaries trained to plan and manage their investments on a sustainable basis - total 185 (target 158).
 The project created employment and income associated with the provision of community infrastructure and services. This was measured through the
 following indicators:
 5.          Number of jobs created from micro-projects - total 661,743 days of employment, generating US$11.1 million (targets 535,000 days and
             US$8.8 million) ;
 6.          Number of jobs created from local manufacturing of new school furniture - total 50,520 days of employment, generating US$555,718 (target
             40,600 days and US$443,000).
 In total, the project reached 1,681,562 beneficiaries. The sustainability of the investments was tested through a study, which provided recommendations
 to ASIF on improving upkeep of investments and these recommendations were taken on board.
