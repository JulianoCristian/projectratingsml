Amendment to the Development Credit Agreement
Excellency,
We refer to the above-mentioned Development Credit Agreement (Agreement) between 
the Republic of Haiti (the Borrower) and the International Development Association 
(the Association), dated April 14, 1995, as amended.  We are pleased to advise you 
that, pursuant to the discussions between staff of the Borrower and the Association, 
and the exchange of letters between the borrower and the Association, the Association 
agrees to further amend the Agreement as follows:
(a)
Delete the Section 3.09(c) of the Credit Agreement.  
(b)
Schedule 1 to the Agreement is hereby amended to read as set forth in the 
Annex to this Amendment Letter.
Kindly confirm your agreement, on behalf of the Borrower, with the terms of the 
Letter Amendment and its Annex by signing and dating the counterpart of this Letter in 
the spaces provided below. Upon receipt by the Association of a fully countersigned 
counterpart of this Letter Amendment, the above amendment will be deemed effective as 
of the date of  January 30, 1998.
Very truly yours,

2.
For the purpose of this schedule:
(a)
The term “training” means expenditures incurred by the Borrower in 
connection with the carrying of training activities under the Project, including 
travel cost, rental of facilities and equipment, and training.
(b)
The term “Himo Unit” (Operational Expenditures of the Ministry’s unit 
responsible for labor intensive works) means all operational expenditures incurred by 
such unit for the purpose of Project execution, including: (i) staff salaries; (ii) 
office suppliers; (iii) travel expenses (including per diems); and (iv) maintenance of 
office equipment and vehicles financed out of the proceed of the Credit.
3.
Payments on categories 1(e) and 6(b) will be made only on direct payments by 
IDA.
4.
All payment requests on category 1(e) above shall be countersigned by the 
consulting firm supervising the works.  At all time this consulting firm and its 
project supervision staff shall be acceptable to IDA.
