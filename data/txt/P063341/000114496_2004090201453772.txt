LOAN AGREEMENT 
AGREEMENT, dated June 30, 2000, between the FEDERATIVE REPUBLIC OF BRAZIL (the 
Borrower) and the INTERNATIONAL BANK FOR RECONSTRUCTION AND DEVELOPMENT (the Bank).
WHEREAS (A) the Bank has received a letter, dated March 7, 2000, from the 
Borrower: (i) describing its macroeconomic framework and a program (the Program) of 
actions, objectives and policies designed to achieve adjustment of the fiscal 
situation of, and to create mechanisms for the reform of the public administration of, 
the Borrower and the Borrower's states (the States), federal district (the Federal 
District) and municipalities (the Municipalities), which Program consists of actions 
that have already been taken as described in Schedule 3 to this Agreement (the First 
Phase of the Program), and actions and policies that the Borrower intends to take and 
adopt in the near future; (ii) informing the Bank of expected impacts of the Program 
with regard to the number of the Borrower's public servants during the period 
2000-2001, the Borrower's policies and projections with regard to the Borrower's 
personnel expenditures, the Borrower's support for improvements in the public 
administration of the States and Municipalities, and the aggregate expected annual 
results for the States, during the period 2000-2001, with regard to primary surplus, 
debt, expenditures with employees and retirees, and privatization; (iii) declaring the 
Borrower’s commitment to the objectives of the Program; and (iv) requesting assistance 
from the Bank in support of the Program during the execution thereof; and (B)
on the 
basis, inter alia, of the foregoing, the Bank has decided in support of the First 
Phase of the Program to provide such assistance to the Borrower by making the loan 
provided for in Article II of this Agreement (the Loan) as hereinafter provided;
NOW THEREFORE the parties hereto hereby agree as follows:

ARTICLE II
The Loan 
Section 2.01.  The Bank agrees to lend to the Borrower, on the terms and 
conditions set forth or referred to in this Agreement, an amount equal to five hundred 
five million sixty thousand Dollars ($505,060,000). 
Section 2.02.  (a) Subject to the provisions of paragraphs (b) and (c) of this 
Section, the Borrower shall be entitled to withdraw the amount of five hundred million 
nine thousand four hundred Dollars ($500,009,400) from the Loan Account in support of 
the Program. 
Page  3
(b)
The Borrower shall open, prior to furnishing to the Bank the first 
request for withdrawal from the Loan Account, and thereafter maintain in the 
Borrower's central bank a deposit account in Dollars on terms and conditions 
satisfactory to the Bank.  All withdrawals of the amount referred to in paragraph (a) 
above shall be deposited by the Bank into the Deposit Account. 
(c)
The Borrower undertakes that the proceeds of the Loan shall not be used 
to finance expenditures excluded pursuant to the provisions of Schedule 1 to this 
Agreement.  If the Bank shall have determined at any time that any proceeds of the 
Loan shall have been used to make a payment for an expenditure so excluded, the 
Borrower shall, promptly upon notice from the Bank, (i) deposit into the Deposit 
Account an amount equal to the amount of said payment, or (ii) if the Bank shall so 
request, refund such amount to the Bank. Amounts refunded to the Bank upon such 
request shall be credited to the Loan Account for cancellation.
Section 2.03.  The Closing Date shall be December 31, 2000 or such later date as 
the Bank shall establish.  The Bank shall promptly notify the Borrower of such later 
date. 
Section 2.04.  The Borrower shall pay to the Bank a front-end fee in an amount 
equivalent to one per cent (1%) of the amount of the Loan. On or promptly after the 
Effective Date, the Bank shall, on behalf of the Borrower, withdraw from the Loan 
Account and pay to itself the amount of said fee. 
Section 2.05.  The Borrower shall pay to the Bank a commitment charge at the 
rate of three-fourths of one percent (3/4 of 1%) per annum on the principal amount of 
the Loan not withdrawn from time to time.  
Section 2.06.  (a) The Borrower shall pay interest on the principal amount of 
the Loan withdrawn and outstanding from time to time, at a rate for each Interest 
Period equal to LIBOR Base Rate plus 4 (four) percentage points.
(b)
For the purposes of this Section: 
(i)
“Interest Period” means the initial period from and including the 
date of this 
Agreement to, but excluding, the first Interest Payment Date 
occurring 
thereafter, and after the initial period, each period 
from and including an 
Interest Payment Date to, but excluding the 
next following Interest Payment 
Date. 
(ii)
“Interest Payment Date” means any date specified in Section 2.07 
of this 
Agreement. 
(iii)
“LIBOR Base Rate” means, for each Interest Period, the London 
interbank 
offered rate for six-month deposits in Dollars for 
value the first day of such 
Interest Period (or, in the case of the 
initial Interest Period, for value the 
Interest Payment Date occurring 
on or next preceding the first day of such 
Interest Period), as 
reasonably determined by the Bank and expressed as a 
percentage 
per annum. 
(c)
The Bank shall notify the Borrower of the LIBOR Base Rate for each 
Interest Period, promptly upon the determination thereof. 
(d)
Whenever, in light of changes in market practice affecting the 
determination of the interest rates referred to in this Section 2.06, the Bank 
determines that it is in the interest of its borrowers as a whole and of the Bank to 
apply a basis for determining the interest rates applicable to the Loan other than as 
provided in said Section, the Bank may modify the basis for determining the interest 
rates applicable to the Loan upon not less than six (6) months’ notice to the Borrower 
of the new basis. The basis shall become effective on the expiry of the notice period 
unless the Borrower notifies the Bank during said period of its objection thereto, in 
which case said modification shall not apply to the Loan.
Section 2.07.  Interest and other charges shall be payable semiannually in 
arrears on January 15 and July 15 in each year.

ARTICLE III
Particular Covenants
Section 3.01.  The Borrower shall exchange views with the Bank on any proposed 
action to be taken after the disbursement of the Loan which would have the effect of 
materially reversing the objectives of the First Phase of the Program, or any action 
specified in Schedule 3 to this Agreement.
Section 3.02.  Upon the Bank’s request, the Borrower shall: 
(a)
have the Deposit Account audited in accordance with appropriate auditing 
principles consistently applied, by independent auditors acceptable to the Bank; 
(b)
furnish to the Bank as soon as available, but in any case not later than 
four (4) months after the date of the Bank’s request for such audit, a certified copy 
of the report of such audit by said auditors, of such scope and in such detail as the 
Bank shall have reasonably requested; and 
(c)
furnish to the Bank such other information concerning the Deposit Account 
and the audit thereof as the Bank shall have reasonably requested.
ARTICLE IV
Effective Date; Termination
Section 4.01.  The following event is specified as an additional condition to 
the effectiveness of the Loan Agreement within the meaning of Section 12.01 (c) of the 
General Conditions, namely, that the macroeconomic policy framework of the Borrower is 
consistent with the objectives of the Program, as measured on the basis of indicators 
agreed between the Borrower and the Bank.
Section 4.02.  The following is specified as an additional matter, within the 
meaning of Section 12.02 (c) of the General Conditions, to be included in the opinion 
or opinions to be furnished to the Bank, namely, that this Agreement has been validly 
registered with the Borrower's central bank.
Section 4.03.  The date August 30, 2000 is hereby specified for the purposes of 
Section 12.04 of the General Conditions.

SCHEDULE 3 
First Phase of the Program 
1.
Entry into force of the Borrower's Law No. 9496, of September 11, 1997, which 
establishes criteria for the consolidation, assumption and refinancing of the debt of 
the States to the Borrower. 
2.
Signing of agreements between the Borrower and 25 States for the consolidation, 
assumption and refinancing of such States' debts to the Borrower (the Debt 
Agreements). 
3.
Issuance of Resolution No. 78, dated July 1, 1998, as amended by Resolution No. 
22, dated June 25, 1999, of the Borrower's Senate, which establishes criteria and 
limitations for the incurrence of debt by the States, the Municipalities and the 
Federal District. 
4.
Entry into force of the Borrower's Law No. 9801, of June 14, 1999, which 
establishes general rules for dismissal of the tenured public servants of the 
Borrower, the States, the Municipalities and the Federal District, when their 
respective expenditures for active and retired public servants exceed the limits 
established under the Borrower's Complementary Law (Lei Complementar) No. 96, of May 
31, 1999.
5.
Entry into force of the Borrower's Complementary Law No. 96, of May 31, 1999, 
which establishes limits for expenditures for employees of the Borrower, the States, 
the Municipalities and the Federal District, based on the respective entity's current 
net revenues, as defined in that Law. 
6.
Entry into force of the Borrower's Decree (Decreto) No. 3151, of August 23, 
1999, which regulates salary reductions of the Borrower's tenured public servants who 
are put under administrative leave pursuant to Article 41, paragraph 3, of the 
Borrower's Constitution. 
7.
Issuance by the Borrower's President of Provisional Measure (Medida Provisória) 
No. 1977-16, of June 1, 2000, which authorizes the Borrower to deduct, until June 30, 
2001, from the monthly payment that any State has to make to the Borrower under the 
Debt Agreement, the amount deposited by such State with the Borrower’s Treasury for 
purposes of making severance payments to such State's employees, such amount not to 
exceed 4% of such State’s monthly net actual revenue (receita liquida real), as 
defined in the Debt Agreement.
8.
Issuance of Administrative Order (Portaria) No. 88, of March 24, 2000, by the 
Borrower's Minister of Finance, which regulates the implementation of the mechanisms 
for effecting the severance payments referred to in paragraph 7 above. 
9.
Entry into force of the Borrower's Law No. 9962, of February 22, 2000, which 
enables the public sector, at the federal, state, municipal and federal district 
level, to hire new employees under the private sector labor regime.
