LOAN AGREEMENT
AGREEMENT, dated December 17, 1997, between REPUBLIC OF PERU (the Borrower) and 
INTERNATIONAL BANK FOR RECONSTRUCTION AND DEVELOPMENT (the Bank).
WHEREAS (A) the Borrower, having satisfied itself as to the feasibility and 
priority of the Project described in Schedule 2 to this Agreement, has requested the 
Bank to assist in the financing of the Project;
(B)
Parts B.1, B.2 and C.3 of the Project will be carried out by, 
respectively, Consejo Nacional de la Magistratura (CNM), Academia Nacional de la 
Magistratura (AM) and Defensoría del Pueblo (DP) with the Borrower’s assistance and, 
as part of such assistance, the Borrower will make available to CNM, AM and DP a 
portion of  the proceeds of the Loan as provided in this Agreement; and
(C)
the Bank has received from the President of  the Comisión Ejecutiva of 
the Poder Judicial, the Director General of the AM, the President of the CNM and the 
Defensor del Pueblo a statement, dated August 22, 1997, describing their objectives, 
policies and actions concerning the reform of the judicial sector (the Program) and 
declaring their commitment to such objectives, policies and actions; and
WHEREAS the Bank has agreed, on the basis, inter alia, of the foregoing, to 
extend the Loan to the Borrower upon the terms and conditions set forth in this 
Agreement and in the Project Agreements (as hereinafter defined) of even date 
herewith;
NOW THEREFORE the parties hereto hereby agree as follows:

ARTICLE II
The Loan 
Section 2.01. The Bank agrees to lend to the Borrower, on the terms and 
conditions set forth or referred to in the Loan Agreement, an amount equal to twenty 
two million five hundred thousand dollars ($22,500,000).
Section 2.02. The amount of the Loan may be withdrawn from the Loan Account in 
accordance with the provisions of Schedule 1 to this Agreement for expenditures made 
(or, if the Bank shall so agree, to be made) in respect of the reasonable cost of 
goods and services required for the Project described in Schedule 2 to this Agreement 
and to be financed out of the proceeds of the Loan.
Section 2.03. The Closing Date shall be June 30, 2003 or such later date as the 
Bank shall establish. The Bank shall promptly notify the Borrower of such later date.
Section 2.04. The Borrower shall pay to the Bank a commitment charge at the rate 
of three-fourths of one percent (3/4 of 1%) per annum on the principal amount of the 
Loan not withdrawn from time to time.
Section 2.05. (a) The Borrower shall pay interest on the principal amount of the 
Loan withdrawn and outstanding from time to time, at a rate for each Interest Period 
equal to LIBOR Base Rate plus LIBOR Total Spread.
(b)
For the purposes of this Section:
(i)
“Interest Period” means the initial period from and 
including the date of this Agreement to, but excluding, the 
first Interest Payment Date occurring thereafter, and after 
the 
initial period, each period from and including an 
Interest Payment 
Date to, but excluding the next following 
Interest Payment Date.
(ii)
“Interest Payment Date” means any date specified in Section 
2.06 of this Agreement.
(iii)
“LIBOR Base Rate” means, for each Interest Period, the 
London interbank offered rate for six-month deposits in 
dollars for value the first day of such Interest Period 
(or, in the 
case of the initial Interest Period, for value 
the Interest Payment 
Date occurring on or next preceding 
the first day of such Interest 
Period), as reasonably 
determined by the Bank and expressed as a 
percentage per 
annum.
(iv)
“LIBOR Total Spread” means, for each Interest Period: (A) 
one half of one percent (1/2 of 1%); (B) minus (or plus) 
the 
weighted average margin, for such Interest Period, 
below (or above) the 
London interbank offered rates, or 
other reference rates, for 
six-month deposits, in respect 
of the Bank’s outstanding borrowings or 
portions thereof 
allocated by the Bank to fund single currency loans 
or 
portions thereof made by it that include the Loan; as 
reasonably determined by the Bank and expressed as a 
percentage per annum.
(c)
The Bank shall notify the Borrower of LIBOR Base Rate and LIBOR Total 
Spread for each Interest Period, promptly upon the determination thereof.
(d)
Whenever, in light of changes in market practice affecting the 
determination of the interest rates referred to in this Section 2.05, the Bank 
determines that it is in the interest of its borrowers as a whole and of the Bank to 
apply a basis for determining the interest rates applicable to the Loan other than as 
provided in said Section, the Bank may modify the basis for determining the interest 
rates applicable to the Loan upon not less than six (6) months’ notice to the Borrower 
Page  6
of the new basis. The basis shall become effective on the expiry of the notice period 
unless the Borrower notifies the Bank during said period of its objection thereto, in 
which case said modification shall not apply to the Loan.
Section 2.06. Interest and other charges shall be payable on February 15 and 
August 15 in each year.
Section 2.07. The Borrower shall repay the principal amount of the Loan in 
accordance with the amortization schedule set forth in Schedule 3 to this Agreement.

(a)

(b)

SCHEDULE 2
Description of the Project
The overall objective of the Project is to assist the Borrower in improving the 
performance of its justice system by enhancing, within the framework and terms of the 
Program, its access, quality, independence, efficiency and integrity. Specific 
objectives are to: (a) modernize the administrative structure and operation of the 
Judiciary; (b) improve the overall performance of Selected Judges in the Selected 
Judicial Districts; (c) strengthen the CNM and consolidate and improve its merit-based 
system of appointment, advancement and removal of magistrates; (d) strengthen the AM 
and enhance the professional competence of civil (including judges with mixed subject 
matter jurisdiction, jueces mixtos), labor and tax judges in the Selected Judicial 
Districts; (e) strengthen OCMA’s disciplinary system to enhance accountability and 
integrity of judges; (f) develop and strengthen alternative dispute resolution 
methods; (g) strengthen the institutional development of DP; and (h) strengthen the 
capacity of civil society t
o analyze, monitor and demand judicial performance. 
The Project consists of the following parts, subject to such modifications 
thereof as the Borrower and the Bank may agree upon from time to time to achieve such 
objectives:
Page  12
Part A:
Administration of Justice
1.
A modernization program of the administrative apparatus of the Judiciary to 
strengthen the management capabilities of the administrative offices, enhance 
timeliness and quality of judicial services, increase and improve the allocation of 
resources to the courts, increase public recognition of the activities of the 
Judiciary and obtain public feedback of the modernization program, such program to 
consist of:
(a)
Strengthening of the administrative capabilities of the District 
Administration Office through: (i) training of administrative personnel of the 
District Administration Offices in each of the Judicial Districts to upgrade their 
skills in areas such as planning and management, operational procedures and statistics 
and information; (ii) studies to evaluate the administrative system; (iii) the design 
and implementation of management information systems for the District Administration 
Offices in the areas of human resource, finance and procurement; (iv) organization of 
workshops for administrative personnel in the District Administration Offices and 
court personnel to focus on results achieved in the administrative modernization 
process and necessary improvement measures; and (v) provision and utilization of 
informatic equipment.
(b)
Strengthening of financial management in the Judiciary through: (i) 
studies on alternative measures to strengthen the economic autonomy of the Judiciary 
such as novel forms of resource generation and improvement of judicial fee systems; 
(ii) design and implementation of a preferential fee structure and schemes for 
low-income groups; and (iii) training and technical assistance to improve the 
capabilities of the administrative personnel in the General Administration Office in 
the design and implementation of program-based budgets.
(c)
Improving the institutional image of the Judiciary through: (i) 
dissemination of the administrative reforms through public campaigns; and (ii) 
evaluation studies with concerned parties including opinion surveys of litigants, 
attorneys, judges, court personnel and general public.
(d)
Modernizing the court administration through the provision and 
utilization of informatic equipment for Selected Judges in Judicial Districts other 
than the Selected Judicial Districts.
2.
A pilot program to improve court performance in the Selected Judicial Districts, 
such program to consist of:
(a)
Improving the quality and predictability of judicial decision-making 
through: (i) the organization of  forums (plenarios) with civil (including mixed 
subject matter jurisdiction, jueces mixtos) labor and tax judges for purposes of 
establishing uniformity and consistency in case law; (ii) design and implementation of 
innovative incentive schemes for the above judges such as research competitions, 
publication of journals and annual reports and study tours; and (iii) enhancement of 
libraries in the courts.
(b)
Improving the efficiency and effectiveness of court procedure and 
organization through: (i) the implementation of the New Court Organization Model 
including the training of judges and auxiliary personnel on case management and 
administration, information skills and performance evaluation and court automation; 
(ii) workshops with Selected Judges to focus on the evaluation of the implementation 
of the new code on civil procedure; (iii) studies on court performance indicators, 
including development and measurement of quality indicators for judicial performance; 
and (iv) workshops with, and surveys of, representatives of civil society to evaluate 
the progress of the New Court Organization Model and the implementation of the new 
code on civil procedure.
(c)
Improving judicial information and statistics through: (i) the design and 
implementation of an automated caseflow management system and an integrated judicial 
information system; (ii) a diagnostic study of the existing judicial statistics system 
and formulation and implementation of improvement plans for the provision of timely 
and reliable data; (iii) design and implementation of a multiple data base for 
legislation and cases; (iv) design and implementation of a modern archive system; and 
(v) court automation.
Page  13
(d)
Improving judicial facilities through the remodeling of the central court 
buildings in each Selected Judicial District on the basis of the Model Courtroom and 
to adapt to the needs of the judicial and administrative modernization efforts. 
Part B:
Judicial Selection, Evaluation and Training
1.
A program to assist the CNM in restoring and maintaining judicial tenure and 
consolidating a depolitized, transparent and merit-based system of selection and 
appointment, ratification and removal of magistrates through: (i) the development and 
upgrading of its regulations and systems for the selection and appointment, 
ratification and removal of magistrates; (ii) the strengthening of its strategic 
planning and management capacity through diagnostic studies and development of 
organizational visions and plans; (iii) training of CNM magistrates on judicial 
selection and evaluation, management and informatic skills; (iv) studies on the 
profile of judges, corrupt practices in the judiciary and the profile of the CNM 
staff; and (v) the development of data bases to support the core functions of CNM.
2.
A program to assist AM in its training functions through: (a) the development 
and implementation of comprehensive and integrated annual training programs for civil 
(including mixed subject matter jurisdiction, jueces mixtos), tax and labor judges in 
the Selected Judicial Districts to upgrade their judicial skills and aptitudes, 
strengthen their knowledge in selected economic law matters, reinforce their 
independent role and ethical standards and instill a culture of service; (b) the 
design and implementation of a system to evaluate the effectiveness and impact of its 
training programs; and (c) institutional strengthening of the AM through the provision 
of technical assistance for designing training courses for candidates to judgeships, 
study tours for AM staff and production and publication of journals.
3.
A program to strengthen the disciplinary system of the OCMA through: (a) a 
diagnostic study of its organization and regulations; (b) a study to evaluate the 
legal framework for a monitoring and control system; (c) training of OCMA staff on the 
methodology for monitoring and control; (d) dissemination of OCMA’s activities; and 
(e) informatic and equipment support for OCMA disciplinary commissions in all Judicial 
Districts.
Part C:
Access to Justice
1.
Improvement of the justice of peace through: (a) the design and implementation 
of annual training programs, including development of training modules and training of 
trainers for jueces de paz popularly elected under the new constitutional system and 
selected community authorities in the Selected Judicial Districts and to primarily 
focus on mediation and conciliation techniques; (b) the design and implementation of a 
system to evaluate the above training programs; (c) initial baseline and follow-up 
surveys on the performance of jueces de paz in the Selected Judicial Districts and 
workshops to focus on the results of the surveys; and (d) analysis of legislation and 
regulations governing the justice of peace system and workshops to focus on proposals 
for improvement and to forge consensus on legal and regulatory changes. 
2.
Development and strengthening of alternative dispute resolution methods and 
strengthening of the capacity of civil society to analyze, monitor and demand judicial 
performance through the execution of Access Subprojects financed under the Access 
Fund.
3.
Strengthening of the DP through: (a) the upgrading of its organizational 
structure and procedures including workshops, studies, training and study tours; (b) 
diagnostic studies and workshops on indigenous communities, environmental protection 
and citizen rights in a market economy; (c) public campaign and dissemination of DP 
activities; and (d) logistic and informatic support for the decentralization of DP 
offices.

The Project is expected to be completed by December 31, 2002.

SCHEDULE 4
Procurement and Consultants' Services
Section I.
Procurement of Good and Works
Part A:
General
Goods shall be procured in accordance with the provisions of Section I of the 
“Guidelines for Procurement under IBRD Loans and IDA Credits” published by the Bank in 
January 1995 and revised in January and August 1996 (the Guidelines) and the following 
provisions of this Section, as applicable.
Part B:
International Competitive Bidding
1.
Except as otherwise provided in Part C of this Section, goods shall be procured 
under contracts awarded in accordance with the provisions of Section II of the 
Guidelines and paragraph 5 of Appendix 1 thereto.
2.
The following provisions shall apply to goods to be procured under contracts 
awarded in accordance with the provisions of paragraph 1 of this Part B.
(a)
Grouping of Contracts
To the extent practicable, contracts for goods shall be grouped in bid packages 
estimated to cost $250,000 equivalent or more each.
Page  15
(b)
Preference for Domestically-manufactured Goods
 The provisions of paragraphs 2.54 and 2.55 of the Guidelines and Appendix 2 
thereto shall apply to goods manufactured in the territory of the Borrower.
(c)
Notification and Advertising
The invitation to prequalify or bid for each contract estimated to cost 
$10,000,000 equivalent or more shall be advertised in accordance with the procedures 
applicable to large contracts under paragraph 2.8 of the Guidelines. 
Part C:
Other Procurement Procedures
1.
Limited International Bidding
Goods estimated to cost less than $250,000 equivalent but more than $50,000 
equivalent per contract, up to an aggregate amount not to exceed $700,000 equivalent, 
may be procured under contracts awarded in accordance with the provisions of paragraph 
3.2 of the Guidelines.
2.
International Shopping
Goods estimated to cost less than $50,000 equivalent per contract, up to an 
aggregate amount not to exceed $100,000 equivalent, may be procured under contracts 
awarded on the basis of international shopping procedures in accordance with the 
provisions of paragraphs 3.5 and 3.6 of the Guidelines.
3.
National Shopping
Goods estimated to cost less than $25,000 equivalent per contract, up to an 
aggregate amount not to exceed $100,000 equivalent, may be procured under contracts 
awarded on the basis of national shopping procedures in accordance with the provisions 
of paragraphs 3.5 and 3.6 of the Guidelines.
4.
Access Fund Procedures
Goods and civil works procured under an Access Subproject, shall be procured in 
accordance with the procedures set forth in the Access Fund Operating Rules.
Part D:
Review by the Bank of Procurement Decisions
1.
Procurement Planning
Prior to the issuance of any invitations to bid for contracts, the proposed 
procurement plan for the Project shall be furnished to the Bank for its review and 
approval, in accordance with the provisions of paragraph 1 of Appendix 1 to the 
Guidelines. Procurement of all goods shall be undertaken in accordance with such 
procurement plan as shall have been approved by the Bank, and with the provisions of 
said paragraph 1.
2.
Prior Review
With respect to each contract procured under international competitive bidding 
or limited international bidding, the procedures set forth in paragraphs 2 and 3 of 
Appendix 1 to the Guidelines shall apply.
3.
Post Review
With respect to each contract not governed by paragraph 2 of this Part, the 
procedures set forth in paragraph 4 of Appendix 1 to the Guidelines shall apply.
