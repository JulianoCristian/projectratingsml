                         Implementation Status Overview
                         The GEF funded ICEMA project assists the Government of Namibia through the Ministry of Environment and Tourism (MET) in collaboration with other Community-Based Natural
                         Resource Management (CBNRM) stakeholders in addressing rural development in the communal lands of Namibia. It has been instrumental to support an evolution from a wildlife-

                                                                                                                                                                                                            Page 1 of 9

                         based to a more integrated CBNRM approach within the existing policy and legal framework. A review of the project's outcome and intermediate outcome indicators during the last
                         month of implementation indicate significant achievements with all outcomes and results nearly achieved. For example, the area under improved community-based management has

                         increased from 0km2 to 38,595km2, all 16 targeted conservancies meet the minimal criteria for effectively managing and deploying efficiently and sustainably their natural, human,
                         financial and other resources according to the objectives of their conservancy plans. A final external site-level assessment of the 16 target sites before project closure (March 30,
                         2011) has been discussed with the Government and will allow to verify and up-date available output and impact information. A follow-up support through the Millennium Challenge
                         Account (MCA) Namibia program has been designed based on lessons learned from the project and includes 12 out of the 16 project sites.
