                         Project Development Objectives
                         Project Development Objective (from Project Appraisal Document)

                         Project development objective is to help conflict-affected communities in the North East Province and the adjoining areas to restore livelihoods, enhance agricultural
                         and other production and incomes, and build their capacity for sustainable social and economic reintegration. The PDO will be achieved by: (i)Creating, restoring, or
                         improving essential village level social and economic infrastructure and facilities; (ii) improving productivity, sustainability and competitiveness of agriculture; (iii)
                         building community skills and capacity and decentralizing powers for decision making, control of resources and implementation of programs at community level; and
                         (iv) implementing targeted livelihood support programs for the most vulnerable people. Project Components are: Component 1: Village rehabilitation and development
                         Component 2: Essential rehabilitation and improvements to selected major irrigation schemes Component 3: Farmer Organization capacity building, empowerment and
                         agricultural support services strengthening Component 4: Capacity building, project implementation support and project oversight

                         Implementation Status Overview
                         This ISR has been prepared based on the findings during the annual (2011) progress review in January 2012, desk review of project documents in February 2012, and short field
                         missions during March/April 2012. All four project components have been rated as Satisfactory. The outputs of these four components are well in line with work program targets. The
                         outcomes have collectively contributed to the achievement of the PDO. The total cumulative disbursement as of March 31, 2012 is 87% in original Credit; 98% in first Emergency
                         Additional Financing; and 15% in the Second & Third Additional Financing. The actual total project expenditure up to March 31, 2011 stands at 79%, 83%, 50% and 64% for
                         Components A, B, C and D respectively, compared to cumulative financial targets of the agreed work plan. The expenditure under flood rehabilitation stands at 5%. The status of
                         fulfilling the actions agreed at the previous missions is Satisfactory. The procurement administration, legal covenants, and social/environmental safeguards are rated Satisfactory,
                         while the financial management is Moderately Satisfactory. In view of the above significant achievements, the project implementation progress is rated Satisfactory.

                         Key Achievements:
                         •         The project has reached 1040 villages and assisted just over one million beneficiaries through 25,755 small groups, since inception.
                         •         56,867 families have started new income generation activities or increase the scale of existing activity with low interest loans, from the village revolving fund provided by the
                         project. Rebuilding business and investment in post war environment is taking shape.
                         •         1,865 community infrastructure and social services subprojects have been completed and maintained by the communities to improve their quality of life.
                         •         1568 youths including 120 ex-combatants are employed after receiving skills development training provided through community skill development fund.
                         •         Number of participatory and transparent CBOs (FOs, RDSs and WRDSs) and VDOs of the poor revived/formed and satisfactorily engaged in subproject implementation and
                         O&M at village level is 1,506 (target 600).
                         •         The project has increased the stock of social capital, through its institution- building interventions. With capacity building and CRPs, the VDOs are actively participating in
                         project management, monitoring and evaluation. 688 women are holding managerial positions in CBOs.
                         •         The consultative planning and participatory implementation strategy, which are assisted by the project, are leading to synergizing the community projects within their village
                         development plans and budgets.
                         •         The project, through its effective livelihood activities, has been able to transform landless labourers to farmers with stakeholding on land.
                         •         Increase of farm area due to increased supply of water resulting from irrigation rehabilitation has reached 44,658 ha.
                         •         The project has initiated 19 cluster level subprojects, of which 5 are in operation, bringing together a number of VDOs in an inter-enterprise linkage that addressed
                         constraints along the value chain and also increased their income.
                         •         10 Private-Public-Community Partnerships have been realized in the project area.
                         •         Communities are facilitated to set up bank linkages in 60 villages.
                         •         Operationalizing/reviving defunct productive investments made by other donor projects.
                         •         The contribution of the project towards the development of national economy is commendable in terms of poverty reduction; production and productivity; and youth
