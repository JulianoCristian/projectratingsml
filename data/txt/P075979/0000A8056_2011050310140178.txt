                         Implementation Status Overview
                         Progress toward meeting the development objectives has been satisfactory. The health sector in Sao Tome and Principe has registered considerable progress over the last five years
                         as a result of the Government commitment and consistent donors support to the sector. Health indicators are progressing steadily. According to the Demographic and Health Survey
                         2008-2009, maternal mortality for the period 1999-2008 is at 158 per 100.000 live births, one of the lowest rates in the Sub-Saharan Africa. Between 2005-2010, the immunization
                         rate for measle increased from 84 percent to 93 percent, and the percentage of pregnant women receiving ante natal care increased from 90 percent (one visit) to 95 percent (4
                         visits). The Demographic and Health Survey also shows that 82% of births were attended by skilled births attendant - a substantial increase compared to the 70 percent rate in
