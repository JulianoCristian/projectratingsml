                         Project Development Objectives
                         Project Development Objective (from Project Appraisal Document)

                         The development objective of the project is to reduce travel costs and improve access to markets, off-farm economic opportunities, and social services for poor rural
                         communities in the 33 participating provinces in Northern and Central Vietnam by: (i) Increasing the number of communities connected to basic access and improved all
                         weather roads; (ii) Improving rural road conditions through better management and maintenance of the network; and (iii) Strengthening the capacity within government
                         and the private sector to plan, implement and maintain improvements in the rural transport network.

                         Implementation Status Overview
                         Overall project implementation is progressing well and it is expected that the project will meet its development objective. The mission's findings confirmed the relevance of the project
                         objectives.

                         The original credit closed on time on December 31, 2012, with disbursements of US$110.4 million, approximately 97 percent of the Credit. Additional financing (AF) of US$ 97 million
                         was declared effective on August 9, 2012. The United Kingdom's Department for International Development (DfID) provided an additional grant funding of US$ 7.2 million (UKÂ£ 5
                         million) to fund road asset maintenance and management activities in 12 provinces, as well as gender mainstreaming activities in three provinces. The first and second work
                         programs for road rehabilitation (Annual Work Plans 1&2 financed under the original credit) have been completed in 33 provinces. 2,349 km of rural roads were improved under 594
Public Disclosure Copy




                         contracts covering 530 road links, with a total contract value of about US$100 million. Annual Work Plans 1&2 for district road maintenance (both routine and periodic maintenance) in
                         32 provinces have also been completed. A total of 16,534 km of district roads have been maintained for two years under 911 maintenance contracts valued at about US$30 million.
                         All consultancy contracts financed under Component C of the original have been completed with satisfactory implementation. Equipment and goods, to support Annual Work Plans
                         1&2, were delivered to Project Management Unit No 6 and the 33 project provinces. 18,748 staff from the MoT, and provincial, district and commune levels have received training
                         under the project.

                         The AF is funding a third annual rehabilitation program (AWP3) in 32 provinces. Rehabilitation works has been either completed or on going in 32 provinces. In 12 provinces, a third
                         annual maintenance work program (AWP3-M) is being funded from the AF and additional grants of USD 7.2 million from the United Kingdom Department for International
                         Cooperation (DfID). Routine maintenance of rural roads managed by the three Women's Unions of Lao Cai, Thanh Hoa and Quang Binh provinces is progressing well with positive
                         impacts for the rural poor, especially for people ethnic minorities in these provinces. All implementation support consultancies financed under the AF have been mobilized to support
                         the rehabilitation and maintenance work under AWP3. Progress on all activities is on track to meet the project development objective. Intermediate outcomes are not yet observable.
