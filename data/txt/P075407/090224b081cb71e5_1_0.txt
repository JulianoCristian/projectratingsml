                         Project Development Objectives
                         Project Development Objective (from Project Appraisal Document)

                         The development objective of the project is to reduce travel costs and improve access to markets, off-farm economic opportunities, and social services for poor rural
                         communities in the 33 participating provinces in Northern and Central Vietnam by: (i) Increasing the number of communities connected to basic access and improved all
                         weather roads; (ii) Improving rural road conditions through better management and maintenance of the network; and (iii) Strengthening the capacity within government
                         and the private sector to plan, implement and maintain improvements in the rural transport network.

                         Implementation Status Overview
                         Overall the implementation is making good progress and it is expected that the project will meet its development objective. The mission's findings confirmed the relevance of the
                         project objectives.

                         The original credit closed on time on December 2012 with disbursements of US$110.4 million, approximately 97 percent of the Credit. Additional financing (AF) of US$ 97 million was
                         declared effective on August 9, 2012. The United Kingdom's Department for International Development (DfID) has provided an additional grant funding of US$ 7.2 million (UK£ 5
                         million) for road asset maintenance and management in 12 provinces, as well as gender mainstreaming activities in three provinces. The first and second work programs for
                         rehabilitation (AWP1&2 financed under OC) had been completed in 33 provinces with the total road length of 2,341 km (594 contracts/530 road links) valued at about US$100 million.
Public Disclosure Copy




                         The AWP 1&2 for district road maintenance (both routine and periodic maintenance) in all 32 provinces have completed site works under the OC. A total of 16,534 km district roads
                         had been maintained for two years under 911 maintenance contracts valued at about US$30 million. All consultancies financed under the component C of the OC that supported
                         implementation have been satisfactorily implemented. Equipment and goods have been delivered to PMU6 and the 33 provinces, which were used to support the operation and
                         execution of the AWP1&2. Procurement of the new external financial audit contract will cover the implementation period through the closings of both the OC and AF.

                         The AF is funding ongoing work on a third annual rehabilitation program (AWP3) in 32 provinces. Construction works has commenced in 25 out of 32 provinces. In 12 provinces a
                         third annual maintenance work program (AWP3-M) is being funded from the AF and additional grants of USD 7.2 million from the United Kingdom Department for International
                         Cooperation (DfID). All implementation support consultancies financed under the AF have been mobilized to support the rehabilitation and maintenance work under AWP3. Progress
                         on all activities is on track to meet the project development objective. Intermediate outcomes are not yet observable.
