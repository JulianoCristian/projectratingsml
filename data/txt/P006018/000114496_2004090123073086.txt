Amendment to the Loan Agreement 
 
Excellency: 
 
Please refer  to the Loan Agreement dated December 5, 1995 entered into between the 
Argentine Republic (the Borrower) and the International Bank for Reconstruction and 
Development (the Bank), as amended. 
 
In response to a letter from Lic. Alfredo Garcia, former Executive Coordinator, dated 
December 18, 2001 requesting: (a) the reallocation of the proceeds of the Loan to continue the 
financing of additional Institutional Development Subprojects (as said term is defined in Section 
1.02 (f) of the Loan Agreement); and (b) a modification in the aggregate amount for procurement 
of works and goods set forth in Parts C.1 and C.2 of Section I of Schedule 4 to the Loan 
Agreement, and further discussions on the subject with Lic. Gerardo Marcelo Hita, 
Director 
Nacional de Proyectos con Organismos Inte
rnacionales de Crédito
 of the Ministry of Economy, 
the Bank hereby agrees to amend the Loan Agreement as follows:

representative of the Borrower for the purposes of Section 11.03 of the General 
Conditions.

3. 
Parts C.1 and C.2 of Section I of Schedule 4 are hereby amended in their entirety 
as follows: 
 
“1.
 
Goods estimated to cost the equivalent of less than $350,000 per contract, 
up to an aggregate amount equivalent to $5,500,000, and works estimated to cost 
the equivalent of less than $5,000,000 per contract, up to an aggregate amount 
equivalent to $64,500,000, may be procured under contracts awarded on the basis 
of competitive bidding, advertised locally, in accordance with procedures 
satisfactory to the Bank.
 
2. 
Goods and works estimated to cost the equivalent of less than $100,000 
and $350,000 per contract, respectively, up to an aggregate amount equivalent to 
$10,500,000 for goods and $1,050,000 for works, may be procured under contracts 
awarded on the basis of comparison of price quotations obtained from at least three 
suppliers or contractors, as the case may be, eligible under the Guidelines, in 
accordance with procedures acceptable to the Bank.”.
