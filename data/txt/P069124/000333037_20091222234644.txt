                                    Introductory Statement

This Project Paper seeks the approval of the Executive Directors to introduce the following
changes in the Kingdom of Morocco Rainfed Agricultural Development Project (Loan 4701-
MOR) and any accompanying amendments to the project's legal documents.

The proposed changes include the mainstreaming· of project implementation in the new
institutional structures created by the Ministry of Agriculture and Marine Fisheries (MAMF) as
part of the new agricultural strategy Plan Maroc Vert adopted in March 2008. Specifically, the
existing Project Management Unit (PMU) is to be transferred from the Land Management
Directorate of the MAMF to the newly created Directorate for Irrigation and Land Management,
Division of Land Management of the same MAMF. In addition, the proposed changes include an
extension of the current closing date of the Project from December 31, 2009 to December 31,
2011, a reallocation of the loan proceeds, a cancellation of a small portion of the loan and the
reformulation of the project development· objectives and associated outcome and output
indicators. While the original project components and activities remain unchanged, the
objectives and indicators would be refined in a manner that makes them more realistic, coherent,
and measurable. Specifically, they would shifttheir current focus on increasing rural incomes to
establishing necessary pre-conditions for increasing rural incomes.

       1. Context and Reasons for the Restructuring

1.1.    The Rainfed Agriculture Development Project (RADP) was approved by the World
Bank's Board on June 10, 2003. The effectiveness date of the project was July 7, 2004. The
current project objective is to improve the livelihood of rural populations in priority rainfed
areas by promoting local participatory development, and fostering sustainable agriculture
growth and the development of non-agricultural activities. To this end, the project currently has
the following sub-objectives:

       (a) To build institutional capacity for project identification and implementation in rainfed
           zones, in partnership with local organizations; and

       (b) In Priority Rainfed Areas in the Project Area: (i) to improve the revenue of local
           populations and their basic rural infrastructure; and (ii) to promote sustainable
           development of rainfed agricultural farming systems and reduce their vulnerability to
           drought.

1.2.      The Project includes two components:

       (a) Institutional strengthening aimed at improving and harmonizing the multiple approaches
           of the Ministry of Agriculture and Marine Fisheries (MAMF) [Ministere de l'Agriculture
           et de la Peche Maritime] to integrated rural development and developing the capacity and
           tools (at both the central and local levels) for identifying, preparing, implementing, and
           monitoring rural development programs in rainfed zones; and

       (b) Development activities, through Communal Investment Plans (CIPs), formulated in a
           participatory manner in the Priority Rainfed Areas in the Project Area, including 12 rural
                                               3


       communes in 7 provinces (Boulemane, Khemisset, Khouribga, Sidi-Kacem, Taroudant,
       Tata, and Errachidia), which are representatives of different agro-ecosystems, among the
       poorest in the country and lag significantly in terms of rural infrastructure and access to
       basic social services.

1.3.   The Project performance has varied depending on the component; however, the overall
achievement of the development objectives and implementation progress are currently rated
marginally unsatisfactory (ISR 14).

1.4.    The activities covered in the first component - institutional strengthening component are
being satisfactorily carried out albeit with some delays. Local and Provincial Human
Development Committees (LHDC and PHDCs), composed of representatives of Government
officials, associations and Government technical services, are now operational and are preparing
integrated local development programs in a participatory manner; furthermore, financing
mechanisms for decentralized research projects have been introduced in the Priority Rainfed
Areas in the Project Area.

1.5.    The capacity of local organizations has been strengthened and 45 partnerships have been
formed to support local initiatives. However, because of lack of experience in participatory and
multidisciplinary exercises and because of cumbersome administrative procedures, it took three
years to prepare the CIPs in a participatory manner and to have them approved by community
and Provincial institutions. The Priority Rainfed Areas in the Project Area now all have approved
CIPs in place which are under implementation.

1.6.    The performance of the second component - development activities as articulated in the
PICs - has been moderately satisfactory (ISR No. 13). CIPs investments in agricultural
production systems are funded under the MAMF budget. Progress is globally satisfactory, in
particular the execution of infrastructure activities (hydro agricultural and land improvements).
Approximately two-thirds of agricultural program activities are under way.

1.7.    However, non-agricultural CIPs investments involving the improvement of basic rural
infrastructure (30% of the project cost, which includes rehabilitation of rural roads, health
centers, schools, bridges, connecting communities to water, etc.) funded by the Rural
Development Fund (RDF) have been delayed largely for institutional and administrative reasons.
The project has experienced complex institutional arrangements involving several sectors, two
sources of government' funding (one managed by the MAMF and one managed by the Governors
of the 7 provinces where the project is being implemented) as well as only partially
deconcentrated levels of decision-making. These bottlenecks were the result of a project design
that was intended to implement integrated (i.e. multi-sector) and participatory rural development,
in all institutional environment with limited deconcentration and limited experience in
participatory approach. The absence of effective, decentralized decision-making made
communication, coordination and execution of activities protracted and arduous.

1.8.   Although these institutional problems remain, all the CIPs are now under
implementation, albeit with delays. Investment to improve agricultural production funded under
the MAMF budget is progressing satisfactorily; most of the feasibility studies that precede the
work t6 be done for the rehabilitation of the socio-economic infrastructure have been completed;

the procurement procedures to rehabilitate these infrastructure would be initiated before the end
of 2009, and the related contracts would be executed in 2010 and 2011. Procurement procedures
for a limited amount of projects identified in 5 CIP (cf. list attached), for an amount equivalent of
17 % of the RDF financing, will not be initiated before the end of 2009 because the feasibility
studies will not be completed in time; it is therefore very unlikely that the execution of these
works would be completed before the end of the extension period (December 2011) because of
the expected long administrative delays to complete the ongoing feasibility studies, to contract
out the execution of these works, and to execute the contracts.

1.9.    On the financial side, the status of compliance with the audit covenants in the loan
agreement is satisfactory. Audit reports are produced on time; there is an ongoing positive
dialogue with the Government to remedy any minor financial management issues. Commitments
as of October 31st, 2009 amount to DH 214 million, representing 53 percent of the overall
project cost. Disbursements have reached DH 128.3 million, DH 94.5 million of which is
financed by the loan. Sixty seven percent of the financing of agricultural activities is already
committed and the remaining budget will be used in the two years to come. Almost 30% of the
financing from RDF was committed as of October 31 st, and 17% of the same RDF is being
cancelled as mentioned above. The remaining 53% which is expected to be committed during the
first quarter of 2010 at the latest (cf. table attached) will be fully executed during the two-year
extension period. As of December 2009,32.44 % of the loan was disbursed and 5.6 % would be
cancelled.

1.10. The project Loan Agreement was amended twice without change in the project
components and activities. A loan amendment with re-allocation of the loan proceeds was done
in April 2005, including the creation of a sub-project grants as a new subproject disbursement
category (cf. attachment), to finance partnership with NGOs and/or research and development
institutions. These partnerships had been envisaged in the project document without adequate
disbursement category. A second amendment of the loan agreement was done in October 2008 to
adjust the project decision making mechanisms at local and provincial level so as.to align them
with the National Human Development Initiative (INDH) that developed similar decision
making mechanisms, all over the country. This second amendment of the loan had no impact on
the credit allocation (cf. attachment) and no impact on the project components and activities.

1.11. In 2009, the Government of Morocco adopted a new agriculture development strategy
entitled "Morocco Green Plan" [Plan Maroc Vert - PMV]. The PMV targets the development of
all agricultural potential and revolves around two pillars: Pillar 1 targets the accelerated
development of high value-added competitive agriculture, while Pillar 2 targets low-performing
actors and addresses poverty. In the context of implementation of PMV guidelines, the MAMF
has restructured its directorates at the central and·provinciallevels with the aim of achieving far-
reaching deconcentration of decision-making. In this regard, 16 Regional Agricultural
Directorates [Directions Regionales de I 'Agriculture - DRAs] and one Agricultural Development
Agency (ADA) have been recently established. The DRAs are responsible for the coordination
and supervision of the DPAs/ORMVAs and the technical services of the MAMF. This new
division of labor envisages that the national Directorate of the Ministry should focus more on
their regulation functions.

1.12. The Government and the Bank have committed with local communities to finance the
implementation of the CIPs with the support of the Bank project. The project has already greatly
assisted in the finalization of the seven CIPs and now the implementation phase is underway
which necessitates the identified investments. Because of these commitments made with local
communities, the Government of Morocco does not envisage the closing of the project as
currently scheduled in December 2009, but rather has requested a restructuring of the project.
This proposed restructuring takes into account the new organization of the MAMF and includes a
reformulation of the project development objectives, a cancellation of a portion of the loan and a
two-year extension of the project to execute all the contracts that will be signed by the end of the
first trimester of2010.

1.13. The proposed restructuring will allow the project to achieve its reformulated project
development objectives and will allow for the provision of the services and most of the socio-
economic infrastructure as planned in the CIPs. These objectives will not be met if the project is
closed as planned on December 31,2009.

1.14. Based on the above, the Government of Morocco formally requested the World Bank to
consider the changes mentioned below.

   2. Proposed Changes

2.1.     Although the project components and activities remain unchanged, a reformulation of the
development objectives of the project and related indicators is proposed so as to make them more
realistic, consistent with project activities, and quantifiable, thus making it possible to evaluate
the success of the project in relation to its expected results. Such an approach will also avoid
confusion between the development objectives of the project and the more long-term and
comprehensive objectives that the Government of Morocco has established for the development
of rural zones, in particular increasing the revenue of farmers and the rural population in general.
While the project contributes to these major objectives, it is merely one of a series of
interventions designed to achieve these objectives.

2.2.    It is proposed to reformulate the project development objectives mentioned in the Loan
Agreement as follows: to promote the development of rainfed based agricultural farming systems
in the Priority Rainfed Areas in the Project Area and improve access by local populations to
basic irifrastructure and socioeconomic services, through an integrated and participatory rural
development approach. The proposed related impact indicators would be: (i) additional are<;lS
irrigated or restored to crop production; (ii) additional agricultural/pastoral areas where the land
has been improved and crops are growing; (iii) the number of additional improved water points
used for agricultural purposes; (iv) the number of farmers who have adopted new technology; (v)
the number of additional households with access to potable water; (vi) visits to health centers and
school attendance; and (vii) the number of douars (communities) that can be accessed by cars all
year round.

2.3.    The mechanisms established for project coordination and supervision at the central,
provincial and communal levels remain unchanged. The Designated Account (DA) will continue
to be managed by the Tresorerie Generale; the responsibilities for the administrative and
financial management of the project and its overall monitoring and coordination which, until
                                                6


now, have been assigned to the former Land Development Directorate (DAF) (which no longer
exist), will be transferred to the new Directorate for Irrigation aild Development of Agricultural
Land [Direction de l'Irrigation et de l'Amenagement de l'Espace Agricole - DIAEA] recently
created. Specifically, the current project management unit would be transferred to the Land
Improvement Monitoring and Planning Service of the DIAEA. The Provincial Management
Units (PMUs) within the DPAs/ORMVAs, which are currently responsible for: (i) compiling the
financial and accounting documents needed' for the preparation of physical and financial project
reports and statements for all project activities conducted in the province; (ii) transmitting them
to the central authorities; and (iii) ensuring compliance with World Bank procedures, particularly
in the areas of procurement and disbursement, would .remain within the relevant
DPAs/ORMV As and would continue to perform their current duties with support from the newly
created Regional Directorate for Agriculture. Procedures related to procurement, disbursement,
financial management, and project monitoring and evaluation would remain unchanged.
Execution of investment projects will continue to be the responsibility of the DPAs/ORMVAs in
the MAMF in the case of agricultural projects; the Governors, who chair the Provincial
Technical Committees (CTPs) and their provincial technical services would retain responsibility
for coordination of execution of the non-agricultural components of the RADP.

2.4.    It is estimated that a two-year extension would be necessary to execute the remaining
project activities. The Government has finalized an action plan for the project that includes
agricultural and non-agricultural projects identified in the PICs as mentioned in the proposed
new disbursement schedule and the new procurement plans attached in the annex. The projected
activities for completion of the PICs have been programmed on the basis of the commitment
from the relevant provincial authorities to begin before the end of the first trimester of 2010, at
the latest.

2.5.    As regard to the utilization of the RDF, no more new feasibility studies will be initiated
in 2010 and 2011; extension will be granted only to execute works for which procurement
procedures have been initiated prior to end-2009. As mentioned previously, the MAMF has
identified a series of activities that are not ready to start implementation in early 2010 and the
Government is thus cancelling the RDF allocation for these activities. In parallel, it has
requested the Bank to cancel the financing of these activities under the loan, for an amount of
Euro 1.4 million. It is also important to note however that these activities are not cancelled;
MAMF may want to use their share of the RDF that is regularly replenished, to finance them
when they are ready to be executed.

2.6.  The proposed new project completion and closing dates are June 30, 2011 and December
31,2011, respectively.

     3. Analysis

 3.1.    In view of the fact that the components and activities designed since the start of the
 project remain the same, the proposed changes should not have a significant impact on the
 economic, financial, social, or environmental aspects of the project. Consequently, restructuring
 will not entail waivers to World Bank policies and the project will continue to be classified as an
 EA Category: B (Partial Assessment) project. From an institutional standpoint, the proposed
 administrative and financial management changes would fall within the restructuring of the
 MAMF. Integration of the project into the reformed MAMF is a positive development that would
 have a beneficial effect on the viability of proj ect results. Furthermore, the experience acquired
 with the integrated rural development approach for agro-ecologically disadvantaged areas could
 provide additional knowledge for the execution of the new Plan Maroc Vert, in particular under
 implementation of its Pillar 2 dedicated to the development of social agriculture (agriculture for
 the poor).

     4. Expected Results

  4.1.    The readjustment of the project to the new MAMF entities and the two-year extension of
  the project should facilitate execution of planned project activities, achievement of clearer
  development objectives, and offer improved viability of results after the project is closed. The
  proposed changes in the logical framework, the reformulation of the development objectives, the
. cancellation of a small portion of the loan and the related reformulation of the impact and results
  indicators, would make the reformulated project development objectives realistic, consistent, and
  quantifiable. The result framework in the annex shows the development objectives, the expected
  results of each component, and the impact and results indicators that would be monitored during
  the remaining project period.
