                         Project Development Objectives
                         Project Development Objective (from Project Appraisal Document)

                         The revised PDO is to assist the Recipient in: (i) increasing the coverage of drained and irrigated areas in Bobojon Gafurov andKannibodom raions, respectively; and (ii)
                         strengthening the early warning system of the Kayrakkum dam as well as carrying out ageotechnical study for assessing the risks associated with said dam.

                         Implementation Status Overview
                         Overall project implementation has significantly improved with the appointment of the new PMU Director. More than six months before the project closure, disbursement is at 94%,
                         with all project funds committed, and most of outstanding activities underway and expected to be completed before April 2014. Most of the indicators have already been achieved or


                                                                                                                                                                                                          Page 1 of 9
                             The World Bank                                                                                                                                             Report No: ISR12601



                         are within the reach. In particular, all irrigation and drainage civil works and rehabilitation and repair of Vertical Drainage Wells (VDWs) are close to completion. As a result of the
                         project interventions improvements in irrigation and drainage are reported for more than 7,000 ha in Bobojon Gafurov and Kanibadam districts which exceeds the project target
                         indicator.
Public Disclosure Copy




                         Two important activities related to the Project Development Objective remain to be completed. Installation of an Early Warning System for Kayrakkum dam is being procured, with an
                         expected completion date in early 2014. With respect to the detailed geotechnical investigations, TORs were approved and a national consultant, with support from an international
                         consultant, is being recruited and will complete the assignment by April 2014.
