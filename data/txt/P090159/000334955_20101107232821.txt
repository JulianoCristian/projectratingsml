                                          Proposed Changes

1. The proposed restructuring of the Emergency Recovery and Disaster Management Project (ERDMP)
   is in response to request from the Government of the Republic of Haiti (GoH) to restructure the
   Project following the earthquake of January 12, 2010 that resulted in over 230,000 dead, 600,000
   homeless and significant damage to public infrastructure and administrative capacities. Currently the
   ERDMP is the only mechanism at the GoH's disposal capable of providing immediate technical and
   financial support to the national civil protection authorities in the aftermath of the earthquake.

2. The proposed restructuring would be limited to (i) an extension of the closing date of the project by
   twelve (12) months, specifically an extension of the closing date of both the Development Grant
   Agreement (IDA Grant No. H143-0-HT) and the Financing Agreement (Grant No. H353-0-HT) until
   Dec 31, 2011; (ii) a reallocation between categories to cover existing and projected category expense
   overruns, (iii) the elimination of one minor subcomponent under Part B4 of the Project (related to
   the installation of a national communication system to coordinate the operations between the
   national and regional levels); and (iv) a revised results management framework (see Annex 2).

3. The proposed restructuring would not entail any change to the ERDMP development objectives.

4. The operational manual would be updated to reflect current circumstances, building on lessons
   learned from successful implementation of the ERDMP to date.

5. The Legal Agreements would be amended to reflect the restructuring.

Background

6. The Grant supporting the Emergency Recovery and Disaster Management Project was approved on
   January 6, 2005, and became effective on June 1, 2005. An Additional Financing was approved on
   January 31, 2008, and became effective on September 5, 2008, extending the Grant's original
   closing date by two (2) years, from December 31, 2008 until December 31, 2010.

   The development objectives of the project are: (a) to support the rehabilitation of the areas affected
   by the recent adverse natural disasters; (b) to strengthen the country's capacity to manage natural
   disaster risks and better respond to emergencies resulting from adverse natural events; and (c) to
   reduce the vulnerability of communities through risk identification and risk mitigation activities.

   The project's overall performance is rated as satisfactory. The project maintained a Satisfactory
   PDO rating in the most recent ISR report based upon the attainment of the targeted outcomes for
   component 1 and 3 under the original grant, and the continued progress toward the attainment of the
   supplemental target outcomes financed through the AF grant. The project also maintained a
   Satisfactory IP rating.

   This project was one in a pool of projects identified for restructuring in March and April, 2010. The
   delay in processing the restructuring of this project can be attributed to: (i) the complexities
   associated with the ever-changing institutional and operational environment in Haiti; and (ii) the

   restructurings of existing complementary projects of partner multilateral agencies and the
   introduction of new operations.

Institutional Arrangement

7. The proposed project restructuring would not involve changes to the institutional arrangements
   under the original ERDMP. Overall project implementation would continue to be managed by the
   DPC's (Direction de la Protection Civile) Project Coordination Unit (PCU). A second PCU housed
   within the Bureau de Monétisation and Programmes d'Aide au Développement (BMPAD) would
   continue to implement components 1 and 3 on behalf of the DPC. NGOs would continue to play an
   important role in implementing sub-projects at the community level (component 3). Both PCUs have
   satisfactorily implemented the project to date.

Financing mechanism/Conduit

8. The proposed project restructuring would not involve changes to the financing mechanisms of the
   project.

Outputs

9. The proposed restructuring would involve minor changes to the outputs associated with the Part B4
   of the Project: Institutional Strengthening (see Annex 2). The changes are the product of the
   Government of Haiti's request that the project finances the replacement of goods lost during the
   earthquake of January 12, 2010 and the preparation of the 2010 hurricane season. The additional
   outputs include: (i) the construction of additional community and departmental emergency
   operations centers; (ii) additional simulation exercises at the central, departmental and local levels;
   (iii) additional training of central DPC and SNGRD staff; (iv) expanded public awareness and
   education campaign; and (iv) the replacement of the first responder equipment.

   These additional outputs would be financed through the utilization of existing allocated resources,
   the cancelation of activities covered by other programs, and the reallocation on non-utilized funds
   from Category 3 and Category 10.

   The activities to be canceled are:

      I.   Risk reduction thematic committees in key line ministries. This activity would be covered by
           the Emergency Bridge Reconstruction and Vulnerability Reduction Project (P114292);
     II.   Construction and equipment of Departmental Emergency Operations Centers (EOC) in
           Haiti's Northern Departments. The priority has been given to construction and equipping of
           Communal EOC in Haiti's Southern Departments, which are at higher risk. The
           Departmental EOC in the Northern Departments are likely to be covered by the European
           Commission;
    III.   Establishment of the policy & procedures for the creation and operationalization of National
           Contingency Fund. This activity is being supported through the GFDRR financed support to
           Haitian National Institutions Grant (P120895-SPN-TF096218).
    IV.    National Emergency Communications System. This activity would be funded in part by the
           United Nations Development Program and the European Commission.

Closing Date

14. The proposed project restructuring would extend the project closing date by twelve (12) months. The

    provide a buffer for any future unforeseen implementation delays associated with the Presidential

                                      Appraisal Summary Update

Economic, Financial and Technical Analysis

17. The proposed restructuring does not involve changes to the economic, financial or technical analysis
    of the project. Given the high levels of poverty, weak public infrastructure, serious fiscal problems
    and degraded environment, adverse natural events of small magnitude that occur on a frequent basis
    have a significant impact both the population's well-being and economic assets. The ERDMP
    rehabilitation and local risk mitigation micro-projects (component 1 and 3) focus on activities with a
    high visibility and impact that utilize local labor and materials when available to provide for the
    quickest and most resilient economic and social recovery available within the financing limitations
    of the project. The proposed restructuring would facilitate the GoH's preparations for the upcoming
    hurricane season thus further contributing to a more resilient population.

18. An ex-post evaluation of the activities executed under the ERDMP would be carried out and would
    include a basic cost-benefit analysis, including communities' perceptions of the benefits of
    rehabilitated infrastructure, better disaster preparedness and response and local risk mitigation. This
    evaluation would help inform future DRM activities within Haiti, as well as contribute to the
    emerging body of knowledge specific to community driven local disaster risk mitigation activities

Environmental and Social

19. The proposed restructuring does not involve changes to the environmental and social safeguard
    aspects of the project. The additional works envisaged under the proposed restructuring (component
    2) would involve the rehabilitation of existing structures and work entirely within existing public
    lands.

20. The proposed restructuring would only affect new activities in the Departments in which the
    ERDMP is already present (Le Sud, Grande Anse, Nippes, Ouest and Artibonite). These
    Departments were already covered by the projects original EMP. In addition to operating in the same
    zones, the scope and type of activities remains unchanged. As a result, no new safeguards would be
    triggered.

21. The Project Coordination Units (PCU) have engaged an environmental specialist who is responsible
    for assuring that those policies laid out in the Environment Safeguard Manual are respected. The
    project has also financed numerous environmental safeguard and environmental training sessions for
    the specialist. A communications expert was also engaged based on the need observed during
    implementation to improve the quality of communications/ public consultations prior to undertaking
    small rehabilitation works.

Exception to Bank Policy

22. The proposed restructuring does not involve exceptions to Bank Policy.
