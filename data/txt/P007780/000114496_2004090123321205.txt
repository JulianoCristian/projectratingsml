Re:
Nicaragua:  Credit No. 2536-NI and Swiss Grant TF 14-03090-NI
(Agricultural Technology and Land Management Project)
Fourth Amendment to the Development Credit Agreement
Excellency:
Please refer to the Development Credit Agreement (Agricultural Technology and 
Land Management Project) dated August 5, 1993 between the Republic of Nicaragua (the 
Borrower) and the International Development Association (the Association) as amended 
on July 29, 1994, November 13, 1995 and June 8, 1998.
Pursuant to your request in letter dated February 25, 1999, we agree to amend 
the Development Credit Agreement as follows:
1.
Section 1.02:
(a)
Paragraphs (b), (c) and (j) are deleted.
(b)
In paragraph (f), the following words are deleted “INRA’s Organic Law of 
38-91”, and the following words are added “Law No. 290 of September 2, 1998, Decrees 
No. 56-98, 71-98, 78-98 and I-94”.
(c)
In paragraph (h), the words “through INRA (as defined below)” are 
deleted.
(d)
Paragraph (i) is deleted.
(e)
In paragraph (r), the words “through INRA” are deleted.
(f)
Paragraph (s) is deleted and replaced by a new paragraph to read:
“(s)  “MAGFOR” means the Borrower’s Ministry of Agriculture and 
Forestry”.
(g)
The following new definition is added as paragraph (qq):
“(qq) “Land Titling Intendency” means “Intendencia de la Propiedad”, the 
Borrower’s 
titling office within MOF;
2.
(a)
All references to “MAG” in the development credit agreement are replaced 
by “MAGFOR”.
(b)
All references to “INRA” in the development credit agreement are replaced 
by “Land Titling Intendency”.
3.
Section 3.01 Subparagraphs 5 (c) (ii) and (e) are deleted.
4.
The references to “INRA” and “INRA Implementation Agreement” are deleted.
5.
Section 7.01:
(a)
The words “and Public Credit” are added after the word “Finance”.
(b)
The acronym “MIFIN” is replaced by “MHCP”.
6.
Section 7.02:  The word “Finanzas” is deleted and replaced by the words 
Page  2
“Hacienda y Crédito Público”.
7.
Schedule 1 is amended as per the attachment to this letter.
8.
Schedule 3 is amended as follows:
(a)
Section I, Part D, paragraph 3:  The first four lines are deleted and 
replaced:
“Goods estimated to cost the equivalent of $50,000 or less per contract, 
and works 
estimated to cost the equivalent of $150,000 or less per contract up to 
an aggregate amount 
equivalent to $2,600,000 for goods and $1,750,000 for works, 
may be procured under 
contracts”.
(b)
Section II, paragraph 2 is amended to read as follows:
“2.  Notwithstanding the provisions of paragraph 1 of this Section, the 
provisions of 
the Consultant Guidelines requiring prior Association review or 
approval of budgets, short 
lists, selection procedures, letter of invitation, 
proposals, evaluation reports and 
contracts, shall not apply to: (a) 
contracts for the employment of consulting firms 
estimated to cost less 
than $100,000 equivalent each; or (b) contracts for the employment of 
individuals 
estimated to cost less than $50,000 equivalent each.  However, said exceptions 
to 
prior Association review shall not apply to:  (a) terms of reference for consulting 
firms; (b) single-source selections of consulting firms; (c) assignments of a 
critical 
nature, as reasonably determined by the Association; and (d) 
amendments to contracts for the 
employment of consulting firms raising the contract 
value to $100,000 equivalent or above; 
and (e) amendments for contracts for the 
employment of individual consultants raising the 
contract value to $50,000 
equivalent or above.”
9.
Schedule 5, in paragraph 1 (d), the amount of “$200,000” is replaced by 
“$500,000”.
Please indicate your agreement with the foregoing amendment by signing this 
letter in the space provided and returning it to us.  This amendment shall be executed 
in two counterparts, each of which shall constitute an original.  The amendment shall 
become effective upon receipt by the Association of one fully executed original of 
this letter and will be applied retroactively as of October 25, 1998.
