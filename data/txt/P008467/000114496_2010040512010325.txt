Description of the Project
The  objectives  of  the   Project   are  to
(1)
strengthen
institutional capabilities to facilitate the further upgrading and
increased effectiveness of technological research and development,
and
(2)
assist in  financing  such technological modernization and
research and development facilities and resources, as will contri-
bute to improved industrial productivity and competitiveness.
 
The Project consists of the  following parts, subject to such
modifications thereof as the Bank  and the Borrower may agree upon
from time to time to achieve such objectives:
 
Part A:
 
(1)
Carrying  out  training  programs  to  upgrade  the technical
skills  of  staff  involved  in  the  selection,  formulation  and
execution  of  technological  research  and  development programs,
including
(a) the staff of the Guarantor and its agencies, and the
staff of industrial  enterprises,  in  the  design, evaluation and
administration of  such  research  and  development  programs, and
(b)
the  Participating  Banks'   staff,  in  the  identification,
appraisal and financing of technology development projects and the
supervision of the execution thereof.
 
(2)
Carrying  out  studies  to  develop  suitable  strategies and
mechanisms for the allocation  of  resources for and the financing
of further expansion of technological research and development.
(3)
Carrying  out  a  program   to  upgrade  the  facilities  and
technical capabilities  of  the  existing  institutes  for quality
control and for metrology.
 
Part B:
 
(1)
Carrying out a program  to  assist the Participating Banks in
the selection and appraisal of Sub-projects.
 
(2)
The financing, through the provision of loans, including Sub-
loans, by the Participating  Banks,  of specific development proj-
ects to be  carried  out  by  industrial enterprises for upgrading
their  research  and  development  facilities,  modernizing  their
products, services  and  production  processes,  and strengthening
their staff.
 
*     *     *
The Project is expected to be completed by December
31,
1993.
