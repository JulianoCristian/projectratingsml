1. INTRODUCTION AND SCOPE OF WORK
U          1.1. Objectives of the Project
The Project is aimed to develop rural distribution power net work for
remoted communes. This will be a base for economic and social development of
reral areas of the sourthern provinces of Vietnam.
3               Project will electrify 16 communes in 6 provinces of Southern of Vietnam;
3 of them are in Mekong river delta (Kien Giang, Soc Trang, Tra Vinh); other
three provinces are in the South East of Vietnam (Binh Thuan, Binh Phuoc. Lam
5          Dong).
By implementation of the Project, an area of 861 km2 with 178.641
U ;)       habitants from 34.693 households will get benefit from electricity supply.
Obiectives of the Project are:
-Extend and strengthen rural power network in 6 provinces: Lam Dong. binh
Phuoc, Binh Thuan, Tra Vinh. Soc Trang, Kien Giang.
|          - Increase electrification ratio from 35.5% (at the end of 2004) to 75.9% at the end
of 2005;
- Decrease power losses in low voltage power network from 25% to approximatelv
5%;
- Increase power supply quality in the Project's areas;
- Improve power quality in the Project's areas.
1.2. Selected Project's communes
Generally, existing power networks in selected provinces are in very bad
*          consition. The networks are the weaknest ones in sourthem provinces of Vietnam.
All of selected communes is 25 km to 30 km far from supply substation. In some
cases this distance is even over 40 km. The quality of power supply, therefore, is
at very low quality, power losses are high, quanlity and quality of power supply
are not satisfactory. Power demand for production and consumption can not be
|          fulfilled.

Six Project's provinces are located in two different regions, the population
distribution is also different.
- Population in South East Provinces are located mainly along transportation
roads. Population distribution is quite event along the roads system and density is
quite high. Therefore, the development of new power supply system for these
provinces are very convinient, the investment cost is not as high as in provinces of
Mekong River Delta.
- In provinces of Mekong River Delta, water canals are very dense. Natural area of
one commune is larger than in South East provinces. Population is mainly
distributed along the roads and the water canals system. In some cases people are
living scatering in quite isolated areas, far from the road and water canals. The
electrification therefore is more difficult and the investment cost higher than in
3          remaining provinces.

4. SCREENING FOR ENVIRONMENTAL IMPACTS
;          4.1. CLASSIFICATION OF IMPACTS
*              The Environmental Impact Assessment focuses on the major environmental issues of  ;,  ,
l  |       the Project's Most Favoured Alternative. The potential impacts of the Project's pre- --
construction, construction, and operation phases were assessed as being in one of five
3 |        categories:
I|            i. NO IMPACT. The potential impact of the Project activity is assessed as NO
I  |             IMPACT if the project activity is physically removed in space or time from the
I                environmental component
* |           ii. MAJOR IMPACT. An impact is said to be MAJOR if the project activity has
potential to affect an environmental component. Major impacts could be "Major
l -              Negative" or "Major Positive." The following criteria were used to determine
*.               whether a given impact is MAJOR:
a) Spatial scale of the impact (site, local, regional, or national/ international);
b) Time horizon of the impact (short, medium, or long term);
*                  c) Magnitude of the change in the environmental component brought about by
the Project activities (small, moderate, large);
d) Importance to local human populations;
e) Compliance with national, provincial, or district environmental protection
laws, standards, and regulations
3                  f) Compliance with Vietnam's international commitments. These include the
Convention on Wetlands of International Importance especially as
Waterfowl Habitat (RAMSAR), the Convention Concerning the Protection
|                     of the World Cultural and Natural Heritage (World Heritage Convention),
and the Convention on International Trade in Endangered Species of Wild
Fauna and Flora (CITES); and
g) Compliance with World Bank guidelines, policies, and regulations.
iii. MINOR IMPACT. If an impact occurs but does not meet the criteria for a Major
I               Impact it is assigned the category MINOR. Minor impacts could be "Minor
negative" or "Minor Positive."
3          MITIGABLE IMPACT. The potential impact of a project activity on an environmental
component is said to be MITIGABLE if there is potential for a major negative impact and
the proposed mitigation measure will prevent the impact or reduce the impact to
acceptable levels.

5. PUBLIC CONSULTATION AND DISCLOSURE
During project preparation stage (2002-2003), the following activities were carried out:
Activity 1: Information & Discussion with Local Authorities on the Line Route
I    Activity 2: Impact Survey and Statistics
Activity 3: Meetings with PAHs
When the survey was completed, District Compensation Committee (in
coordination with the commune officials) held meetings with PAHs having land in the
line ROW and with village representatives. During the meetings, the Consultant
I    presented numerous materials, including: Project Summary, draft RAP and draft EIA,
maps of the project site, figures, tables, photos, pictures, and other support materials.
Meeting officials informed participants of the Project purpose; presented the Project
I)   impacts on land and crops in detail; introduced the principles and policies of
compensation and advised people not to build new structures in the line ROW. PAHs
were consulted on the entitlement policy, property affected, and the compensation
I    amount to each household. Questions posed by the PAHs were addressed and
recommendations/concerns of PAHs and PC were recorded.
The main comments of local peoples on the Project and the environmental issues
of the Project are summarised in Table 5.1.
I    Activity 4: Receiving Further Feedback from PAHs
I    Activity 5: Public disclosure and Clearance on EIA
The Project will receive an investment license only after appropriate modification of
location, design, capacity and/or technology of the project to meet the requirement of
I    environmental protection and resettlement. To address disclosure requirements of OP
4.01, PC2 will:
* Provide Vietnamese-language copies of the EIA report, RAP, and Project
Summary to each commune-level and provincial-level People's Committee in the
Project area.
|  Advertise in major local newspapers several times over a two-month period. The
advertisement will state the EIA, RAP, and Project Summary is available for
public review for a two month period during normal working hours at the
following locations: 1) the provincial-level People's Committees; and 2) The
commune level Peoples' Committees.
I     .* English and Vietnamese-language copies of the EIA report will also be sent to the
Vietnam Information Development Centre at 63 Ly Thai To in Hanoi, for access
by NGOs and public.
I ! |  English language copies of the EIA report will be sent to World Bank for publication on
World Bank Info Shop.
