PROJECT AGREEMENT
AGREEMENT, dated                              , 1980, between
INTERNATIONAL DEVELOPMENT ASSOCIAION (hereinafter called the
Association) and BANK MISR, DEVELOPMENT INDUSTRIAL BANK AND
PRINCIPAL BANK FOR DEVELOPMENT AND AGRICULTURAL CREDIT (herein-
after called the Participating Banks).
WHEREAS by the Development Credit Agreement of even date
herewith between the Arab Republic of Egypt (hereinafter called
the Borrower) and the Association, the Association has agreed to
make available to the Borrower an amount in various currencies
equivalent to forty-five million dollars ($45,000,000), on the
terms and conditions set forth in the Development Credit Agree-
ment, but only on condition that the Participating Banks agree to
undertake such obligations toward the Association as are herein-
after sot forth; and
WHEREAS by a subsidiary loan agreement to be entered into
between the Borrower and the Participating Banks, a part of the
proceeds of the credit provided for under the Development Credit
Agreement will be made available to the Participating Banks on
the terms and conditions therein set forth; and
WHEREAS the Participating Banks, in consideration of the
Association's entering into the Development Credit Agreement with
the Borrower, has agreed to undertake the obligations hereinafter
set forth;
NOW THEREFORE the parties hereto hereby agree as follows:
ARTICLE I
Definitions
Section 1.01. Wherever used in this Agreement, unless the
context shall otherwise require, the several terms defined in
the Development Credit Agreement and in the General Conditions
(as so defined) have the respective meanings therein set forth
and the following additional terms have the following meanings:
(a) the term "PBDAC" shall mean the Principal Bank for
Development and Agricultural Credit;
(b) the term "DIB" shall mean the Development Industrial
Bank; and
(c) the term "BM" shall mean Bank Misr.

ARTICLE II
Execution of the Project and
Other Covenants
Section 2.01. The Participating Banks shall carry out Parts A
and D (1) of the Project described in Schedule 2 to the Develop-
ment Credit Agreement with due diligence and efficiency and in
conformity with appropriate administrative, economic, financial
and investment standards, and shall provide, in regard to their
respective obligations, or cause to be provided, promptly as
needed, the funds, facilities, services and other resources
required for the purpose.
Section 2.02. In order to assist PBDAC and BM in the carrying
out of Part A of the Project, PBDAC and BM shall employ agro-
industries consultants whose qualifications, experience and terms
and conditions of employment shall be satisfactory to the Associ-
ation.
Section 2.03. (a) In accordance with, and subject to, the
provisions of the Development Credit Agreement and the Subsidiary
Loan Agreement, the Participating Banks shall submit sub-projects
to the Association for financing out of the proceeds of the Credit
allocated under Category (1) of the table set forth in paragraph 1
of Schedule 1 to the Development Credit Agreement.
(b) When presenting a sub-loan (other than a free-limit
sub-loan) to the Association for approval, the Participating Banks
shall furnish to the Association an application, in form satis-
factory to the Association, together with (i) a description of the
sub-borrower and an appraisal of the sub-project, including a
description of the expenditures proposed to be financed out
of the proceeds of the Credit, (ii) the proposed terms and condi-
tions of the sub-loan, including the schedule of amortization of
the sub-loan, and (iii) such other information as the Association
shall reasonably request.
(c) Each request by the Participating Banks for authoriza-
tion to make withdrawals from the Credit Account in respect of a
free-limit sub-loan shall contain (i) a summary description of the
sub-borrower and the sub-project, including the cost of the
sub-project and a description of the expenditures proposed to be
financed out of the proceeds of the Credit, and (ii) the terms and
conditions of the sub-loan, including the schedule of amortization
therefor.


﻿-3-
(d) The amortization schedule applicable to each sub-loan
shall provide for an appropriate period of grace, if any, not
exceeding three years, and, unless the Association and the Parti-
cipating Banks shall otherwise agree, shall not extend beyond
twelve years from the date of approval by the Association of such
sub-loan, or in the case of a free-limit subloan, of authorization
by the Association to make withdrawal from the Credit Account
in respect thereof.
(e) In making a sub-loan to a sub-borrower, the Partici-
pating Banks shall charge interest at an annual rate of not less
than eleven per cent (11%) on the principal amount of such sub-
loan withdrawn and outstanding from time to time.
(f) Except as the Association and the Participating Banks
shall otherwise agree, applications and requests made pursuant
to the provisions of paragraphs (a) and (b) of this Section
shall be presented to the Association on or before December 31,
1983.
Section 2.04. (a) The Participating Banks undertake that,
unless the Association shall otherwise agree, any sub-loan will
be made on terms whereby the Participating Banks shall obtain, by
written contract with the sub-borrower or by other appropriate
legal means, rights adequate to protect the interests of the
Association and the Participating Banks, including the right of
the Participating Banks to: (i) require the sub-borrower to carry
out and operate the sub-project with due diligence and efficiency
and in accordance with sound technical, financial and managerial
standards and to maintain adequate records; (ii) require that: (1)
the goods and services to be financed out of the proceeds of the
Credit shall be purchased at a reasonable price, account being
taken also of other relevant factors such as time of delivery and
efficiency and reliability of the goods and availability of
maintenance facilities and spare parts therefor, and, in the case
of services, of their quality and the competence of the parties
rendering them; and (2) such goods and services shall be used
exclusively in the carrying out of the sub-project; (iii) inspect,
by itself or jointly with representatives of the Association if
the Association shall so request, such goods and the sites,
works, plants and construction included in the sub-project,
the operation thereof, and any relevant records and documents;
(iv) require that: (1) the sub-borrower shall take out and main-
tain with responsible insurers such insurance, against such risks
and in such amounts, as shall be consistent with sound business


﻿-4-
practice; and (2) without any limitation upon the foregoing, such
insurance shall cover hazards incident to the acquisition, trans-
portation and delivery of goods financed out of the proceeds of
the Credit to the place of use or installation, any indemnity
thereunder to be made payable in a currency freely usable by the
sub-borrower to replace or repair such goods; (v) obtain all such
information as the Association or the Participating Banks shall
reasonably request relating to the foregoing and to the adminis-
tration, operations and financial condition of the sub-borrower
and to the benefits to be derived from the sub-project; and (vi)
suspend or terminate the right of the sub-borrower to the use of
the proceeds of the Credit upon failure by such sub-borrower to
perform its obligations under its contract with the Participating
Banks.
(b) The Participating Banks shall exercise their rights in
relation to each sub-project in such manner as to: (i) protect
the interests of the Association and the Participating Banks;
(ii) comply with their obligations under this Agreement; and
(iii) achieve the purposes of Part A of the Project.
Section 2.05. The Participating Banks shall furnish to the
Association at regular intervals all such information as the
Association shall reasonably request concerning the expenditure of
the proceeds of the Credit relent to them, Part A of the Project,
the sub-borrowers, the sub-projects, the sub-loans and, where
appropriate, the benefits to be derived from the foregoing.
Section 2.06. (a) The Participating Banks: (i) shall maintain
records and procedures adequate to record and monitor the progress
of Part A of the Project and of each sub-project (including its
cost and the benefits to be derived from it), to identify the
goods and services financed out of the procer-ds of the Credit
relent to them by the Borrower, and to disclose their use in the
Project; (ii) shall take all steps necessary on their part to
enable the Association's accredited representatives to visit all
plants, installations, sites, works, buildings, property and
equipment of each sub-borrower and to examine the goods financed
out of the proceeds of the Credit relent to them by the Borrower
and any relevant records and documents; (iii) shall furnish to
the Association at regular intervals all such information as
the Association shall reasonably request concerning the expen-
ditures of the proceeds of the Credit relent to them, Part A of
the Project, the sub-borrowers, the sub-projects, the sub-loans
and, where appropriate, the benefits to be derived from the

foregoing; and (iv) shall, without limiting the generality of the
foregoing prepare and furnish to the Association a semi-annual
report listing approvals, commitments and disbursements in respect
of sub-loans.
(b) Within six months after the Closing Date or such later
date as may be agreed for this purpose between the Participating
Banks and the Association, the Participating Banks shall prepare
and furnish to the Association a report, of such scope and in such
detail as the Association shall reasonably request, on the execu-
tion and initial operation of the sub-projects, their costs and
the benefits derived and to be derived from them, the performance
by the Participating Banks and the Association of their respective
obligations under this Agreement and the accomplishment of the
purposes of the Credit.
Section 2.07. The Participating Banks shall duly perform all
their obligations under the Subsidiary Loan Agreement. Except as
the Borrower and the Association shall otherwise agree, the
Participating Banks shall not take or concur in any action which
would have the effect of amending, abrogating, assigning or
waiving the Subsidiary Loan Agreement or any provision thereof.
Section 2.08. (a) The Participating Banks shall at the
request of the Borrower and the Association, exchange views with
the Association with regard to the progress of Parts A and D (1)
of the Project, the performance of their obligations under this
Agreement and under the Subsidiary Loan Agreement, and other
matters relating to the purposes of the Credit.
(b) The Participating Banks shall inform the Association of
any condition which interferes or threatens to interfere with the
progress of Parts A and D (1) of the Project, the accomplishment
of the purposes of the Credit, or the performance by the Partici-
pating Banks of their obligations under this Agreement and under
the Subsidiary Loan Agreement.
Section 2.09. Each of the Participating Banks shall furnish
to the Borrower and the Association, not later than June 30, 1980:
(i) the operational procedures for the administration of sub-loans
provided under Part A of the Project including the staffing
requirements thereof; and (ii) an indicative agro-industries
lending program, including projections of future operations and an
action plan for serving small business concerns outside major
urban cities.

ARTICLE HM
Financial Covenants
Section 3.01. The Participating Banks shall maintain records
adequate to reflect in accordance with consistently maintained
appropriate accounting practices their operations and financial
condition.
Section 3.02 (a) The Participating Banks shall establish and
maintain separate, accounts for Parts A and D (1) of the Project.
(b) The Participating Banks shall: (i) have their accounts
(including the separate accounts referred to in sub-Section (a)
of this Section) and financial statements (balance sheets, state-
ments of income and expenses and related statements) for each
fiscal year audited, in accordance with appropriate auditing
principles consistently applied, by independent auditors accept-
able to the Association; (ii) furnish to the Association as soon
as available, but in any case not later than six months after the
end of each such year, (A) certified copies of their respective
financial statements for such year as so audited and (B) the
report of such audit by said auditors, of such scope and in such
detail as the Association shall have reasonably requested; and
(iii) furnish to the Association such other information concerning
the accounts and financial statements of the Participating Banks
and the audit thereof as the Association shall from time to time
reasonably request.
ARTICLE IV
Effective Date; Termination;
Cancellation and Suspension
Section 4.01. This Agreement shall come into force and
effect on the date upon which the Development Credit Agreement
becomes effective.
Section 4.02. (a) This Agreement and all obligations of
the Association and of the Participating Banks thereunder shall
terminate on the earlier of the following two dates:
(i) the date on which the Development Credit Agreement
shall terminate in accordance with its terms; or
(ii) a date 20 years after the date of this Agreement.


﻿-7-
(b) If the Development Credit Agreement terminates in
accordance with its terms before the date specified in paragraph
(a) (ii) of this Section, the Association shall promptly notify
the Participating Banks of this event.
Section 4.03. All the provisions of this Agreement shall
continue in full force and effect notwithstanding any cancellation
or suspension under the General Conditions.
