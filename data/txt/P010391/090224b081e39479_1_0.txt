WHEREAS (A) the Borrower, having satisfied itself as to the
feasibility and priority of the Project described in Schedule 2 to
this Agreement, has requested the Association to assist in the
financing of the Project;
(B)   the Project will be carried out by the State of West
Bengal with the Borrower's assistance and, as part of such assis-
tance, the Borrower will make available to such State the proceeds
of the Credit as provided in this Agreement; and
WHEREAS the Association has agreed, on the basis, inter alia,

ARTICLE II
The Credit
Section 2.01. The Association agrees to lend to the Borrower,
on the terms and conditions set forth or referred to in the
Development Credit Agreement, an amount in various currencies
equivalent to twenty-four million four hundred thousand Special
Drawing Rights (SDR 24,400,000).
Section 2.02. (a) The amount of the Credit may be withdrawn
from the Credit Account in accordance with the provisions of
Schedule 1 to this Agreement for expenditures made (or, if the
Association shall so agree, to be made) in respect of the reasonable
cost of goods and services required for the Project and to be
financed out of the proceeds of the Credit.
(b)   The Borrower shall, for the purposes of the Project,
open and maintain in dollars a special deposit account in the
Reserve Bank of India on terms and conditions satisfactory to the
Association. Deposits into, and payments out of, the Special Account
shall be made in accordance with the provisions of Schedule 3 to
this Agreement.
Section 2.03. The Closing Date shall be September 30, 1997 or
such later date as the Association shall establish. The Association
shall promptly notify the Borrower of such later date.
Section 2.04. (a) The Borrower shall pay to the Association
a commitment charge on the principal amount of the Credit not
withdrawn from time to time at a rate to be set by the Association
as of June 30 of each year, but not to exceed the rate of one-half
of one percent (1/2 of 1%) per annum.


﻿-3-
(b)   The commitment charge shall accrue: (i) from the date
sixty days after the date of this Agreement (the accrual date) to
the respective dates on which amounts shall be withdrawn by the
Borrower from the Credit Account or cancelled; and (ii) at the rate
set as of the June 30 immediately preceding the accrual date and at
such other rates as may be set from time to time thereafter pursuant
to paragraph (a) abovka. The rate set as of June 30 in each year
shall be applied from the next date in that year specified in
Section 2.06 of this Agreement.
(c)   The commitment charge shall be paid: (i) at such places
as the Association shall reasonably request; (ii) without restric-
tions of any kind imposed by, or in the territory of, the Borrower;
and (iii) in the currency specified in this Agreement for the
purposes of Section 4.02 of the General Conditions or in such other
eligible currency or currencies as may from time to time be
designated or selected pursuant to the provisions of that Section.
Section 2.05. The Borrower shall pay to the Association a
service charge at the rate of three-fourths of one percent (3/4 of
1%) per annum on the principal amount of the Credit withdrawn and
outstanding from time to time.
Section 2.06. Commitment charges and service charges shall be
payable semiannually on January 1 and July 1 in each year.
Section 2.07. (a) Subject to paragraphs (b) and (c) below,
the Borrower shall repay the principal amount of the Credit in semi-
annual installments payable on each January 1 and July 1 commencing
July 1, 2002 and ending January 1, 2027. Each installment to and
including the installment payable on January 1, 2012 shall be one
and one-fourth percent (1-1/4%) of such principal amount, and each
installment thereafter shall be two and one-half percent (2-1/2%)
of such principal amount.
(b)   Whenever (i) the Borrower's gross national product per
capita, as determined by the Association, shall have exceeded $790
in constant 1985 dollars for five consecutive years, and (ii) the
Bank shall consider the Borrower creditworthy for Bank lending, the
Association may, subsequent to the review and approval thereof by
the Executive Directors of the Association and after due considera-
tion by them of the development of the Borrower's economy, modify
the terms of repayment of installments under paragraph (a) above by
requiring the Borrower to repay twice the amount of each such
installment not yet due until the principal amount of the Credit

Association may revise such modification to include, in lieu of some
or all of the increase in the amounts of such installments, the
payment of interest at an annual rate agreed with the Association
on the principal amount of the Credit withdrawn and outstanding from
time to time, provided that, in the judgment of the Association,

(c)   If, at any time after a modification of terms pursuant

SCHEDULE 2
Description of the Project
The objective of the Project is to support the State of West
Bengal's forestry development program with the aim, inter alia, to
prevent continuing degradation of forest lands, install a sus-
tainable protection system, -nhance forest productivity and conserve
biodiversity.
The Project consists of the following parts, subject to such
modifications thereof as the Borrower and the Association may agree
upon from time to time to achieve such objectives:
Part A:     Forest Regeneration and Afforestation
Forest regeneration and afforestation on about 290,000 ha of
lands consisting of:
1.    rehabilitation of about 169,000 ha of degraded forest lands;
2.    production forestry on about 7,000 ha of forest lands; and
3.    strip plantations on about 5,000 ha of public lands, and
expansion of farm forestry, including promotion of boundary
plantings and agro-forestry systems on about 109,000 ha of private
lands.
Part B:     Supporting Infrastructure Works
1.    Carrying out of surveys of forest lands for the purpose of
establishing boundaries and constructing about 7,000 kms of
demarcation.
2.    Carrying out of maintenance and improvement of forest roads
in West Bengal consisting of about 150 kms in the southwest, about
150 kms in the north Bengal plains and about 15 kms in the hilly
areas.
3.    Construction of small dams for purposes of soil conservation
and groundwater recharge and construction of facilities for supply
of drinking water and pond excavation.


﻿- 13 -
Part C:     Special Action Program
Undertaking the following programs:
1.    Research and Plant Propagation
(a)   Carrying  out  forestry   research  on  silvicultural
management, seed sources and handling, and plant propagation.
(b)   Provision of technical assistance to FD for strengthen-
ing research staff.
(c)   Construction of seed storage, handling and testing
facilities for FD.
2.    Training
(a)   Provision of in-service training and overseas and
domestic training and study tours to staff of FD, including
participation in national and international conferences and
workshops.
(b)   Construction  of  training  centers, preparation   of
training materials, publications and journals for FD, and provision
of audiovisual equipment and a publicity van to FD.
3.    Joint Management
(a)   Provision of training for forest protection committee
leaders and FD staff in microplanning and group organization,
production management, and processing and marketing.
(b)   Carrying out of research on non-wood forest product
processing and on methods to reduce fuelwood consumption, including
preparation and implementation of pilot action plans based on the
results of such research.
(c)   Services of consultants to assist FD in joint forest
management activities and provision of training to staff of non-
governmental organizations.
(d)   Strengthening institutions engaged in tree planting
programs.


﻿- 14 -
4.    Wildlife and Biodiversity
(a)   Carrying out of studies on preservation of wildlife and
biodiversity, including preparation and implementation of action
plans based on the results of such studies.
(b)   Establishment of a wildlife research unit, including
provision of staff, building, equipment and vehicles.
5.    Mangrove Rehabilitation
(a)   Afforestation of about 3,300 ha of embankments with
mangrove species.
(b)   Protection of about 25,000 ha of degraded forest lands.
(c)   Construction of about 100 fish and shrimp ponds, and
provision of about 1,500 beehives, about 5,000 smokeless chullas
and about 50 solar lights.
(d)   Carrying out of mangrove field trials and studies.
(e)   Provision of laboratory, staff, vehicles and boats.
Part D:     Service Support
(a)   Construction of about 100 staff quarters, about 24
divisional and range offices and about 4 seed stores, rehabilitation
of existing buildings, and provision of staff, vehicles and
equipment for FD.
(b)   Improving the management information system, publicity
and extension services, and carrying out monitoring and evaluation
of Parts A, B, C and D of the Project.
Part E:     Fodder Development
1.    Carrying out of research on forages and seed multiplication.
2.    Provision of training, extension services, staff, vehicles,
equipment, seeds, and training and publicity materials to ARDD.
3.    Construction of seed godowns, seed stores and laboratories,
training centers, offices and staff quarters for ARDD.


﻿- 15 -
4.    Carrying out of monitoring and evaluation of this part of the
Project.
The Project is expected to be completed by March 31, 1997.
