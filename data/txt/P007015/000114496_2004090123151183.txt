First Amendment
Dear Sir,
Please refer to the Loan Agreement (Provincial Health Services Project) dated 
March 9, 1998 between the Dominican Republic (the Borrower) and the International Bank 
for Reconstruction and Development (the Bank).
Pursuant to your request for assistance in the financing of certain 
reconstruction activities for damage causes by Hurricane Georges detailed in the aide 
memoire signed by the Government and the Bank on October 3, 1998, the Bank hereby 
agrees to amend the Loan Agreement as follows:
1.
Section 1.02
(a) new definitions are added as paragraphs (s), (t) and (u) to read as follows:
(s)  "Emergency Subprojects" means investments for the rehabilitation, repair 
and equipment of health facilities and basic water and sanitation systems and for the 
acquisition and distribution of pharmaceuticals and other medical supplies required to 
prevent and/or mitigate the damage caused by Hurricane Georges and which are eligible 
for financing in accordance with the Operational Manual, as hereinafter defined;
(t)  Hurricane Georges means a hurricane, at a variable intensity of 2 to 3 on 
the international scale, that hit the Dominican Republic on Tuesday, 22 September 
1998.
(u)  Operational Manual for Part D of the Project" means the manual, 
satisfactory to the Bank, to be approved by the Borrower in accordance with the 
provisions of this letter, setting forth the procedures for implementation of Part D 
of the project including, inter alia, the eligibility criteria of Emergency 
Subprojects, the contractual and administrative arrangements for implementation of 
Emergency Subprojects, and the flow of funds, and the monitoring and evaluation 
procedures for Part D of the Project, as the same may be amended from time to time by 
agreement between the Borrower and the Bank;
2.  
Section 3.01 is amended:
(a)  by deleting the words "Parts B and C" and adding the words "Part B, C and 
D".
3.  
Section 3.03 (a) is amended:
(a)  (ii) by adding after the words "Participating Provinces" the words  ", as 
well as the implementation of Emergency Subprojects under Part D of the Project."
4.  
A new section is added to read as follows:
Section 3.11.  The Borrower shall ensure that Emergency Subprojects are 
appraised, selected, carried out, financed, monitored and supervised in accordance 
with the provisions of the Operational Manual for Part D of the Project.

Paragraph 4 is amended to read as follows: 
"(a) Works other than those referred to in paragraph (b) below, estimated to 
cost $50,000 equivalent or less per contract, up to an amount not to exceed $2,300,000 
equivalent, may be procured under lump-sum, fixed-price contracts awarded on the basis 
of quotations obtained from three qualified domestic contractors in response to a 
written invitation.  The invitation shall include a detailed description of the works, 
including basic specifications, the required completion date, a basic form of 
agreement acceptable to the Bank, and relevant drawings, where applicable.  The award 
shall be made to the contractor who offers the lowest price quotation for the required 
work, and who has the experience and resources to complete the contract 
successfully.""
"(b) Works for Emergency Subprojects under contracts estimated to cost between 
$5,000 and $200,000 equivalent per contract up to an aggregate amount of $800,000 
equivalent may be procured under lump-sum, fixed-price contracts awarded on the basis 
of quotations obtained from three qualified domestic contractors in response to a 
written invitation.  The invitation shall include a detailed description of the works, 
including basic specifications, the required completion date, a basic form of 
agreement acceptable to the Bank, and relevant drawings, where applicable.  The award 
shall be made to the contractor who offers the lowest price quotation for the required 
work, and who has the experience and resources to complete the contract successfully.  
Works estimated to cost less than $5,000 equivalent may be procured through direct 
contracting up to an aggregate amount of $200,000 equivalent."

be procured under contracts awarded on the basis of direct contracting."
