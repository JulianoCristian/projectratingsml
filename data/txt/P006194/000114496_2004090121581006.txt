(Environmental Technical Assistance Project)
Second Amendment
Dear Sir/Madam:
Please refer to the Development Credit Agreement between the Republic of Bolivia 
(the Borrower) and the International Development Association (the Association) dated 
December 21, 1992, as amended on January 11, 1994.
We are pleased to inform you that pursuant to your request in letter dated 
August 23, 1994 and subject to the Association having received evidence satisfactory 
to the Association that the agreement referred to in Section 3.02 (c) of the 
development credit agreement has been amended incorporating --mutatis mutandis-- the 
provisions of this amendment, the Association hereby agrees to amend the Development 
Credit Agreement as follows:
1.
Section 1.02
(a)
paragraph (a) is deleted;
(b)
paragraph 0) is amended by adding at the end of the paragraph the words 
"as amended on March 14, 1994;"
(c)
paragraph (n) is deleted;
(d)
paragraph (o) is amended by replacing the acronym "LIDEMN' by the acronym 
"SEMTA" in the first line and the acronyms "LIDEMA, CIEC, ASEO and SEMTA" by "SEMTA 
and CIEC" in the second line;
(e)
paragraphs (u), (v), and (w) are deleted and new paragraphs (u), (v), (w) 
and (x) are added to read as follows:
"(u) "NlDSNIN' means NEnisterio de Desarrollo Sostenible v Medio 
Ambiente, the Borrower's ministry of sustainable development and environment;
(v) "SNRNN4N' means Secretaria Nacional de Recursos Naturqles-Y Medio 
Ambiente, the national environmental secretariat within MDSN4A; and
(w) "SNRNMA's Work Program and Budget" means the annual program of 
activities 
to be carried out by SNRNMA, including its policy, envirorunental 
quality control, protected areas and legal aspects, identifying the sources of 
financing for all such activities."
2.

"(ii) SENITA and CIEC in respect of Part C.2 of the Project,"
3.

"(ii) SETMTA and CIEC for purposes of Part C.2 of the Project."
4.
Section 3. 01 (d): The acronym "LIDEMN' in all places where it appears is 
deleted and replaced by "SEMTA".
