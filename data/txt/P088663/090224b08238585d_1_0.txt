                         Project Development Objectives
                         Project Development Objective (from Project Appraisal Document)

                         The objectives of the Project are to (i) expand and enhance the family medicine model of primary health care; (ii) build managementcapacity in the sector; and (iii)
                         strengthen the policy making process through the development and implementation of a system for monitoring and evaluating sector performance.

                         Implementation Status Overview
                         All activities planned under the Additional Financing (AF) are expected to be completed by the closing date of December 31, 2014. Project Development Objective indicators 2, 3 and
                         5 are on track; PDO indicators 1 and 4 have been achieved. Most of intermediate outcome indicators are also on track to be achieved.

                         Implementation of activities in the Federation of Bosnia and Herzegovina (FBiH) have shown significant progress in all components:
                         •      The pilots to incentivize preventive services for Non Communicable Disease by introducing pay-for-performance started in early January 2013 and were completed on

                         December 31, 2013. The final report will be submitted to the Bank by March 30, 2014.
                         •     The procurement process to rehabilitate the last group of ambulantas (phase 5) was launched. These civil works will be financed with proceeds from the CEB Loan.

                         •       Regarding the development of information system for Primary Health Care: the rulebook on Information Technology (IT) Standards was formally approved; and the

                         procurement of all remaining IT equipment was launched and will be financed by both IDA and CEB funds.
                         •       The procurement of the software to be installed in the ambulantas will be launched upon completion of the accreditation of the software application.
                         •       A total of 17 sub-projects (grants) out of 68 applications received were selected; their implementation in the field is being monitored.

                         In Republica Sprska (RS), activities are also on track in accordance with the updated Procurement Plan.
                         All civil works are completed.
                         While only 36 Family Medicine (FM) team have valid accreditation, the training and process to accreditate the remaining FM teams was re-launched and it is expected that 240 teams
                         will complete the process by the end of the Project.
                         The only pending activity is the development and dissemination of clinical guidelines, which is planned by 2014.
