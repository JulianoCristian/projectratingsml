                         Project Development Objectives
                         Project Development Objective (from Project Appraisal Document)

                         The project development objective is to achieve immediate peace dividends for targeted women in Southern Sudan, through the provision of: (a) improvements in
                         access to the existing economic opportunities and (b) support to the Ministry of Gender to develop gender policies and strategies.

                         The Country GenderAssessment under component 3 was delayed duetothewithdrawalandtermination oftheselectionprocess because ofmisinterpretation of the Firm's experience and
                         capacity.AnimmediateExpressionofInterest wassoughtand a newfirm wasshortlisted and on board. Since the supervision of November 2011,theCountry GenderAssessmentis
                         nowcompleteand theconsultantsmade their final presentation to the stakeholders in Juba onJune15,2012.TheConsultants-UgandaWomen'sNetwork(UWONET)presented their report
                         highlighting issues negatively affectingwomensuchashighmaternalmortality,pooraccess toeducation andbasic services such as clean water, and gender based violence.
                         Itwasobservedthat eventhough there waslegislationaiming toprotect women, it was not enforced. Among the recommendations
                         istheneedtohavepoliciesencouragingeconomicempowerment forwomen and to target poverty reduction efforts to women and childreninruralareas.Inthe watersector, itwassuggested
                         that thepolicy should take into account gender issues such as, water for allwithinashortwalkingdistanceinruralareas where its oftenthe women and girls who often have to walk
                         between 3-6 kmstofetchwater.Ineducation,measurestoimproving the retention ofgirls in schools, such as policy against earlyandforcedmarriagesremaincritical.Fortheagricultural
                         sector, increasingwomen's access and control over land as well as toagricultureinput,suchasimprovedseedsandtechnical training program toenhance their productivity and improve
                         livelihoods. In orderfortheMoGC&SWtoperform it'srole,technical capacity for gendersensitivity must be improved, including

                         itisrecommendedthattheMinistrymonitorcompliance with existing lawsthat enhance the participation of of women intheeconomicdevelopmentofthecountry.Therecommendations from
