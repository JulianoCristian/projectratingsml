5. Credit 
related PPF: 
 
A
PPF (project preparation fund) was approved and went into effect in mid-June 2004 to assist the 
Government to lay the preparatory work for the credit.   The PPF  for US$ 542,400.00  consists of 
several sub-component:  (i) Upgrading of PIU set up and skills to match World Bank standards :  
training in procurement, hiring of a procurement specialist; hiring of a consultant to help develop a 
Financial, Administrative and Procurement Manual of procedures;  design and install a Financial 
Management System (FMS) at the PIU (including the information system and necessary training); 
and operational costs of the PIU. (ii) In public finance management, in PRGF negotiations, studies 
on nomenclature harmonization, macro-economics and network system functionsl revision of 
financial manual and PRSP monitoring.  (iii) In the petroleum sector, staffing of the National 
Petroleum Agency and extension; repairs of buildings and data network installation;  and TA 
consultancies for architect consultancy, study on revision of exploration results and technical 
assistance in information system management.  
 
The PPF contains  a stand alone procurement plan and the present procurement plan will not 
contain any PPF activities.    The PPF is slated to close February 15, 2004.
