                         Implementation Status Overview
                         As the first operation in a programmatic series of three, the Uganda PRSC 8 has been implemented successfully. The achievements under the 8th PRSC operation contributed
                         towards fulfilling the Program Development Objective (PDO). The 8th PRSC operation was the first one prepared and executed under the Joint Budget Support Framework (JBSF).
                         All prior actions of the operation were completed. The prior actions were:
Public Disclosure Copy




                         1. (a) Actual annual budget releases for the sum of conditional grants to each of the four JBSF sectors (health, education, water and sanitation, and roads) were at least 95% of the
                         corresponding approved budget allocations for the Fiscal Year 2008/2009; (b) budget releases for the sum of recurrent wage expenditure across the four sectors, the sum of
                         recurrent non-wage expenditure across these sectors, and the sum of development expenditures across these sectors were in each case at least 95% of the corresponding approved
                         budget allocations for the Fiscal Year 2008/2009

                         2. Releases from the treasury to spending agencies are made on a quarterly instead of monthly basis, through the end of the first quarter of Fiscal Year 2009/10

                         3. Develop format for performance agreements for accounting officers (including Chief Administrative Officers) with an incentive and penalty system for non compliance with PFM
                         regulations

                         4. Procurement performance indicator framework agreed and baseline data available

                         5. Adoption by Government of the framework for attracting and retaining public officers in Hard-to-Reach Areas

                         6. Establish Human Resources for Health Management Information System to provide information on levels and distribution of the health workforce

                         7. Sign performance contracts with at least 90 percent of all head teachers in the 12 districts with the weakest education sector indicators (covering customized performance targets)

                         8. Make Community Based Maintenance Systems (CBMS) Water User Committees actively functioning in at least 50% of districts, and within these districts on average at least 40%
                         of water points are covered by Water User Committees

                         9. Road Fund Board and Executive Director appointed

                         10. Cabinet approves policy on strengthening of national construction industry
