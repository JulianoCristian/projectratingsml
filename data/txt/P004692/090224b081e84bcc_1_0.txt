ARTICLE I
General Conditions; Definitions
Section 1.01. The parties to this Agreement accept all the
provisions of the General Conditions Applicable to Loan and
Guarantee Agreements of the Bank, dated March 15, 1974., with the
same force and effect as if they were fully set forth herein (said
General Conditions Applicable to Loan and Guarantee Agreements of
the Bank being hereinafter called the General Conditions).
Section 1.02. Wherever used in this Agreer,ient, unless the
context otherwise requires, the several terms defined in the
General Conditions have the respective meanings therein set forth
and the following additional terms have the following meanings:
(a) "Estate" means the industrial estate to be established
under the Project;
(b) "Estate Enterprise" means any industrial enterprise
located on the Estate;
(c) "TOT" means the Telephone Organization of Thailand;
(d) "MEA" means the Metropolitan Electricity Authority;
(e) "NHA" means the National Housing Authority; and
(f) "Baht" and "Bht" mean the currency of the Kingdom of
Thailand.

terms and conditions in the Loan Agreement set forth or referred
to, an amount in various currencies equivalent to four million
seven hundred and fifty thousand dollars ($4,750,000).

SCHEDULE 2
Description of the Project
The Project is the establishment of an industrial estate
at Lat Krabang, about 35 kilometers east of Bangkok, to accelerate
industrial growth and to assist in the development of medium and
small-scale industries. The Project consists of the following
parts:
Part A:   Land
(1) The acquisition of about 162 hectares of raw land for
the Estate at Lat Krabang, for subdivision and devel-
opment into industrial sites suitable for about 120
medium-scale factories and the establishment of an
Export Processing Zone on about 20 hectares of the
Estate.
(2) Land filling, grading and compacting of selected areas
of the Estate; the construction of a dike system around,
and the installation of a surface draining system
within, the Estate.
Part B:   Roads
The construction of an internal road network on the
Estate (including street lighting) consisting of about
10.2 kilometers of paved roads about 8.5 meters wide to
provide access to each plot in the Estate.
Part C:   Utilities
(1) The installation of an electric power distribution
system on the Estate consisting of:
(a) a power connection of about 25 MW capacity;
and
(b) an   internal   electric   distribution    system
(excluding street lighting).
(2) The installation of a water supply and distribution
system on the Estate, consisting of two ground
level water reservoirs each holding about 500 m3


﻿- 12 -
of water; two pumping stations; two elevated storage
tanks holding about 300 m3 of water and the necessary
main lines and distribution piping, including fire
hydrants.
(3) The construction of a central liquid waste treat-
ment plant and a sewage collection network on the
Estate.
(4) The installation of about 500 telephone lines from
TOT's subexchange at Lat Krabang to the Estate.
Part D:   Buildings
The construction of the following buildings:
(a) a central administration building with a floor
arca of about 900 m2 and a storeroom and workshop
with an aggregate floor area of about 450 m2;
(b) five standard industrial buildings, four of which
would be situated in the Export Processing Zone of
the Estate, with a floor area of about 2,160 m2
each, designed to accommodate a total of about 25
small-scale Estate Enterprises; and
(c) housing accommodation for Estate management,
security and maintenance personnel with an aggre-
gate floor area of about 2,500 m2.
Part E:   Training and Technical Assistance
The provision of short-term overseas training fellow-
ships for key staff of the Borrower and technical
assistance to the Borrower to assist it in: (i) pre-
paring rules and regulations governing the eligibility
of applicants for land or buildings in the Estate;
factory, building and operating standards, and standard
contract forms; (ii) promoting Estate occupancy; (iii)
maintaining liaison with engineering and civil works
contractors; (iv) evaluating new industrial estate
projects and (v) formulating a national industrial
estate development program.
The Project is expected to be completed by December 31, 1980.
