                         Project Development Objectives
                         Project Development Objective (from Project Appraisal Document)

                         The development objective of the proposed project is to establish a decentralized beneficiary-driven land distribution mechanism that allows organized landless or poor
                         farmers to acquire suitable agricultural lands and implement investment subprojects which puts them on a sustainable, higher-income, livelihood path.

                         Implementation Status Overview
                         The Project is progressing well. It has helped to establish 236 Productive Associations, benefiting over 2,800, mainly poor indigenous families; almost 155,000 hectares have been
                         accessed by these associations for potential productive uses. The Project is also providing technical assistance to the associations to ensure that their activities are successful.

                         Key Decisions Regarding Implementation
                         The Project will expand its activities to two new municipalities within Santa Cruz Department -- Concepcion and San Ignacio de Velasco, while enhancing its focus on promoting
                         productive activities in indigenous peoples’ territories (TCOs). The next implementation support mission is planned for February 2013.
