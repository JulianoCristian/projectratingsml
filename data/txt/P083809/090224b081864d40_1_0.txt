                         Project Development Objectives
                         Project Development Objective (from Project Appraisal Document)

                         The Project Development Objective (PDO) is to support the Government’s efforts for the creation of sustainable conditions conducive to enterprise development and
                         growth, encouraging investment, facilitating private sector development, increasing micro, small and medium-sized enterprises, increasing competitiveness in local and
                         export markets and employment opportunities in its territory.

                         Implementation Status Overview
                         The economy has continued to face challenges with a new wave of global financial shocks. The private sector is still important to create jobs and also be competitive. Through this
                         project the World Bank is supporting the development of land reform which is crucial to provide access to land for all investments, improving financial services, enhancement of
                         enterprise competitiveness, improving business environment, all in all to support the development of a vibrant private sector.

                         As of November 2012, the following accomplishments were realized:

                         Component 1

                         Sub-component 1.1 - Providing basic infrastructure services - Establishing the industrial park - Government canceled this.
                         Sub-component 1.2 - Financial sector deepening - Five indicator targets out of 7 achieved and the other 2 are close to be fully achieved. Credit reference bureau established;
                         Securities Central Depository established at the Uganda securities Exchange; FINSCOPE survey done; Four banks linked to bankom inter-switch; mortgages and leasing lending
                         supported;financial market development supported.

                         Component 2 -Enhancing enterprise competitiveness - Indicator targets were achieved - The cumulative performance of the Scheme stands at 1,312 activities allocated and US $
                         3,707,113 disbursed. The number of beneficiaries was 7,528 of which 2,966 (39%) were women. The grant distribution ratio in terms of activities supported outside Kampala stands
                         at 36% which is 6% above the target.

                         Component 3 - Improving business environment -
                         For Land;
                         •         Revival and rehabilitation of the School of Surveying (renamed Institute of Surveying and Land Management – ISLM) to train professionals for modern land administration
                         and Development and approval of a new Curriculum for ISLM.
                         •         Construction/renovation of 13 land offices, Land Information Center, Records & Archival Center & a Resource Center at ISLM and Rehabilitation and basic computerization
                         of land records in the mailo land registry in Kampala for records of Kampala, Wakiso and Mukono districts;
                         •         Scanned all leasehold and freeholds, majority of maps and part of mailo land records and Built a geodetic control & base mapping infrastructure to support LIS development
                         in 6 Ministry of lands zonal offices (MZOs)
                         •         Developed a comprehensive national land information system (NLIS) and Undertook an inventory of government land in 5 districts

                         For Business registry - Computers were purchased to capture data and support provided to prepare for the follow up project.

                         For Uganda National Bureau of statistics - Global good agriculture practice supported and also established standards and quality coordination mechanism.

                         For Legal framework on business and financial services - 21 bills drafted/amended and 10 were assented.

                         Presidential Investor Round-table - PIRT established in 2009 and various meetings were held between the President of Uganda and the business community semi-annually to

                         Key Decisions Regarding Implementation
                         The bank with Government engagement restructured the project and extended it for another one year up to February 28, 2013. This extension will enable completion of key activities

                         Government has agreed to pay for the Internet connectivity between the 6 Ministry of lands zonal offices and the national land Information center, during the maintenance period
                         between March 2013 and February 2014, since the IDA project closes on February 28, 2013.
