                         Implementation Status Overview
                         After the recent improvements the project entering a consolidation phase in view of its closure in June 2013. The project has supported learning activities in more than 3,000 villages


                                                                                                                                                                                                          Page 1 of 8
                             The World Bank                                                                                                                                        Report No: ISR9352



                         to help empower farmers, commercialize village production, strengthen interactions between key stakeholders, and support farmers’ agricultural marketing and production initiatives.
                         Nearly 600 new rural extension centers have been built and 200 existing ones renovated. Scaling up of Farmer Managed Activities in support of agribusiness oriented farmer
                         organizations is progressing at the district and provincial level although capacity constraints of local extension services is a limiting factor. An extension model to improve farmer
                         organizations' skills in marketing and organizational management is being developed by the CPMU with the support of the Task Team and implemented across pilot districts.
