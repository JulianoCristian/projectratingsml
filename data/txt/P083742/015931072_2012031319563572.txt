                         Implementation Status Overview
                         Progress toward achievement of project development objectives has improved during recent months. The project has now supported learning activities for farmers in more than 3,000


                                                                                                                                                                                                              Page 1 of 10
                             The World Bank                                                                                                                                      Report No: ISR6063



                         villages across Indonesia that have helped empower farmers, commercialize village production, strengthen interactions between key stakeholders at the local level, and support other
                         agricultural marketing and production initiatives proposed and undertaken by farmer groups themselves as a step toward developing viable agro-enterprises. Nearly 800 rural
                         extension centers have either been completed or are under construction. Other on-going activities are aimed at goals such as (a) strengthening linkages between research, extension
                         services, and farmers, (b) commercializing cell phone software that would give farmers real-time access to supply chain and market information, and (c) disseminating innovative rice

                         crop management tools developed by the International Rice Research Institute. Project management has also become more proactive in terms of addressing some of the projects
                         critical factors. The scaling up of the Farmer Managed Activities to district and provincial level remains a key challenge.
