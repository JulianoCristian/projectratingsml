                         Project Development Objectives
                         Project Development Objective (from Project Appraisal Document)

                         The Project Development Objective (PDO) is to increase access to and improve the quality of urban infrastructure and services in the country#s two largest cities,
                         Abidjan and Bouak#. The achievement of this objective would support GoCI#s efforts to demonstrate visible and tangible improvements in the lives of its citizens, a
                         critical step for sustaining social and political stability and economic recovery in the country.

                         Implementation Status Overview
                         The project implementation is satisfactory and most activities are finalized and most indicator targets are met. The project disbursement progress since the last mission in April 2012

                         is also satisfactory, with a disbursement rate that went from 87.84% to 91.45% for the initial grant and from 37.72% to 74.07% for the additional financing grant.
                         Regarding improving access and quality of basic infrastructures, the achievements recorded do contribute to improving access and quality of urban services for about 4 million of
                         inhabitants of which 52% are women, in Abidjan, Bouaké, Agboville, Man and the secondary centers of Mayo and Tinhou. The intermediary results of the project can be summarized
                         as follows:
Public Disclosure Copy




                         (i) Reduction of the production deficit for drinking water for more than 59,000 m3/day, and reinforcing the distribution network allowed access to improved drinking water service
                         provision to more than one million additional people in the cities of Abidjan and Bouaké,
                         (ii) About 3,140,000 people have access to all seasons drivable roads, thanks to the rehabilitation of more than 100 kilometers of roads and the construction of more than 10
                         kilometers of roads,
                         (iii) About 3,475,000 people have access to an improved sanitation system with the rehabilitation of the existing sewage network and the construction of about 38 kilometers of new
                         sewage networks,
                         (iv) About 4.500.000 people of the District of Abidjan have regular solid waste collection service.
