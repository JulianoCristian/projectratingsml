                         Project Development Objectives
                         Project Development Objective (from Project Appraisal Document)

                         The Project Development Objective of the emergency Repatriation and Livelihood Restoration for Migrant Workers Project (MWP) is to support the People's
                         Republic of Bangladesh to: (i) repatriate its migrant workers, who have fled the ongoing conflict in Libya (#the emergency#), from refugee camps in neighboring
                         countries in a timely manner; and (ii) provide a one-time cash grant as a transitional safety net measure to help them meet immediate basic needs and
                         commence the process of livelihood restoration.

                         Implementation Status Overview
                         The MWP is the first project on migration funded by the World Bank which used fast track emergency procedures to provide $40 million to support the Government of Bangladesh to
                         repatriate Bangladeshi migrant workers from Libya at the onset of the conflict in the country in February 2011, as well as help them restore their livelihoods with the help of a one-time
                         cash grant. Of the $40 million, $12.6 million has been used to retroactively finance the airfare of 10,000 returnees who were unable to pay for their return home that was borne by the

                         International Organization of Migration (IOM) . The implementing agency, the Bangladesh Bureau of Manpower, Employment and Training (BMET) from the Ministry of Expatriates'
                         welfare and Overseas Employment (MEWOE) has also contracted out the implementation of the cash grant to IOM. IOM has set up a call center to facilitate the registration and
                         verification of the migrant workers who have returned from Libya during the period between end February to end March 2011. The call center was initiated on July 9, 2011 and in-
                         person verification began from July 23rd 2011. The call center operated from 9am to 9pm every day except Friday with 17 numbers available for the returnees to call and obtain
Public Disclosure Copy




                         appointments for in-person verification. Those who were verified , the cash grant of about $700 or Taka 50,000 was transferred directly to their respective bank accounts via Sonali
                         Bank. Till date after a month of operation, over 40,000 persons have been given a appointment, some 12,788 have been served in person, and 12,663 persons have been verified to
                         receive the cash grant. The remaining applicants are being processed via a Grievance committee set up by the Government of Bangladesh. This Committee meets once a week to
                         process applications sent to the appeals process as per the Standard Operating Procedures (SOP) prepared for the cash transfer system which is being implemented by IOM.
