Excellency:
Please refer to the Loan Agreement between International Bank for Reconstruction and 
Development (the Bank) and the Argentine Republic (the Borrower) dated March 29, 1999 (the Loan 
Agreement), and the related Implementation Letter of the same date, both with regard to the 
above-captioned Loan.  
We are pleased to inform you that pursuant to your request to reallocate part of the proceeds of 
the Loan (which proceeds were initially allocated to assist the Borrower in the financing of the Year 
2000 Technical Assistance Project of which Part A of said Project has been completed and whose name 
will be changed to State Modernization Project as a result of the execution of this Amendment Letter) to 
finance the carrying out of the Borrower’s modernization of the public sector under the State 
Modernization Project, the Bank agrees to amend the Loan Agreement and Implementation Letter as set 
forth below. Consequently, as a result of the amendments described in said annexes, the Loan Agreement 
now hereby reads in its entirety as set forth in Attachment 5 to this Amendment Letter.

ATTACHMENT 2
“SCHEDULE 2
Description of the Project
The objectives of the Project are:  (a) to assist the Borrower, on an urgent basis, to prevent or 
minimize the disruption in the Borrower’s social and economic infrastructure that would be caused by the 
failure of critical central government systems to process data properly after December 31, 1999 due to 
the Y2K Problem; (b) to assist the Borrower in designing and implementing a program to modernize the 
non-financial public sector in Argentina at the federal level; and (c) to strengthen the institutional 
capacity at the Borrower’s provincial level.
The Project consists of the following parts, subject to such modifications thereof as the Borrower 
and the Bank may agree upon from time to time to achieve such objectives:
Page  12
Part A
: Support Activities
Carrying out of the following activities: 
1.
(a)
Confirmation of initial estimates of Participating Entities and corresponding systems at 
risk by teams of selected consultants; and (b) identification of common approaches, tools and 
methodologies for use by the Participating Entities.
2.
(a)
Execution of a publicity campaign to alert public and private sector entities to the Y2K 
Problem; (b) provision of assistance through a telephone hot-line system; and (c) creation and 
maintenance of a website with best practices and directions to relevant websites.
3.
Technical analysis of diagnostics and evaluation of the economic impact of not solving the Y2K 
Problem, as an input to decision-making in the selection of prospective Participating Entities.
4.
Acquisition of, and provision of training in the use of, tools and methodologies for the Y2K 
Problem executing units located in the Participating Entities, in order to profit from best practices and 
economies of scale. 
5.
Development of common administrative systems for economies of scale and simplicity.
Part B
: Project Administration
1.
Preparation, review and analysis of non-Y2K Problem-compliance impact studies. 
2.      
Ensuring that all contingency plans presented by the Participating Entities are adequate and 
comply with the Operational Manual.
3.      Strengthening of the administrative capacity of the PCU to monitor and supervise the 
implementation of the Project, including, 
inter alia
,
the provision of administrative support required 
therefor.
Part C
: Preparation of a Program for the Modernization of the Non- Financial Public Sector
Provision of technical assistance and training to Borrower staff to design a program to modernize

SCHEDULE 2
Description of the Project 
The objectives of the Project are:  (a) to assist the Borrower, on an urgent basis, to prevent or 
minimize the disruption in the Borrower’s social and economic infrastructure that would be caused by the 
failure of critical central government systems to process data properly after December 31, 1999 due to 
the Y2K Problem; (b) to assist the Borrower in designing and implementing a program to modernize the 
non-financial public sector in Argentina at the federal level; and (c) to strengthen the institutional 
capacity at the Borrower’s provincial level.
The Project consists of the following parts, subject to such modifications thereof as the Borrower 
and the Bank may agree upon from time to time to achieve such objectives:
Part A
: Support Activities
Carrying out of the following activities: 
1.
(a)
Confirmation of initial estimates of Participating Entities and corresponding systems at 
risk by teams of selected consultants; and (b) identification of common approaches, tools and 
methodologies for use by the Participating Entities.
2.
(a)
Execution of a publicity campaign to alert public and private sector entities to the Y2K 
Page  35
Problem; (b) provision of assistance through a telephone hot-line system; and (c) creation and 
maintenance of a website with best practices and directions to relevant websites.
3.
Technical analysis of diagnostics and evaluation of the economic impact of not solving the Y2K 
Problem, as an input to decision-making in the selection of prospective Participating Entities.
4.
Acquisition of, and provision of training in the use of, tools and methodologies for the Y2K 
Problem executing units located in the Participating Entities, in order to profit from best practices and 
economies of scale. 
5.
Development of common administrative systems for economies of scale and simplicity.
Part B
: Project Administration
1.
Preparation, review and analysis of non-Y2K Problem-compliance impact studies. 
2.      
Ensuring that all contingency plans presented by the Participating Entities are adequate and 
comply with the Operational Manual.
3.      Strengthening of the administrative capacity of the PCU to monitor and supervise the 
implementation of the Project, including, 
inter alia
,
the provision of administrative support required 
therefor.
Part C
: Preparation of a Program for the Modernization of the Non- Financial Public Sector
Provision of technical assistance and training to Borrower staff to design a program to modernize 
the non-financial public sector in Argentina, at the federal level, based on: (a) systemic reforms 
involving, 
inter alia
,
human resource management, training, information technology; (b) agency-specific 
reforms involving a process of transformation through restructuring and change management aiming at 
results oriented administration; and (c) strategic reforms involving creation of a national performance 
evaluation system and a program of assistance to facilitate modernization of provincial governments.
Part D
: Streamlining the Macro-Structure of the National Government
(a) 
Carrying out of an analysis of the macro-structure of the non-financial public sector in 
Argentina, at the federal level, for purposes of restructuring, if necessary, said macro-structure, including 
but not limited to the elimination of duplicative and overlapping functions and responsibilities amongst 
Public Agencies and Decentralized Entities and the elimination of the functions and responsibilities 
assigned to said agencies and entities which, in the Borrower’s opinion, are non-essential given the 
ongoing fiscal constraints experienced by the Borrower; and (b) design and implementation of a program 
which will inform Borrower’s officials, civil servants and the population in general, with respect to the 
contents and the recommendations of the analysis mentioned in paragraph (a) herein.

restructure their functions and responsibilities and/or introduce reforms in  the areas of, 
inter alia
,
human 
resources, procurement, physical infrastructure, budget and planning, training of staff, results evaluation

preparation of their multi-annual performance arrangements or multi-annual performance agreements,

administrative procedures governing the disputes between said agencies and/or entities and the

population served by said agencies and/or entities.

SCHEDULE 4
Procurement
Section I
.
Procurement of Goods 
Part A
: General
Goods shall be procured in accordance with the provisions of Section I of the “Guidelines for 
Procurement under IBRD Loans and IDA Credits” published by the Bank in January 1995 and revised in 
January and August 1996, September 1997 and January 1999 (the Guidelines) and the following 
provisions of Section I of this Schedule.
Part B
: International Competitive Bidding
1.
Except as otherwise provided in Part C of this Section, goods shall be procured under contracts 
awarded in accordance with the provisions of Section II of the Guidelines and paragraph 5 of Appendix 1 
thereto.
2.
The following provisions shall apply to goods to be procured under contracts awarded in 
accordance with the provisions of paragraph 1 of this Part B:
(a)
Grouping of Contracts
To the extent practicable, contracts for goods shall be grouped in bid packages estimated to cost 
$350,000 equivalent or more each.
(b)
Preference for Domestically Manufactured Goods
The provisions of paragraphs 2.54 and 2.55 of the Guidelines and Appendix 2 thereto shall apply 
to goods manufactured in the territory of the Borrower.
Page  39
Part C
: Other Procurement Procedures
1.
National Competitive Bidding
(a)
Goods estimated to cost less than $350,000 equivalent per contract or per bid package, 
up to an aggregate amount not to exceed $1,000,000 equivalent, may be procured under contracts 
awarded in accordance with the provisions of paragraphs 3.3 and 3.4 of the Guidelines.
(b)
In the procurement of goods in accordance with this Part C.1, standard bidding 
documents acceptable to the Bank shall be used.
2.
Shopping
Goods estimated to cost $100,000 equivalent or less per contract, up to an aggregate amount not 
to exceed $500,000 equivalent, may be procured under contracts awarded on the basis of shopping 
procedures acceptable to the Bank in accordance with the provisions of paragraphs 3.5 and 3.6 of the 
Guidelines.
Part D
: Review by the Bank of Procurement Decisions
1.
Procurement Planning
Prior to the issuance of any invitations to bid for contracts, the proposed procurement plan for 
the Project shall be furnished to the Bank for its review and approval, in accordance with the provisions 
of paragraph 1 of Appendix 1 to the Guidelines.  Procurement of all goods shall be undertaken in 
accordance with such procurement plan as shall have been approved by the Bank, and with the provisions 
of said paragraph 1.
2.
Prior Review
(a) 
The procedures set forth in paragraphs 2 and 3 of Appendix 1 to the Guidelines shall 
apply with respect to: (i) all contract for goods procured under Part B of this Section; and (ii) the first 
two contracts for goods procured under Part C.1 of this Section. 
(b)
With respect to the first two contracts for goods to be procured in accordance with the 
procedures referred to in Part C.2 above, the following procedures shall apply:
(i)
prior to the selection of any supplier under said Part, the Borrower shall provide 
to the Bank a report on the comparison and evaluation of quotations received;
(ii)
prior to the execution of any contract procured under said Part, the Borrower 
shall provide to the Bank a copy of the specifications and the draft contract; and
(iii)
the procedures set forth in paragraphs 2(f), 2(g) and 3 of Appendix 1 to the 
Guidelines shall apply.
3.
Post Review

: General

1.
Least-cost Selection
Consultants’ services for the Project estimated to cost less than $100,000 equivalent per contract,

Section III
.
Special Provisions
(a) 
In addition and without limitation to any other provisions set forth in this Schedule or the 
Guidelines, the following principles and rules of procurement shall expressly govern all procurement of 
goods referred to in Part C.1 of Section I of this Schedule. The principle and rule of procurement referred 
to in subparagraph (ii) (B) of this paragraph shall also govern all procurement of goods referred to in Part 
B of Section I of this Schedule:
(i) 
Except as the Bank may otherwise agree, bidders shall not be requested or 
permitted to alter their bids after the deadline for receipt of bids. The Borrower 
may ask bidders for clarification needed to evaluate their bids but shall not ask 
or permit bidders to change the substance or price of their bids after the bid 
opening. 
(ii) 
Foreign bidders shall: (A) be allowed to participate in the method of 
procurement referred to in Part C.1 of Section I of this Schedule; and (B) not be 
required to: (1) register in the national registry (
Registro Nacional
); and (2) 
establish domicile in Argentina, as a prerequisite of bidding.
(iii)   
A two envelope procurement system is not permitted. 
(iv) 
After the public opening of bids, information relating to the examination, 
clarification and evaluation of bids and recommendations concerning awards 
shall not be disclosed to bidders or other persons not officially concerned with 
the bidding process until the successful bidder is notified of the award.
(b) 
In addition and without limitation to any other provisions set forth in this Schedule or the 
Consultant Guidelines, the following principles and rules of procurement shall expressly govern all 
procurement of consultants’ services referred to in Parts C.1 and C.2 of Section II of this Schedule. The 
principle and rule of procurement referred to in subparagraph (ii) below shall also govern all 
procurement of  consultants referred to in Part B of Section II of this Schedule:
(i) 
After the public opening of proposals, information relating to the evaluation of 
proposals and recommendations concerning awards shall not be disclosed to the 
consultants who submitted the proposals or to other persons not officially 
concerned with the selection process, until the award of contract is notified to 
the successful firm or individual, as the case may be.
(ii) 
Foreign consultants shall not be required to: (A) register in the national registry (
Registro Nacional
); and (B) establish domicile in Argentina, as a prerequisite for 
presenting their proposals.
