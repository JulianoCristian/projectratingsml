                         Project Development Objectives
                         Project Development Objective (from Project Appraisal Document)

                         The objective of World Bank support is to contribute to the NACP III goal of halting and reversing the AIDS epidemic by attaining the following project
                         development objectives in accordance with the national program's strategic objectives of:
                         1) achieving behavior change by scaling up prevention of new infections in high risk groups and general population; and
                         2) increased care, support and treatment of PLHIV.

                         Implementation Status Overview
                         The national AIDS Control Program, Third Phase (NACP III) continues to make steady progress in the coverage of targeted interventions, scaling up of Integrated Counseling and
                         Testing Centers, Anti Retroviral Therapy services and condom distribution. The targeted prevention interventions have reached almost 82% of female sex workers, 66% of men who

                         reduction in adult HIV prevalence, which declined from 0.39% in 2004 to 0.31% in 2009. Recent estimates show a reduction in new infections of more than 50% over the past decade

                         Key Decisions Regarding Implementation
                         Since the last Joint Implementation Review (December 2010), the National AIDS Control Organization has focused on improving the quality as well as the each of targeted
                         interventions, through development of guidelines, manuals and training and the annual action planning. As of March 31, 2011, a total of 1,671 targeted interventions were operational,
                         with steady increase especially in reaching men having sex with men and migrants. Reports of stock-outs of condoms, drugs and testing kits were discussed during last JIR, and
                         NACO will identify local and generic actions to address this problem including further decentralization of the procurement to meet emergency requirements.
                         Reaching the hard to reach vulnerable population groups remains a priority for the program. A review of harm reduction for the prevention of HIV among injecting drug users has
                         been concluded and the results were discussed at national consultation 27 May. Likewise, a study of HIV prevention among men having sex with men has been conducted; findings
                         of this study will also be disseminated and the recommendations incorporated into program strategies. NACO has made impressive progress in scaling up basic services, including

                         safe blood, management of sexual transmitted infections and the provision of free treatment services, and will continue efforts to ensure increased coordination between testing and
                         treatment services, other related programs, and convergence with the general health services under National Rural Health Mission.
