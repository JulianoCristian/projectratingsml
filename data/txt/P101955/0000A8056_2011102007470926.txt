                         Implementation Status Overview
                         1.         The project has succeeded in rehabilitating 28 water sub-projects supporting livestock-dependent pastorals communities, rehabilitated/upgraded six livestock markets and
                         supported 28 Village Development Committees (VDCs), including training and capacity building packages for pastoral communities and private veterinarian to enable them access
                         financial resources. To dates the project benefited more than 113,425 people ( were 33% of them are women) in the six targeted localities of the following four key pastoral states: (i)
Public Disclosure Copy




                         North Kordofan State (Gebaish, En Nuhood and Abu Zabad localities); (ii) Blue Nile (Damazin locality); (iii) Sinnar (Singa/Abu Hujar locality); and (iv) White Nile State (Al-Gabalain
                         locality). The project received approval for extension of closing date to June 2012 and updated the result framework and relevant indicators.
                         2. The PDO for improving livestock production and marketing in those targeted localities, as a pilot, is addressing growth challenges for the livestock sector in the country by
                         contributing to increase livestock numbers and creating better outlets for production through improvement in the marketing infrastructures and negotiation platform. This objective is in
                         line with the CAADP pillars to provide access to reliable water source, increase market access and trade-related activities, strengthen and sustain pastoral livelihood, introduce
                         appropriate technologies in support to livestock productivity and diversify sources of income for the targeted pastoral communities.
