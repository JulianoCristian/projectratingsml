LOAN AGREEMENT
AGREEMENT, dated September 19, 1992, between FEDERATIVE REPUBLIC OF BRAZIL (the 
Borrower) and INTERNATIONAL BANK FOR RECONSTRUCTION AND DEVELOPMENT (the Bank).
WHEREAS (A) the Borrower, having satisfied itself as to the feasibility and 
priority of the Project described in Schedule 2 to this Agreement, has requested the 
Bank to assist in the financing of the Project; and
(B)
the Project will be partly carried out by the State of Rondonia 
(hereinafter "Rondonia") with the Borrower's assistance and, as part of such 
assistance, the Borrower will make available to Rondonia part of the proceeds of the 
Loan as provided in this Agreement; and
WHEREAS the Bank has agreed, on the basis, inter alia, of the foregoing, to 
extend the Loan to the Borrower upon the terms and conditions set forth in this 
Agreement and in the Project Agreement of even date herewith between the Bank and 
Rondonia;
NOW THEREFORE the parties hereto hereby agree as follows:

ARTICLE II
The Loan
Section 2.01. The Bank agrees to lend to the Borrower, on the terms and 
conditions set forth or referred to in the Loan Agreement, various currencies that 
shall have an aggregate value equivalent to the amount of one hundred sixty-seven 
million dollars ($167,000,000) being the sum of withdrawals of the proceeds of the 
Loan, with each withdrawal valued by the Bank as of the date of such withdrawal.
Section 2.02. (a) The amount of the Loan may be withdrawn from the Loan Account 
in accordance with the provisions of Schedule 1 to this Agreement for expenditures 
made (or, if the Bank shall so agree, to be made) in respect of the reasonable cost of 
goods and services required for the Project described in Schedule 2 to this Agreement 
and to be financed out of the proceeds of the Loan.
Page  5
(b)
The Borrower, through SDR-PR, shall open and maintain in dollars a 
special account in its Central Bank for the purposes of the Project, on terms and 
conditions satisfactory to the Bank.
Deposits into, and payments out of, the Special Account shall be made to meet 
expenditures incurred in respect of the reasonable cost of goods and services required 
to carry out the Project and to be
financed by the Bank pursuant to paragraph (a) above and in accordance with the 
provisions of Schedule 4 to this Agreement.
(c)
Deposits into, and payments out of, the Special Account shall be made in 
accordance with the provisions of Schedule 4 to this Agreement. The Borrower, through 
the Central Bank, shall
furnish to the Bank each month certified statements of the Special Account.
Section 2.03. The Closing Date shall be December 31, 1996 or such later date as 
the Bank shall establish. The Bank shall promptly notify the Borrower of such later 
date.
Section 2.04. The Borrower shall pay to the Bank a commitment charge at the rate 
of three-fourths of one percent (3/4 of 1%) per annum on the principal amount of the 
Loan not withdrawn from time to time.
Section 2.05. (a) The Borrower shall pay interest on the principal amount of the 
Loan withdrawn and outstanding from time to time, at a rate for each Interest Period 
equal to the Cost of Qualified Borrowings determined in respect of the preceding 
Semester, plus one-half of one percent (1/2 of 1%). On each of the dates specified in 
Section 2.06 of this Agreement, the Borrower
shall pay interest accrued on the principal amount outstanding during the preceding 
Interest Period, calculated at the rate applicable during such Interest Period.
(b)
As soon as practicable after the end of each Semester, the Bank shall 
notify the Borrower of the Cost of Qualified Borrowings determined in respect of such 
Semester.
(c)
For the purposes of this Section:
 
(i)
"Interest Period" means a six-month period ending on the date 
immediately 
preceding each date specified in Section 2.06 of this 
Agreement, beginning 
with the Interest Period in which this 
Agreement is signed.
(ii)
"Cost of Qualified Borrowings" means the cost, as reasonably 
determined by the 
Bank and expressed as a percentage per annum, of the 
outstanding borrowings of 
the Bank drawn down after June 30, 1982, 
excluding such borrowings or portions 
thereof as the Bank has allocated 
to fund: (A) the Bank's investments; and (B) 
loans which may be made by 
the Bank after July 1, 1989 bearing interest rates 
determined otherwise 
than as provided in paragraph (a) of this Section.
(iii)
"Semester" means the first six months or the second six months of 
a calendar 
year.
 
(d)
On such date as the Bank may specify by no less than six months' notice 
to the Borrower, paragraphs (a), (b) and (c) (iii) of this Section shall be amended to 
read as follows:
"(a)  The Borrower shall pay interest on the principal amount of the Loan 
withdrawn 
and outstanding from time to time, at a rate for each Quarter equal to 
the Cost of Qualified 
Borrowings determined in respect of the preceding Quarter, 
plus one-half of one percent (1/2 
of 1%). On each of the dates specified in 
Section 2.06 of this Agreement, the Borrower shall 
pay interest accrued on the 
principal amount outstanding during the preceding Interest 
Period, calculated 
at the rates applicable during such Interest Period."
"(b)  As soon as practicable after the end of each Quarter, the Bank 
shall notify the 
Borrower of the Cost of Qualified Borrowings determined in respect 
of such Quarter."
Page  6
"(c)  (iii) `Quarter' means a three-month period commencing on January 1, 
April 1, 
July 1 or October 1 in a calendar year."
 
Section 2.06. Interest and other charges shall be payable semiannually on May 15 
and November 15 in each year. 
Section 2.07. The Borrower shall repay the principal amount of the Loan in 
accordance with the amortization schedule set forth in Schedule 3 to this Agreement.
ARTICLE III
Execution of the Project
Section 3.01. (a) The Borrower declares its commitment to the objectives of the 
Project as set forth in Schedule 2 to this Agreement, and, to this end, without any 
limitation or restriction
upon any of its other obligations under the Loan Agreement, shall, through SDR-PR 
Operating Agreements, cause Rondonia and the Executing Entities to perform in 
accordance with the provisions of the Project Agreement and the Loan Agreement all the 
obligations of Rondonia and the Executing Entities therein set forth, shall take or 
cause to be taken all action, including the provision of funds, facilities, services 
and other resources, necessary or appropriate to enable Rondonia and the Executing 
Entities to perform such obligations, and shall not take or permit to be taken any 
action which would prevent or interfere with such performance.
(b)
Without limitation or restriction upon its other obligations under this 
Section 3.01, the Borrower in each fiscal year shall make available to Rondonia and 
the Executing Entities, through SDR-PR, all funds for expenditures to be financed by 
the Borrower either from the counterpart funds to be allocated by the Borrower or from 
the proceeds of the Loan, required to carry out the Project in accordance with, and 
within the time period set forth in, the relevant Plano de Aplicacao, to enable 
Rondonia and the Executing Entities to carry out their respective obligations under 
the Project Agreement and the Loan Agreement.
Section 3.02. Except as the Bank shall otherwise agree, procurement of the 
goods, works and services required for the Project and to be financed out of the 
proceeds of the Loan shall be governed by the provisions of Schedule 1 to the Project 
Agreement.
Section 3.03. (a) The Bank and the Borrower hereby agree that the obligations 
set forth in Sections 9.04, 9.05, 9.06, 9.07, 9.08 and 9.09 of the General Conditions 
(relating to insurance, use of goods and services, plans and schedules, records and 
reports, maintenance and land acquisition, respectively) in respect of the Project 
shall be carried out by Rondonia pursuant to Section 2.03 of the Project Agreement.
(b)
The Borrower, through SDR-PR, shall furnish to the Bank, the reports 
referred to in Section 2.06 of the Project Agreement.
Section 3.04. The Borrower shall ensure throughout the duration of the Project 
that: (a) policy reforms under Rondonia's existing Reform Program shall be enforced; 
and (b) the federal investment program for Rondonia, which currently takes into 
account land use capabilities, ecological and agro-ecological zoning considerations, 
shall be maintained consistent with such
considerations and compatible with such zoning.
Section 3.05. The Borrower shall, through SDR-PR: (i) furnish, by August 15, the 
annual budgetary allocation proposals in respect of the Project to the Bank for 
comment; (ii) furnish, by November 15, a draft POA in respect of the whole Project to 
the Bank for its review and comment; (iii) furnish to the Bank a copy of the final POA 
and the budgetary allocation for the forthcoming year as soon as formal approval of 
such documents is obtained; and (iv) ensure that the Project is implemented in each 
year in accordance with the POA.
Section 3.06. The Borrower shall:
(a)
complete, or cause FUNAI to complete, the registration with DPU of the 
Page  7
Pacaas Novos, Roosevelt, Tubarao Latundc and Uru-eu-wau-wau Amerindian reserves, and 
take, or cause to be taken, all necessary steps to register such reserves with the 
appropriate local land registries in Rondonia; and
(b)
transfer, assign or allocate or cause to be transferred, assigned or 
allocated, by December 31, 1992, the health staff necessary for the efficient carrying 
out of the health care of the Indigeneous Population under Part C.1 (d) of the 
Project.

The Borrower shall:
 
(i)

the Bank shall have reasonably 
requested; and
 
(iii)
furnish to the Bank such other information concerning said records 
and 
accounts and the audit thereof as the Bank shall from time 
to time reasonably 
request.
 
(c)
For all expenditures with respect to which withdrawals from the Loan 
Account were made on the basis of statements of expenditure, the Borrower, through 
SDR-PR, shall:
(i)
maintain or cause to be maintained in accordance with paragraph 
(a) of this 
Section, records and accounts reflecting such expenditures;
 
(ii)
retain, until at least one year after the Bank has received the 
audit report 
for the fiscal year in which the last withdrawal from the 
Loan Account was 
made, all records (contracts, orders, 
invoices, bills, receipts and other 
documents) evidencing such 
expenditures;
(iii)
enable the Bank's representatives to examine such records; and
 
(iv)
ensure that such records and accounts are included in the annual 
audit 
referred to in paragraph (b) of this Section and that the 
report of such audit 
contains a separate opinion by said auditors 
as to whether the statements of 
expenditures submitted during such 
fiscal year, together with the procedures 
and internal controls 
involved in their preparation, can be relied upon to 
support the 
related withdrawals. 
ARTICLE V
Remedies of the Bank
Section 5.01. Pursuant to Section 6.02 (k) of the General Conditions, the 
following additional events are specified:
(a)
Rondonia shall have failed to perform any of its obligations under the 
Page  8
Project Agreement.
(b)
As a result of events which have occurred after the date of the Loan 
Agreement, an extraordinary situation shall have arisen which shall make it improbable 
that Rondonia will be able to perform its obligations under the Project Agreement.
(c)
Decree No. 3782 shall have been amended, suspended, abrogated, repealed 
or waived so as to affect, in the reasonable opinion of the Bank, materially and 
adversely the ability of
Rondonia to perform any of its obligations under the Project Agreement.
(d)
Either: (i) the Borrower or any other authority having jurisdiction shall 
have taken any action for the dissolution or disestablishment of SEPLAN-RO or SDR-PR, 
or for the suspension of
either of their operations; or (ii) the dissolution or disestablishment of any of 
ITERON, SEDAM-RO, EMATER-RO, IBAMA, INCRA, or FUNAI or the suspension of any of their 
respective operations shall occur; provided that, in the reasonable opinion of the 
Bank, the occurrence of any such event materially or adversely affects the carrying 
out of the Project.
(e)
The Borrower or Rondonia shall have amended the Reform Program or taken 
any other action or failed to take any action so as to affect, in the reasonable 
opinion of the Bank, materially and adversely their ability to carry out the Project.
Section 5.02. Pursuant to Section 7.01 (h) of the General Conditions, the 
following additional events are specified, namely, that any of the events specified in 
paragraph (a), (c), (d) or (e) of Section 5.01 of this Agreement shall occur and shall 
continue for a period of sixty days after notice thereof shall have been given by the 
Bank to the Borrower.

SCHEDULE 2
Description of the Project
The objectives of the Project are to assist the Borrower and Rondonia in 
managing the development and protection of the Amazon basin in the territory of 
Rondonia and to: (a) implement policies, regulations and public investment programs 
all created to provide a framework for sustainable development in Rondonia; (b) 
conserve the biodiversity of Rondonia; (c) protect and enforce the borders of all 
Conservation Units, Indigenous Areas, public forests and extractive
reserves, and control and prevent deforestation, wood transport and forest fires in 
Rondonia contrary to Brazilian laws and regulations; (d) develop integrated farming 
systems in areas suitable for permanent agriculture and agro-forestry; (e) support 
priority investments in socio-economic infrastructure and services to implement 
agro-ecological zoning in Rondonia; and (f) consolidate the technical and operational 
capacity of Rondonia's institutions.
The Project consists of the following parts, subject to such modifications 
thereof as the Borrower and the Bank may agree upon from time to time to achieve such 
objectives:
Part A:
Environmental Conservation, Management and Enforcement
 
To conserve the biodiversity of Rondonia and create a basis for sustainable 
utilization of natural resources including:
1.
Land Regularization, Zoning:
 
Executing Entities: ITERON, INCRA and FUNAI
Development of legal measures and policies in relation to land tenure 
regularization; preparation and distribution of small-scale maps for detailed planning 
and implementation (including agro-ecological, climatological, soil and topographic 
maps) by ITERON; creation of a land registration system to record land transactions; 
and carrying out of cartographic work necessary for the creation, border demarcation, 
management and protection of Conservation Units,
public forests, extractive reserves and Indigenous Areas.
2.
Establishment and Management of Conservation Units:
 
Executing Entities: SEDAM-RO, IBAMA and municipalities.
(a)
Establishment of state parks, biological reserves and ecological stations 
and of municipal nature parks;
(b)
Rehabilitation of certain existing federal Conservation Units and 
Page  14
management of the Conservation Units including familiarization with the units' 
environment, gathering information,
preparation and implementation of management plans, and integration and involvement 
with the local community; and
(c)
Construction of lodging facilities and control posts and provision of 
equipment for the Executing Entities.
3.
Environmental Protection:
 
Executing Agencies: SEDAM-RO, IBAMA, and PMF.
Strengthening of the institutional capabilities of:
(a)
SEDAM-RO and PMF to afford protection against, and prevent, 
deforestation, wood transport and forest fires contrary to Brazilian laws and 
regulations;
(b)
IBAMA and SEDAM-RO to protect, supervise and promote fishing activities;
 
(c)
IBAMA and SEDAM-RO to protect endangered wildlife species, including 
construction of field offices;
(d)
SEDAM-RO to control and reduce water pollution (including provision of a 
laboratory) through survey and registration of polluted areas and related educational 
campaigns and training
activities; and
(e)
PMF to carry out its activities, including construction of outposts.
 
4.
Forest Management:
 
Executing Entities: SEDAM-RO, PMF and ITERON
(a)
Demarcation, establishment and protection of Rondonia's forest reserves;
 
(b)
Development of the Sao Domingos state forest reserve as a pilot project;
 
(c)
Development and implementation of management plans for existing block 
reserves in the vicinity of Urupa, Machadinho and Cujubim;
(d)
Development of a block reserve in Machadinho as a pilot project;
 
(e)
Promotion and supervision of forest activities Rondonia; and
 
(f)
Forestry sector studies.
 
5.
Extractive Reserves:
 
Executing Entities: INCRA, ITERON, SEDAM-RO and PMF
(a)
Creation, demarcation and protection of extractive reserves for 
sustainable production of rubber, nuts and other products;
(b)
Provision of land use rights, technical assistance and support for 
marketing of native products to rubber tappers and riverine inhabitants;
(c)
Development of the Ouro Preto extractive reserve as a pilot project;
 
(d)
Market development and limited in-situ processing/packing activities; and
 
(e)
Provision of civil works including infrastructure necessary for the 
development and management of the reserves, equipment, materials.
6.
Support to Indigenous Communities:
 
Executing Entities: FUNAI, INCRA, PMF, SEPLAN-RO and ITERON
Page  15
(a)
Safeguard and protection of areas occupied by indigenous groups against 
encroachment;
(b)
Border demarcation of certain Indigenous Areas and renewal of boundaries 
of certain areas previously demarcated;
(c)
Identification, interdiction and demarcation, where applicable, of areas 
inhabited by isolated Indigenous groups;
(d)
Improvement of health services through training of FUNAI's field health 
staff and strengthening its mobile health teams; construction, equipment and operation 
of at least 4 infirmaries for indigenous patients; and
(e)
Creation of a FUNAI/SEPLAN-RO team to ensure support to the Indigenous 
Populations and solve problems related to the protection of such Populations.
7.
Institutional Strengthening, Studies and Technical Assistance:
 
Executing Entities: SEPLAN-RO, ITERON, SEDAM-RO, SEDUC-RO and SDR-PR
(a)
Provision of courses in environmental protection for SEDAM-RO staff;
 
(b)
Development of courses for forestry specialization at the agricultural 
secondary school of Ji-Parana;
(c)
Training courses for university-trained foresters/agronomists (master of 
science degrees); internships; provision of educational materials for teachers' 
training on environmental
issues; preparation of public information materials; and
(d)
Technical assistance and consultant's services to support Project 
implementation, training and preparation of studies, with the assistance of an 
institution to be agreed upon between the Borrower and the Bank.
Part B:
Agro-Forestry Development and Research
 
Executing Entities: EMBRAPA, CEPLAC, SEDAM-RO, EMATER-RO and SEAGRI-RO
To consolidate deforested areas in two Zones of the Project (Zones 1 and 2) 
having better soils and being capable of sustainable agriculture by:
(a)
concentration of agricultural support services in the most productive and 
fertile areas;
(b)
promotion of cultivation of suitable perennial crops (excluding coffee 
and cocoa);
(c)
expansion of the cropping area on fallow land or abandoned pasture land;
 
(d)
diversification of the crop mix by promotion of new crops (excluding 
coffee and cocoa), development of mixed cropping systems and crop rotation techniques; 
and
(e)
reinforcement of efforts to retain forest reserves at the farm level by 
development of replanting and extraction techniques. 
1.
Agro-forestry Research:
 
(a)
Applied research work by EMBRAPA, EMATER-RO, CEPLAC and SEDAM-RO in 
Rondonia on mixed cropping systems, improved forest-fallow management systems; 
agro-forestry, disease control, genetic improvement, tree crop diversification, 
introduction of new tree species and research for natural forest management, with 
priority to Zones 1 and 2, as appropriate; and (b) construction of staff houses, 
offices and laboratories and provision of equipment.
2.
Rural Extension:
Page  16
Provision of sustained agro-forestry practices, and increase in efficiency and 
integration of the extension work of EMATER-RO, CEPLAC and SEDAM-RO through:
(a)
joint planning and staff training;
 
(b)
establishment of combined on-farm demonstration areas, promotion of 
farmer's associations, provision of communication programs, and technical assistance 
to, and training of, farmers; and
(c)
construction of staff houses and offices, and provision of training 
facilities and equipment.
3.
Rural Credit:
 
(a)
Formal establishment of a denominated in-kind credit system (FUNDAGRO); 
(b) administration of such system by BERON; and (c) gradual provision of denominated 
in-kind credit on the terms and conditions set forth in the FUNDAGRO Operating Manual 
for investments and essential inputs, for crop diversification (excluding coffee and 
cocoa) and forest-fallow enrichment in crop
rotation systems. 
4.
Input Supply:
 
Establishment of small nurseries in Zones 1  and 2 for clonal gardens and 
planting materials and production of seeds and seedlings for commercial sale.
Part C:
Socio-Economic Infrastructure and Services
 
Executing Entities: SESAU-RO, SEDUC-RO, CAERD, ENARO, DER-RO and SEOSP-RO
1.
Health:
 
Consolidation of the existing health systems in Zones 1 and 2 through:
(a)
training of rural health agents, nurses and doctors;
 
(b)
strengthening of supervision at state, municipal and field levels;
 
(c)
provision of equipment, medicines and permanent materials; and
 
(d)
health assistance for acute care of Indigeneous Populations (including 
the construction of infirmaries).
2.
Education:
 
Consolidation of the rural education system in Zones 1 and 2 through:
(a)
rural teacher training;
 
(b)
improvements in the supervision system at the state, municipal and field 
levels; and
(c)
provision of equipment and materials for use, inter alia, in the 
rehabilitation of rural schools.
3.
Water Supply:
 
Completion of water supply and water treatment systems commenced by established 
rural service centers for settlers, to reduce health problems caused by 
water-transmitted diseases.
4.
Road and River Transport:
 
Repair and maintenance of road network systems in Zones 1 and 2 including:
(a)
Rehabilitation of at least 3,900 kilometers of state and municipal feeder 
Page  17
roads;
(b)
Pavement of approximately 81 kilometers of feeder roads in Zone 1 
populated areas;
(c)
Training in road rehabilitation and maintenance techniques;
 
(d)
Completion of a master road plan for Rondonia; and
 
(e)
Improvement of river transportation through: (i) construction, repair and 
maintenance of several small ports; (ii) dredging of rivers to maintain navigability; 
and (iii) construction,
repair and maintenance of freight and passenger boats to operate in certain river 
basins.
Part D:
Project Administration, Monitoring and Evaluation, and Technical 
Assistance
Executing Entities: SDR-PR and SEPLAN-RO
Strengthening of the State Project Coordinating Unit and Federal Project 
Coordinating Unit to improve their monitoring and evaluation capability; provision of 
training, technical assistance
and consultancy advice; construction of offices and provision of equipment; provision 
of processing facilities for implementation of a satellite monitoring system to detect 
deforestation in accordance with federal guidelines for the sector; and engagement of 
the Independent Evaluation Committee to evaluate annual progress of the Project and 
make recommendations in relation to such progress with the participation of MEFP.
