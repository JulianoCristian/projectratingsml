                         Project Development Objectives
                         Project Development Objective (from Project Appraisal Document)

                         The project aims to improve the efficiency, effectiveness and sustainability of urban services under the control of Da Nang City through a package of priority
                         investments including urban upgrading, environmental improvement and strategic road access, as well as measures for institutional strengthening for management o f
                         urban services.

                         Implementation Status Overview
                         The Final Implementation Review mission was carried out in Da Nang from May 13-17, 2013. Overall, project development objectives have been substantially achieved. Project
                         investments were and going to be handed-over for operation, the project is concretely completing implementation of entire Credit by its original closing date of June 30. Up to date,
                         the project has brought benefit to about 350, 000 citizens (of which female account for 60%). It was clearly recognized that the project has improved city environment by improving


                                                                                                                                                                                                          Page 1 of 6

                         drainage/sewer collection and treatment systems, rehabilitation of river and canal; improved the living conditions for low-income people by providing better basic infrastructure service
                         and support the economic growth through investments of strategic roads. About 93% of the Low income households in Low-income Areas (LIAs) have water supply pipe installed,
                         95% have drainage pipe connected. About 65% of low income households in LLIAs was satisfied with the quality of urban service provided by the project, this percentage will be
                         increase by the end of June, 2013 when all of project investments completed. Long term impacts have also been realized through the institutional development outcomes that the
