PROGRAM AGREEMENT 
 
AGREEMENT dated June 7, 2007, entered into between the INTERNATIONAL 
BANK FOR RECONSTRUCTION AND DEVELOPMENT (the 
“Bank”) and the 
PROVINCE OF PUNJAB (“Punjab or the “Program Implementing Entity” indistinctly) 
(the “Program Agreement”) in connection with the Loan Agreement of same date 
between the ISLAMIC REPUBLIC OF PAKISTAN (the “Borrower”) and the Bank (the 
“Loan Agreement”).  The Bank and the Program Implementing Entity hereby agree as 
follows: 
 
ARTICLE I - GENERAL CONDITIONS; DEFINITIONS 
 
1.01. 
The General Conditions (as defined in the Appendix to the Loan Agreement) 
constitute an integral part of this Agreement. 
 
1.02. 
Unless the context requires otherwise, the capitalized terms used in this 
Agreement have the meanings ascribed to them in the Loan Agreement or the 
General Conditions. 
 
ARTICLE II - PROGRAM
 
2.01 
The Program Implementing Entity declares its commitment to the Program and 
its implementation.  To this end: 
 
(a) 
the Program Implementing Entity, the Borrower and the Bank shall from 
time to time, at the request of either party, exchange views on the 
progress achieved in carrying out the Program; 
 
(b) 
prior to each such exchange of views, the Project Implementing Entity 
shall furnish to the Borrower and the Bank for their reviews and 
comments a report on the progress achieved in carrying out the Program, 
in such detail as the Borrower and Bank shall reasonably request; and 
 
(c) 
without limitation upon the provisions of paragraphs (a) and (b) of this 
Section, the Program Implementing Entity shall exchange views with the 
Borrower and the Bank on any proposed action to be taken after the 
disbursement of the Loan which would have the effect of materially 
reversing the objectives of the Program, or any action taken under the 
Program including any action specified in the Schedule to this 
Agreement.

SCHEDULE 
 
Actions Taken by Punjab under the Program  
 
I. 
Institutional and Policy Reforms
1. 
Punjab’s budgetary allocations for IPD maintenance and rehabilitation activities 
in FY 2006-2007 have been allotted on the basis of revised standards approved in 
August 2005, as updated in accordance with market prices.  
 
2. 
(i) At least six hundred (600) fresh groundwater tube wells listed in the Assets 
Management Plan have been transitioned (i.e. either closed down, or transferred 
to FOs for operation and maintenance or replacement by smaller tube wells); and 
(ii) IPD has notified for closure one thousand six hundred (1,600) saline 
groundwater tube wells. 
 
3. 
IPD has institutionalized a system of fortnightly departmental accounts 
committee (DAC) meetings for the review and settlement of audits observations, 
and at least twenty percent (20%) of the backlog of audit observations (baseline 
of March 31, 2006) has been reviewed and resolved.  
 
4. 
Abiana
 (water charges) collection in non-FO managed canals has improved to 
reach fifty eight percent (58%) of the assessed amounts. 
 
II. 
Water Resources Management Reforms
5. 
Data on water accounts (including water entitlements, actual water discharges 
and balance shares) has been posted on IPD’s website and updated every ten 
days. 
 
6. 
The installation and calibration of flow metering gauges in the LCC (Lower 
Chenab Canal) East canal system have been completed, and the distributary 
discharge data posted on IPD’s website.   
 
III. 
Irrigation Service Delivery Reforms
7. 
IPD has established a fully functional Reform Unit which has prepared semi-
annual monitoring and evaluation reports.  
 
8. 
IPD’s stakeholder communication strategy has been prepared, approved and 
launched.   
 
9. 
Approximately one hundred (100) new FOs have been established in new Area 
Water Boards, which have entered into irrigation management transfer 
agreements with IPD.

10. 
PIDA has developed guidelines for the identification and mitigation of 
environmental and social risk in the operation, maintenance and rehabilitation of
