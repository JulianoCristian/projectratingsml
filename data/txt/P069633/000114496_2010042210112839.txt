“Section 2.03
The Closing Date shall be December 31, 2002 or such later date as the 
Bank shall establish. The Bank shall promptly notify the Borrower of such later date.”
3.
Section 3.07 of the Loan Agreement is hereby amended and replaced to read in its entirely as

Without limitation on the provisions of Section 9.01 of the General

each year until completion of the Project, annual reports in a form satisfactory to the 
Bank on the progress of the Project and based on the timetable and targets set forth in the 
Project Monitoring Letter.”
4.
Sections 6.01 and 6.02 of the Loan Agreement are hereby deleted.
5.
Schedules 1 and 2 to the Loan Agreement are hereby amended and replaced in its entirely to read

Attachment II
SCHEDULE 2
Description of the Project
The objective of the Project is to assist the Borrower in preparing for, mitigating against and 
responding efficiently to, disasters such as hurricanes, tropical storms, floods, landslides and storm 
surges, through physical strengthening of infrastructure, institutional development and training of human 
resources.
The Project consists of the following parts, subject to such modifications thereof as the Borrower 
and the Bank may agree upon from time to time to achieve such objectives:
Part A
: Physical Mitigation Works
1.
River control and flood damage reduction works at the Pichelin River and Colihaut River; and
2
Retrofitting of six existing shelters.
Part B
: Strengthening the Office of Disaster Management (ODM)
1.
Strengthening the human resources capacity of the ODM in disaster preparedness and 
improvement of disaster planning, policies and procedures.
2.
Establishment of stocks of specialized disaster equipment and loss reduction materials such as 
portable generators, water purification, storage and distribution equipment, reinforced plastic sheeting 
and equipment for emergency response teams at the national and community levels.
3.
Establishment of an island-wide communications system with base radio stations in each of the 
seven districts, mobile radios for local Disaster Management Committees, and other communications 
equipment to ensure adequate communications under disaster conditions.
Part C
:  Early Warning System
1.
Replacement and acquisition of meteorological equipment for the National Meteorological 
Service; and
2.
Establishment of an amateur ham weather radio observation network; and
3.
Carrying out training courses, workshops and symposia necessary to ensure the effectiveness of 
the foregoing, including in computer technology, meteorology and equipment maintenance and repair.
Part D
: Community-based Disaster Management
1.
Implementation of community training programs, including training in community organization, 
disaster preparedness and other competencies such as search and rescue.
Page  6
2.
Establishment of a pilot vulnerability reduction fund to finance, at the community level, 
micro-projects for disaster management.
