AGREEMENT, dated April 3, 2001, between REPUBLIC OF NICARAGUA (the Borrower) and 
INTERNATIONAL DEVELOPMENT ASSOCIATION (the Association).
WHEREAS the Borrower, having satisfied itself as to the feasibility and priority 
of the Project described in Schedule 2 to this Agreement (the Project), has requested 
the Association to assist in the financing of the Project; and

Agreement;

ARTICLE II
Page  4
The Credit
Section 2.01. The Association agrees to lend to the Borrower, on the terms and 
conditions set forth or referred to in the Development Credit Agreement, an amount in 
various currencies equivalent to twenty two million Special Drawing Rights (SDR 
22,000,000).
Section 2.02. (a) The amount of the Credit may be withdrawn from the Credit 
Account in accordance with the provisions of Schedule 1 to this Agreement for 
expenditures made (or, if the Association shall so agree, to be made) in respect of 
the reasonable cost of goods and services required for the Project and to be financed 
out of the proceeds of the Credit.
(b)
Promptly after the Effective Date, the Association shall, on behalf of 
the Borrower, withdraw from the Credit Account and pay to itself the amount required 
to repay the principal amount of the Project Preparation Advance withdrawn and 
outstanding as of such date and to pay all unpaid charges thereon. The unwithdrawn 
balance of the authorized amount of the Project Preparation Advance shall thereupon be 
canceled.
Section 2.03. The Closing Date shall be December 30, 2004 or such later date as 
the Association shall establish. The Association shall promptly notify the Borrower of 
such later date.
Section 2.04. (a) The Borrower shall pay to the Association a commitment charge 
on the principal amount of the Credit not withdrawn from time to time at a rate to be 
set by the Association as of June 30 of each year, but not to exceed the rate of 
one-half of one percent (1/2 of 1%) per annum.
(b)
The commitment charge shall accrue: (i) from the date sixty days after 
the date of this Agreement (the accrual date) to the respective dates on which amounts 
shall be withdrawn by the Borrower from the Credit Account or canceled; and (ii) at 
the rate set as of the June 30 immediately preceding the accrual date and at such 
other rates as may be set from time to time thereafter pursuant to paragraph (a) 
above. The rate set as of June 30 in each year shall be applied from the next date in 
that year specified in Section 2.06 of this Agreement.
(c)
The commitment charge shall be paid: (i) at such places as the 
Association shall reasonably request; (ii) without restrictions of any kind imposed 
by, or in the territory of, the Borrower; and (iii) in the currency specified in this 
Agreement for the purposes of Section 4.02 of the General Conditions or in such other 
eligible currency or currencies as may from time to time be designated or selected 
pursuant to the provisions of that Section.
Section 2.05. The Borrower shall pay to the Association a service charge at the 
rate of three-fourths of one percent (3/4 of 1%) per annum on the principal amount of 
the Credit withdrawn and outstanding from time to time.
Section 2.06. Commitment charges and service charges shall be payable 
semiannually on March 1 and September 1 in each year.
Section 2.07. (a)  Subject to paragraphs (b), (c) and (d) below, the Borrower 
shall repay the principal amount of the Credit in semiannual installments payable on 
each March 1 and September 1 commencing September 1, 2011 and ending March 1, 2041. 
Each installment to and including the installment payable on March 1, 2021 shall be 
one percent (1%) of such principal amount, and each installment thereafter shall be 
two percent (2%) of such principal amount. 
(b)
Whenever: (i) the Borrower's per capita gross national product (GNP), as 
determined by the Association, shall have exceeded for three consecutive years  the 
level established annually by the Association for determining eligibility to access 
the Association's resources; and (ii) the Bank shall consider the Borrower 
creditworthy for Bank lending, the Association may, subsequent to the review and 
approval thereof by the Executive Directors of the Association and after due 
consideration by them of the development of the Borrower's economy, modify the 
repayment of installments under paragraph (a) above by: (A) requiring the Borrower to 
repay twice the amount of each such installment not yet due until the principal amount 
Page  5
of the Credit shall have been repaid; and (B) requiring the Borrower to commence 
repayment of the principal amount of the Credit as of the first semiannual payment 
date referred to in paragraph (a) above falling six months or more after the date on 
which the Association notifies the Borrower that the events set out in this paragraph 
(b) have occurred, provided, however, that there shall be a grace period of a minimum 
of five years on such repayment of principal.
(c)
If so requested by the Borrower, the Association may revise the 
modification referred to in paragraph (b) above to include, in lieu of some or all of 
the increase in the amounts of such installments, the payment of interest at an annual 
rate agreed with the Association on the principal amount of the Credit withdrawn and 
outstanding from time to time, provided that, in the judgment of the Association, such 
revision shall not change the grant element obtained under the above-mentioned 
repayment modification.
(d)
If, at any time after a modification of terms pursuant to paragraph (b) 
above, the Association determines that the Borrower's economic condition has 
deteriorated significantly, the Association may, if so requested by the Borrower, 
further modify the terms of repayment to conform to the schedule of installments as 
provided in paragraph (a) above.
Section 2.08. The currency of the United States of America is hereby specified 
for the purposes of Section 4.02 of the General Conditions.
Section 2.09. (a) The Executive President (Presidente Ejecutivo) of INIFOM or 
any other person he or she may authorize in writing are designated as representatives 
of the Borrower for the purposes of taking any action required or permitted to be 
taken under the provisions of Section 2.02 of this Agreement and Article V of the 
General Conditions with respect to Part A of the Project.
(b)
The Minister of MARENA or any other person he or she may authorize in 
writing are designated as representatives of the Borrower for the purposes of taking 
any action required or permitted to be taken under the provisions of Section 2.02 of 
this Agreement and Article V of the General Conditions with respect to Part B of the 
Project.

SCHEDULE 2
Description of the Project
The objective of the Project is to improve the performance of rural 
municipalities for sustainable rural development and environmental management in: (a) 
the provision of local public infrastructure and the delivery of municipal services; 
(b) the protection of the environment and the sustainable management of natural 
resources; and (c) the promotion of local economic development.
The Project consists of the following parts, subject to such modifications 
thereof as the Borrower and the Association may agree upon at any time in the future, 
through an amendment to this Agreement, to achieve such objectives:
Part A:
Rural Municipal Development/ Evaluation and Policy Development.
1.
FONDEM Subprojects
Provision of FONDEM Subproject Grants to finance municipal projects to:
(i)
foster municipal autonomy in the design and implementation of 
local policies;
(ii)
increase the equity in revenue generation across Municipalities 
and create 
an incentive for local revenue generation;
(iii)
improve Municipalities’ infrastructure and service delivery 
responsibilities, through, inter alia, the 
construction, repair, 
maintenance and operation of 
roads and bridges, health care and training 
facilities and 
equipment, market facilities, slaughterhouses, transport 
terminals, solid waste disposal systems, water and sanitation systems, 
power supply and distribution systems, parks and sport and cultural 
facilities;
(iv)
promote municipal economic development through the creation of 
information 
flows on market conditions and opportunities, the 
incorporation of local 
economic development activities in 
municipal development plans, and the 
elaboration of instruments 
and incentives to mobilize productive investment 
and increase local 
economic activity; and
(v)
strengthen municipal capacity for local development management 
through the 
provision of training and technical assistance.
2.
Strengthening of Municipal Institutions
(a)
Strengthening of municipal capacity to generate revenues through:
(i)
the revision of the legal framework for local government finances; 
and 
(ii)
the development and extension to the Municipalities of:
(A)
improved systems and manuals for local fiscal 
administration;
Page  19
(B)
standards and procedures for financially sustainable 
delivery of 
municipal services; and
(C)
a system for land and property registration (cadastre).
(b)
Provision of technical assistance to the Municipalities to develop and 
implement a participatory municipal planning system including strategic planning, 
investment programming and annual budgeting.
(c)
Provision of technical assistance to INIFOM in the development of a 
municipal organization and management system to: (i) complement the existing manuals 
and guidelines for municipal administration and services delivery management; and (ii) 
develop financial and other incentives for the  Municipalities to demand capacity 
building services and create a network of certified suppliers of such services.
(d)
Provision of technical assistance to INIFOM to develop and implement a 
municipal supervision, monitoring and evaluation system to: (i) monitor the execution 
of this Part B.2 of the Project; (ii) facilitate technical improvement in the 
municipal administration; and (iii) facilitate management of municipal information 
towards the definition of policies, strategies and municipal regulations. 
(e)
Provision of technical assistance and training to indigenous groups (as 
identified in the INIFOM Operational Manual) in order to train such groups in 
management of local projects and community participation.
3.
Strengthening of INIFOM
(a)
Provision of technical assistance and equipment to: (i) facilitate the 
restructuring of INIFOM’s organizational structure; (ii) monitor INIFOM’s performance 
and business practice; (iii) strengthen INIFOM’s administrative and financial 
management capacity and INIFOM’s capacity to supervise and monitor municipal 
performance; and (iv) allow INIFOM to carry out external audits of the Municipalities.
(b)
Carrying out of minor works to repair or improve INIFOM’s offices.
4.
Evaluation and Policy Dissemination
Provision of technical assistance and equipment to: (a) design and implement an 
evaluation system including baseline and follow-up evaluation studies to evaluate key 
Project activities and the institutional performance of INIFOM, MARENA and the 
Municipalities; (b) disseminate within the Borrower’s Legislative and Executive Power 
the lessons learned with the Project; and (c) develop decentralization policy 
proposals and regulations on municipal activities, such as the municipal 
administrative career and municipal cadastres.
Part B:
Municipal Environmental Management
1.
Support to Decentralization of Environmental Management
(a)
(i)
Creating or strengthening municipal capacity in appropriate 
environmental 
management through the provision of technical 
assistance for:
(A)
the improvement of the municipal legal environmental 
framework, and
(B)
the facilitation of municipal environmental planning 
(including the 
consolidation of municipal environmental 
commissions and the 
training of municipal 
government officials in municipal 
environmental 
management).
(ii)
Provision of technical assistance and training to indigenous 
groups (as 
identified in the MARENA Operational Manual) in order 
to train such groups 
in environmental management.
(b)
Development of policy guidelines related to the role of municipal 
Page  20
government with respect to environmental policies through: (i) the training of MARENA 
officials in different aspects of decentralized environmental management; (ii) the 
strengthening of MARENA’s capacity to provide technical assistance and training to 
support Municipalities in their local environmental management responsibilities; (iii) 
the improvement of the environmental policy coordination between MARENA, its 
territorial delegations (delegaciones territoriales) and the Municipalities; and (iv) 
the development of technical norms and procedures for environmental management by

(c)
Supporting the deconcentration of MARENA through:
(i)
the institutional strengthening of MARENA territorial offices; and
(ii)
the carrying out of external audit of MARENA.
(d)
Increasing the environmental awareness of entities that adversely affect 
the environment through: (i) the provision of training and technical assistance to 
such entities on environmental beneficial technologies and clean production;  (ii) the 
carrying out of public dissemination campaigns; and (iii) agreements between the 
Municipalities and private sector entities to achieve an improved environmental 
management of said entities.
(e)
Strengthening of the Borrower’s formal and informal alternative dispute 
resolution mechanisms at the municipal level through the provision of technical 
assistance and training for: (i) policy development on alternative dispute resolution 
mechanisms for environmentally related conflicts; (ii) strengthening the Borrower’s 
environmental unit of the General Attorney’s office, the Municipalities and the 
departments within the Executive Branch of the Borrower responsible for environmental 
management on alternative dispute resolution; (iii) promoting a body of alternative 
dispute mechanisms’ experts; (iv) public dissemination of alternative dispute 
resolution mechanisms; and (v) pilot implementation activities.
(f)
Carrying out of minor works to repair or improve MARENA’s offices.
2.
Environmental Information Systems
Establishment of a national environmental information system (SINIA) through: 
(a) the provision of technical assistance to design the SINIA; (b) the appointment of 
a coordinator and technical specialists to administer the SINIA; (c) the development 
and dissemination of education materials; and (d) the development of the physical 
infrastructure of the SINIA.
3.
FAM Subprojects
Provision of FAM Subproject Grants to finance local environmental projects 
(including education campaigns, training of municipal officers in environmental 
policies and management, equipment and rehabilitation of municipal environmental 
offices) related to, inter alia, land use planning, pollution control, industrial 
decontamination, water treatment systems for agroindustrial facilities, soil erosion 
control, prevention and control of forest fires, reforestation and tree nurseries, 
management and administration of natural protected areas, watershed management, 
sustainable use of wild flora and fauna (including fisheries), ecotourism, sustainable 
forest use and bio-gas production.

Works required under Parts. A.3 (b) and B.1 (f) of the Project, estimated to

contractors in response to a written invitation. The invitation shall include a 
detailed description of the works, including basic specifications, the required 
completion date, a basic form of agreement acceptable to the Association, and relevant 
drawings, where applicable. The award shall be made to the contractor who offers the 
lowest price quotation for the required work, and who has the experience and resources 
to complete the contract successfully.
