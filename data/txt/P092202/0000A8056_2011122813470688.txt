                         Implementation Status Overview
                         Project implementation in the past 6 months has been slower than expected. The project has made some important progress, including: (a) the completion of the Rationalization and


                                                                                                                                                                                                          Page 1 of 7
                             The World Bank                                                                                                                                              Report No: ISR5429



                         Prioritization of the National Protected Areas System study, which is a key input for decision making on the update of the National Protected Areas System strategy; (b) the
                         delineation and demarcation of the pilot area of San Diego and San Felipe La Barra; and (c) the successful implementation of sustainable productive subprojects, which are having a
                         significant impact on the communities in identifying conservation-compatible livelihoods. However, some of the Global Environment Objective (GEO) indicators are unlikely to be
                         achieved, unless there is a focused effort to: (a) finalize the delimitation and demarcation of the pilot area of Bahia of Jiquilisco, including ensuring the availability of counterpart funds;
Public Disclosure Copy




                         (b) update the regulations of the Protected Areas Law; and (c) internalize the results of the field and legal work into the elaboration of an updated strategy for the sustainability of the
                         national protected areas system (NPAS).
