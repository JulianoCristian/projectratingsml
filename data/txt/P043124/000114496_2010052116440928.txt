WHEREAS (A) pursuant to a letter agreement dated July 30, 1990 between Japan and 
the International Bank for Reconstruction and Development (the Bank) and the Association, 
Japan has requested the Bank and the Association, and the Bank and the Association have 
agreed, to administer grant funds to be made available by Japan for the financing of certain 
programs and projects supported by the Bank and the Association, as the case may be, in 
accordance with the provisions of such letter agreement;
WHEREAS (B) Japan has agreed to make available to the Recipient a grant out of said 
grant funds (the Grant) to finance the cost of carrying out the technical assistance described in 
Schedule 1 to this Agreement (the Technical Assistance) on the terms and conditions hereinafter 
set forth; 
WHEREAS (C) the Recipient has requested the Association to carry out the Technical 
Assistance;
WHEREAS (D) the Association has agreed to carry out the Technical Assistance on the 
terms and conditions hereinafter set forth;
NOW THEREFORE the parties hereto hereby agree as follows:
ARTICLE I
Definitions; Terms of Technical Assistance
Section 1.01. The Technical Assistance shall be undertaken by consultants (the 
Consultants) who shall, as far as practicable, include Consultants with expertise in the following 
areas:
For Education:
(a)
school mapping;
(b)
technical and vocational training;
(c)
manpower needs and training assessment;
(d)
educational services and institutions; and
(e)
institutional development.
For Health:
(a)
household surveys and analysis;
Page  3
(b)
burden of disease and demographic analysis;
(c)
cost and financial data compilation;
(d)
hospital management and financing;
(e)
economic analysis; and
(f)
health services and institutions.
For both Education and Health:
community consultation and participatory development.
Section 1.02. As used in this Agreement, the terms "yen" and "¥" mean the currency of 
Japan, and the term "Consultants" includes individual consultants as well as any consulting firm 
that provides any of the experts referred to in Section 1.01.
Section 1.03. The Consultants commenced their services on February 5, 1996 and are 
expected to provide a total of 46 man-months of service between that time and February 23, 
1997.

(b)

proceeding. Within thirty days after such notice, the other party shall notify to the party 
instituting the proceeding the name of the arbitrator appointed by such other party.
(e)

parties shall not have agreed upon an Umpire, any party may request the appointment of an 
Umpire as provided in paragraph (c) of this Section.
(f)
The Arbitral Tribunal shall convene at such time and place as shall be fixed by 
the Umpire. Thereafter, the Arbitral Tribunal shall determine where and when it shall sit.
(g)

shall, subject to the provisions of this Section and except as the parties shall otherwise agree,

SCHEDULE 1
Description of Technical Assistance
The objective of this Technical Assistance is to assist in the preparation of human capital 
development/social sector projects.  Two projects are envisaged: (a) an education sector project, 
and (b) a health sector project.  The overall objectives of both projects are to improve the quality 
and access to education and primary health care in a cost-effective and sustainable manner.
The specific objectives of the Education Project are to:
(a)
construct and/or rehabilitate primary and junior secondary schools in 
remote areas;
(b)
increase girls participation in basic education;
(c)
improve the technical and vocational training system; and
(d)
strengthen the capacity of the Ministry of Education to manage 
decentralized education services.
The specific objectives of the Health Project are to:
(a)
construct two regional referral hospitals in Mendefera and Barentu;
(b)
equip and furnish existing health centers and health stations;
(c)
strengthen the regional capacity for the management of health services 
delivery;
(d)
strengthen the capacity to collect, monitor, and assess the countrys 
burden of disease; and
(e)
support promising PHC pilot initiatives.
The Technical Assistance consists of the following parts, subject to such modifications 
thereof as the Recipient and the Association may agree upon from time to time, to achieve the 
objectives defined above.
Page  11
For the Education Project, the activities will include:
Part A
:
School Mapping Exercise
The design and undertaking of a school mapping exercise and analysis of its results.  The 
study should include information on: (a) the number, location and physical structure of education 
facilities; (b) the current and potential student capacity of each facility; (c) student enrollment; 
(d) the number of teaching staff; (e) each facilitys need for school equipment, furniture and 
instructional materials, including textbooks; (f) roles of local communities in the management of 
schools; and (g) potential number of students by grade.
Part B
:
Study on Technical and Vocational Training
The design and undertaking of a study on technical and vocational training and analysis 
of its results.  The study will examine various mechanisms for providing training geared to 
ensuring employment and assess various scenarios and test the cost effectiveness of promising 
approaches identified.  Specifically, the study will: (a) examine the needs in the areas of 
technical and vocational training skills; (b) evaluate past and current training programs; (c) 
undertake a study of other country experience; and (d) evaluate alternative options for providing 
training.
For the Health Project, the activities will include:
Part C
:
Household Survey
The design and undertaking of a baseline survey of approximately 1,200 households and 
analysis of its results to determine different modalities and options for cost containment, cost 
sharing and cost recovery.  The survey should include information on the following aspects: (i) 
health status; (ii) health seeking behaviors; (iii) health care expenditures; and (iv) willingness to 
pay for hospital and other health care.
Part D
:
Sustainability Issues for Hospitals and Health Centers
In addition to conducting the household survey, grant resources will be used to address 
other Project component design issues, as resources permit, to define: (i) equipment needs in 
health stations and health centers; (ii) hospital and related rural health services costs; (iii) 
utilization of hospital and rural health facilities in the Project zones, including referrals; and (iv) 
options for facility and financial management.
