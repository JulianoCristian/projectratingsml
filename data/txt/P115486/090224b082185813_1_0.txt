                         Implementation Status Overview
                         1. The implementation of the LRIP and two additional financing have been satisfactory and the development objectives have been met. All civil works contracts have been completed
                         and disbursement, currently at 97.4% of LRIP AF2 loan proceeds, is scheduled to be completed by the end of April 2014.
                         2. Overall, the number of kilometer of roads improved and number of person-months of jobs created exceeded estimated targets. Since approval of the LRIP, about 39,855 of person-
                         month jobs was created during construction and road users are seeing a reduction in travel time of 58.5% on the road sections improved by the project. The second additional
                         financing to the LRIP is expected to close on December 31, 2013
                         3. The review of the pilot DBST concluded that this solution provide satisfactory results and could be mainstreamed. However, the requirement for contractors to have specific
                         equipment and to ensure proper summer and winter maintenance should be factored in the decision.

                         4. The Bank team informed the Ministry of Transport and Communication of the closing date of the additional financing on December 31, 2013 and of the upcoming preparation of the
                         Implementation Completion report.
                         5. As the LRIP will close on December 31, 2013, there is a need to ensure that maintenance will continue on sections of the network improved by the project. This may be an issue
                         for roads under the responsibility of Marzes and the Ministry of Transport and Communication was asked to confirm how these roads would be maintained by end-January 2014.
