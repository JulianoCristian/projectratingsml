                         Implementation Status Overview


                                                                                                                                                                                                          Page 1 of 8

                         The project continues to make good progress and has the successful achievements its development objectives in all of the four participating cities. To date, approximately 295 low
                         income neighborhoods have been upgraded, benefiting directly over 2.5 million people in low-income areas and indirectly 5 million more. The related primary and secondary
                         infrastructure improvements will benefit more than 4 million people. Progress to date includes 28,267 households with improved water connections and 409,000 households with
                         sewer connections and improved drainage (70,000 for Hai Phong; 46,000 for Nam Dinh; 248,000 for HCMC; 45,000 for Can Tho). The condition of primary and secondary
Public Disclosure Copy




                         infrastructure has also significantly improved with about 336 km of improved drains/sewers/roads and cleaned canals with embankments; and 5.9 ha of lakes cleaned and improved.
                         In addition, 49,402 micro loans have been made in these neighborhoods for housing improvement and 45,458 micro loans for income generation. Overall, the communities and local
                         authorities continue to express a high level of satisfaction with the progress and quality of implementation in all four cities. Considerable experiences has been gained and shared
                         between project PMUs and with other urban upgrading project in Mekong Region, especially on promoting community participation and contribution. The PMUs and city authorities
                         are hiring consultants to prepare Project Completion Report (PCR) and evaluation report expected to finalize by the end of December 2014.
