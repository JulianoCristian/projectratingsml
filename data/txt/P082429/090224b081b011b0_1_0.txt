                         Implementation Status Overview
                         The Project continues to make significant progress towards achieving its Project Development Objective (PDO). As of November 30, 2012, it has reached 75% of the revised end
                         target of the PDO indicator, with 472 municipalities with a hazard risk management program. The National Disaster Vulnerability Reduction Project (APL1) has been instrumental in
                         reforming the national policy and legal framework and strengthening the National Disaster Risk Management System and newly established DRM unit. Main achievements to date


                                                                                                                                                                                                          Page 1 of 5
                             The World Bank                                                                                                                                       Report No: ISR10046



                         include: (i) the modernization of the Volcanological and Seismic Monitoring networks. The Network is maintained by the Colombian Geological Service (SGC) and is comprised of 3
                         volcanological observatories and 380 volcanological stations and 136 seismic stations. The modernization process supported by this project has established an adequate monitoring
                         of the earthquake activity, ground deformation, gas chemistry, and other geophysical conditions and the dissemination of real-time warnings and emergency information to the
                         authorities and communities;

                         (ii) the integration of Disaster Risk Management in the planning process of 986 municipalities (90% of all the municipalities), with improved risk assessment to guide territorial
                         planning.
