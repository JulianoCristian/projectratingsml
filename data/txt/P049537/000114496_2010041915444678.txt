PROJECT AGREEMENT
AGREEMENT, dated March 5, 1999, between INTERNATIONAL BANK FOR RECONSTRUCTION 
AND DEVELOPMENT (the Bank) and  TRANSMISSION CORPORATION OF ANDHRA PRADESH LIMITED 
(APTRANSCO) and ANDHRA PRADESH POWER GENERATION CORPORATION LIMITED (APGENCO).
WHEREAS (A) by the Loan Agreement of even date herewith between India, acting by 
its President (the Borrower) and the Bank, the Bank has agreed to lend to the Borrower 
an amount of two hundred ten million dollars ($210,000,000), on the terms and 
conditions set forth in the Loan Agreement, but only on condition that APTRANSCO and 
APGENCO agree to undertake such obligations toward the Bank as are set forth in this 
Agreement; 
(B)
by the Andhra Pradesh Project Agreement of even date herewith between the 
Bank and the State of Andhra Pradesh (Andhra Pradesh), Andhra Pradesh has undertaken 
certain obligations towards the Bank as set forth in the Andhra Pradesh Project 
Agreement;
(C)
by the Subsidiary Loan Agreement to be entered into between Andhra 
Pradesh and APTRANSCO and APGENCO, a portion of the proceeds of the loan provided for 
under the Loan Agreement and made available to Andhra Pradesh will be relent to 
APTRANSCO and APGENCO on the terms and conditions set forth in said Subsidiary Loan 
Agreement; 
(D)
the Bank has received a letter dated November 12, 1998 from Andhra 
Pradesh enclosing a detailed policy statement approved by Andhra Pradesh designed to 
restructure, reform and develop the power sector in Andhra Pradesh (hereinafter the 
Reform Program or Program) and declaring Andhra Pradesh’s commitment to the execution 
Page  2
of such Program; and
WHEREAS APTRANSCO and APGENCO, in consideration of the Bank’s entering into the 
Loan Agreement with the Borrower, has agreed to undertake the obligations set forth in 
this Agreement;
NOW THEREFORE the parties hereto hereby agree as follows: 
ARTICLE I 
Definitions
Section 1.01. Unless the context otherwise requires, the several terms defined 
in the Loan Agreement  and in the General Conditions (as so defined) have the 
respective meanings therein set forth.
ARTICLE II 
Execution of the Project
Section 2.01. (a) APTRANSCO and APGENCO declare their commitment to the 
objectives of the Project as set forth in Schedule 2 to the Loan Agreement, and, to 
this end, APTRANSCO shall, subject to paragraph (e) of this Section and Section 2.01 
of the Andhra Pradesh Project Agreement, carry out Parts A, B, C and the relevant 
activities under Part D.2 of the Project and APGENCO shall carry out the relevant 
activities under Part D.2 of the Project, all with due diligence and efficiency and in 
conformity with appropriate administrative, financial, engineering, and technical 
practices, and shall provide, or cause to be provided, promptly as needed, the funds, 
facilities, services and other resources required for their respective Parts of the 
Project.
(b)
In carrying out Parts A, B and C of the Project, APTRANSCO shall at all 
times comply with the objectives, policies and procedures set out in the Social 
Policies and Procedures and in the Resettlement Action Plan.
(c)
Without limitation upon the provisions of paragraph (a) of this Section, 
and except as the Bank shall otherwise agree, works to be carried out by APTRANSCO 
under Parts A, B and C of the Project respectively shall meet the criteria set forth 
or referred to in the Schedule to this Agreement and APTRANSCO shall submit to the 
Bank for its approval, the works proposed to be financed out of the proceeds of the 
Loan including all details in respect of the criteria to be met and such other 
information as the Bank shall reasonably request.
(d)
APTRANSCO shall carry out the works approved for financing pursuant to 
the provision of paragraph (c) of this Section 2.01 under the following arrangements: 
(i)
for activities under Parts A, B and C of the Project, in accordance with 
technical specifications and standards satisfactory to the Bank; and (ii) for 
activities under Parts A and B of the Project, in accordance with the objectives, 
policies, principles and time schedule of the environmental management plan, the 
resettlement and rehabilitation plan and the materials management plan formulated 
pursuant to the provisions of Part I (c) of the Schedule  to this Agreement and in 
accordance with the Environmental Framework and Safeguards for Transmission Projects, 
the Environment Management Plan, the Social Policies and Procedures and the 
Resettlement Action Plan  as the case may be. 
(e)
APTRANSCO shall be responsible for carrying out the activities under Part 
B of the Project and the relevant activities under Part C of the Project only until 
such time that Distribution Companies are established as set out in Section 2.06 (a) 
(i) of this Agreement and the transfer scheme referred to in Section 2.06 (a) (ii) of 
this Agreement is implemented.
Section 2.02. Except as the Bank shall otherwise agree, procurement of the 
consultants’ services required for the Project and to be financed out of the proceeds 
of the Loan shall be governed by the provisions of Schedule 4 to the Loan Agreement.
Section 2.03. (a) APTRANSCO and APGENCO shall carry out the obligations set 
forth in Sections 9.04, 9.05, 9.06, 9.07, 9.08 and 9.09 of the General Conditions 
Page  3

reports, maintenance and land acquisition) in respect of their respective Parts of the 
Project.
(b)

limitation thereto, APTRANSCO and APGENCO shall: (i)
prepare, on the basis of 
guidelines acceptable to the Bank, and furnish to the Bank not later than six (6) 
months after the Closing Date or such later date as may be agreed for this purpose 
between the Bank and APTRANSCO and APGENCO, a plan for the future operation of their 
respective  Parts of the Project; and (ii)
afford the Bank a reasonable opportunity 
to exchange views with APTRANSCO and APGENCO on the said plan.
Section 2.04. APTRANSCO and APGENCO shall duly perform all its obligations under 
the Subsidiary Loan Agreement. Except as the Bank shall otherwise agree, APTRANSCO and 
APGENCO shall not take or concur in any action which would have the effect of 
assigning, amending, abrogating or waiving the Subsidiary Loan Agreement or any 
provision thereof.
Section 2.05. APTRANSCO and APGENCO shall, with the participation of Andhra 
Pradesh, by September 30, 1999 submit to the Bank a detailed plan, agreed between its 
lenders and suppliers, for the purpose of rescheduling APTRANSCO’s and APGENCO’s 
overdue payables, current debt from lenders, debt service in default, and debt service 
falling due from FY 1999 to FY 2002.

each wholly-owned by APTRANSCO, which shall be responsible for carrying out 
electricity distribution and related activities in Andhra Pradesh, including carrying 
out: (A) the activities under Part B of the Project; and (B) the relevant activities 
under Parts C and  D of the Project, all subject to paragraphs (b) and (c) of this 
Section; and (ii) prepare and implement a transfer scheme, in accordance with the 
provisions of the Reform Act and satisfactory to the Bank, for the purpose of 
transferring to the Distribution Companies its distribution undertaking, including 
such property, interest in property, rights, liabilities and personnel relating

on or about the date that the transfer scheme referred to in Section 2.06 
(a) (ii) of this Agreement is implemented: 
(i)
agree with Andhra Pradesh on necessary revisions to the Subsidiary 
Loan

protect the interest

with each Distribution Company.

Section 2.09. APTRANSCO and APGENCO shall: 
(a)
maintain policies and procedures adequate to enable each of them to 
monitor and evaluate on an ongoing basis, in accordance with indicators satisfactory

Section 2.10. (a) APTRANSCO and APGENCO shall, at the request of the Bank,

the respective Subsidiary Loan Agreement. 
ARTICLE III 
Management and Operations of APTRANSCO and APGENCO
Section 3.01. APTRANSCO and APGENCO shall:  
(a)
carry on their operations and conduct their affairs in accordance with 
sound administrative, financial and public utility practices under the supervision of 
qualified and experienced management assisted by competent staff in adequate numbers;  
(b)
at all times operate and maintain their plant, machinery, equipment and 
other property, and from time to time, promptly as needed, make all necessary repairs 
and renewals thereof, all in accordance with sound engineering, financial and public 
utility practices; and  
(c)
take out and maintain with responsible insurers, or make other provision 
satisfactory to the Bank for, insurance against such risks and in such amounts as 
shall be consistent with appropriate practice.

SCHEDULE
Eligibility Criteria
The following eligibility criteria shall apply for activities to be carried out 
under Parts A, B and C of the Project by APTRANSCO referred to in Section 2.01 (c) of 
this Agreement and for activities to be carried out by End-Users under Part C of the 
Project referred to in Section 2.02 (h) (i) of the Andhra Pradesh Project Agreement.
Part I:
Parts A and B of the Project
(a)
The justification (in the context of the overall transmission and 
distribution development plan) and technical feasibility of the proposed transmission 
Page  10
and distribution investment shall have been established to the satisfaction of the 
Bank;
(b)
all clearances/approvals including environmental clearances shall have 
been obtained from the relevant ministries, departments, agencies or other authorities 
of the Borrower and Andhra Pradesh and documented in the project report;
(c)
an environmental assessment shall have been carried out,  and an 
Environmental Management Plan, if needed, based on the environment assessment report 
and the Environment Framework and Safeguards for Transmission Projects, and a plan for 
the handling of materials to be replaced in the rehabilitation program, satisfactory 
to the Bank, shall have been prepared; 
(d)
a Resettlement Action Plan in accordance with the Social Policy and 
Procedures, satisfactory to the Bank, shall have been prepared; and
(e)
a procurement plan shall have been prepared in accordance with the 
overall procurement strategy, namely bulk procurement of main items, turnkey 
construction by prequalified contractors (except for these owner-supplied main items).
Part II:
Part C of the Project
(a)
Justification and technical feasibility of the proposed activity shall 
have been established to the satisfaction of the Bank;
(b)
financing and procurement plans acceptable to the Bank shall have been 
prepared; and
(c)
activities below a threshold value of $200,000 equivalent shall not 
require prior Bank approval.
