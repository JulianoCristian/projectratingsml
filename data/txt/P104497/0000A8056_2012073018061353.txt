                         Implementation Status Overview
                         The project was extended for one more year to allow the completion of urban road works. The project disbursed US$39.7 million in the fiscal year ending June 30, 2012.

                         Significant results have already been achieved under the project: 26 141 schools received payments; this has positively impacted the learning conditions of over 13 million pupils. The
                         rehabilitation of 17 km of roads in Kinshasa was completed allowing 640,000 people living in isolated neighborhoods to have access to all-season roads. With regards to the malaria
                         component, the number of people benefiting from bed nets in Kinshasa is 1,090,000 compared to a goal of 1,010,000, exceeding the original target (65 percent versus 60 percent of
                         children under five sleeping under bed net). Some 2,800,000 bed nets are being distributed in the Bandundu province and it is expected to benefit more than 5 million people. The

                         ongoing works to expand the distribution network from the recently-rehabilitated Lukaya water treatment plant (in the Western part of Kinshasa) will enable 150,000 people living in
                         low-income Kinshasa neighborhoods to have access to safe water. These works are expected to be completed by the end of September 2012.
