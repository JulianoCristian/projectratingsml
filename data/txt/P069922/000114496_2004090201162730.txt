WHEREAS (A)  the Bank and the International Development Association (the 
Association) have received a letter dated April 27, 2000, from the Borrower to the 
Bank and to the Association describing the Borrower’s emergency recovery and disaster 
management program which consists of a series of activities to strengthen the 
Borrower’s capacity to respond to, and manage, natural disasters; 
Page  8
Page  9
(B)
the Borrower, having satisfied itself as to the feasibility and priority 
of the project described in Schedule 2 to this Agreement (the Project), has requested 
the Bank to assist in the financing of the Project;
Page  10
Page  11
(C)
the Borrower has also requested the Association to provide additional 
financial assistance towards the financing of the Project and, by the Development 
Credit Agreement of the same date herewith entered into between the Borrower and the 
Association (the Development Credit Agreement), the Association is agreeing to provide 
such assistance in an aggregate principal amount equivalent to three million eight 
hundred Special Drawing Rights (SDR 3,800,000) (the Credit); 
Page  12
Page  13
(D)
the Borrower and the Bank intend, to the extent practicable, that the 
proceeds of the Credit be disbursed on account of expenditures for the Project before 
disbursements of the proceeds of the loan provided for in this Agreement (the Loan) 
are made; and

WHEREAS the Bank has agreed, on the basis, inter alia, of the foregoing, to

SCHEDULE 2
Page  220
Page  221
Description of the Project
Page  222
Page  223
The objective of the Project is to strengthen the Borrower’s resilience, 
preparedness, and ability to respond to the effects of natural disasters.
Page  224
Page  225
The Project consists of the following parts, subject to such modifications 
thereof as the Borrower and the Bank may agree upon from time to time, to achieve such 
objective:
Page  226
Part A:
Strengthening Disaster Management Capacity
1.
Strengthening of NERO’s effectiveness through: (a) the review and updating of 
the Borrower’s emergency and disaster management procedures; (b) the rehabilitation 
and expansion of NERO’s emergency operations center; (c) the provision of equipment 
and supplies to NERO for both daily and emergency operations; and (d) the preparation 
and implementation of: (i) a comprehensive disaster preparedness and management 
training program for NERO’s staff and other related agencies; and (ii) a public 
awareness program on disaster management.
2.
Development of an emergency communications system by: (a) strengthening NERO’s 
capacity to receive and transmit data and information and to communicate effectively 
with all stakeholders during and after an emergency; and (b) strengthening the early 
warning capacity of the Borrower’s Meteorological Office through the provision of 
technical assistance, equipment, and staff training.
3.
Carrying out of a program to: (a) retrofit high priority shelters; (b) develop a 
comprehensive national shelter program; and (c) design and construct a prototype 
multipurpose shelter. 
4.
Strengthening of MCWPU’s disaster management response and environmental 
assessment and monitoring capacity through:  (a) the acquisition of prefabricated 
metallic bridge components to enhance the Borrower’s ability to carry out emergency 
repairs to re-establish access after disasters and training with respect thereto; and 
(b) the provision of technical assistance and training. 
Part B:
Preventive Physical Mitigation
1.
Improvement of sea defenses through: (a) the construction of sea defenses at 
Grand Mal Bay and the rehabilitation of the adjacent roads along the Western Coast 
Main Road, including the carrying out of a supplemental environmental study to 
identify the borrow pits and other material sites that will be used for the 
construction of such sea defenses; and (b) the carrying out of coastal protection and 
sea defenses studies.
2.
Carrying out of a detailed feasibility study, including detailed engineering and 
design studies and an environmental impact assessment, for the improvement of drainage 
and flooding control at the Tanteen and Grand Anse areas of St. Georges and Grenville.
3.
Strengthening of MCWPU’s capacity to protect against landslides, subsidence and 
flood hazards, through training and the carrying out of an engineering feasibility 
study on slope protection, river training and flood risks in the vicinity of all the 
main roads, which study shall include the relevant environmental assessment.
Part C:
Project Management
1.
Strengthening of the PCU including the provision of technical assistance, 
training  and equipment for the operation of said unit.
2.
Carrying out of the technical and financial audits required under the Project.
* * *
The Project is expected to be completed by November 15, 2003.
