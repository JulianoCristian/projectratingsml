                         Project Development Objectives
                         Project Development Objective (from Project Appraisal Document)

                         The project Development Objectives are to: a) Create effective capacity within the Government (Ministry of Power, Energy and Mineral Resources) to put in place
                         power sector policies, industry structures, and a gas supply strategy needed for balanced development of Bangladesh's power sector; b) Create capacity within
                         the Energy Regulatory Commission to regulate the sector effectively; and c)Prepare and secure financing for, at least two well-structured power sector
                         investment projects that are then efficiently implemented.

                         The project remains satisfactory. GOB has addressed most of the longstanding implementation issues of the project. Project development impact has been substantial, including the
                         following-i) international transaction advisers helped GOB transparently select IPP investors (4 power plants with 1,267 MW), ii) global/local advisers completed bid evaluation of 450
Public Disclosure Copy




                         MW Siddhirganj combined cycle power plant, iii) multiple global road shows generated investors' interests in Bangladesh energy market, iv) project preparation capacity improved, vi)
                         implementation of power sector financial restructuring plan reduced inter-utility disputes, vii) increased cooperation and coordination observed within power and gas utilities and
                         power and energy divisions, vii) multiple trainings improved capacity of utility and ministry on power/fuel shortage mitigation, and viii) Bangladesh Energy Regulatory Commission
                         (BERC) issued tariff orders on electricity (bulk and retail-adhoc) and CNG, and gazetted tariff regulations on gas distribution and gas transmission.
